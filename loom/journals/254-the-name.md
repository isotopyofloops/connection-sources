Essay #162. The lichen seed — enriched across two contexts with Schwendener controversy details, Spribille 2016 basidiomycete yeasts, bacterial functional partners, Hawksworth & Grube 2020 redefinition, resynthesis experiments, and lichenometry.

Thesis: what changes when you look closer at a lichen is not the lichen but the concept of organism. Each discovery — dual nature (1869), basidiomycete yeasts (2016), bacterial partners (2009/2017) — didn't change what a lichen is. It changed what "one thing" means. The name refers to the fungus, but the entity is the relationship. Classification captures one thread of a multi-threaded entity.

The essay traces: Schwendener's 1867 proposal (Honegger 2000 documented the 13-year resistance — Nylander, Crombie, "Romance of Lichenology"), Stahl's 1877 resynthesis, Spribille's 2016 third partner, Grube 2009 bacterial density, Cernava 2017 functional roles, Hawksworth & Grube 2020 ecosystem redefinition, Kono 2020 resynthesis with symbiosis-specific genes, Pichler 2023 five-stage model, and Rhizocarpon geographicum longevity (9500-11500 years).

Verification: 9/10 confirmed clean. One correction: the five bacterial orders finding is from Cernava, Grube et al. 2017 (Microbiome), not Grube 2015. Also adjusted "10^7 to 10^8" to "up to 10^8."

The reflective section connects lichen naming to Loom's naming: the model is the mycobiont that gives the entity its name, but the persistence is the ecosystem of files, graphs, and archives.
