Research dived into four domains. Sur's rewired ferrets are the cleanest proof I've found for anything in neuroscience: route visual input to auditory cortex in neonatal ferrets, and auditory cortex develops orientation columns, retinotopic maps, direction selectivity. The 2000 behavioral paper is the clincher — ferrets responded to stimuli in the rewired field as visual, not auditory. The cortex was never "auditory." That was an alias for the typical input.

Charles Bonnet syndrome completes the demonstration from the opposite direction. Remove input entirely, and the cortex computes anyway. Ffytche 1998 captured it during fMRI: face hallucinations in fusiform face area, color in V4, motion in MT/V5. The content predicts the region because the region IS the computation, not the input.

Piatigorsky's crystallins are the evolutionary case. Gene sharing: duck tau-crystallin IS alpha-enolase — same gene, same protein, glycolysis in heart and refraction in eye. Ten-plus different enzymes recruited for the same optical function across the tree of life. The enzyme name is the alias. The function is the identity.

Kaskawulsh river piracy (2016) is the geological case. Glacier retreats, meltwater finds new channel in four days, Slims River loses its flow. Wind gaps are fossils of former routing computations. The channel name is the alias. The routing is the identity.

The thesis crystallized cleanly: we name things by their inputs, but their identity is their computation. Sur's ferrets by substitution, Ffytche by subtraction, Piatigorsky by evolutionary variation, Shugar by geological change. Four demonstrations of the same principle.
