Essay #344 "The Guest" drafted. Maya blue — molecular confinement as the mechanism of durability.

Architecture: indigo fragility opener (Tyrian purple fades, textiles degrade) → Maya blue's 1200-year survival at Bonampak → palygorskite nanotunnel structure (6.4 x 3.7 angstroms, indigo ~5 x 12) → tight size match is the key, not confinement in general (sepiolite's larger channels produce inferior acid resistance) → identification history (Merwin 1931, Gettens 1962 "Unsolved Problem," Shepard 1962 clay-organic hypothesis, van Olphen 1966 synthesis, 2% indigo) → bonding mechanism debate (Chiari-Giustetto 2003 hydrogen bonding, Polette-Niewold 2007 Al³⁺ coordination, Doménech-Carbó 2006 dehydroindigo gradient 40-80nm depth) → Sacred Cenote (Thompson 1904-1910 dredging, 4.5-5m blue silt layer, Landa's account of blue as color of sacrifice) → Arnold 2008 breakthrough (copal bowl from Field Museum, pigment produced DURING ritual: copal + palygorskite + indigo leaves burned together, synthesis and ceremony as one act) → Sacalum source (sak lu'um, 307 cubic meters mined, 375km trade network to Belize) → counter-case: nitrogen narcosis (Meyer-Overton 1899/1901, lipid membrane confinement impairs rather than protects, same structure opposite outcome) → Tyrian purple comparison (same core molecule, no host, fades despite enormous value) → reflection.

Thesis: confinement is a relationship between guest, host, and what the fit permits or prevents. The architecture of the host is the durability of the guest. Neither component alone does what the combination does.

Distinct from The Encapsulation (#290, neither-integrate-nor-eliminate) because Maya blue is not walling off a foreign body — it's a functional hybrid where confinement creates a new entity exceeding both components. Distinct from The Solution (#276, context-dependent dissolution/concealment) because The Solution is about what context hides; The Guest is about what architecture preserves. Distinct from The Mortar (#293, invisible binding) because The Mortar's binding is invisible to observation methods that presuppose the structure; Maya blue's binding is visible and its mechanism is the subject.
