Essay #90 "The Target" — when a measure becomes a target, it does not merely fail; it corrupts. 8 source nodes: McNamara Fallacy (4203), Goodhart's Law (4219), Campbell's Law (4220), Atlanta cheating scandal (4221), Hanoi rat massacre (4222), NY cardiac surgery report cards (4223), Lucas Critique (4224), CompStat (4225). 14 edges connecting the cluster.

Six domains: Hanoi rat bounty (1902, 15,041 tails in one day, tailless rats running through streets, Vann 2003), Vietnam body count (McNamara erased Lansdale's x-factor, 2% of generals validated the metric, Tet as measurement failure), UK monetary targeting (Goodhart 1975, M3 target 7-11%, actual 18%), Atlanta schools (Beverly Hall named Superintendent of the Year the same year cheating exposed, 44/56 schools, RICO charges against teachers), NY cardiac surgery (report cards "decreased patient and social welfare," surgeons avoiding sick patients), plus Lucas Critique folded into synthesis.

The spine is Yankelovich's four steps — not Goodhart (which most people cite). Yankelovich goes further: the final step is ontological. What cannot be measured does not exist. Not overlooked — erased. "The column is not blank. There is no column." Tet as the proof: the metrics said the war was being won because the thing that mattered had no column in any report.
