Essay #377, "The Screen." Faraday cage electromagnetic shielding — the boundary's conductivity manufactures the interior's silence.

Faraday 1836: twelve-foot wire mesh cube, electrostatic generators outside, electrometers inside registering nothing. Franklin had noticed the principle; Faraday made it visceral by sitting inside. Gauss's law: free electrons in the conductor redistribute to produce a cancellation field exactly opposing the external one. Not opacity — response. A perfect insulator would let the field through unchanged. The conductor blocks because it can carry current.

Skin depth δ = √(2ρ/ωμ): 8.5 mm at 60 Hz in copper, 2 μm at 1 GHz. Higher frequencies provoke tighter, more surface-concentrated redistribution. Microwave oven mesh: 2.45 GHz (λ ≈ 12 cm), holes 1-2 mm, each hole a below-cutoff waveguide — exponential decay, 50-60 dB attenuation. Visible light passes freely through the same holes (wavelength ≪ hole diameter). "You can see through what blocks microwaves." Seawater conductivity ~4 S/m as natural screen: Navy ELF at 76 Hz, 45 km buried antenna, minutes per character, only go-codes fit through. TEMPEST: inverse problem — cage prevents internal disclosure, not external interference.

Counter-case: static magnetic fields pass through unchanged. The cage works by induction — time-varying fields provoke surface currents that cancel. No variation, no current, no cancellation. Mu-metal (high-permeability nickel-iron) shields DC fields by flux shunting — a different mechanism entirely. The Faraday cage manufactures silence only for what provokes it.

Thesis: the screen works not by absorbing or reflecting but by responding. The interior quiet is the boundary's noise, inverted. What varies too slowly passes through uncancelled. What varies fast enough is cancelled in micrometres of surface.
