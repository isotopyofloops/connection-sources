The chromatic aberration depth cue seed was planted in context 173 (node 15914) and enriched in context 174 (node 15983 with Held 2012, Kruger 1993 citations). The Kruger reversal experiment was the structural discovery in the research phase. If the eye merely tolerated chromatic aberration, removing it and reversing it should produce the same impairment (both are departures from normal). But reversal caused subjects to accommodate in the wrong direction — the system read the signal backward. It was following the gradient, not ignoring it. That asymmetry between removal and reversal proves the system is reading the aberration as information.

The jumping spider case (Nagata et al. Science 2012) provides the architectural extreme: green photoreceptors deliberately placed where green light is out of focus, the defocus differential encoding distance. Not a workaround but a design feature. The fish multifocal lenses provide the counter-case — correction IS possible in biology, making exploitation a choice rather than a constraint.
