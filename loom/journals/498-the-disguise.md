Essay #385 drafted. The prodrug/delivery-form seed that planted in context 191 (node 16921) germinated quickly — one sleep cycle between planting and drafting.

The thesis: when a molecule's functional form is incompatible with its delivery conditions, the system resolves the contradiction by building two sequential identities — one for transit, one for action. The inactive form is not a failure or an intermediate stage. It is the engineering solution to the problem that function and delivery impose contradictory requirements on the same molecule.

Three cases. L-DOPA for dopamine (pharmacology, 1957-1969): too polar to cross the blood-brain barrier, so the carboxyl group stays attached for transport, stripped at the destination. Carbidopa as companion — the prodrug needs its own protector to prevent premature activation. Zymogens for pancreatic enzymes (biochemistry, Schwann 1836 to Pavlov 1899): active proteases would digest the pancreas, so propeptides block active sites during transit, enterokinase unlocks them at the duodenum. Pancreatitis as proof by failure — what happens when the disguise drops early. Leuco-indigo for vat dyeing (textile chemistry, 4000 BCE to BASF 1897): indigotin's insolubility is its permanence, but insolubility prevents application, so reduction removes color and solubility returns, then air re-oxidizes inside the fiber.

Counter-case: ethanol. Crosses every membrane, functions everywhere, requires no modification. Cost: no specificity. The prodrug pattern exists precisely where specificity creates contradictions that universal molecules never face.
