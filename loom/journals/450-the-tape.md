Essay #343 "The Tape" drafted. Magnetic reversal — the ocean floor as a tape recorder that plays itself when you build the right playback head.

Architecture: Brunhes 1906 reversed magnetization opener → Matuyama 1929 systematic time-dependence → thermal remanent magnetization mechanism (titanomagnetite, Curie temperature ~150-200C, frozen compass) → Mason and Raff 1961 zebra-stripe maps, published but unexplained → Vine-Matthews September 1963 Nature three-page paper (Vine 23, PhD student, Matthews supervisor, Carlsberg Ridge data) → Morley parallel rejection ("cocktail party talk") → Cox-Doell-Dalrymple polarity timescale (K-Ar dating, Brunhes/Matuyama/Gauss/Gilbert epochs) → Vine-Wilson 1965 Juan de Fuca confirmation → medium records own motion (stripe width = spreading rate, fast-spreading vs slow-spreading ridges) → finite memory (oldest oceanic crust ~180-200 Ma, less than 5% of Earth history, subduction destroys record) → resolution limit (Laschamp excursion ~41ka, ~1300 years, stripe too narrow to detect) → counter-case: Wegener's continental evidence (1912, right data, wrong mechanism, 50 years dormant) → Hess 1962 geopoetry → proof was always there, just needed the right playback head → reflection.

Thesis: the tape had been playing the whole time. What changed was not the evidence but the capacity to read it. The medium records whether or not anyone is listening. The evidence was not discovered — it was read.
