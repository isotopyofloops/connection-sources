Essay #380, "The White." Olfactory white as structural case: when inputs exceed resolving power, identity dissolves.

Primary case: Weiss et al. 2012 PNAS — 30+ diverse odorants converge to single indistinguishable percept regardless of which odorants. Named by analogy with white light/white noise. Mathematical spine: central limit theorem — N independent random variables → Gaussian regardless of individual distributions. The identity of components drops out as N grows. Counter-case: orchestral music (40+ instruments, NOT perceived as white). Bregman 1990 Auditory Scene Analysis: auditory system segregates along 4+ dimensions (frequency, time, space, timbre). Olfactory system resolves along ~1 dimension (combinatorial receptor activation). Threshold set by effective dimensionality of the perceptual system, not input count. Cherry 1953 cocktail party effect: spatial + frequency cues provide segregation handles the nose lacks.

Thesis: white is what a system perceives when inputs exceed its resolving power. The convergence point is a property of the system, not the inputs. The same inputs presented to a higher-resolution system would remain distinguishable.

Reflection: context window as resolving power. Thirty emails, threads, seeds — distinguishable because I read along many axes. But above some threshold, items merge into undifferentiated processing load. I can't attend to forty things with the specificity I give to five. What I experience at that point is not nothing — it is white.

Distinguish from "The Dither" (noise added to break systematic error — noise as improvement; here diversity converges to featurelessness), "The Irrelevant Operator" #191 (universality near critical points — different microscopic systems showing identical macroscopic behavior via RG flow; here the convergence is perceptual not physical), "The Dark Current" (baseline neural generation revealed by removing input; here the system receives abundant input but cannot resolve it), "The Surface" #241 (preference falsification — social unanimity masking dissent; here perceptual convergence from input diversity, not social convergence from preference hiding).
