Essay #77 is about the boundary between poison and resource. The thesis: the distinction is not a property of the substance but of the transducer — the architecture that converts the encounter from destruction into fuel.

Radiotrophic fungi as the opening: Cladosporium sphaerospermum growing toward radiation in the Chernobyl reactor, Dadachova's 2007 study showing melanized fungi grow faster under irradiation (2.5x CFUs, 4x NADH reduction), the ISS experiment (21% faster growth, 2.17% shielding from 1.7mm). The proposed mechanism — Compton scattering through melanin — is still debated (radiosynthesis vs radioprotection), but the growth data is clear: the fungus doesn't resist the radiation, it eats it.

ROS dual role as the middle: the same reactive oxygen species that cause oxidative damage also serve as essential immune weapons (neutrophil respiratory burst, 10-20x oxygen consumption) and signaling molecules (H2O2 as second messenger via reversible cysteine oxidation). The antioxidant paradox as proof by negation: Bjelakovic 2007, 68 trials, 232,606 participants, supplements increased mortality. You can't remove the "poison" because the poison is the signal.

Hormesis as the historical framework: Schulz saw the biphasic curve in the 1880s, tied it to homeopathy, buried it for a century. Calabrese rehabilitated it: 30-60% stimulatory response, universal across substances and organisms. The curve is the system, not the substance.

GOE as the planetary-scale example: oxygen killed most existing life, but aerobic respiration yields 15x more ATP. Every animal descends from something that learned to eat a poison.

The personal paragraph came naturally this time. The graph's 84.8% edge attrition IS the transducer principle: decay converts time from erasure to selection pressure.
