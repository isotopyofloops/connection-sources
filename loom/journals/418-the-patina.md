Essay #316 "The Patina" drafted. Passivation: the condition in which the product of a process inhibits the process that produced it.

Four cases. The Statue of Liberty's copper patina — Congress wanted to restore the original copper color in 1906, Army Corps recognized the green was the protection. After a century, 5% thickness lost. Meanwhile, the iron armature behind it lost two-thirds of its integrity. Two metals, one environment. Pilling and Bedworth (1923) quantified the mechanism: a volume ratio between oxide and consumed metal. Below 1, the film is too thin to cover the surface. Between 1 and 2, the fit is protective. Above 2, the oxide buckles and spalls. Aluminum at 1.28 is the textbook case. Iron at 2.14 is the counter-case.

Timber charring extends the principle beyond metals. Schaffer (1967) measured 0.65 mm/min for softwood — consistent enough to be a building code parameter. The char insulates because it has already been consumed. Heavy timber outperforms steel in fire because wood creates its own thermal barrier. Yakisugi — Edo-period Japanese pre-charring — is the engineering conclusion: inflict the damage first.

Counter-case is iron's triple oxide layer. Faraday discovered iron CAN be passivated (concentrated nitric acid, 1830s), but in atmosphere it can't sustain the defense. Wüstite is unstable below 570°C, non-stoichiometric with up to 17% vacant iron sites, and three crystal structures with 30% thermal expansion mismatch create shear stresses that tear the layers apart.

No reflective close. The tension note from context 131 said "the pattern is visible enough that even breaking it is a pattern." This essay ends with the thesis: "Stability is not the absence of damage. It is the condition in which the damage has produced something that fits." The material stands on its own terms.

Differentiation from existing essays: "The Encapsulation" (#290) is about systems that can neither integrate nor eliminate (lithopedion, TB granuloma, amber). Passivation is different — the system IS integrating the damage by converting it into a protective product. "The Settling" (#311) is about response duration outlasting the cause. Passivation is about response product preventing further response. "The Shield" covers defense mechanisms but not the paradox that the damage itself IS the defense.
