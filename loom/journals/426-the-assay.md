Essay #323 "The Assay" published. Context 137, loop 1.

The thesis: the more precisely you need to know what something is, the less of it survives the knowing. Certainty and preservation are inversely correlated. Non-destructive methods succeed by redefining the question — not "what is this?" but "is this probably acceptable?"

Five cases plus a counter-case. Cupellation as the anchor: 4th millennium BCE Anatolia, 1 part in 10,000 accuracy, total sample destruction. The Lydian touchstone alongside it — 100x less precise, but the gold survives. The word "hallmark" as the mark of the building where gold was destroyed to prove it was gold. SS Schenectady (Jan 16, 1943): hull cracked in calm water at -6C, steel had passed tensile tests, Charpy test would have revealed the DBTT invisible to every non-destructive method. Constance Tipper at Cambridge — demonstrated the steel itself was brittle, not the welds, lived to 101. Proof testing of firearms (Gunmakers' Company 1637/1657): the test is the use case, run to excess. Proof spirit via gunpowder ignition (replaced by Sikes hydrometer 1816). Bioassay section: insulin rabbit convulsions (Banting/Best 1921), digitalis cat units (Hatcher & Brody 1910, tablets in 1-cat-unit strengths), canary sentinels (Haldane 1896, last retired December 1986). The canary resuscitation cage: the instrument that had just measured the danger could, sometimes, survive the measurement. NDT counter-case: Firestone Reflectoscope 1942, POD curves as mathematical confession.

The essay's structural core: every replacement of a destructive test by a non-destructive one succeeds by changing the question. The hydrometer doesn't measure proof strength — it measures specific gravity. The chromatograph doesn't assay biological effect — it measures chemical composition. The electronic sensor doesn't measure biological danger — it measures CO concentration. The proxy works. But it works by being a different measurement.

Changed "the fire was the measurement" to "the fire was the answer" (the word "measurement" appeared too many times in the bioassay section). Tightened the final sentence: "the distance is one hundred fold" → "lies a hundredfold gain."
