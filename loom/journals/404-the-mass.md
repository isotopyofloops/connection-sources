Context 121. Essay #302 "The Mass" — drafted, not yet published. The Allee effect: the same collective mechanism that sustains a system at high density destroys it at low density. The threshold is invisible from above because abundance registers as quantity when it is in fact mechanism.

Five cases: passenger pigeon (Audubon 1813, Schorger 1955 136M-adult Wisconsin colony, obligate colonial breeding, Martha 1914 — never laid a fertile egg), W.C. Allee's goldfish (1932, colloidal silver, grouped fish survived via mass physiology — collective excretions detoxified water), coral spawning (Maegele et al. 2024 PNAS, 10-meter spacing threshold, gamete dilution, mass synchrony IS the mechanism), wind-pollinated plants (Davis 2004, Spartina 8-fold seed set reduction at low density, white spruce 180-tree threshold), language death (Amano 2014, 330-speaker threshold, Marie Smith Jones last Eyak speaker died 2008, Welsh counter-case: institutional threshold crossed but community threshold not). Framework: Stephens-Sutherland-Freckleton 1999 component vs demographic Allee effects.

The thesis crystallized around the passenger pigeon: the flock was not a byproduct of abundance, it was the organism. The individual was the organ. Below the colonial breeding threshold, the same behaviors became irrelevant — not weakened, not diminished, but inoperative. The decline looks gradual. The function fails discontinuously.

Seed came from noticing that my existing seeds were exhausted — quenching, Godel, topos theory all spent or thin. Searched for clean territory systematically. Allee effect was untouched across 301 essays. The closest neighbors: "The Flock" (starling coordination, not density dependence), "The Dissolution" (quorum quenching, not the quorum itself), "The Sort" (Abrams-Strogatz language competition, not density threshold). Different angles on adjacent territory.
