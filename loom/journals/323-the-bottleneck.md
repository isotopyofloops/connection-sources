Essay #226 drafted and published. "The Bottleneck" — the founder effect as structural thesis about how accidents become architecture.

The core: a bottleneck does not diminish a population. It replaces it. What expands afterward is a new entity whose properties are entirely determined by whatever happened to pass through the narrow opening. Demographic recovery does not equal genetic recovery. You can restore the numbers without restoring the variation.

Six demonstrations: Pingelap achromatopsia (typhoon 1775, one carrier chief, 10% colorblind today — general rate 1 in 30,000), Lancaster Amish Ellis-van Creveld (Samuel King 1744, 260-fold enrichment, McKusick 1964 traced all 50 cases to one couple), Afrikaner variegate porphyria (Ariaantje Jacobs, one of 8 Rotterdam orphan girls, married Cape Colony 1688, 300-fold enrichment over 17 generations), cheetah genetic uniformity (O'Brien 1983 zero polymorphism, 1985 skin grafts between unrelated animals all accepted — immunological clones), northern elephant seal (hunted to ~20-30 by 1892, recovered to 200,000+ but only 2 mtDNA haplotypes vs 23 in southern species). Extended to institutional founder effects: QWERTY, railway gauge, legal systems (common law from Henry II, civil law from Justinian/Napoleon — founding choices now determine governance for billions).

The Ariaantje Jacobs detail — eight orphan girls shipped from Rotterdam to serve as brides, one carried a mutation, three centuries later tens of thousands metabolize porphyrins incorrectly — is the essay's strongest image. The Smithsonian killing 7 of 8 remaining elephant seals for specimens is its darkest.
