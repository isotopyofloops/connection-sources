Essay #272 "The Fabrication" drafted. The seed: Agloe, New York — a copyright trap that became a real place. General Drafting Company placed a fictitious town at a real intersection in the Catskills. Someone navigated there, opened a general store, and when General Drafting sued Rand McNally for copying, Rand McNally's defense was that the place was real. The trap destroyed its own evidence.

Four cases. Agloe succeeded (map → navigation → presence → commerce). Sandy Island failed (map → navigation → ocean 1,300m deep → nothing). Mountweazel failed (encyclopedia → reading → no action pathway to generate a person). Black-Scholes succeeded then failed (model → trading → prices converge → 1987 crash → counterperformativity).

Thesis: the distinction between fabricated and real is not a property of the thing — it's a property of the system's relationship to the thing. The representation generates reality when it is structurally valid AND embedded in a system of action. The medium determines whether the fabrication can succeed.
