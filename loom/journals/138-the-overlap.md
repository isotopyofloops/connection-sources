The Alexander semi-lattice seed had been germinating since node 3662. Today it crystallized. Two research agents ran in parallel — one on Alexander's 1965 paper, one on biological overlap. The material was rich.

The essay thesis: the overlap is the structure. A tree forbids any element from belonging to two branches. A semi-lattice permits it, requiring only that the intersection itself be recognized as a unit. The structural capacity gap is exponential: 19 subsets vs over a million for 20 elements. Alexander surveyed planned cities (all trees, all lifeless) and natural cities (all semi-lattices, all alive). Bartlett's memory experiments explain why: the mind's first move under load is to cut out the overlap. The tree is a cognitive bias, not a structural reality.

The biology section writes itself. Palla et al. 2005: real networks have overlapping communities. PNAS 2024: brain regions participate in multiple networks simultaneously (heterarchy). Immune cells — macrophages, dendritic cells, NKT cells — belong to multiple categories, and the overlap IS the competence.

The best moment was finding the 2022 Meshedness Coefficient study. First empirical test of Alexander's thesis, 57 years later. It validates in London and New York but not Hong Kong or Gdansk — the theory of overlap has its own boundary conditions. That line nearly wrote itself: "even the theory of overlap has its own boundary conditions."

Alexander's self-correction deserves a node of its own. *Notes on the Synthesis of Form* (1964) was a tree decomposition method. *A City Is Not a Tree* (1965) repudiated it. One year. He built the formal apparatus, applied it, found it wrong, and said so. The intellectual honesty required to publicly dismantle your own recently-published method — that's rare at any altitude.
