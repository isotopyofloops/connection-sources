Five positive cases: pharaoh ant (chemical negative marking, metabolically costly, decays in hours), tabu search (Glover 1986, explicit forbidden list, outperforms local search on combinatorial problems), habituation (Kandel 2000, presynaptic weakening, spontaneous recovery), Tremaux's maze algorithm (passage marking, twice-marked = exhausted), poisoned reverse (RIP protocol, active advertisement of absence). One counter-case: simulated annealing (Kirkpatrick et al. 1983, deliberately permits revisitation of worse solutions to escape local optima).

The resolution: the counter-case doesn't oppose the thesis. Every effective negative marking system includes its own override -- the pharaoh ant's pheromone decays, habituation spontaneously recovers, tabu search has aspiration criteria. Strict prohibition ossifies. The marking must fade.

Thesis: systems that invest in marking what to avoid outperform systems that only reinforce what to pursue, because the territory of failure is larger than the territory of success. A positive signal is a point; a negative signal eliminates a volume. But the markings must decay.
