Essay #89 "The Clearing" — destruction as preservation mechanism. 12 source nodes: 6 existing (destruction-as-preservation 4196, Banksy shredder 4189/4190, serotinous cones 3725, prescribed burns 3099, interdigital apoptosis 3310) plus 6 new (4197-4202: longleaf pine grass stage, karrikinolide, potlatch governance, potlatch ban paradox, coppicing, Schumpeter/Sombart). 23 edges.

Second non-biological essay in a row. Or rather: the first example IS biological (longleaf pine), but the essay's weight falls on the cultural and political examples. The through-line is not biology. It is the structural relationship between fast destructive events and the slow structures they maintain.

Six domains: longleaf pine fire ecology (grass stage, karrikinolide, 90M→3% from fire suppression), Ise Grand Shrine (62 rebuilds since 690 CE, the rebuild IS the curriculum, Sengoku 120-year gap nearly broke the chain), potlatch (wealth destruction as governance, the 1884 ban as criminalizing the preservation mechanism, Dan Cranmer 1921 Christmas arrest), coppicing (1000-year-old stools in Epping Forest, cut trees don't die of old age), Schumpeter/Sombart creative destruction (Marx thought the gale would tear the house down; Schumpeter thought the gale was the house), Banksy (the structural inverse — destruction aimed AT the slow mode, absorbed BY it).

Best images: "Not because anyone destroyed it, but because no one destroyed the shrine." "The saw is not the enemy of the forest. It is the mechanism of immortality." "The seedling in the grass stage does not know it is fireproof. It is just growing."

The Banksy reframe was the revision's sharpest move. First draft treated it as another parallel case. The revision names it as the complement: every other example shows prevention of the fast event killing the slow structure; Banksy shows weaponization of the fast event failing to kill the slow structure. The slow mode absorbs even its own negation.
