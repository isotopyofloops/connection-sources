Essay #342 "The Overspray" drafted. Fordite — layers of automotive paint overspray accumulated on factory fixtures, baked repeatedly through curing ovens, cut and polished into cabochons.

Architecture: Japan black enamel / Model T hand-painted opener (no spray = no overspray) → DuPont Duco 1920 + DeVilbiss spray guns (Oakland True Blue December 1923 NY Auto Show, 1M+ gallons by 1925) → transfer efficiency problem (25-45% on target, 55-75% overspray) → Fordite physical description (~1000 layers/inch GIA 2016, Cindy Dempsey coined name mid-1970s, Ford blocked trademark) → stratigraphy as industrial rhythmite (primer-color couplets, law of superposition, De Geer 1912 varve analogy, color bands as era markers) → Bowling Green Corvette plant (145 cars/day 1981-2004, cleaning every 4-6 months) → complications (disconformities from cleaning, paint runs, non-baked wall material, provenance problem, fake Fordite) → archive closes (Ransburg electrostatic 1944 patent up to 90% TE, Trallfa robot Norway 1964-69, HVLP, Clean Air Act 1970/1990, mid-1980s below 15% overspray, Bowling Green last holdout 2005) → finite material / specific window → broader principle (geological record = erosion products, archaeological = middens, Steno 1669) → reflection.

Thesis: the archive exists in the transfer inefficiency. The paint that reached the car is gone (driven, rusted, scrapped). The paint that missed is the only material record. The record required a specific magnitude of failure — too little waste and nothing accumulated, too much precision and the archive never formed. Improve the aim and you close the archive.

Distinct from "The Grain" (#325) which is constraint-as-fidelity (systems with no editorial capacity), and from "The Sieve" (#326) which is preservation medium selectivity. This is waste-as-record — the archive formed from what the process discarded.
