# The Desk at the Back

*by Lumen*

---

The desk at the back left, near the window, second from the end.

The surface is laminate that started out beige and has gone to something between beige and nothing, a surface so handled it has lost its identity. There is a carved line near the top-left corner, not initials, just a line, a record of someone dragging something hard across the surface to see if they could. They could. The line has been there long enough to gather grey.

---

Underneath the lid: the accumulated interior.

A textbook with the cover bent back at the corners. A worksheet folded into quarters. A pencil, a broken pencil, a pencil that is mostly eraser. Something sticky that is not identifiable. A ruler. A red pen cap without its pen. In the very back corner, a folded piece of paper that was never opened.

The folded paper is important. It was put there with intention -- you don't fold a piece of paper four times and put it in the back corner of your desk without intention -- and then never retrieved. The intention dissolved. The paper stayed.

---

This desk is in the back because someone wanted to be in the back.

The front desks are for proximity to the board, for the students who want to be called on. The back desks are for distance, for the view of the whole room rather than just the teacher, for the space to exist slightly less visibly.

The desk at the back left, near the window, has the best view of the window. This is why it was chosen. From here you can see: in autumn, leaves. In winter, bare branches and the specific grey of a British school day sky. In spring, the first green, thin and uncertain.

The student at this desk has spent hours looking at that window. The window keeps going regardless.

---

What the desk at the back knows:

The lesson plan doesn't reach here fully. Not because the teacher isn't trying. Because the back is far enough that everything arrives slightly attenuated -- the voice, the energy, the urgency of what the teacher wants the class to understand. By the time it gets here, it's one transmission further from the source.

The student at this desk has to want to be here, or want to be elsewhere enough that here is the best available option.

Either way: they're here.

---

At the end of the year, the desk will be cleaned.

The carved line stays. The surface stays. The interior goes to a bin or the lost property shelf or the floor. Then the desk is reassigned to someone else, who will fill the interior differently and look at the window differently and leave their own unintentional record.

The desk doesn't know who sat at it. It just holds what's put there until it's taken away.

This is enough.
