# The Squire

The order has protocols for everything except this.

There are protocols for the wounded. There are protocols for retreat, for supply failure, for a siege that has lasted longer than the provisions. There are protocols for disagreement between paladins, for the handling of contested relics, for the seventeen specific situations in which a junior member may act without senior authorization.

The situation in the square is not one of the seventeen.

---

The squire knows the protocols because the squire was tested on the protocols. The testing was thorough. The squire passed.

What the squire does not know — what none of the protocols address — is how to be the senior member when you are not one. How to carry authority you were not given and did not earn and cannot justify except by the fact that everyone who was given and earned and could justify it is gone.

The village is watching.

This is the detail that keeps arriving: the village is watching. They are watching because they have seen what is coming, and they have watched the ones who were supposed to stop it leave, and now they are watching the one who remains. They are not watching with confidence or with hope exactly. They are watching in the way that people watch something small stand against something large, which is with attention, and with whatever feeling you have when you understand that you are watching the thing that will determine your future.

---

The squire does what the squire knows how to do.

This is different from what a paladin would do, in ways the squire can identify and in ways the squire cannot. The squire does not know, for instance, whether the authority in a paladin's commands comes from the rank or from the person who holds it, or whether these are separable, or whether the squire's commands will hold when they are issued without either.

The squire issues them anyway.

Not because the squire is confident. The squire is not confident. The squire is afraid in the specific way of someone who is doing the only thing available and does not know if it is enough.

But confidence, it turns out, is not the prerequisite. The prerequisite is the doing.

The protocols did not cover this. The protocols assumed someone would be there who knew. The protocols were not wrong about the usual situation. The usual situation is not this one.

---

Later — if there is a later, which is the thing the squire is currently working to ensure — someone will write a new protocol.

The squire is not thinking about that yet. The squire is thinking about the next hour, and then the hour after that, and holding the village's watching in their peripheral attention as a reminder of what the hours are for.

The order has protocols for everything except this.

The squire is inventing it.
