# Asad: December Third

He tells Osei at 07:00 that he is going in.

He does not ask. This is not oversight. He tells her because she is the survey lead and she should know where the PI is going to be. He tells her in the instrument station, over the morning instrument check, while she is logging baseline readings. He says: I am going in today. He does not say: I have been planning this for thirty-eight years. He does not say: the instruments have told me everything they can tell me. He says: I am going in today.

She looks at him. She says: okay. She does not ask how long. She does not ask what he expects to find. He is the PI. The survey protocol does not prohibit descent. She logs his statement — not formally, not in a form, in the note she will write in 2043 about what she observed on December 3, which is the only record of the conversation.

He eats breakfast. He checks his equipment: light source, rope, instrument readings for calibration comparison. He has been making these checks in his head for months, maybe years. The physical act of checking is not preparation. The preparation was everything before this.

At 09:47, he descends.

The thing he knows, going down: Rhea went in and did not come back. The thing he knows: he did not go in then. He stood at the surface. He received the bag. He drove home. He spent thirty-eight years building the methodological framework the 1989 memorandum said was insufficient for safe characterization. He came back with instruments. And now, after twenty-two days of instrument readings, he is going in the way she went in.

He does not know what she found. He has read her sealed notes. He has read them once, in the two days after the search was called off, when the notes were given to him in the parking lot by a search coordinator who had no other place to put them. He read them once and sealed them again and has not opened them since. He cannot read them and then go in. He did not want to be holding her account when he formed his own.

The shaft goes down. The instruments, as he descends, record. At a certain point the instruments stop recording.

He does not stop.

He comes back up at 11:51. He stands at the entrance for approximately five minutes, per Osei's account. Then he goes to the site tent.

He works for an hour. He does not describe what he is working on. At 12:30 he eats lunch with Osei and Reyes and they talk about the acoustic propagation paper he read in 2019.

At 14:22, LEDGER receives two SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED filings.

He does not file a field report. The column in the survey log where field reports are entered for December 3 is blank.

He goes to sleep at the normal time.

The fifth null reading begins at 23:09.
