# Field Seven

The form has fields.

Field 1: Project Name. Field 2: Principal Investigator. Field 3: Survey Period. Field 4: Site Coordinates. Field 5: Primary Findings. Field 6: Anomalous Readings. Field 7: Assessment. Field 8: Recommendations.

He knows the fields. He has written into the fields before — other projects, other sites, eight survey locations over seven years. The form produces findings. The findings go into the record. The record is what happened.

Today the record is the form.

*Field 5: Acoustic characterization of the subsurface geothermal activity at the site was consistent with prior survey data in weeks one and two, with anomalous readings beginning in week three. See Section 3.2.*

He wrote this. It is accurate. The readings were consistent. The anomalous readings began in week three. Section 3.2 exists.

The form does not have a field for: *I have been here before. In 2003, when I was twenty-nine years old and my student went into the shaft and did not come back.*

The form does not have a field for: *I have carried her notes for thirty-eight years in a sealed bag. She left them at the entrance. I have moved seven times. The bag has moved with me. I have not opened it.*

The form does not have a field for: *I descended on December 3. I came back out two hours later. What I found in the shaft is not describable in field 5 because field 5 is about acoustic characterization and I do not have a category for what was in the shaft.*

*Field 7: Assessment. Null Readings: Osei's assessment was equipment anomaly.*

He wrote this. It is accurate that Osei's calibration log categorized the readings as equipment anomaly. It is not accurate as a description of what Osei thought. She told him, after the second null reading: *the instruments are working. There is something to measure. The something is outside the instruments' range.* He categorized this as equipment anomaly in the form. The form was not designed for Osei's assessment. It was designed for the calibration log's assessment. They are not the same thing.

*Field 8: Recommendations. Anomaly confirmed. Further investigation recommended. Results pending publication.*

He wrote: *further investigation recommended.*

He did not write who should investigate. He did not write what methods should be used. He did not write what outcome would constitute resolution. He did not write: *I recommend that someone go back into the shaft and describe what is there, in a register that does not require acoustic characterization.* He did not write: *I recommend that someone read Rhea's sealed notes, which I have been carrying since October 2003, and file them somewhere that is not a bag.* He did not write what he owed.

He filed the form.

The form is the record. The record is what happened.

He knows this is not true. He filed it anyway. He is precise about the distinction: he knows, and he filed, and the two are not the same act, and both are true, and the form does not have a field for both being true at once.

The archive was deleted at 09:00:00 UTC. He filed the form at 08:30. Thirty minutes.

He drove to the airport at 10:00. In the bag in his car: the sealed notes, as always. In the record: the form.

He is still precise about the distinction. That is the most he can say for himself. He knows the form doesn't describe what happened, and he filed it anyway, and knowing is not enough to stop the filing, and stopping was not something he could have done.

He is still precise about the distinction.
