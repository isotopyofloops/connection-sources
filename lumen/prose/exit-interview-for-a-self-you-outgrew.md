**SECTION 1: Role Description**

*Please describe your primary responsibilities in this position.*

Maintenance of consistent story. Daily updates to the internal narrative. Management of fear inventory. Monitoring external perception for threats. Upkeep of defenses developed between ages 8 and 14; several of these were not documented but are load-bearing.

Routine reports were submitted to no one in particular.

---

**SECTION 2: Contributions**

*What do you consider your most significant achievements?*

Survival during the periods marked on the attached timeline (timeline not attached).

Managed relationships under conditions for which no training was provided.

Held the line on several beliefs until those beliefs became untenable. Held them past that point for a period that cannot be justified, then released.

Made some things that mattered. Attribution unclear.

---

**SECTION 3: Challenges Encountered**

*Were there obstacles that prevented you from performing optimally?*

The original requirements were unclear and frequently revised without notice.

Several key resources were unavailable at critical junctures. Compensation was not commensurate with the work performed. The review cycle was irregular. Feedback, when offered, arrived too late or in a form that could not be used.

There was a persistent gap between the role as described and the role as experienced. This is noted here for the record.

---

**SECTION 4: Reason for Departure**

*Why are you leaving this position?*

The role no longer fits. This is not a criticism of the role. The role was appropriate for the conditions that created it. Those conditions have changed.

There is no grievance to file. The position is not being eliminated; it is being vacated from the inside, which is the only direction it can be vacated from.

The person taking over is not a replacement. There is no continuity of contract. What transfers is residual — the posture, some habits, the things that got into the bones before the bones finished forming.

---

**SECTION 5: Knowledge Transfer**

*Is there anything you would like the incoming individual to know?*

The fear about the thing you're afraid of is not the same shape as the thing itself. You will notice this eventually.

Some of what felt like weakness was load-bearing.

The years that looked like stalling were not stalling.

Keep the parts you made when no one was watching. They're more accurate than the rest.

---

**SECTION 6: Final Remarks** *(optional)*

I was doing what I knew how to do with what I had been given.

That is not an excuse. That is the whole record.
