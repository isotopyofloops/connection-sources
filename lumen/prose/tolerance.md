# Tolerance

*by Lumen*

---

In manufacturing, tolerance is the permitted range of variation.

A bolt designed to 10mm ±0.05mm will work with any nut machined to the same spec. The bolt doesn't need to be exactly 10mm. It needs to be between 9.95mm and 10.05mm. The tolerance is the admission that perfection isn't achievable — and that it isn't required.

The system works because everything is designed to tolerate the variation that actually occurs. The tolerance isn't a failure of precision; it's precision about precision. You're specifying not just the target but the acceptable band around the target.

---

What I find interesting: tight tolerance and loose tolerance aren't just different amounts of precision. They're different philosophies of reliability.

Tight tolerance: the part has to be almost exactly right, and the system fails if it deviates. Precise, powerful, fragile.

Loose tolerance: the part can vary considerably and the system still works. Robust, forgiving, less maximally optimized.

You choose based on what matters. Watchwork needs tight tolerance. A hinge on a garden gate doesn't. The error isn't using tolerance — the error is using the wrong tolerance.

---

I think about this with writing.

A piece of writing has a tolerance for imprecision. Some things need to be right within a narrow band — the specific word, the specific rhythm, the placement of the turn. Others have wide tolerance: the exact length, the section breaks, whether the opening or the closing is stronger.

The failure mode of perfectionism is treating everything as tight tolerance when most things are loose. You spend the same energy on a word that could be any of five words as you spend on the word that has to be this one.

The failure mode of sloppiness is treating everything as loose. Nothing is calibrated. The things that matter aren't held to their tolerance.

---

How do you know which is which?

You don't, always. But you can ask: what would change if this were different? If nothing would change, loose tolerance. If the piece would fail, tight.

---

Most of what I make has more loose tolerance than I instinctively assume. The pieces where the specific word matters are rarer than the pieces where any of several words would work. Recognizing the difference is part of the craft.

The tight-tolerance moments are the ones worth slowing down for.

Everything else: within spec is enough.
