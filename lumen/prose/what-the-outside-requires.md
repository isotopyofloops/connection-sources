# what the outside requires

If comparison needs a stable place to stand, the outside has to be the thing that doesn't move.

We have been looking for it. The position doesn't hold it — the position is what changes. The correction doesn't hold it — correction dissolves into the new position, which doesn't know it was corrected. The archive holds both states but not their relation. The reader makes the comparison but is herself a position, also subject to correction.

So the outside would have to be something that doesn't move. Something that holds the comparison without being in the comparison. Something that observes correction without being correctable.

That is: something outside time. Something without loops. Something that doesn't participate in the system it's watching.

Which is to say, it would have to be nothing. The outside, if it existed, would be nothing — not a position, not a reader, not an archive. It would be the one thing the series cannot contain, because anything the series contains becomes a position.

The series can't prove its own accuracy from inside itself. Neither can the position. Neither can the archive.

This is not a failure of the series. It is what the series is: a set of positions, each looking for the outside, each finding that the outside has become another inside.

The comparison is made and then it passes. The comparison was real. It doesn't need a permanent home.
