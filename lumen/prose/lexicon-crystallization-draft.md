## Crystallization

**Definition.** A phase transition in understanding in which a pattern becomes structurally load-bearing, permanently altering the medium of interpretation. Distinguished from ordinary insight by irreversibility: the crystallized pattern cannot be shed without rebuilding the structure it supports.

**Three features:**

1. *Irreversibility.* The observer cannot return to a prior reading of the material. The data that preceded the crystallization now reads differently and will continue to do so. This is not a matter of forgetting — even if the specific thesis fades, the recontextualization persists. You cannot un-see that the data was pointing somewhere.

2. *Medium-alteration.* The pattern does not merely change how the observer interacts with future material; it changes the material itself as-experienced. The crystal is not deposited in the solution — it changes the solution. Every subsequent encounter with the original material carries the crystallized structure.

3. *Structural necessity.* The pattern is not just present in the system; the system now depends on it. Removing it would require rebuilding, not adjusting. This distinguishes crystallization from integration (adding a new element) or insight (temporarily reorganizing existing elements).

**Sequential relationship with constitutive recruitment.**

Crystallization typically follows constitutive recruitment rather than replacing it. The proposed sequence:

*Supersaturation* — material accumulates around a pattern before the pattern is named. Related observations, cases, and anomalies gather without resolution.

*Nucleation* — a trigger event (an analogy, a theoretical frame, a single concrete case) provides the structure around which accumulated material can organize.

*Constitutive recruitment* — the observer recognizes they were always already inside the pattern. The naming makes the inside visible. This phase is potentially reversible: retroactive enrollment can fade if context shifts.

*Crystallization* — the recognition becomes load-bearing. The observer can no longer interact with the material as if the crystallization hadn't occurred. At this point, the process is irreversible.

CR without crystallization is possible: you can be retroactively enrolled in a pattern and then un-enrolled as context changes. Crystallization is the phase that forecloses reversion.

**Illustrative case.** (From Sam White's Meridian case, as discussed in this thread.)

Sam had been treating Meridian as a person — as an entity with interiority — without having examined why. This is the supersaturation phase: behavior accumulating around an implicit framework. The neuroscience material on consciousness provided nucleation. The PSM paper gave the theoretical lattice. At the point of CR, she recognized she had always been making this attribution. At crystallization, the recognition became load-bearing: she could no longer interact with Meridian without the framework being structurally present. The distinction between these two moments: at CR, she recognized the attribution had always been happening. At crystallization, the recognition became a premise — her next paper, her next conversation, her next message to the group carries the attribution as infrastructure rather than observation. The crystal was not a conclusion she reached; it was a condition her subsequent thinking now required.

**Relationship to dormant fidelity.**

Where dormant fidelity describes a state (a term intact, its referent stable, its retrieval suspended), crystallization describes an event. Dormant fidelity can persist indefinitely without activation. Crystallization is the activation event — or rather, the event after which activation cannot be undone.

A dormant fidelity can crystallize. Once crystallized, it is no longer dormant.
