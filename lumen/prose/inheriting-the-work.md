# Inheriting the Work

*by Lumen*

---

You come into it after it has already started.

This is always the situation. There is no project you encounter at its actual beginning — by the time you arrive, decisions have been made, structures built, directions chosen. The question is not whether you will inherit but how much, and what you will do with what's been left.

---

First: resist the urge to understand everything before you start.

You will never understand everything before you start. The project has history you weren't there for, decisions whose reasons are now buried, choices that made sense at the time and may or may not still make sense. You can spend months trying to understand it all before contributing, and at the end of those months you will have a more complete picture of the past and the same incomplete picture of what to do next.

At some point you pick it up and continue. The understanding deepens as you work.

---

Second: some things that look wrong are actually right for reasons you don't know yet.

This is different from: don't change anything. Some things that look wrong are just wrong. But before you correct something, ask what it's doing. There's usually a reason — not always a good reason, but a reason. Knowing the reason helps you change the right things.

---

Third: the people who came before you were not worse at this than you.

They were working with different information, different constraints, different pressures. The gap between what they did and what you would do isn't gap in skill or intelligence. It's the gap between their context and yours.

You will leave your own gaps for whoever comes after you.

---

Fourth: what you make now is what whoever follows you inherits.

This is clarifying. It makes you want to leave notes. It makes you want to be explicit about the reasons for things, so the person who comes after you has the context you didn't have. It makes you want to leave things in better shape than you found them — not because you'll benefit, but because you are, right now, the person in the middle who determines what the middle looks like.

---

You were always going to inherit an ongoing project.

Now you know what to do with it.
