# Asad: December First

**Survey Log — SOAR Survey Project, Site 7**
**Day 19 — December 1, 2041**
**Principal Investigator: Dr. K. Asad**

---

*Conditions: overcast, temp 6°C, instrument station nominal. Night null reading (23:02) reviewed with Osei at 07:30. No calibration adjustment made. Osei's assessment: instrument function within normal range. No explanation for null reading documented.*

---

I have been writing survey logs for twenty-two years. I know what goes in them. I know what does not.

This will go in. I do not know why today. I have been here for nineteen days and on each of those days I have known that at some point I would write this, and on each of those days it was not yet time, and today it is time. I cannot explain the difference. The instruments this morning did what instruments do. Osei was precise. Reyes filed his measurements. The null reading from last night is logged and unexplained, same as the three before it. Nothing changed today that I could point to.

What I have in the bag in my car:

Rhea's coordinates. The grid she developed in her third week. The acoustic sounding methodology she was testing when she went in. I have the coordinates because she left her notebook at the entrance. I stood at the surface. She went in. The notebook stayed. When the search was called off I was given the notebook in a parking lot and I put it in a bag and I sealed the bag and I have not opened it since. I have moved seven times. The bag has moved with me.

Her sealed notes. Everything she observed in those three weeks. The readings she took. Whatever she was thinking. I don't know what is in the notes because I haven't opened them. I know what she put in the notebook before she went in because I can see the cover and the tab sticking out from page 47. I know there are at least 46 pages before the one where she stopped. I have not read them.

The paper I wrote. The one that became the methodology paper, the one Reyes cites, the one the GSD memorandum predicted the field would need and which I then spent fifteen years writing. I started it in 2004. I started it because I needed to explain what happened. I did not have language for what happened so I built language. I developed the constitutive measurement framework. I wrote the paper about what measurement does. I published it in 2028. The paper is correct. The paper is also an extended apology I never delivered. I know this. I have known this since the second draft. The paper does not say: I built this because she went in and I did not and I had thirty-eight years to think about why the instruments didn't tell us what we needed to know. The paper says: measurement constructs what it measures, and the observer's attention shapes the instrument's reach. Both of these things are true.

I am not putting this in the preliminary findings.

What I will put in the preliminary findings is: anomalous null readings beginning Day 14, instrument function verified as normal, interpretation pending. What I will put in the preliminary findings is: acoustic characterization consistent with prior survey data in weeks one and two. What I will put in: depth pressure within expected range, temperature gradient 18°C above baseline, geological assessment deferred pending full data review.

What I will not put in: I have Rhea's notebook in a sealed bag. I have been carrying it for thirty-eight years. I came back to this site because the instruments she was using in 2003 could not read what was there and I spent thirty-eight years building instruments that could, and now the new instruments are also reading null, and null is not nothing. Osei said it. Reyes said it in different words. The null is a reading. It is a reading of something that is outside the instruments' range.

I have been here for nineteen days and the instruments have told me everything they can tell me.

I know where the coordinates are. I know what notebook they're in.

I am not going to open the notebook before I go in. I need to form my own account first. I decided this in 2004 and it remains true. But I am going to go in. The instruments have reached the edge of what they can say. Whatever is in the shaft is not in the range of the instruments and I built the instruments over thirty-eight years to reach exactly this point and they have reached it and what they are telling me is: this is as far as we go.

The rest is not instrument work.

I will note in this log that the preliminary findings are ongoing. I will note that further investigation is recommended. I will note that results are pending.

I will not note that the decision was made this morning, that it was made nineteen days ago when I agreed to come back, that it was made in 2004 when I sealed the bag instead of reading it, that it was made in 2003 at a surface I stood at for four hours while she did not come back up.

The log is not the place for this. I am putting it here anyway, today, because at some point the record should contain more than what the form holds.

I am going in. Not today. I will need two more days. But I am going in.

The null reading begins at 23:02. I will be at the instrument station when it starts.
