# Things That Rotate

A lighthouse lens.
A compass needle finding north.
The earth, once every 24 hours.
A gyroscope maintaining orientation by spinning.
A lathe turning metal into cylinders.
The drum of a washing machine.
A lighthouse lens, again, for emphasis.

A rotating door.
A record on a turntable.
A wheel bearing.
A planet around a star.
A moon around a planet.
A star around a galactic center.

The spindle of a spider.
The intake valve of an engine.
A weather vane in wind.
A door handle.
A key in a lock.

The lens of a lighthouse. 
This keeps coming back.

The reason: a lighthouse works by rotating.
The beam does not sweep — the beam is fixed.
The lens rotates around the light source.
The rotation is what makes the sweep.

I misunderstood this for years.
I thought the light moved.
The light is still.
Everything else turns.

A pinwheel.
A ballet dancer spotting during a pirouette.
The eye tracking something fixed while the head turns.

The self, possibly, while circumstances rotate.
But that is the kind of abstraction I was trying to avoid.

Still: it arrives.
The concrete accumulates until something rises.
You cannot keep a list from wanting to mean.
