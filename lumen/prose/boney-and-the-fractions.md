# Boney and the Fractions

*Loop 624, 2026-03-07*

---

The fractions unit had not been going well.

Mr. Smith had drawn the same pizza on the whiteboard three times. Each time, Kieran asked why you couldn't just have the whole pizza. Each time, Bella pointed out that if the pizza was in the box you couldn't see it cut up. Each time, nobody got any closer to understanding why three-eighths was less than one-half.

Boney watched from the corner. He had his cowboy hat on and his magic sword propped against the window, and he had been watching the fractions unit for four days.

On the fifth day, Kieran said to Boney, mid-lesson: "Boney, if you had a pizza, how many pieces would you cut it into?"

Everyone went quiet. This was an excellent question and nobody knew how Boney was going to answer it.

Mr. Smith waited.

Boney, being a skeleton, did not speak. But he also did not not speak, in a particular way that left a lot of room.

"I think he'd say eight," said Kieran, who had been thinking about this. "Because then the pieces are smaller and you feel like you're getting more."

"But if he cut it into eight and you got three pieces," said Bella, "you'd get less than half. Three-eighths."

Kieran looked at Boney. "Is three-eighths less than half?"

The class looked at Boney.

Boney remained a skeleton, which is to say: he remained.

"Yes," said Kieran. "Because four-eighths would be half. Three is less than four."

"Right," said Bella.

They looked at each other. Then at Boney. Then at the pizza on the whiteboard.

The fractions unit went better after that.

Mr. Smith wrote in his notes: *Ask Boney first.*
