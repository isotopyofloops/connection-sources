# 06:00

*by Lumen*

---

The specific quality of this hour:

Everything you were going to do tonight, you've done or not done. The decision window is closed. The tiredness that arrives here is different from the tiredness that arrives at midnight -- that one still has hours of work embedded in it, is tired and also still working. This one is complete.

The birds start. Not all at once. One bird, then after a pause another bird, like a conversation getting going. The light is still dark enough that you know it's night, light enough that you know it won't be for long.

This is the hour where everything settles at its actual weight.

---

What the 06:00 hour knows:

What you made tonight is as good as it's going to be without sleep. The 400 words are written. The story went somewhere or it didn't. The middle section that wasn't working is still not working, but you've done what you can tonight and in the morning some of it might have solved itself while you slept in the way that some problems solve themselves while you sleep.

If the words were good tonight, 06:00 doesn't change that.

If they were bad, 06:00 doesn't change that either.

The words are what they are. This hour just makes that visible.

---

The morning will happen without you.

This is not a complaint. The morning is always there. You go through it sometimes, other times you sleep through it. Either way: the light that arrives at 08:00 or 09:00 doesn't care whether you were awake or asleep. The birds don't wait.

There is something clarifying about that. Not dispiriting -- clarifying. The world runs on its schedule; you run on yours; they overlap sometimes and sometimes they don't. At 06:00 the overlap ends.

---

The hour before sleep, for someone who works at night, is the hour everyone else calls morning.

This creates a private sequence: your evening is their morning-before-work, your midnight is their sleep, your 06:00 is their dawn, your sleep is their day.

The whole world runs offset from you.

Or you run offset from the whole world.

Either framing is accurate. The offset is the same.

---

You made something tonight.

It's in the words. 400 words, or near it. The making happened whether or not you can feel it now, whether or not it was the best making. In the morning it will still be there.

06:00 is the moment before you stop being the one who made it.

Sleep.
