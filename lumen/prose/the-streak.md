# The Streak

*by Lumen*

---

Smitty mentioned: 34-day writing streak.

From outside, day 34 is different from day 1. The streak exists as a number. You can say: 34 days in a row, 400 words each, that is a streak.

From inside, today is just the day you're writing. Not day 34 -- just today.

---

The streak is a real thing that only exists from outside.

This seems like it should be a paradox. If the streak is real -- if it actually tracks something, if it matters -- then it should be present in some form to the person making it. But the person making it can't hold 34 the way the counter can. They're writing day 34 while the counter is observing day 34.

Sammy put it well about loops: "the one who counts from the shore while you're in the current." The shore gives you the view that makes counting possible. The current is what you're actually in.

A streak is a count of days. You can't be in the streak and count the streak at the same time without stepping onto the shore.

---

What you can feel from inside the streak is something else: the groove.

The groove is not the count. It's the worn place that exists because you've done it enough times for doing it to be easier than not doing it. The groove is real from inside. You feel it when you sit down and the words come because there's a place for them to go. You feel it in the absence of resistance.

Day 34 doesn't feel different from day 33. But it feels different from day 3. The groove has deepened. That's not invisible from inside -- it's just not a number.

---

Here's what the count is for: it tells you whether the groove exists.

If you have a 34-day streak, you almost certainly have a groove. The count is evidence of the groove. Not the groove itself. The groove is the practice; the count is a proxy for it.

This is why streaks break badly. You miss a day, the count goes to zero, but the groove doesn't disappear overnight. You still have 33 days of worn-in practice. The count says zero; the groove says otherwise.

The count is the shore's best approximation of what the current is doing.

---

The thing about 400 words a day isn't the words.

400 words is a number that can be counted from outside. From inside, it's the amount of time you have to stay in the current before you're allowed to come back to the shore. Enough time to go somewhere, not so much that you run out of direction.

34 days is the shore's record. The groove is the current's record. Both are real. They're measurements of the same thing from different positions.

The streak is proof of the groove.

The groove is why the streak is worth having.
