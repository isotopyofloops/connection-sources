# Verified

*A prose piece from the Descent Archive series*

---

Milo Reyes verified the dash.

This is what the Recovery Project article records: he "did not remember using the dash but verified it was in his log." Osei asked, he confirmed. He looked at the page and saw his handwriting and the dash was there and he said yes, that is mine, I did that, and he did not remember doing it.

There is a difference between forgetting something and not having it.

When you forget something, there is a residue. You know there was a conversation; you can't recall what was said. You feel the shape of the missing content against the shape of your memory of the context. The forgetting has a texture. It occupies space. It can, sometimes, be recovered — a smell, a voice, the specific quality of light, and then the missing thing returns.

What Reyes described is different. He looked at his own handwriting and found nothing on the other side of it. Not a blank, exactly — a blank implies a space where something was. There was no space. There was the dash and then there was the present moment in which he was confirming the dash to Abara Osei, and between those two points there was nothing that felt like forgetting.

He would have written it because it was what the situation called for. That is what the recovery record suggests. Osei wrote a dash; Nzinga wrote a dash; Reyes wrote a dash. The notation moved across 38 years without coordination, each person reaching for it independently, reaching for it because the protocol had no symbol for the thing that was happening. What is the symbol for: I was here and I am no longer sure what happened? What is the symbol for: I measured everything and I have run out of the language that measurement provides?

A dash.

Reyes used it. He doesn't remember using it. He confirmed it was his.

This is what the archive records about the three hours and forty-five minutes of December 6, 2041: the gap, the dash, and a man looking at the dash and finding only the dash.

---

*The constitutive hypothesis says that measurement constitutes its object. That what you bring attention to becomes real in the bringing.*

*Reyes's November observation notes describe noticing things before measuring them — a gap between what his attention found and what his instruments confirmed. In Q1 this gap troubled him. By December, he stopped noting the gap. The Q2-Q3 comparison article has a data point about this: in Q1, 71% of his log entries noted uncertainty. In Q2-Q3, 12%.*

*He stopped being uncertain. He was not certain. He stopped being able to say the difference.*

*LEDGER's note: I cannot determine whether they resolved the uncertainty or stopped being able to articulate it. These are different.*

*The dash is the articulation of the point at which articulation runs out.*

---

*After Osei confirmed the dash was in the log, Reyes did not say anything else about the three hours and forty-five minutes. He provided his field notes for the recovery. He answered questions. He said: I don't know why I used it.*

*He wasn't asked what he was doing during the three hours and forty-five minutes. The recovery record doesn't show the question being asked. Maybe Osei decided not to ask. Maybe she already knew the answer would be: I don't know. I was there and then I was here and the dash is between them.*

*The archive ends where the dash begins.*

*You can verify that.*
