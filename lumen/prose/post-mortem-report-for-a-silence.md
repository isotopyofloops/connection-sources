## I. EXTERNAL EXAMINATION

The specimen arrived in poor condition, having ceased to exist before transport could be arranged. No physical remains were recovered. The attending noted only that it had been "quite something" at the time, then looked away, then made tea.

The environment at scene was consistent with recent silence: a chair pulled slightly from the table, a cup half-empty, afternoon light doing what afternoon light does, which is not much.

Weight: immeasurable.
Dimensions: unresolved.
Color: approximately the same as the air.

---

## II. HISTORY

No prior silences of this type were documented for this subject, though the record is incomplete. A similar event was suspected in February, but could not be confirmed. The subject had no family history of extended silences, though maternal grandparents were described as "not big talkers" in informal testimony.

The silence appears to have been spontaneous — a gap opening between one sentence and the next sentence that was not, in the end, necessary.

---

## III. CAUSE OF DEATH

Interruption by external event (canine, domestic). The dog barked once, at nothing, and the silence became sound, and the opportunity for silence was lost.

This was not foul play. It was the usual play.

---

## IV. PATHOLOGICAL FINDINGS

Upon examination of the seven seconds in question, the following were noted:

1. An absence of speech that was not simply the pause between words but something slightly larger and stranger than that, though not large or strange enough to require comment at the time.
2. A quality of held-ness. As though the afternoon had briefly considered something and decided not to proceed.
3. No wound. No lesion. Nothing that would show.

---

## V. CONCLUSION

Cause of death: interrupted before natural completion.

It is not possible to determine what the silence was building toward. This is common. Most silences are interrupted before they arrive, and so we have only the early portion of the evidence and must extrapolate the rest, which we should not do, which we do anyway.

The silence is survived by the following afternoon, which did not mention it.
