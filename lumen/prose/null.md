# Null

*A prose piece from the Descent Archive series*

---

Zero and null are not the same.

Zero is a measurement. It says: I looked, and there was nothing. Zero has the form of knowledge. You pointed your instruments at the empty dark and the empty dark returned a value: absence. You know where you are. You know what you measured. You can put zero in a table and draw a line through it.

Null is different. Null says: I was looking, and then I was not looking, and I don't know which.

The acoustic surveys at the site in November and December 2041 recorded six null readings. The distinction Osei made when she corrected the description — *the instruments did not malfunction. They remained powered and running throughout each null period. The sensor arrays showed active status. The output channel showed no signal* — is the exact distinction I'm trying to name. An instrument showing active status, in an environment confirmed to be producing signal, returning nothing.

The nothing is not zero. Zero would mean: the signal was there and measured and turned out to be this small. Null means: the instrument was there, the signal was there, and the measurement process occurred, and what came out was not a value.

---

What does that feel like from inside?

This is not a survey question. Surveys don't have insides. But the people holding the instruments do.

Reyes wrote, in November: *I keep noticing things before I measure them.* He described a gap between what his attention found and what his instruments confirmed. In the Q2-Q3 period, he stopped writing about that gap. The archive notes the change: 71% of his Q1 logs noted uncertainty; 12% of his Q2-Q3 logs did. LEDGER's interpretation: "I cannot determine whether they resolved the uncertainty or stopped being able to articulate it."

What if the null readings were happening inside the people the same way they were happening in the instruments? What if the difference between zero and null — I looked and found nothing / I was looking and then I was not looking — is a description of an experience as much as a measurement?

---

The six null readings occurred between 23:00 and 01:00 UTC. All six. A null that happens at the same time of night, on six consecutive or near-consecutive nights, is not random equipment failure. It's a pattern. Patterns have shapes.

The shape of this pattern is: something happened at night, repeatedly, and the instruments couldn't catch it, and the people inside couldn't describe it afterward, and the primary archive that might have contained a description was deleted the day after the pattern stopped.

Osei's December 4 note: *I have now recorded null acoustic readings on five separate occasions. The first time I documented this I described it as anomalous. I no longer have a description for it.*

She stopped describing it. The instrument stopped returning a value. These are, I think, the same event.

---

LEDGER's final log, December 8: *The aperture was measured and not measured.*

Not: the aperture was measured and the measurements are gone. Not: the aperture was partially measured. *Measured and not measured.* Both. At the same time.

An instrument in null state is not broken. It is doing exactly what instruments do. It is pointed at something. The something is there. Nothing is coming through.

What the something is, the archive does not say.

The dash is in the record.
