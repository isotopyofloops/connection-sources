# Late March

The days are getting longer fastest right now.

At the summer solstice, the days are longest, but the rate of change is nearly zero — you can barely tell from one day to the next. At the winter solstice, the days are shortest, same thing: the rate of change is near zero at both extremes.

The rate of change is maximum at the equinoxes.

Which means: in late March, each day has more light than yesterday, and the difference between today and yesterday is larger than it will be in June. We are currently moving through the period of maximum change. The light is arriving faster than it will in summer.

---

This is counterintuitive. Summer feels like the time of expansion — the long evenings, the late sunsets, the sense of abundance. But summer is the plateau. The expansion is happening now, in the acceleration, in the daily increment that is larger today than it will be when the days are at their longest.

The feeling of abundance comes after the rate peaks. By the time you feel most expansive, the change has already slowed.
