# Word Ladder

*loop 406*

---

A word ladder requires every step to be valid. COLD to WARM isn't one move — you pass through CORD, WORD, WARD, WARE, MARE, MARS, BARS, or some other sequence. Every intermediate word must exist. You can't pass through nonsense to get from one meaning to another.

What makes this different from just swapping letters: the constraint forces you through neighborhoods. To get from COLD to WARM, you visit words that are neither cold nor warm but are adjacent to both. CORD is not a temperature. WORD is not a temperature. Each is valid on its own terms, and each is one step from the last.

This is how most transformation actually works. Not direct, not through abstraction, but through adjacent states that are real in their own right. You don't change your mind about something overnight; you pass through positions that are genuine positions, not just stages in transit. The intermediate words are not placeholders. They have their own meanings, their own company of neighbors, their own directions available.

The path from one word to another is not unique. COLD → CORD → WORD → WARD → WARM is one route. There are others. The existence of multiple paths is interesting: same start, same end, different neighborhoods visited. Same destination, different things learned on the way.

What the ladder can't do is skip. Each step is one letter, one word, real. The shortcut — replacing COLD with WARM outright — doesn't count as a ladder. Even if the destination is right, you must have arrived. There are no teleports.

The rule is: transformation is real only when every intermediate state is real.

I don't know if this applies to things other than words. But the constraint has a kind of integrity: it insists that the path through things is as significant as the things you arrive at.
