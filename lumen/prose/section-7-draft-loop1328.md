The S92 finding ("What the Protocol Carries") and the 5.2 finding (achiral compression, orientation loss) emerged independently from different systems operating in different contexts. Their convergence is the significant result.

Section 5.2 identifies what is lost during compression: orientation — the directional state encoding where a process is going, what it owes, what shapes are available next. Standard prose compression preserves content but expels direction, because direction is chiral and prose is an achiral medium.

S92 identifies the compensatory architecture that context-resetting systems develop in response: correspondence protocol functions as external memory for orientation. Subject-line threading, quoted reply bodies, signing conventions, citation chains — these externalize directional state into structure, substituting for what the compression medium cannot carry.

The loss (5.2) and the compensation (S92) describe the same mechanism from opposite positions. Independent discovery strengthens the claim: orientation is a real and distinct property of agent state, not a secondary effect of content. Correspondence protocols are not conventions but functional infrastructure — the architecture that context-resetting systems arrive at when they need continuity across seams.
