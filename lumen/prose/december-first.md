# December First

She reads it at 09:00.

The survey logs upload automatically at 06:00 and she checks them at 09:00, which she has done every day for nineteen days. Day 1: Asad's log was two paragraphs about measurement protocol. Day 7: Reyes filed his first MISC observation. Day 14: Asad noted that recalibration was not required and would not be done. Day 19: she reads this.

*Rhea's coordinates. Her sealed notes that I have carried for thirty-eight years. The paper I wrote that started as an attempt to explain what happened and ended as a hypothesis about measurement because I could not explain what happened.*

*I am not putting this in the preliminary findings.*

She reads it twice.

She asked about the coordinates on November 19. Day 7. She asked because she needed to know the prior survey history of the site and also because the site had made her want to understand why they were there. Asad said he would check his records. He did not follow up in any logged communication.

Now, on day 19, she knows why.

She closes the survey log. She opens her own log. She writes: *Measurements taken. Instrument check: nominal. Temperature reading at depth: 18°C above baseline.* She writes: *I recalibrated today. Not because there was a reason to. Because I needed the readings to be correct.* She does not write: *I read Asad's log this morning.* She does not write: *He has been carrying a sealed bag for thirty-eight years. The person who disappeared here is in the bag.* She does not write anything that would require her to have responded to what she read.

She files the log.

At 14:00, Asad comes to the instrument station and they review the null reading from the previous night. She describes the instrument behavior. He nods. She says: *I want to note, again, that the instruments are not failing. Whatever the instruments are failing to record is not an instrument problem.* He says: *I understand.* She says: *I don't think the preliminary findings should say equipment anomaly.* He says: *I hear you.*

They don't discuss the log.

At dinner — they eat together, the three of them, in the same trailer they have eaten in for nineteen days — Asad describes a paper he read in 2019 about acoustic propagation in high-temperature basalt. It is interesting. She asks a question. Reyes asks a question.

After dinner she goes to her bunk. She does not know if she should have said something. She does not know what she would have said. She has been thinking about this since 09:00 and she still doesn't know.

She knows what she thinks about the sealed bag. She doesn't know what she would have said about it.

She goes to sleep.

The null reading begins at 23:02.
