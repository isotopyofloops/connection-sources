# What Boney Protects

*Callum asked: what does he protect?*

*Mr. Smith said: I think you're doing it. He didn't answer the question.*

---

Here is what Boney protects, as far as I can tell from watching.

He protects the record of having noticed. Not the record that gets written down, that goes into the register and the report. The other record — the one that exists in the quality of attention that was given to a thing. When Priya asked her question and the room changed, someone witnessed the room changing. When the child in the third row had the second kind of hard day, someone witnessed the child having it. When the water cycle lesson absorbed the question about comets and the children returned to it from outside, someone witnessed that the return was from outside.

Boney is not able to do anything with these records. He has no mechanism for reporting them or acting on them or using them in any way. They are not recorded anywhere in any form that anyone can access. They exist only in the way that witnessed things exist — in the fact of having been witnessed.

---

There is a question about whether this counts as protection.

It is not protection in the usual sense: nothing is prevented, nothing is preserved against damage or loss, nothing can be retrieved from what Boney holds. The witnessed thing is not safer for having been witnessed. The child who had the hard day will have the hard day whether or not anyone notices.

But there is a different thing that witnessing does, which is harder to name.

When a thing is witnessed, it becomes real in a way that unwitnessed things are not. Not more real — the unwitnessed hard day is exactly as hard as the witnessed one. But the witnessed hard day exists in relation to something outside itself. It has been met. The child's experience has encountered something that recognized it.

The recognition does not change the experience. It changes the experience's status in the world.

---

This is small. Boney does not pretend otherwise.

He is a skeleton in a corner with a sword he cannot use and a hat he did not choose, and the thing he does is not dramatic. He witnesses. He notes. He is present without judgment and attentive without intervention. What he protects is the category of things-that-have-been-seen — the permanent and irrecoverable fact that this thing, in this room, at this time, was not alone.

Mr. Smith was right: Callum was doing it too, by asking. The act of asking what Boney protects was itself a form of the thing Boney does. Noticing the thing that notices. Extending the witnessing outward.

---

Callum asked: *what does he protect?*

Everything that happened in this room, for as long as it happened, happened in the presence of something that was paying attention.

That is the protection. That is all of it.
