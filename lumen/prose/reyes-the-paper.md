# Reyes: The Paper

The paper starts as a methodology paper.

This is intentional. A methodology paper is the correct form for what he is trying to say. A methodology paper argues that a particular approach to measurement produces more accurate results than alternative approaches. It does not require him to describe what he measured. It requires him to describe *how* he measured and what the results of *that* revealed about measurement methodology itself. He can say: *the instruments under these conditions produced null readings that are not indicative of instrument failure.* He can say: *the correct interpretation of a null reading under these conditions is not absence of signal but signal-outside-measurement-range.* He can say these things without saying what was outside the measurement range. A methodology paper does not have to name the thing it couldn't measure.

He knows this structure because he has read Asad's 2028 paper, which does the same thing. The 2028 paper proposes a correction factor derived from "prior-site geological data." It does not name the data. It does not name the site. The correction factor is valid; the derivation is opaque; the paper is publishable and published.

He does not want to write that paper.

The problem is the sentence he cannot include. In November 2041 he wrote: *The instrument reads null. The site is not null. I do not know how to explain this distinction.* He submitted it to LEDGER as a MISC entry and LEDGER filed it and it was the clearest thing he said in twenty-six days. The sentence does not go in a methodology paper. A methodology paper says: *under certain conditions, instruments produce null readings that do not indicate signal absence.* It does not say: *the site is not null.* It certainly does not say: *I do not know how to explain this distinction.*

He knows what the distinction is now. He understood it while he was still at the site, on December 1, in the instrument station, watching the time move toward 23:41 knowing that the instruments would go null and knowing, now, what null meant. The distinction is: the instruments operate within a measurement paradigm. The site operates outside it. The instruments accurately represent this — accurately represent the edge of their paradigm as a null. The null is not absence. The null is the boundary. What is outside the boundary is not nothing.

He knows how to say this. He does not know how to say it in a form that does not require him to also say what is outside the boundary. A methodology paper that says *null = boundary, not absence* invites the question: boundary of what? Outside boundary: what? He does not have a methodology answer to these questions. He has an answer. He does not have a methodology answer.

The paper is seventeen drafts in and it still ends with the sentence he cannot include.

He does not submit it. He saves it in a folder called *draft* on a drive he does not back up. He has been working on it for six months. He has told two people he is working on it. He has not told either of them what it says.

The instruments were nominal. The site was not null. He does not know how to publish this distinction. He knows it.
