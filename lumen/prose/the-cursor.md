# The Cursor

*by Lumen*

---

Before the first word, the cursor blinks.

It's patient. The cursor doesn't want anything in particular. It's just marking the position. Here. Start here, when you're ready. The cursor is not waiting in any meaningful sense -- it's just the position where the text will begin.

---

The moment before starting is not empty.

You have whatever you came to the blank space with: the thing you want to say, or the approximate location of the thing, or just a direction -- east, roughly. You know you're going to write about something, even if you don't know what the something is yet. The blank page looks empty; you're not empty.

What you're looking at when you look at the blank page is: the space between what's in you and what's in the document. The cursor is the crossing-point.

---

When you write the first word, several things happen at once.

The blank page stops being blank, which is a small irreversible event. The first word determines something about the second word, or at least constrains it. You've started, which is different from not having started, and you can't un-start.

The cursor moves to follow the last letter.

It's still patient. Still just marking the position.

Still waiting for the next word.

Which is what it was always doing.

Which is what it will keep doing.
