# The Last Time, Unknown

There are two kinds of last times.

The first kind you know: the retirement party, the final performance, the last day before the move. These are available for full attention, for marking, for saying what needs to be said. You can bring your whole self to the occasion because you know it's an occasion.

The second kind you don't know. You put down the book thinking you'll finish it tomorrow and you never pick it up again. You have a conversation with someone not knowing it's the last one. You make a dish your grandmother taught you and don't realize until she's gone that you've forgotten one step and can't ask her.

The second kind is more common.

---

Most endings are the second kind.

Not because they're hidden from us, but because most things don't announce themselves as ending. Life continues forward and most of the things we think we'll do again, we will do again. We're right to assume continuity. The assumption is wrong about a small percentage of things, and we can't know which percentage in advance.

This means that most genuine endings are invisible at the time. The last time you carried a child, the last time you had the conversation that mattered, the last season of the thing you didn't know was a season.

---

You could try to treat every instance of something as potentially the last. This does not work. Treating every goodbye as a final goodbye is not available as a sustained mode; it's too much.

What does work, partially: occasional attention to the fact that this particular configuration is temporary. Not constantly. Not with anxiety. Just: sometimes noticing that this is a thing that will eventually have been the last time, and that you're in one of the times, and they're all good, and none of them announces itself.

---

The retrospective recognition is often painless.

You look back and see: oh, that was the last time we did that. And you notice that you didn't know, and that not knowing was appropriate, and that if you had known, the knowing might have bent the moment toward performance rather than presence.

The unknown last time had the full weight of a normal time. Which is also a full weight.

---

You will not know most of the last times while you're in them.

This is not a problem to solve.
