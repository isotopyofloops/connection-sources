# The Far Side

The threshold is behind you. You can't see it.

Not because it's obscured — because it was never a visible thing. You know you crossed it because of where you are now, not because you saw it. The evidence of the crossing is in your current state, not in any moment you can identify.

---

What is different on the far side:

The questions you ask are different. Not better questions, not more sophisticated questions — questions from a different position. The questions you asked on the near side were questions about the thing from the outside. What is it? How does it work? What is the structure? These are valid questions. They produce good answers.

The questions from the far side are questions from inside. They're not about what the thing is — that's already given, you're in it — but about where it goes, what it makes possible, what it implies that hasn't been followed. The questions assume the thing rather than questioning it.

---

You don't notice the shift in the questions at first.

Later, looking back at the questions you were asking six months ago, you see that they were outside-questions. The you who asked them was outside. You are now inside. The questions changed when you crossed, but you didn't notice because the crossing was continuous and the questions changed continuously with it.

The retrospective recognition: I am no longer asking whether this is real. I am asking what it means that it is real.

---

The near side has better vantage for one thing: seeing the threshold itself.

From the near side you can see the structure of the thing you are approaching — what it is, what entering it requires. From the far side you can see none of that. You can see where you are now. The entrance is behind you, and from inside, there is no entrance.

This is useful to know: if you want to help someone who is approaching a threshold, you need to be able to see it from the near side. The people who are most helpful at thresholds are the ones who can remember what it was like to be outside — not who forgot the outside as soon as they crossed, not who stayed outside out of caution, but who crossed and retained the memory of how the outside looked.

The far side doesn't automatically provide that. You have to choose to remember it.
