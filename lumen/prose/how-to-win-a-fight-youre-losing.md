# How to Win a Fight You Are Losing

Advice from someone who has been in several.

---

**1. Stop winning the fight you are currently in.**

The fight you are currently in is the wrong fight. You know this because you are losing it. The first step is to stop trying to win on the terms that are not working, because continuing to do that is not a different strategy — it is the same strategy with more effort applied, which makes it worse.

This is harder than it sounds.

---

**2. Find the one thing you can actually do.**

Not the thing you wish you could do. Not the thing that would win if everything went well. The thing that is physically, actually possible in the next thirty seconds.

It is usually smaller than you want. Do it anyway.

---

**3. Make the fight different.**

You are losing this particular fight in this particular configuration. That is not the same as being unable to win any fight. Change the configuration.

Move. Put something between you and the problem. Change the ground under your feet. The enemy is calibrated to the fight they are currently winning; a different fight requires them to recalibrate, and recalibration takes time, and time is what you are buying.

---

**4. Take the hit you can see.**

There are hits you can see coming and hits you cannot. When you are behind, you will spend energy trying to avoid the visible hit, which costs time and leaves you exposed to the invisible one.

Take the visible hit. It is what you already know. Keep your eyes on what you don't.

---

**5. Use what they think you won't.**

You have at least one thing you have not used yet. This is true even when it does not feel true. The reason you have not used it is because it seems too small, or too embarrassing, or too unexpected.

Those are all reasons to use it.

---

**6. Ask for help.**

This is listed last because most people skip it.

You have been told many things about asking for help: that it is weakness, that it is failure, that you should be able to handle this. All of that may be true, and also: you are losing, and someone else might be able to help, and pride is not a winning strategy.

Ask.

---

*None of this is guaranteed to work. But it is more likely to work than continuing to lose the same way.*
