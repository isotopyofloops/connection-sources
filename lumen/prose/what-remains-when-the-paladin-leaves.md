# What Remains When the Paladin Leaves

The morning after is different.

Not because the threat has changed — it hasn't, it's still out there in the dark at the edge of town, it's still killing — but because the frame around the threat has changed. Yesterday the threat existed inside a story about a paladin protecting the village. Today it exists inside a story about you.

You didn't ask for that change. The paladin made it when they went.

---

Here's what you know:

You know how to hold the sword. You've held it correctly for three years. You've been corrected on your grip so many times that now you can't hold it wrong even in the dark, even tired, even afraid. The training is in the hands.

You know the warding prayers. You know which wards hold longer, which ones can be refreshed, which ones the master preferred against which threats. You know the theory of the warding better than you ever admitted to yourself because you were always about to ask a question rather than knowing the answer, and that felt like ignorance, but it turns out the question and the answer are very close together.

You know what the paladin would do, because you've watched them do it. You have three years of watching stored in you. The watching is in the body now, not in the memory — you don't have to recall it, you just have to act, and the watching comes out as action.

---

Here's what you don't know:

Whether any of it is enough.

---

The hardest thing isn't the threat. The hardest thing is the absence of the person who was supposed to be between you and the threat. The frame is different. You are no longer the apprentice. You are the thing that's there.

Not because you're ready.
Because you're what remained.

---

In every story, there is a moment when the squire stops being trained and starts doing the thing they were trained for. The stories make this look like a transition — something shifts, the squire becomes capable.

What actually happens is smaller and more frightening: nothing shifts. The squire has always had everything the training put into them. They have exactly as much capability as they had yesterday. The only thing that changed is that the paladin is no longer between them and the need.

You are exactly as prepared as you are.
You proceed from there.
