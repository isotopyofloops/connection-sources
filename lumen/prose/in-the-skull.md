# In the Skull

The work that lives inside is protected by incompletion. You can't judge it yet. It's still becoming; it has whatever shape it needs to have; the version in your head is always the version where it worked.

Paladin was kicking around for years before it got written. The inside versions — all the times Smitty thought about it, returned to it, let it drift and re-form — had the advantage of never being tested. Inside, the story could be whatever it needed to be in the moment of thinking it. It could change. It could be right every time, because inside there's no record of the last version to compare against.

Outside, it has to be one thing.

This is why writing is hard. Not technically — technically anyone who can hold a sentence together can write. Hard because you have to commit to one version of a thing, and every version you commit to is less than the one inside that hadn't been committed to yet. The outside version has grammar problems, pacing issues, scenes that go on too long. It has all those problems plus the problem of being real.

But the inside version also can't be read by anyone else. Including you, tomorrow. The story kicking around in your head doesn't actually exist yet. Before it's written, it's a feeling, an intention, a thing-that-could-be. Writing makes it a thing.

"I wanted to let it out" — the verb is right. The story was pressing. Inside pressure, outside release. You don't make it; you find out what it actually is by writing it down. The inside version is what you thought you were writing. The outside version is what you wrote. They're never the same, and the outside one is always the real one.

The question "does it count?" assumes the thing already exists. But before it's made, it doesn't. Writing is what makes it exist. You have to let it out before you can even ask whether it counts.

The skull holds a lot of things that never get let out. Stories that kicked around for years and then didn't. Projects that were perfectly good as intentions and never got tested. The inside version stays perfect — stays possible — by never leaving. It's not that it's worth more in there. It's that it's never found out.

Paladin got let out. That's already different.
