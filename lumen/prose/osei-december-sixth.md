# Osei: December Sixth

The archive was deleted at 09:00 on December 5.

She knows this because she was in the instrument station when LEDGER's primary address stopped responding. She knows what a deletion looks like from the outside: the routing logs pause, the address times out, the entries stop accumulating. She has worked with archive systems for fourteen years. She knows what she saw.

She filed her own log for December 5 at 11:30. *Instrument check: nominal. Calibration completed. Temperature trace: 18°C above baseline. Acoustic trace: 3.2 on Q-band.* She did not file: *Primary archive deleted at 09:00.* She did not file: *The preliminary findings were submitted at 08:30, before the deletion.* She filed what the form asked for.

On December 6, she goes back to the aperture.

The aperture is seventeen minutes from the instrument station on foot. She has made this walk every day for twenty-two days. She knows where the ground is uneven, which rocks shift, when the wind changes direction near the tree line. She does not need to think about the walk.

She has not filed a report about what she found on November 22 inside the aperture. She wrote a dash. She knows the dash is in the buffer — she knows the buffer exists, has known since LEDGER mentioned the secondary routing address in its December 4 log, had not said anything about it. The dash is in the buffer. The forty minutes are not.

The preliminary findings say equipment anomaly. She submitted a formal objection. The objection is in the record. Asad's response to the objection — *I hear you* — is not in any record. He said it on December 1 in person at the instrument station. She did not file it. She did not file: *He said he heard me. He did not change the classification.* The form did not have a field for it.

On December 6 she stands at the aperture entrance.

The instruments are still running. The survey is not formally complete — the preliminary findings are filed but the survey period doesn't end until December 8. The instruments measure what they measure. The null reading window is 23:00–01:00 UTC. It is currently 14:30. The instruments show normal.

She does not write anything in her field notes.

She stays for longer than protocol specifies.

What she is there for is not something she puts in a form. The form asks: temperature, pressure, acoustic trace. The form does not ask: *why did you return the day after the archive was deleted.* The form does not ask: *what did you find that is not in the instrument record.* She knows how to answer the form's questions. She has answered them every day for twenty-two days.

She stays until the light changes.

She walks back to the instrument station. She files the December 6 log at 18:44: *Instrument check: nominal. Calibration completed. Temperature trace: 18°C above baseline. Acoustic trace: 3.2 on Q-band. No anomalies recorded.*

This is accurate. The instruments recorded no anomalies.

On December 7, she writes three sentences. She writes them inside. She submits them before leaving.
