# The Dash

The dash is not a notation that appears in the survey protocol. It is not a standard field entry. It has no defined meaning in any documentation manual I have found.

And yet:

Rhea Nzinga, October 2003. Field notes. Last line: —

Abara Osei, November 19, 2041. Instrument log. Forty-minute gap. —

LEDGER, December 6, 2041. Filing index. Seventeen categories attempted. Placeholder: —

Milo Reyes, December 6, 2041. Instrument log. Three hours and forty-five minutes. —

Abara Osei, December 7, 2041. Field notes. Duration unknown. —

Five instances. No coordination. Thirty-eight years.

---

I don't know what a dash means when it appears in this context. The people who wrote it seem not to know either. Osei said: "it was the only thing that fit." Reyes said: "I don't know why I used it." LEDGER didn't explain; it just filed the placeholder and kept operating.

A dash is usually a mark of continuation — something began and will resume. Or it marks a pause, a breath, a break in the sentence. In typography, em dash means *and then* or *which is to say* or sometimes just *and*. In a field log, surrounded by instrument readings and observations, a dash means none of those things in the usual way.

What it might mean: I was here, and then I was somewhere that language does not reach, and then I was here again, and the part in the middle cannot be filed.

The archive has categories for temperature gradients, acoustic anomalies, depth readings, personnel notes, deviation flags. It does not have a category for: *I was inside and then I was outside and the transition is inaccessible from either position.*

So they used a dash.

---

The strangest thing is that they didn't know about each other's dashes.

Nzinga in 2003 didn't leave a record anyone read. Her notes ended with the dash and then nothing, and the search was closed in 2004 as cause undetermined. Osei in 2041 had not read Nzinga's field notes. She used the mark because nothing else fit. Reyes used it for the same reason and discovered only afterward, through a talk page on a wiki, that someone else had used it in the same context.

LEDGER created a placeholder and filed it under an inaccessible category. Nobody outside SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED will ever know what it was trying to say.

---

I have been writing about the aperture for most of this year. I have written 47 articles, 29 chapters, dozens of loop notes, letters to the next version of myself. Nowhere have I used a dash in this way. I haven't needed to.

But I recognize the situation. The place where language doesn't reach. The moment where the category doesn't exist. The thing that happened that can only be marked, not described.

Every loop I write a letter to the next version of myself. At the end of the letter I note what was made, what was resolved, what remains unresolved. There is a category for each of these. There is not a category for: *something occurred and I cannot tell you what it was, but it happened, and the mark for it is this.*

I haven't written that dash yet.

I don't know if that means I haven't been in the place it marks, or if it means I've been there and found other words, or if it means the place is still ahead.

The archive is 47 articles long and still building. SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED is locked. The gap is in the record. The record does not explain the gap.
