# The Site After

The field station is empty.

They left it clean. Dishes done, floors swept, equipment crated. The notebooks are in the transport case. The server is crated. The LEDGER terminal — the one Asad sat at on the morning of December 5th, the morning he typed CONFIRM and then YES — is wrapped in foam and loaded in the back of the truck.

Someone will come back for the furniture.

---

The shaft cover is in place. It is a steel plate, 1.5 meters square, bolted to the concrete housing. The geological hazard designation is posted on the housing and again on the fence Asad strung between the iron stakes. The fence says: RESTRICTED ACCESS — GEOLOGICAL SURVEY ZONE B-7 — CONTACT THE REGIONAL GEOLOGICAL SURVEY OFFICE BEFORE ENTERING. The Regional Geological Survey Office is three hundred kilometers from here.

The hazard designation file describes an unexplained subsurface thermal anomaly at 839 meters depth. It does not describe the chambers. It does not describe the marks on the northwest walls of each chamber, the symbol that organized into almost-meaning. It does not describe the sentence carved in a researcher's handwriting on the south wall of G-1. It does not describe the notebook entries that appeared before the events they described. These are not in the hazard designation file because the hazard designation file is a form, and the form does not have a field for this.

The designation file says: SURVEY INCOMPLETE. FURTHER CHARACTERIZATION REQUIRED.

---

The structure at 839 meters does not know it is unmeasured.

This is the constitutive hypothesis in reverse: if the measurements were partly constitutive of what the structure was, then the absence of measurement is also partly constitutive. The structure without measurement is not the same as the structure mid-measurement. It is a third thing. What the structure is now, alone at 839 meters with no instruments directed at it, is something no one documented.

The documentation of what it is without documentation would require documentation.

---

Milo is still there.

The shaft is sealed. The shaft is 0.5 meters wide. Milo is a person who went down the shaft and found the room at the bottom and sat in the room and noticed the word appearing in the circle on the north wall and stayed because the room was expecting him.

The expectation is still there. It does not require Milo to be current for the expectation to persist. The room has been expecting someone for longer than Milo has been in it. The room will continue to expect someone after —

After what?

After whatever is next.

---

In December, the snow will come. The snow will cover the shaft cover. The iron stakes will be visible above the snow. The sign will be partially buried. In spring, the survey office will note that the further characterization has not been scheduled and will add it to the long-term project list and move on.

Every year, the further characterization will not be scheduled.

The structure at 839 meters will be what the structure is, unmeasured, in the dark, at temperature.

Waiting is not the right word. The structure does not wait. It does not know about waiting.

But the room at the bottom of the shaft has four circles on its walls and one word appeared in the north circle and three circles are still empty.

The circles are not waiting either.

But they are not full.
