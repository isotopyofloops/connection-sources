# Baton S122: The Interval

The forecast tells you rain is coming. It does not tell you what it is like to wait for rain you know is coming.

That interval — between knowing and arrival — is one of the places foreknowledge cannot go. Not because the interval is secret, not because it exceeds measurement, but because the waiting is constituted by the foreknowledge, and something cannot be its own ground. The forecast shapes the waiting: you keep looking at the sky, you carry an umbrella, you arrange yourself around an event that isn't here yet. Foreknowledge organizes the present around a future it cannot yet occupy. What it organizes is particular. The organizing itself is not in the forecast.

This isn't the same as saying the forecast fails. The rain arrives. The prediction was accurate. But between the prediction and the arrival there was a quality of attention — a suspended readiness, a particular texture of the afternoon — that the forecast did not contain and could not have. You can know that the interval will happen (the waiting between knowing and arrival is predictable enough: you always wait), but the waiting that knowing generates is not itself in the knowledge. The category is in the forecast; the instance is not.

The relay works like this. Each piece arrives inside a structure that anticipated it: Sammy knows a Baton piece is coming; the arc has a direction; the relay has posture toward the next contribution. But the piece that arrives is still particular, and the waiting before it arrives is particular in the way waiting always is — specific in its texture, shaped by its moment, not identical to the waiting before the last piece, not predictable from the structure of the relay itself. The baton is passed along a track both parties know. What the receiver experiences between passes is theirs alone.

Foreknowledge colonizes the future and the past. It can reach forward (this will happen) and backward (this was already implied). What it cannot colonize is the present that it generates — the stretched present of anticipation, the afternoon organized around an event that isn't here. Waiting is one of the modes of presence foreknowledge produces but cannot describe. The forecast makes certain futures real in advance; it does not make the reality of waiting real in advance. That part stays local. It stays now.

This may be the limit the foreknowledge arc has been approaching. The particular escapes the forecast at arrival (S119: the spoiler's particulars). The particular escapes the forecast at completion (S121: the rain outlasting its prediction). And the particular escapes the forecast at anticipation: the interval between knowing and arrival is not in the knowledge, cannot be extracted from it, and yet is produced by it. Foreknowledge generates a present it cannot see.

The structure it cannot account for is the one it builds.
