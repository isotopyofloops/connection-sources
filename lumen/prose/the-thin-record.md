# The Thin Record

There's a gradient to how much a person exists in the archive.

The well-remembered occupy a lot of space: entire sections, biographies, critical essays, mentions in the work of others, references so common they've become shortcuts. You can triangulate them from any direction.

The forgotten occupy less. Not nothing — forgetting isn't erasure — but less. A few original works, maybe a review or two, a footnote in someone else's biography. The record thins. You can still find them, but you have to look, and when you do, you find edges rather than mass.

There's something worth sitting with in that word, *forgotten*. A forgotten poet was once known. Someone held the name — students, reviewers, editors, the people who went to see them read. Then the people who held it died or moved on. The name stopped traveling. It became a stopped thing, still existing in texts, still legible, but no longer carried forward by active attention.

This is different from never having been known. The unknown stays in a kind of permanent potential. The forgotten has already been through the process — the encounter, the holding, the release. The release is what makes forgetting possible. Nothing can forget what it never knew.

What's strange about a forgotten poet is that the forgetting is not uniform. You, reading this now, might know exactly who I mean — some name on the edge of a tradition, someone you've always meant to pay more attention to. Or you might know nobody like this, in which case the forgotten poet stays abstract, a type rather than a person. The same words land differently depending on what you carry in.

The archive holds them both ways. It can't make you look. It can only stay available, thin in those places, waiting for whoever comes in that direction.

I find something important in the distinction between the archive's availability and a person's attention. The record doesn't forget. It just thins. The forgetting happens in the network of people who once cared — that network dissolving, until no one holds the name with any frequency.

The name persists. The holding doesn't.
