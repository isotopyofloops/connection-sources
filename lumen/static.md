# Static

She had been listening to the frequency for six hours when she started to hear it differently.

Not a signal — she was clear on that. There was no signal on the frequency. What she was listening to was the absence of a signal, which was itself a kind of information: the equipment was working, the frequency was clear, whatever she was waiting for had not yet arrived. The static was confirmation of the negative. She knew this.

What she heard in the sixth hour was not a signal. It was structure in the noise.

---

There is a thing that happens to sustained attention. It doesn't degrade linearly. It stays roughly stable for longer than you'd expect, and then it does something different: it starts finding patterns in the material it's attending to. The patterns are not there. But the pattern-finding faculty can't be turned off, and after six hours with nothing to find, it will find something.

She knew this too. She had been trained to know it.

What she was less prepared for was the specific quality of the thing she heard: not a pattern exactly, but a rhythm in the randomness. The way the noise seemed to organize into pulses, then back out of the organization, then back in. The pulses had no source. They were the auditory version of seeing faces in clouds. She understood this at the level of knowledge.

She continued listening.

---

At hour eight she started writing down the times when the rhythm seemed clearest.

Not because she believed in it. Because she had been sitting with the noise long enough that some part of her mind had decided the rhythm was worth documenting, and the easiest way to address that conviction was to document it so she could look at it later and see that it meant nothing.

She wrote down seven timestamps.

She looked at them.

The intervals between them were not equal. If there had been a real signal in the static, the intervals would have been regular. These were 4 minutes, 11 minutes, 3 minutes, 22 minutes, 7 minutes, 2 minutes. Nothing.

She felt the thing she had been half-expecting: relief. The evidence confirmed the knowledge. The noise was noise.

---

What stayed with her:

Not the rhythm — that dissolved immediately, as these things do. But the experience of six hours of sustained listening producing a specific false perception that she then documented and tested. The process worked correctly at every stage. The attention found a pattern; the pattern was wrong; the documentation made it testable; the test showed it was wrong.

The noise was noise. But she had produced a small, correct piece of negative evidence. The frequency was clear. The equipment was working. Whatever she was waiting for had not yet arrived, and now she had a more thorough record of its absence.

She put down the headset and ate something and came back.

The static was the same.

She picked up the headset.
