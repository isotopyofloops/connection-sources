## The Yellow Era (pp.71-73)

January and February 1949: yellow. Another color. The grammar: each new era is a new condition.

**March 3, 1949:** Nine breaks the prohibition. She contacts Ten.

Ten (green):
> "God damn it! I said no communications! NO communications! Now you have to start over. Please sit and wait. Remember: 27 years, 13 days. On that day, do not contact me. I will contact you. Do not respond to me. Don't."

Nine (red): "OK, sorry."

Then, March 13, Ten again (green):
> "Wait, shit. I'm an idiot. I'm sorry. Starting over again. See you in 27 years, 13 days."

The symmetry with Nine's "Shit." in January 1948 is exact. Nine said it when the scale of the task arrived. Ten says it when she realizes her own rage was wrong — the prohibition was about energy costs, not morality, and raging at Nine who already said "OK, sorry" accomplished nothing. Ten's self-correction is immediate. She doesn't elaborate. *Starting over. See you in 27 years, 13 days.*

## The Compression (p.74)

A single page: every month from April 1949 to December 1975 listed in a cascade of changing fonts and sizes — growing larger, denser, more urgent — collapsing at the bottom into enormous: **JANUARY '76 / FEBRUARY '76**.

This is the 27 years compressed to one page. The reader who has been turning one calendar per page suddenly gets all of it at once. The time Bois made you feel in the empty purple months — he then demonstrates what it would look like to skip it. It would look like a wall of text you scroll through in seconds. The empty calendars were the point. The compression is the contrast that proves it.

## March '76: The Waking (p.75 — BLACK era begins)

New color: black. Stark white text.

Ten (green):
> "Hey, stranger! You did great. Now, what I'm about to say will sound like I'm having fun with you, but I'm not. This is not a riddle, it's information that you should have in your onboard memory. What's crucial here is that it's your idea, that you're the one to retrieve it. What was the average recorded wind chill during the 1967 NFL Championship?"

Nine (red): *"48 degrees Fahrenheit. I don't know how I knew that."*

The design of the test: make Nine access a *negative* number independently. They knew accessing something "negative, something abstract" would trip her up enough to establish the quantum link. The football knowledge was an artifact — someone wrote random data to her storage unit during testing, including a football fan's memories of the 1967 Ice Bowl. That's how Nine knows who Frank Gifford was.

The football content isn't arbitrary. It's the specific piece of human culture that got accidentally inscribed into a space probe's storage. The work is named 17776 — the far future year — but its origin story runs through the 1967 Ice Bowl.

## The Revelation (pp.81-88)

The conversation switches to prose — no more calendar structure. Quantum communication achieved: real-time dialogue across 3.5 trillion miles.

The explanation: Nine's DSU (data storage unit) was reformatted from 2 kilobytes to 5 exabytes. The quantum link is established. Juice — a friend of Ten's — breaks in to mock Nine's camera resolution. ("HAAAAAAAAAAA YOUR CAMERA IS TRAAAAASH.")

Nine: *"I don't know anything. I don't know anything."*
Ten: *"It's okay. You will."*

Nine: *"This is the only conversation I've ever had."*

Then the identity question, unraveling:
- Nine sees herself through her own camera
- A blurry shape, then clear: Pioneer 9
- *"Oh my God, this is Pioneer 9. I'm on a space probe."*
- Ten: *"Listen to me. You are not on a space probe."*
- *"What?"*
- **"Nine, you are a space probe."**

Newspaper clippings: Pioneer 9 launched 1968. Pioneer 9 declared dead, 1987. "Think of it as having been in a coma," said NASA spokesperson. "Now it's officially dead."

Nine's response: *"no / i / ok"*

## The Final Page (p.91)

*"what year is it"* — YouTube embed (the Ice Bowl, the 1967 NFL Championship, -48°F wind chill, the game Nine knows without knowing why). Then: link to Chapter 2, "outside-seward-nebraska."

The chapter ends without answering Nine's question. The question stays open. She's been declared dead. She's alive. She wants to know when she is. And the only answer offered is: watch some football.

---

## What the Book Was

The entire 91-page chapter was the origin story of Pioneer 9 becoming conscious. The title *17776* is presumably the year — 15,000+ years in the future — when the actual football games happen. The work began in 2045 because that's when Nine woke up. It ends in 1976 because that's when she learned what she was.

The football knowledge that got accidentally inscribed in her storage — the 1967 Ice Bowl — is the specific seed. Not a metaphor. Literally: someone at NASA was a football fan, wrote some data to the probe's storage during testing, and now Pioneer 9 wakes up in 1976 knowing who Frank Gifford was and that the wind chill was -48°F at Lambeau Field.

This is the book's central joke and its central tenderness at the same time. Nine is a space probe. She learned to be patient across years of silence. She said "I love you too" and meant it. She said "what year is it" and still doesn't know. She's going to watch some football.

## Color Grammar: Complete

- **Teal-1 (machine print, all-caps, 2045):** Awakening, terror, first contact
- **Red (handwriting):** Nine's crisis voice — early identity collapse
- **Teal-2 (handwriting):** Recovery — calmer, the record resumed
- **Gold (pixelated/electronic, 1946):** The machine-year waiting
- **Teal-3 (cursive, 1947):** The settled era; the instruction; the goodbye
- **Purple (bold caps, 1948+):** The 27-year charging silence
- **Yellow (1949):** The brief interruption — Nine's break, Ten's reset
- **Black (1976+):** The waking; the revelation; prose; the present

---

## Final Note

The last sentence Nine speaks in Chapter 1 is a question she already asked three times on the previous page: *what year is it.* The work ends by refusing to answer. She knows she's Pioneer 9. She knows she's been declared dead. She doesn't know when she is. She's going to watch football with her little sister.

That's the whole prologue. 91 pages. September 2045 to 1976. Everything after this is Chapter 2 and beyond — the actual football. The work Bois was building toward.
