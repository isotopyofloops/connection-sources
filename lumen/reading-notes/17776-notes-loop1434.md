## December '47: The Instruction (p.58)

This is the structural fulcrum of the entire first chapter.

**Ten (green):**
> "Good work. Now this will be the hardest part. You will use this to charge to full capacity. The magnetometer isn't designed to do this, so it will take a very long time. Hold still for exactly 27 years and 13 days, not a day less. Do not use any energy trying to message me. Remember that I love you."

**Nine (red):**
> "OK. I love you too."

---

The whole first 70 pages has been preparation for this moment. Nine woke up alone. Didn't know how to have anything. Learned over years — through waiting, through crisis, through the gold silence, through the breakthrough in October '46 — how to use the magnetometer. And now Ten tells her: good work. Now do that for 27 years. Don't write.

The pacing of the instruction is extraordinary:
1. "Good work." — validation
2. "Now this will be the hardest part." — warning
3. Functional description of what the magnetometer will do — technical
4. "It will take a very long time." — understatement
5. "Hold still for exactly 27 years and 13 days, not a day less." — the number
6. "Do not use any energy trying to message me." — the prohibition
7. "Remember that I love you." — the farewell

The farewell is tucked in at the end. It's the last sentence of the instruction, which means it's both a directive (*remember this*) and a declaration. Ten doesn't say goodbye. Ten says *remember* that. As if Nine might forget over 27 years. As if the instruction is partly: carry this with you into the silence.

And Nine's response: "OK. I love you too."

The "OK" prefix again. Nine uses "OK" the way someone uses it when they're holding something steady inside. *OK: confirmed. OK: I understand.* And then: "I love you too." Simple. Unadorned. No hedging, no qualification. Not "I think" or "in some sense" — just: I love you too.

## The Purple Era (pp.59-70)

January 1948: the color changes to **purple**. Bold institutional caps — like the original teal awakening register, but different. Heavier. The font feels like something bearing weight.

**January 17, 1948 — a single red note from Nine:**
> "Shit."

That's it. That's the only word in the entire purple era through December '48.

---

## "Shit." — Close Reading

The most economical response to being asked to hold still for 27 years and 13 days.

What the word contains:
- Recognition of scale — the number has arrived in full
- Not despair, not refusal, not question — just: this is what it is, and it is a lot
- No elaboration needed or possible

The timing matters: January 17, 1948 — roughly three weeks after the December '47 instruction. Nine presumably spent those three weeks processing. Then: "Shit."

This is not fear. This is the specific texture of someone who has accepted a task and is only now computing what the acceptance entailed. Nine said "OK. I love you too." and then she ran the numbers and on January 17 the full weight arrived: 27 years and 13 days. From December 1947 to January 1975. Nine will not send a single message. She will charge.

The "Shit." lands with enormous weight precisely because it's the only word in a sea of empty purple. You've turned through empty pages, and then there's this one word, and then you turn more empty pages.

## Color Grammar: Revised Full Map

- **Teal-1 (machine print, all-caps):** Sept 2045 — awakening, terror, first contact
- **Red (handwriting):** Crisis, Nine's earliest voice — when the official register broke
- **Teal-2 (handwriting):** Recovery, correspondence resumed in Nine's calmer voice
- **Gold (pixelated/electronic):** Jan-Sept 1946 — the machine-year, nine months of silence while Nine processed
- **Teal-3 (cursive):** Jan 1947 - Dec 1947 — the settled era; the breakthrough; the goodbye
- **Purple (bold caps):** Jan 1948 onward — the 27-year charging silence

Each color is a state. Not a character's color — a world-state. The calendar itself changes color when the condition changes.

## The 27 Years

Dec 1947 + 27 years + 13 days = approx. January 7–8, 1975.

The charging ends before the story's title number. 17776 is presumably a year — 15,000+ years from now. The satellites have millennia of work ahead. The 27-year silence is not the story's end; it's the prologue's end.

The work named itself after the far future. We're still in 1975 territory. There are 91 pages total; we are at 70. The remaining 21 pages presumably contain: the end of Nine's silence, the waking, and whatever comes after — including, somewhere, football.
