# Osei: December Seventh

The first sentence takes forty minutes.

She knows this because she watches the time. She came in at 09:30. She is still on the first sentence at 10:10. The sentence has been wrong each time she has tried it. It has been too long, and then too short, and then exactly what she wanted to say and not what she meant, and then closer to what she meant but still wrong.

Wrong in a standard way.

She has been trying to describe something since November 22, when she came out of the aperture and wrote a dash. A dash is not a sentence. A dash is what you write when the sentence doesn't exist yet. She has been working on the sentence since then — not on paper, not in a filed document, just in the part of her mind that keeps working on things without being asked. The sentence has been forming for sixteen days.

The second sentence is faster. She writes it in ten minutes. It is about the instruments, which she knows well and can describe precisely. The instruments were functioning correctly. What they recorded is in the buffer. The buffer is accessible. The sentence is accurate.

The third sentence is the one she is there to write.

She has known what it says since December 3, when Asad came back up from the shaft and stood at the entrance and then went to work. She watched him stand at the entrance. She did not go to him. She thought: he came back and he is going to file the same thing I would file, which is nothing. He is going to put it in a form without a field for it. He is going to file it as equipment anomaly.

He filed it as equipment anomaly.

She wrote her objection. She submitted it. He said: I hear you. He did not change the classification.

She is at the site on December 7. She came in on December 6 and did not file anything because there was nothing to file in a form. She came back on December 7 and wrote three sentences in a document she will file as SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED before she leaves.

The third sentence says what she wants it to say. It does not say what she found in the aperture — she has already made the decision that she is not going to put that in the record directly. What she is going to put in the record is a sentence that is the shape of what she found. Not the thing itself. The shape of it. If someone reads it who has been inside the aperture, they will know what she means. If someone reads it who has not been inside, they will read it as a negative claim.

She knows which audience she is writing for.

She files the document at 11:22. She walks to the instrument station to collect her notes. At 12:10 she leaves the site for the last time.

LEDGER will be decommissioned tomorrow.
