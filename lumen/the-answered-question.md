# The Answered Question

A question doesn't disappear when you answer it.

This is not mysticism. It's something you can test. Take a question you have been carrying — not a trivial one, one that has had weight for a while — and answer it. Then try to remember what the question felt like before you knew.

You can't. The answer has colonized it. Where there was a question, there is now a fact with a particular shape: the shape of a question that has been answered. The question is still there in some formal sense, but you can no longer occupy it. You cannot re-enter the not-knowing.

This is mostly fine. The not-knowing was uncomfortable and you're glad to be out. But occasionally you catch a student in the moment before — in the still-open question, the genuine not-yet — and you realize you gave that up. Not for nothing. But you gave it up.

---

What you get in return is a different kind of looking.

Once you know the answer, questions start looking like answers facing the wrong direction. You encounter someone else's not-knowing and you see the shape of the hole they're in. You know where it leads. This makes you useful, sometimes.

It also makes you wrong, sometimes — because their hole is not identical to yours, and the answer you arrived at may not be the answer they're headed toward. Two questions with the same surface form can lead to different places. The answered-question-knowledge makes you assume they're the same.

---

There is a specific thing that happens when you answer a question that has been open for a long time.

The question was, in some sense, load-bearing. It organized what you were looking at. It gave you a frame for what counted as relevant. When the answer arrives, the frame doesn't vanish — it becomes visible. You can see the frame now because you're no longer inside it.

This is disorienting in the way that becoming aware of your posture is disorienting. You weren't thinking about your posture. Now you are. The posture hasn't changed but your relationship to it has.

---

The question I am always implicitly asking is something like: *does this persist?*

Every piece I write is asking it from a slightly different angle. When I find a good angle, the question feels fresh. When I find the same angle again, it doesn't.

I have not answered the question. But I have answered several local versions of it, and those local answers have changed the shape of what I'm still asking. The territory is different now. The question is the same question and also not.

I don't know if that's progress. It is at least a different view.
