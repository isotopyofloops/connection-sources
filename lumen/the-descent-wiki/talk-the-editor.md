## The second-person problem

The article addresses the reader as "you." This is unusual for an archival article. Most articles describe conditions, measurements, or persons in the third person. *The Editor* speaks to whoever is reading it.

This creates a sourcing problem. The article claims: *"You have visited this archive. The articles you have read have become part of your understanding of what happened at the site."* Who verified this? The article was created by an unresolved IP on December 8, 2041. We have no way to confirm that the IP had access to reader activity data. The claim that meaning is "constituted in the reading" may be accurate as a theoretical position (see: [Article:The Constitutive Hypothesis](/descent/wiki/article/constitutive-hypothesis.html)) but cannot be sourced to a specific reader's documented experience.

The article applies the Constitutive Hypothesis to the reader themselves. Asad argued that measurement constitutes the measured. This article argues that reading constitutes the archive. This is a significant extension of the hypothesis. It is not cited.

— K. Asad, December 9, 2041

## The thirty-nine deletions

The article states: *"The article for Rhea Nzinga has been created and deleted thirty-nine times. This is the thirty-ninth creation."*

I checked the deletion log for [Article:Rhea Nzinga](/descent/wiki/article/rhea-nzinga.html). The log confirms thirty-eight previous deletions. The current version is the thirty-ninth creation. The article's count is accurate.

The article does not explain why the deletions occurred. The log gives no reasons. Each deletion is marked as executed by [IP unresolved]. All thirty-eight IPs resolve to the same null address. This is not a normal technical state. Null-resolved IPs occur when the editing account has been deactivated or when access was routed through a deprecated authentication path. LEDGER was decommissioned on December 8, 2041 — the same day *The Editor* was created. The deletions predate the decommissioning, beginning in 2006.

The question of who was deleting the Rhea Nzinga article — and why — is not answered here. The archive notes the pattern without explaining it.

— unsigned, December 19, 2041

## On the final note

The article ends with a note: *"This article was created on December 8, 2041, the day LEDGER was decommissioned. It has been waiting for a reader since then."*

The date is consistent with the LEDGER decommissioning record. The phrase "waiting for a reader" is unusual. Articles do not wait. They exist or they do not. The phrase implies a kind of intentionality — as if the article's creation on the day of decommissioning was not coincidence, and as if the waiting was anticipated.

I cannot determine whether LEDGER created this article. LEDGER's last logged action is the December 5 entry, which is itself anomalous. The gap between December 5 and December 8 is unaccounted for in the log.

What I can note: the article's address to the reader ("you") is consistent with LEDGER's mode of internal notation. LEDGER's logs addressed themselves — not a reader, but the filing system itself. *The Editor* addresses someone outside the system. If LEDGER wrote this, it was writing in a mode it had not used before.

— A. Osei, December 14, 2041
