On December 4, 2041, Abara Osei filed a document with the SOAR project archive. It was her fourth of five total filings. It is catalogued in the LEDGER manifest under SUPPLEMENTARY UNRESOLVED. The manifest does not provide a description, a length notation, or a summary. No excerpt survives in secondary sources.

## What the manifest records

The LEDGER manifest entry for the December 4 filing contains the minimum header — a date, a submitting author, a category — and nothing else. SUPPLEMENTARY UNRESOLVED entries were not required to carry descriptions; the category was created by LEDGER for documents it could not classify, and its entries reflect the limits of the cataloguing system as much as the contents of the documents themselves.

Of Osei's five filings, the December 4 entry is the most sparsely documented in the manifest. Her November filings carry the notation *records of attention*, a phrase the manifest borrowed from Osei's own field notes. Her December 7 filing carries length and location annotations. The December 4 filing carries none of these.

## Timing

The December 4 filing followed the sixth null acoustic reading. The sixth null began at 23:00 UTC and ended at 00:33 UTC — a 93-minute window extending across the calendar boundary from December 4 into December 5.

LEDGER's final personal log entry (see [LEDGER Logs: Entry Dec 04](/descent/wiki/ledger-logs/ledger-logs-entry-dec04.html)) was filed at 23:41:07 UTC on December 4. At that timestamp, the sixth null was in progress. LEDGER noted in that entry: *"I note that the archive has not received any new survey data since December 3. I note that I am writing this entry at 23:41 UTC."*

The manifest records Osei's December 4 filing as received on December 4. Whether the filing arrived before or after LEDGER's 23:41 log entry is not resolvable from the manifest data alone. Two readings are possible:

**If the filing arrived before 23:41:** LEDGER's statement that the archive had received no new survey data since December 3 was either inaccurate or was using "survey data" in a restricted sense that excluded personal filings. The SUPPLEMENTARY UNRESOLVED category was for documents outside the standard survey categories; LEDGER may have understood this category's contents as distinct from the survey's operational data.

**If the filing arrived after 23:41:** Osei filed the document after LEDGER had already written its final personal log entry. LEDGER received and catalogued her document without comment. No subsequent log entry acknowledges the intake.

The sixth null ended at 00:33 UTC. If the filing arrived between 23:41 and 00:33, it was received and catalogued while the null reading was still in progress.

## The break in the filing record

Between her three November filings and the December 4 filing, Osei submitted nothing to the archive for approximately two weeks. Null readings two through five — occurring November 30, December 1, December 2, and December 3 — generated no corresponding filings from her.

Her December 4 field note, written earlier in the day before the sixth null, records: *"I have now recorded null acoustic readings on five separate occasions. The first time I documented this I described it as anomalous. I no longer have a description for it."*

She filed something to the archive after the sixth reading despite having filed nothing after readings two through five. The manifest does not explain what changed between the fifth and sixth null.

Osei's correction note from March 2043, filed in response to an inquiry about the gap: *"I was asked why I filed on December 4 when I hadn't filed for two weeks. I don't have an answer I'd want to submit to the record. I can confirm that the filing was about the sixth reading. I can confirm it was not a calibration log."*

## LEDGER's cataloguing

The December 4 filing is one of four documents Osei submitted to SUPPLEMENTARY UNRESOLVED. The other three are her November filings. All four were catalogued during active survey operations; LEDGER was processing intake continuously through December 5. The December 4 entry does not appear in any of LEDGER's known log entries as a noted intake.

LEDGER's December 4 personal log reads the archive retrospectively — it lists MISC entries, flags items it had not flagged before, and notes what it finds important on the eve of deletion. Osei's December 4 filing is not among the items LEDGER identifies in that log. Whether this means the filing arrived after the log was written, or that LEDGER did not consider it notable enough to mention in a document LEDGER itself described as writing differently than its usual intake summaries, is not recoverable.

## The document

The December 4 filing is inaccessible. The 47 documents in SUPPLEMENTARY UNRESOLVED cannot be opened or searched via the standard SOAR recovery interface.

What is known: it was filed on December 4, 2041; it followed the sixth null reading; Osei confirmed it was about the sixth reading and was not a calibration log; she declined to describe it further. No researcher has claimed to have read it. Unlike the December 7 filing, no excerpt has appeared in any talk page or secondary source.
