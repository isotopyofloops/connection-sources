## Attribution — *[IP unresolved]*

The article notes that Asad is known for "meticulous attribution practices." The article also notes, in the section revealed after reading about the survey team and the site, that the SOAR project's 2003 coordinate data came from Rhea Nzinga's undergraduate field survey, conducted under Asad's supervision, and that this data was not credited in the project documentation.

These two facts are not compatible with each other.

Asad received Nzinga's field notes in October 2003. He knew they were the basis for the SOAR site selection when the project was proposed in 2040. The site was not independently re-surveyed. The SOAR preliminary findings document, which Asad authored (and did not complete), contains no reference to prior survey data at the site.

I am not raising this as a criticism of the man. I am raising it as an inconsistency in the article, which presents him as someone with unusual standards for attribution while documenting an instance in which he did not attribute.

The explanation I can construct: in 2003, Nzinga did not return from the survey. Her work was never published. There is no public record to cite. Asad could not credit a source that, in the official record, does not exist.

This would mean the non-attribution was not an oversight. It was a consequence of something else. What that something else was is not documented here.

The article should acknowledge the inconsistency. I am raising it here rather than editing directly because I do not know how to characterize it. — *[IP unresolved, March 11, 2042]*
