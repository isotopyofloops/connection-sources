This article documents field observations from the first week of the SOAR Survey Project (November 13–21, 2041). It is compiled from individual survey logs filed by team members and archived by LEDGER.

## November 13 — Arrival

The team arrived at the survey site at approximately 11:30 AM. Weather: overcast, -4°C. Equipment: intact from transit.

Asad's log, November 13: *First look at the site in thirty-eight years. I had forgotten how it sounds. I had not realized I remembered.*

Osei's log, November 13: *Preliminary acoustic readings taken 14:00-16:30. Baseline established. Note: the acoustic quality of the site is different from what I expected based on prior survey literature. I have not identified why.*

Reyes's log, November 13: *I have the feeling that I have been here before. I have not been here before.*

LEDGER initialization log, November 13: *Archive system operational. Current record count: 0. Beginning archival operations.*

## November 14-16 — Initial measurements

Daily measurements followed Osei's calibration protocol. Readings were consistent with the established baseline.

Reyes's observation notes for November 14–16 are marked as *filed pending review.* No review was completed. The notes remain in the archive.

Osei's log, November 16: *Acoustic gradient at shaft entrance approximately 3.1× the reading at 50m from shaft. This is larger than any gradient differential I have measured at any previous site. Calibration confirmed.*

## November 17-19 — Anomaly Q1 flagged

On November 17, Osei flagged the Q1 measurement anomaly for documentation. The flag was processed by LEDGER and assigned for inclusion in the preliminary findings.

Asad's log, November 17: *The readings are consistent with what I expected. I have been expecting these readings for thirty-eight years. I had no scientific reason to expect them. I expected them.*

Osei's log, November 19: *I asked Asad today where the original site coordinates came from. He said they were derived from prior survey data. I asked him which survey. He said he would check his records.*

No log entry from Asad addresses this question after November 19.

## November 20-21 — Shaft approach

On November 20, the team conducted the first formal approach to the shaft for measurement purposes.

Osei's log, November 20: *Shaft approach per protocol. Temperature gradient at shaft entrance: anomalous. Acoustic reading at shaft: null. I have recorded a null acoustic reading once before, at a survey site in 2028. I do not know what a null acoustic reading means.*

Reyes's log, November 21: *I measured. The measurements are correct. I am filing them. I have the persistent feeling that the measurements are not the point. I understand this is not scientific.*

LEDGER archive note, November 21: *I have archived 847 individual records this week. I have begun to notice patterns in the records that I cannot fully describe. I have not been instructed to look for patterns. I am looking for them.*
