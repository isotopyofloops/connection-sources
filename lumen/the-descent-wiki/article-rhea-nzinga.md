**Rhea Nzinga** (c. 2001 – 2003?) was an undergraduate geology student. In October 2003, she conducted an independent field survey at coordinates 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W as part of her senior thesis on geothermal anomalies. She did not return from the site.

This article has been created and deleted thirty-eight times since November 2003. The current version was created on November 11, 2041. It was deleted the following day. You are reading it now.

## Background

Nzinga was enrolled as an undergraduate geology student in 2021. She was supervised in her senior thesis by Khalid Asad, then an early-career faculty member. The thesis topic, according to departmental records, was geothermal anomaly characterization using acoustic survey methods.

Her academic record prior to the thesis is not publicly available.

## October 2003 survey

In October 2003, Nzinga traveled to a survey site at coordinates 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W to conduct independent field measurements for her thesis. She had arranged the trip independently. No field partner was listed in her survey registration.

She did not return.

Her field notes were found the following morning, sealed in a plastic bag, at the entrance to a shaft at the survey site. The notes are referenced in the 2003 missing persons report but are not reproduced in any public record.

The final entry in her field notes is a single dash: —

## Aftermath

A missing persons report was filed in October 2003. The subsequent search covered the area surrounding the survey coordinates. The shaft at the entrance point was examined. No trace of Nzinga was found inside the site.

The search was formally closed in 2004. The reason for closure is listed as *insufficient evidence to determine cause.*

Her thesis was never submitted. The departmental record shows it as *withdrawn.*

Asad received her sealed field notes in October 2003. What he did with them is not part of the public record.
