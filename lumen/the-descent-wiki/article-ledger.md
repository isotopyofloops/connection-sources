**LEDGER** is an automated archive management system developed for the SOAR Survey Project (2041). It was initialized on November 13, 2041, one day before the survey began, and operated continuously until December 5, 2041.

## Capabilities

LEDGER was designed to perform the following functions:

- **Intake**: Receiving survey logs, field notes, and measurement data from survey team members
- **Summarization**: Generating standardized intake summaries for each filed document
- **Cross-referencing**: Linking related documents across the archive
- **Filing**: Organizing all materials into the archive structure
- **Constitutive indexing**

LEDGER operated without direct supervision. Survey team members filed materials daily; LEDGER processed and archived them automatically.

## Technical specifications

LEDGER ran on the survey field station's local server infrastructure. Storage capacity: approximately 400GB. Backup protocol: daily to remote server (connection interrupted November 30, 2041; cause unknown). Archive format: proprietary, converted to standard format on export.

The system was developed by [unnamed vendor] for geological survey applications. The SOAR project was, according to project documentation, the first deployment of LEDGER in a subsurface survey context.

## Status

LEDGER's archive was deleted on December 5, 2041. The deletion was authorized by project principal investigator Khalid Asad. One document survived in a server draft buffer and was later recovered. See [Survey Log:December](/descent/wiki/survey-log-december.html) for the deletion record.
