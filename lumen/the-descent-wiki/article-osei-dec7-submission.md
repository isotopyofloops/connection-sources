<p class="hatnote">This article concerns Abara Osei's final filing to the SOAR project archive, submitted on December 7, 2041 — one day before LEDGER decommissioning. For her complete filing record, see <a href="article-ledger-filing-index.html">Article:LEDGER Filing Index</a>. For the forty-minute gap this document references, see <a href="article-the-forty-minutes.html">Article:The Forty Minutes</a>.</p>

---

On December 7, 2041, Abara Osei submitted the last document she would file with the SOAR project archive. The LEDGER manifest catalogues it in the SUPPLEMENTARY UNRESOLVED category. The manifest's length annotation reads: *three sentences*. It is the only filing in the entire survey manifest whose length is recorded this way.

## What the LEDGER manifest records

The LEDGER manifest entry for the December 7 filing contains three metadata fields beyond the standard header:

- **Length:** *three sentences*
- **Location:** *written inside*
- **Submission time:** *06:17 UTC, December 7, 2041*

The length annotation is unusual. The manifest records document lengths in other ways: word counts, page counts, estimated ranges. The phrase *three sentences* suggests the cataloguing system was provided with the number directly, either by the author or by an automated count that produced an unusually precise result.

The location annotation — *written inside* — appears in no other manifest entry. The LEDGER cataloguer applied location notes sparingly and inconsistently. Their presence on this filing indicates the location was notable enough to record, though the annotation's purpose is ambiguous. It may describe the station building (inside rather than at the aperture site), or it may reference the aperture structure itself. Osei's November filings were documented near the aperture; the December 7 filing may have been written elsewhere. No clarifying note survives.

The submission time, 06:17 UTC, falls approximately two hours before the station's standard morning operations window. The transport manifest for December 7 shows a departure logged at 09:00 UTC. Osei submitted the document before the day's logistical activity began.

## The document

The December 7 filing, catalogued as SUPPLEMENTARY UNRESOLVED, is inaccessible in the current archive. The 47 documents in this category cannot be opened or searched via the standard SOAR recovery interface. What survives is the manifest record and, in the Talk:SOAR Survey Team discussion from 2043, an excerpt cited by an unidentified participant who claimed to have read the document before the category became inaccessible.

The cited excerpt, reproduced in the talk record:

> *There is a period I cannot account for. I have written about it three times and each version is wrong in a different way. I am leaving this here because the versions belong to the archive even if they are not true.*

The talk record participant who cited this excerpt offered no further context and did not respond to follow-up requests. The excerpt's provenance has not been verified. It may be complete; it may be a partial quotation.

## Context

The December 7 filing is Osei's fifth document in the SOAR archive. Her previous filings:

- **November, three filings:** Classified by the LEDGER as *records of attention*. All three are associated in the manifest with proximity to the aperture site. Their contents are in the SUPPLEMENTARY UNRESOLVED category and cannot be recovered.
- **December 4, one filing:** Filed following the sixth null reading. The manifest does not provide a description. No summary or excerpt survives in secondary sources.

The phrase "records of attention" is Osei's own; it appears in her field notes as a descriptor for observations she considered relevant but not yet interpretable. The LEDGER appears to have adopted her phrasing rather than reclassifying the filings.

Between her three November filings and the December 4 filing, Osei did not submit anything for approximately two weeks. The null readings during that period (readings two through five) generated no corresponding filings from her.

Her decision to file after the sixth null reading, and then again on the final day, is not explained in any accessible document.

## The phrase "wrong in a different way"

If the cited excerpt is accurate, the document contains a claim that Osei made multiple attempts to write about "a period I cannot account for" and found each version incorrect in distinct ways — not variations of the same error, but different kinds of failure.

This claim is consistent with the date discrepancy documented in Article:The Forty Minutes. Osei's recollection places a forty-minute unexplained gap on November 19; the compiled survey logs and Asad's correction place a corresponding instrument gap on November 22. Whether these refer to the same period or two separate incidents is unresolved.

Multiple attempts to record the same event producing different errors is a pattern that appears elsewhere in the archive. The SUPPLEMENTARY UNRESOLVED category may contain other instances. The category is not accessible.

## The final sentence

The excerpt ends: *I am leaving this here because the versions belong to the archive even if they are not true.*

This sentence is the most discussed line in the talk record. Its structure — preservation justified not by accuracy but by archival belonging — recurs in LEDGER's own decommissioning log, in which the cataloguing system's final entry describes itself as the archive in a formulation that sidesteps the question of whether the description is correct.

Whether Osei's final sentence represents a deliberate archival philosophy or an operational note to the cataloguer is not determinable from the document's context. She submitted it and left the station.
