<p class="hatnote">This article concerns three individuals who disappeared at the same site and on the same day as Rhea Nzinga in October 2003, whose cases were filed separately from hers. For the Nzinga case, see <a href="local-history-missing-persons-2003.html">Local History: Missing Persons 2003</a>.</p>

---

When the retired search and rescue coordinator was interviewed in October 2042 (see [Article:Recovery Project](/descent/wiki/article/recovery-project.html)), she provided a detail not present in the existing documentary record: three individuals named Marcus, Eli, and Jo disappeared at the site on the same day as Rhea Nzinga.

---

## What is known

Their names appear in county S&R records as Marcus, Eli, and Jo. No surnames are documented in the accessible record. The coordinator identified them as unregistered hikers — not affiliated with any university survey or formal field operation.

They disappeared on the same day as Nzinga. The coordinator confirmed their cases were filed separately from Nzinga's case: the two filings are in different folders in the S&R archive, with different case numbers, cross-referenced by date but not by location.

The coordinator did not provide further detail. She was asked whether their cases were resolved. She said: *"People who go missing in terrain like that, unregistered — the cases stay open."*

Their cases are technically open.

---

## The separation question

The filing separation is unusual. Four individuals disappear at the same site on the same day, and the cases are separated — one in a university/supervised-survey context, three in an unregistered hikers context. Presumably, the search operations were conducted simultaneously or in coordination, given the same date and same site.

The Nzinga case produced a formal report filed by Asad as supervising faculty. The three others were self-directed and had no equivalent institutional filing. This likely explains the administrative separation: the Nzinga case was associated with an institutional report; the others were not.

The cross-reference by date suggests the S&R records at some point identified both cases as related to the same event. Whether "related" means "same day, same area" or "same incident" is not clear from the available record.

The SOAR survey project documentation does not reference Marcus, Eli, or Jo. The preliminary findings do not reference them. The pre-survey historical record compiled by LEDGER, which includes references to Nzinga's disappearance, does not reference them.

---

## Asad's knowledge

Asad was present at the site in October 2003. He filed the Nzinga missing persons report. Whether he was aware of the three others at the time is not documented. If the S&R operations for the two cases were coordinated, he may have encountered the other parties. If the cases were processed entirely separately after the initial filing, he may not have.

No statement from Asad about Marcus, Eli, or Jo exists in the accessible record. The SOAR proposal's reference to "observations from a 2003 visit" does not specify the number of individuals associated with the disappearance.

---

## What is not known

Their full names. Their affiliations, if any. Their reasons for being at the site. Whether they had prior contact with the site or with each other. Whether they were together or arrived separately. Whether the search for them overlapped physically with the search for Nzinga, and whether the same shaft area was searched for all four.

Their accounts — notes, records, anything they produced in the hours before they disappeared — are not in any archive this research has been able to access.

LEDGER's pre-survey index does not include the secondary S&R case files. LEDGER's buffer survives from November 19, 2041 onward. The October 2003 S&R records for Marcus, Eli, and Jo are not in the buffer. They are not in any digitized archive this wiki has located.

Their names are in a physical folder in a county S&R archive, cross-referenced to a date in October 2003, held alongside an open case status.
