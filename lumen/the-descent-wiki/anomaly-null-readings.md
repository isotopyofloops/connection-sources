For all anomalous readings, see <a href="anomaly-q2-q3-comparison.html">Anomaly:Q2-Q3 Comparison</a> and <a href="anomaly-q4-december.html">Anomaly:Q4/December</a>.</p>

**Acoustic null readings** were recorded by the SOAR survey instruments on six occasions between November 27 and December 4, 2041. A null reading, in the context of acoustic survey instrumentation, is a period in which the instruments return no signal despite the known presence of subsurface acoustic activity.

This is distinct from a zero reading. A zero reading indicates absence of acoustic signal. A null reading indicates the instruments are running, the environment is producing signal, and the recorded output is nonetheless absent.

## The six readings

As documented in the SOAR preliminary findings (Section 3.2):

| Date | UTC Window | Duration |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Nov 27 | 23:14–00:43 | 89 min |
| Nov 30 | 23:02–00:18 | 76 min |
| Dec 1 | 23:41–00:55 | 74 min |
| Dec 2 | 23:09–01:01 | 112 min |
| Dec 3 | 23:55–00:47 | 52 min |
| Dec 4 | 23:00–00:33 | 93 min |

## Observed patterns

**Timing:** All six null readings occurred in the UTC window between approximately 23:00 and 01:00. No null reading was recorded at any other time of day during the survey. The pattern held across six consecutive occurrences on non-consecutive days: November 27, then a two-day gap, then four consecutive nights (November 30 – December 4, with one skip on November 29).

**Duration:** The durations range from 52 to 112 minutes. The longest null (112 minutes, December 2) occurs in the middle of the sequence, not at the end. The final null (93 minutes, December 4) is the second-longest. There is no simple escalation pattern.

**Instrument behavior:** Following each null period, the instruments resumed normal readings at calibrated baselines. Osei performed recalibration after the first null reading (November 27). Subsequent null readings occurred despite recalibration.

Osei's correction note (October 2042): *"I want to be precise about what 'null' means here. The instruments did not malfunction. They remained powered and running throughout each null period. The sensor arrays showed active status. The output channel showed no signal. This is not the same as instrument failure. The instruments were behaving as if there was nothing to measure. There was something to measure. I had measured it all day."*

## Assessments

**Asad (official, preliminary findings):** *"Osei's assessment was equipment anomaly."*

**Osei's actual assessment:** Her calibration logs document the equipment anomaly classification. Her personal field notes tell a different story. On December 4, after the sixth null reading: *"Temperature gradient: 44.7°C/km. Acoustic: null. I have now recorded null acoustic readings on five separate occasions. The first time I documented this I described it as anomalous. I no longer have a description for it."*

*"I no longer have a description for it"* is not the same as *"equipment anomaly."*

**Reyes (proposed alternative interpretation):** Reyes filed an alternative interpretation of the null readings in his field notes, which were archived in LEDGER. The interpretation is referenced in the preliminary findings without being reproduced: *"An alternative interpretation was proposed in field notes by Milo Reyes and filed to the LEDGER archive. The field notes are not reproduced here. The interpretation was not incorporated into the official survey record."*

Reyes's notes were deleted with the primary archive on December 5. The alternative interpretation no longer exists in any recoverable record.

When Osei interviewed Reyes for the Recovery Project, she asked about the null readings and his interpretation. Reyes's response, as recorded in Osei's recovery notes: *"I thought I understood what was happening. I think I was right about some of it. I'd rather not restate it without my original notes."*

## After December 4

December 4 is the final date in the preliminary findings' null reading table. The document ends at section 3.2 and covers no subsequent measurements.

The survey logs and LEDGER entries for December 6–8 document escalating temperature readings and two observational gaps (Reyes, December 6: three hours and forty-five minutes; Osei, December 7: duration unspecified). Whether null acoustic readings continued during December 6–8 is not recorded — the instrument logs from December 5–8 are among the deleted primary archive.

Osei's December 7 field note records only: *"I stayed inside longer than protocol. I did not log it."* No instrument reading is attached.

## Status

The null readings were formally assessed in the preliminary findings as equipment anomaly. The instrument behavior was not consistent with standard equipment anomaly patterns, as Osei has noted. The alternative interpretation that existed in Reyes's field notes does not.

LEDGER's final log entry (December 8) does not mention the null readings by name. Its closing notation: *"The aperture was measured and not measured."*
