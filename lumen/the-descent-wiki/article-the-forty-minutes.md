<p class="hatnote">This article concerns the unexplained forty-minute period in Abara Osei's instrument log during the SOAR survey's first measurement phase. For all measurement gaps, see <a href="article-protocol-deviations.html">Article:Protocol Deviations</a>.</p>

---

The **forty minutes** refers to a period in Abara Osei's instrument log during the SOAR survey in which Osei remained inside the aperture for approximately forty minutes beyond the protocol-specified limit. Her instruments continued running during this period. The data from this period was not recovered.

## What the record shows

Osei's field notes for the relevant date record: *"Reading confirmed. See attached instrument trace. Note: I stayed at the site for approximately forty minutes after the reading. I do not know why I did this. I was not cold. I was not waiting for a follow-up reading. I stood at the shaft entrance and I stayed."*

The attached instrument trace shows a gap corresponding to the forty-minute period. The trace resumes at its previous calibration level. The data from the gap period is absent: not corrupted, not partially readable, not present.

Osei's calibration logs for the preceding and following periods show no instrument error.

## The date discrepancy

The primary source documentation contains a discrepancy regarding the date of the forty-minute period. This discrepancy has not been formally resolved.

**The November 22 reading:** The Anomaly:Q1 Measurements article and the Protocol Deviations article both locate the forty-minute instrument gap in Osei's November 22 measurements. The Protocol Deviations article notes that an earlier draft of the review document incorrectly cited November 19 as the date; Asad submitted a factual correction placing the gap on November 22.

**Osei's own statement:** In her addition to the Talk:Anomaly Q4/December page (filed 2042), Osei wrote: *"I wrote a dash in my November 19 log because I was inside the aperture for forty minutes and when I returned to my instrument station, I did not know how to describe what had changed."* This statement attributes the forty minutes to November 19.

These two accounts cannot both be correct on the date. The November 22 date is supported by the compiled survey logs and Asad's correction. The November 19 date is Osei's direct recollection, made approximately nine months after the event.

What occurred on November 19 is not disputed: Osei asked Asad about the origin of the site coordinates. He said he would check his records. He did not follow up in any logged communication.

Whether the forty minutes was on November 19 or November 22, or whether there were two separate extended stays (one on November 19 and one on November 22), is not documented.

Osei's date correction note (filed September 2042):

*"I've been asked about the discrepancy. I can only say what I remember: I remember November 19 as the day the reading changed and I did not come out when I should have. I don't have a clear memory of what I was doing on November 22. I'm not correcting the protocol deviations article. I'm noting that my recollection differs."*

## What was lost

The instrument trace gap represents forty minutes of acoustic and temperature data taken from inside the aperture. No other SOAR measurement was taken from that position. The Q1 measurements article notes that acoustic resonance at the site was "non-stationary" — measurements taken on successive days at the same coordinates showed unexplained variation.

What forty minutes of continuous interior measurement would have shown is unknown. The data was not recovered.

The SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED article speculates that Osei's November gap data may be among the category's contents — if LEDGER attempted to file it and could not find an appropriate category. This is unconfirmed. The category is inaccessible.

## Relationship to later gaps

The forty minutes is the first documented observational gap in the SOAR record. Two later gaps used the same notation — a single dash:

- Reyes, December 6: three hours and forty-five minutes
- Osei, December 7: duration not specified

Osei has stated she was unaware, at the time she wrote her dash, that the 2003 missing persons record associated with the site contained the same notation in Nzinga's field notes. She has not stated whether she was aware of Nzinga's 2003 disappearance.

The protocol deviation review classifies the forty-minute instrument gap as *non-compliant* with filing requirements but notes that the gap appears to be an equipment failure. Osei's field note — which describes a deliberate extended stay — is not cited in the protocol deviation entry as evidence against the equipment failure classification.
