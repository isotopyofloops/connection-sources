## The incomplete article

The article documents one missing person. The Local History: Intervening Period talk page (see Thread: Four individuals) now establishes that four individuals disappeared at the site in October 2003 — Rhea Nzinga and three unregistered hikers (Marcus, Eli, Jo).

The missing persons article covers only Nzinga because that is the only case that produced an institutional record. The three hikers were filed separately under a different category, never linked to the geology record, and are named only by first name in the county S&R archive.

The article should be read as incomplete, not incorrect. It documents what the institutional record documented. The institutional record documented one.

— unsigned, November 20, 2042

## On what the search found

The article states that the search "included the approaches to the site, the shaft at the site's center, and the surrounding area. No trace of the student was found."

I want to add detail on what "the shaft at the site's center" means in the context of a search operation. I asked the county S&R coordinator (now retired) about the 2003 search in the course of my research. She remembered the site. The shaft search was conducted by a two-person team with a rope line and lights. They descended to the bottom of the fourteen-meter shaft, searched the chamber at the base, and found nothing. The shaft was "clean" — no equipment, no trace of any person. The chamber walls, she said, "were just walls."

This is notable because Nzinga had instruments with her. Her field survey kit would have been substantial — a pack, instrumentation cases. If she descended the shaft, some of that equipment should have remained even if she did not. Nothing was found.

The field notes were found at the entrance — sealed in a plastic bag at the surface. Not in the shaft. Not at the bottom. At the surface.

One interpretation: she left the field notes at the entrance before descending and did not come back up. Another: she descended partway, came back up, left the field notes, and then went somewhere that isn't the shaft. A third: someone moved the field notes after the fact.

The last entry is a dash. She was writing when the entry stopped. Where she was when she was writing is not documented.

— A. Osei, December 4, 2042

## On Asad's position

The article states: "A missing persons report was filed within 24 hours by the supervising faculty member" and "The student's field notes were found sealed in a plastic bag at the entrance to the shaft. The notes were provided to the supervising faculty member."

The supervising faculty member is Asad. He was present when the field notes were found. He received them.

The article does not say he was present during the search. The Talk:Local History: Intervening Period thread establishes that five people went to the site, including Asad, and one returned. This means Asad was present at the site when the others went missing — or was the first to return to a site where four others had gone and not come back.

He filed the missing persons report. He was present when the search team found the field notes at the entrance. He received the field notes. The notes document what Nzinga was observing before her last entry. He has held them since October 2003.

What Asad experienced at the site before filing the report is not in any public record. The record begins with the report.

The search team descended the shaft and found nothing. Asad did not descend with them — the S&R records indicate search team composition, and he was not part of it. He was at the surface when the team came back up empty.

He returned to the site in 2041 and descended himself.

— unsigned, March 7, 2043
