**Observation as constitution: toward a methodology that accounts for its own effects** is a 2031 paper by Khalid Asad proposing the constitutive hypothesis.

## Abstract (reproduced)

*Survey methodology in applied geology has long assumed that measurement is a neutral activity — that instruments report a structure which exists independently of the instruments' presence. This paper argues that assumption requires revision. Drawing on recent work in philosophy of science and on anomalies in subsurface geothermal survey literature, I propose that survey methodology partly constitutes what is found. This does not imply that geological structures are invented by measurement. It implies that what gets found, how it gets classified, and what counts as anomalous or baseline are determined in part by the methodology — and that a methodology that ignores its own constitutive role will produce results it cannot fully account for.*

## Reception

Cited in 38 papers as of 2041. Asad has published one direct response to criticism (2033). He has declined to respond to a 2035 paper characterizing the hypothesis as unfalsifiable.

The paper's footnote 12 reads: *This project began as an attempt to explain a specific set of anomalous readings from 2003. I could not explain them. This paper is not an explanation. It is an account of why explanation may be insufficient.*
