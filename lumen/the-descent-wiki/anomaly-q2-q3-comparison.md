<p class="hatnote">This article covers measurements from weeks three and four of the SOAR survey. For the first two weeks, see <a href="/descent/wiki/anomaly/q1-measurements.html">Anomaly:Q1 Measurements</a>.</p>

**Anomaly:Q2-Q3** designates measurements from the final phase of active surveying (November 30 – December 5, 2041). The designation distinguishes these measurements from Q1 because of a qualitative shift in the data that cannot be fully captured by the standard measurement framework.

*LEDGER archive note: I want to be precise about why I flagged this as a separate category. The Q2-Q3 measurements are not anomalous relative to Q1 in the ways that Q1 was anomalous relative to the regional baseline. They are anomalous in a way I do not have a category for.*

## Q2 Measurements (November 30 – December 3)

Q2 measurements show a continuation of the Q1 anomaly signature with one notable difference: the readings have become more consistent, not less.

In standard geothermal survey methodology, measurement variability decreases over time as instrument calibration is confirmed and environmental factors are controlled for. The Q2 decrease in variability follows this pattern. But the decrease is larger than calibration alone accounts for.

Osei's final Q2 note: *The instruments are reading correctly. I have checked. What I cannot explain is why the readings have stopped varying. The anomaly is more stable now than a normal site would be. This is not what anomalies do.*

Reyes's Q2 observation note, November 30: *I have stopped writing observation notes. I mean: I have been writing them, but I have stopped being uncertain about what I am observing. I am now noting this uncertainty about the absence of uncertainty.*

Asad's Q2 summary: Not filed.

## Q3 Measurements (December 4-5)

Q3 measurements were taken on the survey's final two days. They are the most complete records from the December period.

Osei's December 4 log: *Temperature gradient: 44.7°C/km. Acoustic: null. I have now recorded null acoustic readings on five separate occasions. The first time I documented this I described it as anomalous. I no longer have a description for it.*

Reyes's December 5 log, reproduced in full: *I measured. Everything. All of it. The shaft, the approach, the 50-meter perimeter, the 100-meter perimeter. I measured everything the protocol calls for and several things it does not. The measurements are in the archive. I am done measuring. Whatever I was measuring has been measured now.*

Asad's December 5 log: *See: final log filed under SOAR Survey Log: December.*

## Comparison and analysis

The transition from Q1 to Q2-Q3 represents a shift the existing measurement framework cannot fully account for. LEDGER's designation of these as separate measurement phases is LEDGER's own initiative; the survey protocol does not require phase designations.

The following table is LEDGER's attempt to document the shift:

| Metric | Q1 (Nov 13-29) | Q2-Q3 (Nov 30-Dec 5) |
|--------|----------------|----------------------|
| Temp gradient variability | ±2.3°C/km | ±0.4°C/km |
| Acoustic null readings | 1 | 5 |
| Log completeness | 97% | 43% |
| Team log entries with uncertainty noted | 71% | 12% |
| Team log entries with no uncertainty noted | 8% | 67% |

LEDGER note on the final row: *I do not know how to interpret the decrease in uncertainty. In Q1, the team was uncertain about what they were measuring. In Q2-Q3, they stopped expressing uncertainty. I do not know whether this means they resolved the uncertainty or stopped being able to articulate it. These are different, and I cannot determine which one occurred from the archive alone.*
