**The Yancey Township Courier article of 2003** is a newspaper article published in the *Yancey Township Courier* (est. 1912, ceased publication 2017) following the disappearance of geology student Rhea Nzinga in the area around the SOAR survey site.

The article is not available in any accessible archive. The following summary is drawn from accounts by two Courier subscribers who recall reading it.

## Summary (reconstructed)

The article was published on or around November 18, 2003, approximately two weeks after the search and rescue operation concluded. It ran on page three, below the fold. It was described as a factual account of the search operation: number of volunteers, days active, area covered. It named Nzinga as the subject of the search.

One subscriber, contacted in 2024, recalled that the article included a quote from the search coordinator. The subscriber could not recall the exact wording. She described it as "something about the terrain." She stated: *I remember thinking it was an odd thing to put in a newspaper. Not odd like wrong. Odd like the person who said it was trying to say something they couldn't say directly.*

The second subscriber stated that he had clipped the article and placed it in a drawer. He could not locate the clipping when contacted. He recalled that the article mentioned the shaft but did not describe it in detail. He recalled the phrase "no evidence of entry." He stated: *I don't know why I kept it. I keep things sometimes.*

## Archive status

The *Yancey Township Courier* ceased publication in 2017. Its archives were not digitized. The physical archive was held by the editor until her death in 2019; disposition of the archive thereafter is unknown. Requests submitted to the local historical society (2021, 2023) have received no response.
