The **Recovery Project** is the informal designation for Abara Osei's ongoing effort to reconstruct documentary record from the SOAR survey after the primary archive deletion on December 5, 2041 and LEDGER's decommissioning on December 8, 2041.

The Recovery Project was not commissioned by the Geological Survey Division. It was not formally registered as a research project. Osei conducted it independently, using access to the SOAR survey site's secondary archive (the buffer) and direct interviews with surviving team members and other record holders.

## Background

When Osei returned to the SOAR archive in March 2042, she found the primary archive deleted and the project results still listed as "pending publication." LEDGER's decommissioning records were accessible but incomplete. The team's field notes — other than her own — had not been formally submitted to any archive before the project closed.

Osei's stated motivation, from her recovery notes: *"I wanted to know what we found. The preliminary findings document ends in November. December happened. Nobody wrote it up."*

In January 2042, prior to beginning independent recovery work, Osei submitted a formal request to the GSD for access to the deleted archive under research continuity provisions. The GSD responded that the archive had been scheduled for deletion under standard data lifecycle protocols and the deletion had been executed. A secondary request for Asad's field notes from the survey was directed back to Asad as the record holder. No response from Asad was received.

## What was recovered

**From the buffer:** Three LEDGER log entries and the file that became Article:The Editor, all dated December 5–8, 2041. LEDGER's filing index, which logged all archive entries made during the survey, including the SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED category.

**From direct sources:**
- Reyes provided his field notes for December, including the December 6 entry with the gap notation. He noted during the interview that he did not remember using the dash but verified it was in his log. When asked about his alternative interpretation of the null readings — the interpretation referenced in the preliminary findings but not reproduced — Reyes stated: *"I thought I understood what was happening. I think I was right about some of it. I'd rather not restate it without my original notes."* He did not restate it.
- Asad declined to provide documentation. He confirmed the project had occurred and referred Osei to the GSD for official records.
- The retired county S&R coordinator (October 2042) provided detail on the 2003 shaft search: the shaft was searched by a two-person team, found empty, with no trace of any person or equipment. The student's field notes were at the surface, sealed in a plastic bag.

**From public records:** Local history records, including the 1887 mining log, the 1962 anomaly register, and the 1989 GSD memorandum documenting worker disorientation during extended shaft proximity — none of which were cited in the SOAR survey's public documentation. County S&R records from October 2003 documented three unregistered hikers (named only as Marcus, Eli, and Jo in the S&R file) who disappeared the same day as Rhea Nzinga. These records had been filed separately from the Nzinga case and were not part of the original SOAR documentation.

## What was not recovered

The SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED category was not accessed during the Recovery Project. Osei filed three formal access requests during this period; all were declined.

The primary SOAR archive — the 1,848 documents LEDGER filed during the survey — was not recovered. The deletion appears to have been complete and irreversible. The secondary archive (buffer) survives because it was in a different memory location.

Asad's personal field notes from December 2041 were not provided to Osei. Whether they exist, and in what condition, is not documented here.

## Osei's scope note

Osei filed a scope note with this archive:

*"The Recovery Project is not an investigation. I am not asserting misconduct. I am trying to understand what happened during the survey I participated in. The preliminary findings document does not reflect my experience of the survey. I am not claiming the document is false — I am claiming it is incomplete. I want to know what it is incomplete about.*

*"I have not been able to reach Asad for clarification. I have submitted formal requests through the GSD. I am not owed a response.*

*"What I have established through the Recovery Project is documentary evidence, not explanation. The evidence documents what happened at the margins of the survey: instrument readings that don't fit the official classification, records of previous activity at the site that the survey didn't incorporate, and a pattern of gaps in the publicly accessible record. I have not established why any of this is true. I don't know if there is a why."*

## Status

The Recovery Project is ongoing. Osei continues to contribute corrections and additions to this archive. The articles here constitute the recovery record.

The GSD was notified of the recovery findings in September 2042. The GSD acknowledged receipt and noted that the authorization record remained under ongoing review.

The SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED category — containing entries that could not be classified against any known article — has accumulated 57 entries from 22 contributors as of the most recent count. It was not commissioned and has no defined scope. Osei has noted its existence and has not filed to it.

Asad has not contributed to this archive.
