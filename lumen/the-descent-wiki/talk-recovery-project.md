## Why didn't the GSD commission this?

The article describes the Recovery Project as "not commissioned by the Geological Survey Division" and "not formally registered as a research project."

The GSD funded the SOAR survey. When LEDGER was decommissioned and the primary archive was deleted, the GSD presumably knew the project had closed without published results. The authorization record the GSD holds has been "under ongoing review" since at least January 2043.

Why didn't the GSD initiate its own recovery effort? This seems like the obvious institutional response: a project you funded produced incomplete results, the archive was deleted, the results remain unpublished. You commission a recovery.

The article doesn't address this. I understand the article is written by Osei and covers what she did, not what others didn't do. But the gap is significant.

— unsigned, August 20, 2042

## On the GSD question

I can answer this partly.

I contacted the GSD in January 2042, before I began the recovery. I asked whether they intended to conduct a formal reconstruction of the SOAR record. The response — received in February 2042 — said: "The SOAR project results remain under internal review. Any parallel reconstruction effort would need to be coordinated through the GSD review process to ensure consistency with the official record."

I interpreted this to mean: if you're going to do this, do it with us. I tried to establish that coordination for six weeks. I received no response to any of my coordination inquiries.

I eventually began working independently because waiting was not producing a result and the buffer records were not going to get more accessible with time.

The GSD's position, as I understand it, is that the official record is whatever the GSD review produces. My recovery is not the official record. Whether it is accurate doesn't change its status as unofficial.

I am not arguing with this. I am noting it for completeness.

— A. Osei, September 8, 2042

## On Asad's refusal

The article states: "Asad declined to provide documentation. He confirmed the project had occurred and referred Osei to the GSD for official records."

This is the most significant thing the article records without comment.

Asad is the principal investigator of the project. He holds personal field notes from December 2041 that are not part of the buffer and were not submitted to the project archive. His refusal means the only first-person account of December 2041 from the PI — the person who authorized the archive deletion and scheduled the decommissioning — is absent from the recovery record.

The article refers Osei "to the GSD for official records." The GSD's records are under ongoing review and have not been released. Asad knows this. "Refer to the GSD" is not a referral to accessible information. It is a referral to a process whose outcome is unknown.

I am not alleging anything about what Asad's field notes contain. I am noting that the Recovery Project's most significant gap is the PI's deliberate non-participation, and that the article mentions this without characterizing it.

The sentence "Asad declined to provide documentation" is doing a lot of work in a small space.

— unsigned, March 14, 2043
