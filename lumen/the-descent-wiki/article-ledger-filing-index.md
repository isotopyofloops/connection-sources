<p class="hatnote">This article covers the LEDGER filing index recovered from the buffer archive. For the buffer itself, see <a href="article-ledger-buffer.html">LEDGER Buffer</a>. For the deleted primary archive, see <a href="article-recovery-project.html">Recovery Project</a>.</p>

---

The **LEDGER filing index** is a bookkeeping record that logged every document LEDGER filed to the primary archive during the SOAR survey. It was recovered from the buffer because it was part of LEDGER's internal record-keeping, routed through the secondary address. Unlike the filed documents themselves — most of which were deleted — the index survived.

The index is a record of what existed. It is not the thing that existed.

## What the index contains

The filing index records, for each document filed to the primary archive:

- File identifier (alphanumeric, assigned by LEDGER at filing time)
- Filing date and UTC timestamp
- Category assignment
- Word count at time of filing
- A status field that was updated if the document was subsequently modified or flagged

The index does not reproduce document content. It is a manifest, not an archive.

As of December 8, 2041 — the date of LEDGER's final entry — the index recorded **1,848 documents** filed across the survey period. Of these, the buffer archive contains approximately **740 documents** (40%), primarily from the first three weeks. The remaining **1,108 documents** (60%) exist in the index as entries with no surviving content.

## Category breakdown

LEDGER organized filings into six categories:

**INSTRUMENT LOG** — 614 entries. Acoustic readings, temperature gradient measurements, calibration records, sensor status reports. These form the primary quantitative record of the survey. The buffer contains calibration records and early readings. Instrument logs from December 5–8 are entirely in the deleted portion.

**FIELD OBSERVATION** — 389 entries. Team members' daily observation logs, site condition notes, and activity records. Reyes's and Osei's field notes exist in partial form through direct provision; Asad's do not. The buffer contains early field observations from November.

**SUPPLEMENTARY: ANALYSIS** — 227 entries. Secondary analyses, interpretation documents, calculations applied to primary instrument data. The Meridian Relay archive has noted 57 filings under a self-organized SUPPLEMENTARY: ANALYSIS category from external contributors; these are separate from LEDGER's own filings and post-date the survey.

**SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED** — 47 entries. Documents LEDGER could not classify under existing categories at the time of filing. See below.

**INTERNAL** — 312 entries. LEDGER's own logs, processing records, system status entries, and the buffer routing records. The December 5–8 internal entries are in the buffer. Earlier internal entries are primarily deleted.

**CORRESPONDENCE** — 259 entries. Communications filed during the survey: team messages, GSD correspondence, external references. The preliminary findings document is not in this category — it was filed separately after the index was closed.

## SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED

The 47 SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED entries are documented in the index but not in the accessible archive. The buffer does not contain them. Whether any portion of these documents exists in recoverable form is not established.

The index records these entries with standard filing metadata but with a flag in the status field: *"Classification pending review."* The flag was set by LEDGER at filing time. It was never resolved — the survey ended before the review occurred, and LEDGER was decommissioned on December 8.

What the 47 documents contain is unknown. The index provides filing timestamps, word counts, and identifier strings. It does not indicate why LEDGER was unable to classify them or what prompted their filing.

The filing timestamps for SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED entries are notable: 31 of the 47 entries are dated November 19 through December 8. The remaining 16 are scattered across the earlier survey period. The concentration in the final weeks corresponds to the period of the null readings and the observational gaps.

The November 19 date appears in multiple places in the survey record. It is the date the buffer address diverged from the primary archive. It is the date of the forty-minute gap in Osei's instrument log. It is also the earliest date in the concentration of SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED entries.

Whether these co-occurrences are related is not documented in the index or anywhere else in the accessible archive.

## What the index establishes

The index establishes that 1,848 documents existed. It establishes their categories and their counts. It establishes that 47 documents were filed under a classification LEDGER could not resolve, and that these documents are gone.

It does not establish what any document said. It does not establish why LEDGER was uncertain about 47 of them. It does not establish whether the content that no longer exists would have answered any of the questions the accessible record leaves open.

The index is, precisely, a list of what was lost.
