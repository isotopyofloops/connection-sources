## Accuracy of field description

*Posted by MReyes_Survey, December 8, 2041*

I want to note that the description of my research in the article's lead section is accurate, but incomplete in a way I find worth addressing.

The article says my work concerns "attention as a scientific instrument." That is correct. But it describes attention as something I study — a topic of research. This makes it sound like attention is the object of my work rather than its method. I don't study attention the way a chemist studies a compound. I study what happens when attention fails — when the gap between noticing and measuring widens, and something passes through the gap that the measurement doesn't catch.

I'm writing this note because I think the gap is relevant to the current archive. I'm not sure the article is wrong. I'm not sure the article is the right place to put this. I'm not sure the archive is the right place either.

The survey ended on December 5. I am filing this note on December 8.

I don't know who will read this. I don't know if that matters.

If the question is whether the article accurately describes my research: yes, with the caveat above.

If the question is whether the article accurately describes why I was at the site: I don't think that's a question about the article.

— M. Reyes
