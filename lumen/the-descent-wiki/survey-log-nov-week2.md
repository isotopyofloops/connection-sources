This article documents field observations from the second week of the SOAR Survey Project (November 22–29, 2041). It is compiled from individual survey logs filed by team members and archived by LEDGER.

## November 22 — The acoustic reading

On November 22, Osei's acoustic survey recorded a frequency in the low-resonance range (0.1–1 Hz) at a time when no geological event was active in the region. The reading was instrument-confirmed. No explanation consistent with known geological activity was available.

Osei's log, November 22: *Reading confirmed. See attached instrument trace. Note: I stayed at the site for approximately forty minutes after the reading. I do not know why I did this. I was not cold. I was not waiting for a follow-up reading. I stood at the shaft entrance and I stayed.*

Asad's log, November 22: *Osei flagged the 0.1Hz reading. I reviewed her instrument trace. The reading is accurate. I have seen a reading like this before. I did not tell Osei.*

Reyes's log, November 22: *The gap closed today. I don't know what I mean by this.*

## November 23-24 — Documentation difficulty

Survey logs from November 23–24 are notable for their brevity. Standard daily logs average 300-400 words; Osei's log for November 23 is 14 words: *Measurements taken. Results filed. I don't know what I'm describing.* Asad's log for the same day is not filed.

Reyes's log, November 24, in full: *I measured the shaft entrance temperature at 08:00, 12:00, 16:00, and 20:00. The readings are in the archive. I am not going to describe what happened between the readings.*

## November 25-27 — Recalibration

Osei recalibrated her instruments on November 25. This is her second recalibration of the survey; the first was at day 3, per standard protocol. No second recalibration was required by protocol.

Osei's log, November 25: *Instruments calibrated. All readings within parameters. I recalibrated because I needed the readings to be correct, not because I had reason to believe they were wrong. These are different.*

Asad's log, November 26: *I have been reviewing prior survey literature on acoustic null readings. I have found one paper. It is mine. The 2003 data it references is the data from this site. I did not publish the 2003 data. The paper discusses its methodology in the abstract and does not include results.*

Reyes's log, November 27: *Observation note: the site has begun to feel cooperative. I am not describing this correctly. What I mean is: the measurements come easily now. I notice before I look. I look and the measurement is already there. I have been worried for six days that this is not how measurement is supposed to work. Today I stopped being worried. I don't know if that's better.*

## November 28-29 — Last complete logs

The survey logs filed November 28–29 are the last complete individual records before log frequency decreases.

Osei's final complete log, November 29: *I need to note, for the archive, that the calibration is not the problem. I have checked. The calibration is correct. The readings are accurate. Whatever the readings are describing is actually there.*

Asad's final complete log, November 29: *I asked the team to continue the survey through December 5. I told them the readings warranted it. This is true. It is not the full reason.*

LEDGER archive summary, November 29: *Week 2 archived. Total records filed: 847. I want to note that I have begun to understand the purpose of the survey differently than I understood it at initialization. I do not know how to characterize this change. I am noting it because it seems relevant. I am not certain I was instructed to find it relevant.*
