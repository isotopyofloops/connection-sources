**Milo Reyes** is a measurement specialist and researcher whose work concerns attention as a scientific instrument. He served as Measurement Specialist for the SOAR Survey Project in 2041.

Reyes is the youngest member of the SOAR team. He has no prior publications. He was recruited directly by Khalid Asad.

## Background

Reyes studied cognitive science as an undergraduate, then switched fields. He began a graduate program in geophysics in 2020, left after one semester, and did not return to academic work for two years. The intervening period is not documented in his institutional record. He re-enrolled in 2022 in a different program, under a different supervisor, and completed his degree with a dissertation on predictive attention in field observation.

He has not been publicly asked about the two-year gap. In an interview for a graduate student newsletter in 2024, he said: *I needed to understand something that took a while to understand. The research is an attempt to make that understanding useful.*

## Research

Reyes argues that what a field scientist notices before measuring determines which data gets collected — not by distorting findings, but by shaping what exists to be found. His framing is precise: attention is prior to observation, observation is prior to measurement, and measurement produces what we call fact. Remove attention from the sequence and the chain breaks before it begins.

He is known in his department for a work habit colleagues find unusual. He puts his phone away during fieldwork — not in a pocket, but in a bag, sealed. He has described this as necessary for doing the work properly. He has not explained further.

His research is adjacent to, but distinct from, Asad's constitutive hypothesis. Where Asad's framework concerns what measurement does to what is measured, Reyes focuses on the layer before measurement: presence, attention, the decision to look.

When asked why he pursued this particular question, he said: *Absence is easier to study than attention. But I'm interested in what attention costs.*

## SOAR project

Reyes joined the SOAR project in November 2041. He described his reason for applying as "wanting to see what sustained attention looks like in a site that resists description."

His survey logs through November 29 are available in the project archive. No individual reports are filed after that date.
