**Calibration methodology for subsurface geothermal acoustic surveys**

*Khalid Asad*
*Journal of Applied Geothermal Methods, Vol. 14, 2028*

---

## Abstract

Standard calibration procedures for acoustic survey instruments assume a homogeneous subsurface medium. In geothermal contexts, this assumption fails systematically: temperature gradients, fluid saturation, and mineral composition produce medium heterogeneity that displaces acoustic null points from their predicted positions. This paper proposes a revised calibration protocol that accounts for expected medium variance and introduces a correction factor derived from prior-site geological data. The methodology was validated against eight survey sites in controlled conditions; mean calibration accuracy improved by 23% over the standard protocol. Limitations of the method are discussed, including dependence on prior-site data quality and performance degradation under high-temperature conditions exceeding 180°C at depth.

## Notes

This paper established the calibration protocol later adopted by the SOAR project. Abara Osei, who served as field survey lead on the SOAR project, cited this paper in his own 2026 methodology publication. See [Publication:Osei_2026](/descent/wiki/publication/osei-2026.html).
