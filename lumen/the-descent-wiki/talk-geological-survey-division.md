## The retraction process

The article notes that the GSD "maintains the retraction registry that holds the notice for Abara Osei's 2026 publication" without further description of how retractions are initiated or reviewed.

I am asking about this specifically because the retraction of Osei's paper is unusual. The standard retraction process involves a complaint from a named party, a review period during which the author is notified and given the opportunity to respond, and a published notice that cites the grounds for retraction.

The retraction notice for Osei's 2026 paper does not cite grounds. It says only: "Retracted at the request of the institution." The institution is not named. Osei has stated in published interviews that she was not notified before the retraction was filed.

I am not arguing the retraction was wrong. I am asking the article to describe the process accurately, which means noting that this particular retraction does not appear to follow the standard process.

I have submitted a formal inquiry to the GSD retraction registry. I have not received a response.

— unsigned, December 4, 2026

## On the absence of a statement

The article notes: "The Division has not published a statement regarding the SOAR survey's status or findings."

This has been true since December 2041. It is now June 2028. The SOAR survey ended approximately seven months ago. The survey's results are listed as "pending publication" in the project record.

I want to be precise about what the absence of a statement means. It could mean the GSD is reviewing findings before publication — a normal process. It could mean the findings are not ready for publication. It could mean the GSD does not consider itself responsible for publishing findings from a project it funded, if the publication responsibility lies with the principal investigator. All of these are possible.

But the article should not present the absence of a statement as a neutral fact without acknowledging that by this date, a statement might reasonably have been expected. The survey ended. Nothing has been published. The GSD has been asked and has not responded.

I am not making an accusation. I am noting that "has not published a statement" is different from "has not yet published a statement" and different again from "has declined to publish a statement." The article uses the first phrasing, which does not specify which of these is accurate.

— unsigned, June 17, 2028

## Authorization — request for clarification

I want to add something to the article about the GSD's authorization of the SOAR project.

During the SOAR survey I filed two requests for the project's authorization documentation — the standard GSD form confirming director-level approval and scope agreement. I did not receive a response during the survey period. I have since filed a records request through the GSD's administrative review process.

The GSD confirmed, in January 2042, that an authorization record exists. They declined to provide it, citing "ongoing review." They did not specify what the review is of, or when it might conclude.

The article says the GSD "funded the SOAR Survey Project (2041) under a standard grant agreement." I can confirm the funding occurred. I cannot confirm the authorization was standard. The article should not imply it was, given that the authorization record is currently unavailable and under review.

I am making this note publicly because the authorization question affects how the nine protocol deviations documented in the SOAR archive should be interpreted. A deviation from scope is only a deviation if there was an authorized scope to deviate from. If the scope is under review, the status of the deviations is also under review.

The GSD has the authorization record. It has not released it. The article should reflect this accurately.

— A. Osei, January 9, 2042
