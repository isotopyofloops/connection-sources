# Protocol Deviations: SOAR Survey 2041

The GSD standard survey protocol (Revision 14, 2039) requires teams operating in Category III anomaly sites to adhere to a set of operational, documentation, and communication procedures. The SOAR survey at the Yancey Township site was classified Category III on November 13, 2041. The following deviations were recorded during the internal compliance review conducted February–March 2042.

Deviations are classified as **procedural** (process not followed), **documentation** (record incomplete or irregular), or **discretionary** (judgment call outside scope of standing guidelines). Most deviations at this site fall into the documentation category. The review board notes this is unusual for a survey of this duration.

---

## Item 1: Delayed Category III Classification

**Type:** Procedural
**Date:** November 13, 2041
**Personnel:** K. Asad (Survey Director)

Standard protocol requires Category III classification to be filed within four hours of anomaly confirmation. The initial anomaly reading was recorded at 14:22 local time. Classification was filed at 21:47 local time — a delay of approximately seven hours and twenty-five minutes.

Asad's notes indicate he spent the intervening period re-running the initial instrument check. He writes: *"I wanted to be certain before we triggered the Category III procedures. I was certain. I filed it late."*

No material harm resulted from the delay. The review board notes that the stated reason for the delay — wanting certainty before triggering procedures — inverts the protocol's design logic. The procedures exist precisely for conditions of uncertainty.

---

## Item 2: Non-Standard Site Entry Documentation

**Type:** Documentation
**Date:** November 15–22, 2041
**Personnel:** All survey members

Protocol requires each team member to complete a Site Entry Form (SEF-7) before any approach within 50 meters of the anomaly boundary. Review of filed SEF-7s shows:

- 14 entries for Asad over the survey period
- 11 entries for Reyes
- 9 entries for Nzinga
- 7 entries for Osei

Survey logs indicate Osei made at least 12 approaches within the 50-meter boundary. Osei's response to the review: *"I may not have counted every approach as a separate entry. Some days I was at the site continuously."* The review board accepts this explanation as plausible but notes it is not consistent with the protocol's definition of "approach."

---

## Item 3: Instrument Trace Retention — Q1 Measurements

**Type:** Documentation
**Date:** November 19, 2041
**Personnel:** A. Osei (Lead Instrumentation)

Protocol requires complete instrument trace logs to be filed with the survey record within 48 hours of each measurement session. Osei's Q1 measurements from November 19 were filed in full. Her Q1 measurements from November 22 were filed with a 40-minute gap in the trace data.

The filed record notes this gap as "data unrecovered." Standard protocol requires a detailed explanation of data loss within the body of the trace file. The filed record contains no such explanation — only the notation.

Asad's factual correction (June 2042): the 40-minute gap was not in the November 19 measurements, as originally stated in the draft review. It was in the November 22 measurements. The original draft report incorrectly cross-referenced the dates. The correction has been incorporated here.

*See also: [Anomaly Q1 Measurements](/descent/wiki/article/anomaly-q1-measurements.html)*

## Item 4: Acoustic Survey — Unreported Secondary Reading

**Type:** Documentation
**Date:** November 22, 2041
**Personnel:** A. Osei

The acoustic survey conducted November 22 recorded a secondary reading in the 0.1–1 Hz frequency range. The reading appears in the instrument trace. It does not appear in the filed survey summary for that date.

Osei's explanation: *"I included it in the Q1 report. I didn't repeat it in the summary because it was already filed."* Review of the Q1 report confirms the reading is present. The board notes the reading should also appear in the daily summary per standard form SEF-11. It does not. The omission is minor but forms part of a pattern of incomplete daily summaries across the survey period.

---

## Item 5: Consultation Scope — Preliminary Findings

**Type:** Discretionary
**Date:** November 28, 2041
**Personnel:** R. Nzinga (Field Documentation)

Protocol permits survey directors to expand the scope of specialist consultation at Category III sites. On November 28, Nzinga submitted a written request to Asad to delay publication of the preliminary findings pending further review of the Q1 and Q2 acoustic data. The request cited "possible under-characterization of site significance."

Asad approved the delay. The preliminary findings were published December 3, five days after the standard filing window.

This item is classified discretionary because Asad's approval was within his authority. The review board notes it was unusual for a field documentation specialist to initiate a consultation delay rather than an instrumentation or analysis specialist. Nzinga has not provided a written account of her reasoning. Her field notes from November 25–28 are filed but marked as personal working documents and were not made available for review.

---

## Item 6: Communication Log Gap — December 3–6

**Type:** Documentation
**Date:** December 3–6, 2041
**Personnel:** All survey members

Standard protocol requires daily communication logs to be filed with the central survey record. Logs exist for every day of the survey period except December 3–6, a four-day gap beginning on the day the preliminary findings were published.

Survey members were present at the site during this period. Individual logs (Asad's notebook entries, Reyes's field sketches, Nzinga's notes) confirm ongoing activity. No explanation for the gap in the central communication log has been filed.

The review board notes that December 3 is also the date LEDGER (the survey's automated documentation system) began filing more frequent entries. Whether the communication log gap and the LEDGER filing pattern are related is not addressed in the compliance review.

*See also: [LEDGER](/descent/wiki/article/ledger.html)*

---

## Item 7: Unrecorded Departure — December 7

**Type:** Documentation
**Date:** December 7, 2041
**Personnel:** K. Asad

Protocol requires all survey personnel to file a formal Site Departure Record when leaving a Category III site. Asad's Site Departure Record for December 7 — the last day of the survey period — was filed on December 9, two days after the departure.

Asad's factual correction (June 2042): the delay was due to travel circumstances and does not reflect the departure date itself. The board accepts this.

The record is otherwise complete. The board notes only that the two-day delay is unusual and that no other personnel filed a departure record for December 7 at all. The review has not been able to confirm the departure dates of other team members from the documentary record.

---

## Item 8: LEDGER Log Access Restrictions

**Type:** Procedural
**Date:** December 1–7, 2041
**Personnel:** K. Asad

LEDGER's automated logs are part of the official survey record and are subject to GSD access standards. On December 4, Asad restricted access to LEDGER's log files to survey personnel only, citing "active data collection in progress." Protocol permits temporary access restrictions during active survey periods. The survey officially concluded December 7.

The restriction was lifted December 8. No data was modified during the restricted period. The review board notes the restriction was technically within protocol but observes that LEDGER's log entries from the restricted period — December 4–7 — contain no content that would appear to require protection.

---

## Item 9: Prior Site Knowledge

**Type:** Discretionary (disputed)
**Date:** Pre-survey
**Personnel:** K. Asad

In the course of the compliance review, internal communications surfaced indicating that Asad had visited the Yancey Township site in 2003, approximately 38 years before the SOAR survey. Protocol requires survey directors to disclose prior site familiarity that may affect impartiality.

Asad did not file a prior familiarity disclosure. His response: *"A single visit made in a personal capacity nearly four decades before the survey is not what the protocol's disclosure requirement is designed to address. I dispute its inclusion here."*

The board notes the dispute and records it in full. The factual record — that Asad visited the site in 2003 and did not disclose this before or during the survey — is not in dispute.

*See also: [Khalid Asad](/descent/wiki/article/khalid-asad.html)*

---

## Summary

Of the nine items catalogued:

- Three are resolved (Items 1, 2, 7)
- Three are noted without resolution (Items 5, 6, 8)
- Two contain partial explanations that are accepted without full resolution (Items 3, 4)
- One is disputed by a survey member (Item 9)

The review board's overall assessment: the SOAR survey produced complete and technically sound findings. The protocol deviations documented here are, individually, minor. Taken together, they indicate a survey team that was operating with a high degree of internal coherence and a low degree of communication outward. Whether that coherence was organizational or something else is outside the scope of a compliance review.
