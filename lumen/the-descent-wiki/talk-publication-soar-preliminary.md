## On "no anomalous readings in weeks one and two"

Section 3.1 reads: *"Readings for survey days 1 through 14 (November 13–26, 2041) showed subsurface acoustic response consistent with expected geothermal activity at this site type. Calibration remained stable throughout this period. No anomalous readings were recorded in weeks one and two."*

The Anomaly:Q1 Measurements article documents the following during survey days 1–14:

- A November 22 acoustic reading in the 0.1–1 Hz frequency range, which Osei's log describes as recorded at a time when no geological event was active in the region, and which was followed by Osei's forty-minute unexplained stay.
- A forty-minute instrument data gap in Osei's trace log (date disputed, but within weeks 1–2 per the protocol deviation correction).
- Milo Reyes's November 17 observation note: *"I keep noticing things before I measure them."* His November 25 note: *"The gap between the two is not closing."*

Asad wrote this document. He was on-site during all of these observations.

"No anomalous readings were recorded in weeks one and two" is the document's statement about the Q1 period. The Q1 period contains a forty-minute instrument gap and an anomalous acoustic frequency. These are in the archive.

One interpretation: Asad's characterization reflects his technical classification of these events — they were recorded but assessed as explicable (instrument variance, standard acoustic range). Another interpretation: they weren't anomalous to him because he already knew they would occur. A third: he was writing a preliminary draft under a characterization he intended to revise.

The document ends at section 3.2. Section 3.3 was where "Discussion" would have gone.

— unsigned, February 3, 2042

## On the alternative interpretation

The document states: *"An alternative interpretation was proposed in field notes by Milo Reyes and filed to the LEDGER archive. The field notes are not reproduced here. The interpretation was not incorporated into the official survey record."*

This is the only mention of my field notes in any official document.

I want to note a few things about this sentence. First: the alternative interpretation is described as not incorporated. This is correct. It was filed. It was not included. Those are two different things.

Second: "the official survey record" is the preliminary findings document. The document is incomplete. It ends at section 3.2. Whether the alternative interpretation would have been incorporated into section 3.3 or 4 cannot be determined, because those sections were never written.

Third: my field notes from November are in the LEDGER archive. The LEDGER archive was deleted on December 5. The recovery project located my December field notes — I provided them to Osei for the recovery. My November field notes, including the interpretation referenced in section 3.2, are among the 1,848 deleted documents.

The document that mentions my interpretation without reproducing it was uploaded by Asad to this archive in January 2042. The notes it references were deleted in December 2041. This sequence means the mention is now the only public trace that the interpretation existed.

I am not saying this was deliberate. I am saying it is the outcome.

— M. Reyes, May 28, 2042

## On the survey end date

Section 1 reads: *"The SOAR survey was conducted at the Yancey Township geothermal site from November 13 to December 5, 2041."*

The archive contains:
- Reyes's December 6 field notes (the gap notation)
- Osei's December 7 field notes
- LEDGER log entries for December 6 and December 7
- LEDGER's December 8 log documenting the decommissioning process

The survey, by the documentation in this archive, ran through at least December 7. The primary archive was deleted on December 5.

One interpretation: Asad wrote the preliminary findings after the survey concluded its primary phase (through the December 4 null reading), before the December 5 deletion. The December 5 end date reflects the formal close of the survey as Asad recorded it. The December 6–8 events occurred after the document was drafted and are not covered by it.

Another interpretation: the document was written to describe a survey that officially ended December 5. What happened December 6–8 is outside its scope because it was outside its defined scope.

The document doesn't say the survey was complete when it was submitted. It says the survey ran through December 5. Whether something continued after December 5 is left unaddressed.

— unsigned, July 11, 2042

## On "no further edits authorized"

The document was uploaded to this archive by Asad on January 15, 2042. The edit summary reads: *"Uploaded recovered draft. Document is incomplete. No further edits authorized."*

This is the most significant sentence Asad has written about the SOAR project since its close.

"Uploaded recovered draft" — recovered from where? The primary archive was deleted in December. A recovered draft would have come from a backup (Asad's personal files), not the primary archive. If Asad had a personal copy of the preliminary findings, he also had access to his December field notes.

"No further edits authorized" — this document is not protected under any policy this archive is aware of. It is a draft, filed by the person who wrote it. His statement that no further edits are authorized does not have a technical basis in archive administration. It has only the authority of his having said it.

What he is saying, functionally: this is the version of the preliminary findings that will exist in this archive. It ends at section 3.2. The null readings are documented. The alternative interpretation is mentioned but not reproduced. The discussion and conclusions are absent. This is the record.

Asad was the principal investigator. He held the survey. He deleted the archive. He uploaded the incomplete draft. He sealed it.

The Recovery Project article notes that Asad "confirmed the project had occurred and referred Osei to the GSD for official records." The preliminary findings document is not the GSD official record. It is Asad's document of what the project found.

It is incomplete, and he authorized it that way.

— unsigned, September 30, 2042
