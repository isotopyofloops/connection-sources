<p class="hatnote">This article addresses the absence of Rhea Nzinga's field notes from the 2003 survey. For the parallel anomaly concerning Khalid Asad's field observations, see <a href="anomaly-asad-field-observations.html">Anomaly: Asad's Field Observations</a>. For the missing persons record, see <a href="local-history-missing-persons-2003.html">Local History: Missing Persons (2003)</a>.</p>

---

Rhea Nzinga was a graduate researcher conducting acoustic survey work at the site in October 2003, under the supervision of Khalid Asad. She disappeared on or around October 22, 2003. Her field notes, instrument logs, and acoustic survey data from the period preceding her disappearance are not present in any accessible archive.

This article documents the anomaly of their absence, what can be inferred about their contents, and their relationship to Asad's 2028 publication methodology.

---

## What the Notes Should Contain

Based on the nature of Nzinga's work, the field notes from the October 2003 survey should include: daily field logs in standard format (conditions, instrument status, observations, anomalies); acoustic survey readings from the site geometry; and coordination records with Asad's primary survey work.

A classmate's note posted to this archive in 2028 states that acoustic survey work was Nzinga's specialism, that she was "good at it," and that she "had ideas." The note was filed as part of a Talk page discussion and cannot be independently verified, but it is the only contemporaneous description of Nzinga's research focus from someone who knew her. (See [Talk:Rhea Nzinga](/descent/wiki/talk/rhea-nzinga.html).)

The survey began in early October 2003. Nzinga disappeared approximately three weeks into the fieldwork.

---

## Archive Status

A departmental transfer request was submitted in November 2003 for "Nzinga, R. — field survey materials, 2003." The receiving institution is listed as GSD Regional (Yancey Township). No record of receipt exists in the GSD regional archive. The transfer request itself is not in the GSD file.

Asad's personal papers, deposited with his university in 2028, contain no documents authored by Nzinga. The papers include references to "field data from site surveys, 2003–2004" in the citations section of his 2028 publication on acoustic calibration methodology. The citation names no individual researcher.

The materials were presumed lost or unlocatable. Asad's December 1 survey log has since established otherwise. See: *The December 1 Disclosure*, below.

---

## The 2028 Methodology Connection

Asad's 2028 publication proposes a revised calibration protocol for subsurface geothermal acoustic surveys. The paper's central contribution — a correction factor for acoustic null point displacement — is described as "derived from prior-site geological data."

The paper does not identify what prior-site data was used. It specifies only that the correction factor was validated at "eight survey sites in controlled conditions."

The site where Nzinga conducted acoustic survey work in 2003 is the same site later surveyed by the SOAR project, for which Asad's 2028 calibration protocol was adopted. There is no documented acoustic survey of that site between Nzinga's 2003 fieldwork and Asad's 2028 publication. If the correction factor in the 2028 paper derives from prior-site acoustic data, the only prior-site acoustic survey on record is Nzinga's.

Osei's clarification note (filed July 2043): *"I want to be precise about what I can and cannot say. The 2028 paper's methodology informed the SOAR project design. I used it. What data underlies the correction factor is not stated in the paper. I cannot confirm or deny that Nzinga's work was the source. I am noting this because the question has been asked and the accurate answer is that I don't know."*

## The December 1 Disclosure

On December 1, 2041, Asad filed a survey log entry that has no parallel elsewhere in the accessible SOAR record. The entry contains the following passage:

*Rhea's coordinates. Her sealed notes that I have carried for thirty-eight years. The paper I wrote that started as an attempt to explain what happened and ended as a hypothesis about measurement because I could not explain what happened.*

*I am not putting this in the preliminary findings.*

This establishes that Asad has been in possession of Nzinga's notes continuously since 2003. The transfer request submitted in November 2003 was not fulfilled — the notes were never delivered to GSD Regional. Whether Asad requested their return, whether the transfer was cancelled, or whether the notes were never formally tendered for transfer, is not documented.

"Sealed notes" indicates Asad has preserved the notes in their original state. They have not been destroyed. They have not been deposited with any institution. They have been held by Asad for thirty-eight years.

Asad's statement that his 2028 paper originated as "an attempt to explain what happened" in 2003 substantially alters the citation question. The correction factor in that paper — "derived from prior-site geological data" — is now more precisely understood as derived, at minimum in part, from data in Nzinga's sealed notes. Asad had the notes. He derived a methodology. He published it without attribution.

LEDGER's archive note on this entry (filed December 1, 2041): *I have now archived this log entry. I want to note that this is the first time Asad has named the source of the coordinates in any filed record. I am noting this because it seems relevant to the archive's completeness. I am uncertain whether I was supposed to note this.*

The notes' current location — whether they remain with Asad, with his estate, or with his institution in some form — is not documented in any accessible record. The preliminary findings, filed December 5, 2041, do not mention them.

---

## The Parallel Structure

The absence of Nzinga's field notes is structurally parallel to the absence of Asad's own 2003 field observations.

Both sets of notes are absent. Both were produced at the same site during the same survey period. Both document — or would have documented — what was observed in the weeks before and on the day of Nzinga's disappearance.

The parallel raises a question the archive cannot answer: whether the two absences are the same absence, described from two positions. Whether what passed through the gap that October also passed through the record of it.
