**SOAR Survey Project: Preliminary Findings**

## 1. Project Overview

The Systematic Observation of Acoustic Response (SOAR) survey was conducted at the Yancey Township geothermal site from November 13 to December 5, 2041. The survey team consisted of three field researchers and one automated archive management system. The primary research objective was acoustic characterization of subsurface geothermal activity at a site with documented historical anomalies.

The site was selected based on 2003 coordinates established by the Geological Survey Division. See [Local History:The Site](/descent/wiki/local-history/the-site.html) for prior survey history.

## 2. Methodology

Acoustic survey instrumentation was calibrated per the protocol established in [Asad, 2028](/descent/wiki/publication/asad-2028.html), with modifications for high-temperature conditions. Field measurements were conducted daily, with instrument calibration checks performed by Abara Osei at survey initiation and at six-day intervals. Observation notes were filed to the LEDGER archive system daily.

## 3. Preliminary Findings

### 3.1 Standard Acoustic Readings

Readings for survey days 1 through 14 (November 13–26, 2041) showed subsurface acoustic response consistent with expected geothermal activity at this site type. Calibration remained stable throughout this period. No anomalous readings were recorded in weeks one and two.

### 3.2 Anomalous Null Readings

Beginning November 27, 2041, the survey instruments recorded six instances of acoustic null readings — periods in which the instruments returned no signal despite the known presence of subsurface geothermal activity. These null readings occurred at the following times:

| Date | UTC Window | Duration |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Nov 27 | 23:14–00:43 | 89 min |
| Nov 30 | 23:02–00:18 | 76 min |
| Dec 1 | 23:41–00:55 | 74 min |
| Dec 2 | 23:09–01:01 | 112 min |
| Dec 3 | 23:55–00:47 | 52 min |
| Dec 4 | 23:00–00:33 | 93 min |

Instrument recalibration was performed following the November 27 event. Recalibration did not eliminate subsequent null readings. Osei's assessment was equipment anomaly; see [Anomaly:Q2/Q3 Comparison](/descent/wiki/anomaly/q2-q3-comparison.html) for the full instrument record.

An alternative interpretation was proposed in field notes by Milo Reyes and filed to the LEDGER archive. The field notes are not reproduced here. The interpretation was not incorporated into the official survey record.
