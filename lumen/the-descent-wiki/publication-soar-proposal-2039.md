**Proposal for a Systematic Geothermal Acoustic Survey at Site 47°N-112°W**

*Khalid Asad*
*Submitted to the Geological Survey Division, November 14, 2039*
*Reference: GSD/PROP/2039/0047*

---

## Summary

This proposal requests GSD authorization and funding for a systematic geothermal and acoustic survey at a documented anomaly site at coordinates 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W. The proposed survey — designated the Subsurface Observation and Anomaly Research (SOAR) project — would be the first systematic characterization of this site since 2003.

The site presents a geothermal anomaly of significant magnitude. Prior measurement records indicate heat flux substantially exceeding the regional baseline. The anomaly's heat source has not been determined. Depth characterization has not been completed. The 2003 data, while preliminary, suggests the site merits investigation using updated instrumentation.

## Background and rationale

The site at 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W has been documented in the geological record since at least 1962. A state geological survey entry from that year recorded temperature anomaly and noted equipment limitations that precluded depth characterization.

Subsequent informal observation has produced data consistent with the 1962 record, indicating continued anomaly persistence over a period exceeding sixty years. No mechanism capable of sustaining geothermal anomaly of this magnitude for this duration has been identified in published literature on sites of comparable type.

The 2028 calibration methodology published by the applicant (*Journal of Applied Geothermal Methods, Vol. 14*) addresses systematic limitations in acoustic survey instrumentation under high-temperature subsurface conditions. This methodology was specifically developed, in part, to address the measurement challenges presented by sites of this type.

It is the applicant's professional judgment that a systematic survey at this site, conducted under updated methodology, would produce results of significant scientific value.

## Team composition

The applicant will serve as principal investigator. An acoustic measurement specialist with experience in high-anomaly subsurface readings will be recruited to serve as field survey lead. Supporting research staff will be drawn from the GSD associate pool.

An automated archive management system will be deployed for real-time documentation.

## Prior data

Prior measurement data at this site is limited. Available records consist of the 1962 state geological survey entry and preliminary field observations conducted by the applicant in 2003. The 2003 data is not formally archived and should be considered indicative rather than systematic.

The proposal relies on the documented 1962 entry as the primary evidence base. The applicant's 2003 observations are noted as corroborating but are not reproduced here.

## Access and logistics

The site is accessible by a vertical shaft descent of approximately 14 meters. The shaft entrance is located at the coordinates above. Access does not require permits beyond standard GSD survey authorization.
