<p class="hatnote">This article concerns a 2031 geological survey led by Abara Osei. The survey was not formally reported. The primary source for this article is Khalid Asad's account of a conversation with Osei at a 2038 conference, recorded in the <a href="/descent/wiki/talk/abara-osei.html">Talk:Abara Osei</a> page. Osei has provided limited additional information; see below.</p>

---

The **Osei Survey (2031)** refers to an unnamed geological survey led by Abara Osei in 2031. The survey's location, funding source, team composition, and formal results are not documented in any publicly accessible record.

The survey exists in the archive record only through Asad's account of a 2038 conversation in which Osei described it informally.

## What is known

The following is drawn from Asad's Talk:Abara Osei entry (December 1, 2041):

Osei described the survey as having produced readings that "weren't possible given the substrate." She did not file a formal report on the anomalous readings. She described her decision as: "I had decided they were instrument error."

Asad's interpretation: "She said this with a precision that made me think she had decided no such thing."

This is the complete primary source.

## Osei's account

Osei provided the following addition to this article in June 2042, after the recovery project was underway:

*"The 2031 survey was a shallow geothermal assessment for an infrastructure project. The readings I described to Asad at the 2038 conference were a single anomalous sequence on day four, during routine calibration checks. The instruments showed a depth response that was inconsistent with the substrate geology.*

*I filed the calibration records as 'instrument variance — environmental' and continued the survey. The infrastructure project was approved on the basis of the survey results. The anomalous sequence was not in the submitted report.*

*What I told Asad at the conference was more than I had told anyone else about it. I don't know why I told him. The conversation was informal. I think I was looking for a methodological explanation.*

*He asked me if I thought the instruments had been wrong. I said yes. He asked me how confident I was. I said enough to file the report. That is all I will say on this."*

## Significance

Asad cited Osei's "experience with anomalous subsurface readings" as one of two reasons for inviting her to join the SOAR project. The other was her "exceptional calibration record."

The 2031 survey is the only documented instance prior to SOAR where Osei encountered readings she categorized as anomalous in a context other than the SOAR site. She did not report the anomaly in the 2031 case. Her decision to join the SOAR project — and to report her November 19, 2041 observations, even as an undocumented gap — constitutes a different choice.

Whether these choices are related is not documented.
