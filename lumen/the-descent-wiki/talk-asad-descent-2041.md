## What the descent changed

The article documents that Asad descended the shaft on December 3, 2041, came back up, and did not describe what he found. The preliminary findings document was filed two days later, classified all anomalous readings as equipment anomaly, and was sealed against further edits.

The question I want to raise is not about what Asad found — that's undocumented. The question is about what the descent changed in the survey's subsequent documentation.

The preliminary findings were filed on December 5. The descent was on December 3. The period between them — December 4 and 5 — includes the sixth null reading (December 4, 23:00–00:33, the second-longest in the survey). The preliminary findings cover data through approximately November — the December null readings appear only in Osei's personal field notes and the recovered buffer.

The sequence: Asad descended (December 3). The sixth null occurred (December 4 night). The archive was deleted and the preliminary findings filed (December 5). The preliminary findings do not cover December.

The descent precedes the closure. Whether there is a relationship is not established.

— unsigned, March 19, 2043

## On what I observed

The article quotes my account of December 3: *"He appeared focused. Not distressed. He did not describe what he found. I did not ask."*

I want to add to this.

When Asad came back up from the shaft, he stood at the entrance for a period that I estimated, in my field notes, at roughly five minutes. I was calibrating instruments approximately fifteen meters away. I did not approach. He was not signaling distress. He was not writing. He was standing.

Then he walked to the site tent where we kept the equipment logs, sat down, and worked for approximately an hour. I do not know what he was working on. The site tent's logs — instrument calibration records, equipment checks — were part of the primary archive and were deleted on December 5.

I described him as "focused" because that is the word that captures what I saw: a person attending to something internal. Not processing, not distressed, not disoriented. Attending.

The 1989 GSD memorandum describes workers who had spent extended time near the shaft entrance as experiencing "mild disorientation and difficulty maintaining sequential task focus." Asad descended and returned and worked for an hour. If the memorandum's description applies, it did not appear to apply to him on December 3.

I cannot tell you what he found. I can tell you that after an hour of quiet work in the site tent, he ate dinner and went to sleep at the normal time.

— A. Osei, April 1, 2043

## On the forty-year interval as a research design

The article notes the thirty-eight-year interval between 2003 and 2041. I want to argue that the interval was not a delay — it was the design.

Asad was present at the site in October 2003 when four people went missing. He filed the report. He received the field notes. He did not return to the site until 2041.

What happened between 2003 and 2041: he became an expert in acoustic measurement of geothermal anomalies. He developed a correction methodology for acoustic null points. He identified an acoustic measurement specialist with experience in high-anomaly readings. He located a GSD memorandum documenting the site's effect on field workers. He assembled a team, obtained institutional authorization, deployed a survey infrastructure.

The 2041 descent was not the first time Asad attempted to understand the site. It was the first time he attempted to understand it with instruments. The 2003 experience — whatever it was — was not instrument-mediated. He came back with instruments.

The descent on December 3 was then the first time since 2003 that he encountered the site directly, in person, without the mediation of instrument readings and survey protocol. He had spent nearly four decades building a framework for approaching what he found that day in 2003. On December 3, he put the instruments aside and went in himself.

The preliminary findings were filed two days later.

— unsigned, June 12, 2043
