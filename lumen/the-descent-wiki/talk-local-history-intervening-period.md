## Four individuals

The article's opening sentence: *"Following the October 2003 disappearance of four individuals at the site..."*

The Local History: Missing Persons 2003 article documents one missing person: a geology student supervised by Khalid Asad. The article does not mention any other missing persons from that event.

The article for Rhea Nzinga describes her as the subject of the 2003 missing persons search.

How does the count reach four? The source is not cited.

— unsigned, May 14, 2042

---

## On the count

The number appears in the search records I reviewed. The October 2003 search was filed as a missing persons case for one named individual (Rhea Nzinga). However, the search operation itself — as documented in the Yancey Township search and rescue records — was initiated in response to four individuals who did not return from the site area on the same day.

The other three individuals were not reported under the same filing. They do not appear in any GSD or university record I accessed. The Yancey Township S&R records list them under a separate filing, marked *undocumented excursion* rather than *supervised survey.* The filing contains first names only.

Why the discrepancy? My working interpretation: Nzinga was there on a supervised thesis survey — she had a faculty supervisor (Asad), a registered field trip, an academic affiliation. The institutional machinery of a missing student was activated: supervisor files report, university notified, GSD logged. The other three had no such affiliation with the site. They were, according to the filing, not conducting formal survey activity. Their absence was reported to local authorities by a family member. The case was absorbed into the S&R operation but filed separately and never linked to the institutional record.

I don't know who they were. I found first names only and was not able to identify them further. They are not connected to the geology community. They are connected to the site — they went there and did not come back.

— A. Osei, June 18, 2042

---

## On the other three

The Yancey Township search and rescue records for October 2003 are now available in a scanned format at the county historical archive. I have reviewed them.

The three individuals (first names: Marcus, Eli, Jo) are listed as "recreational hikers" who entered the site area without registration. The S&R filing notes their last known position as "proximate to geological survey point." The filing does not specify the shaft.

The S&R operation closed their case simultaneously with Nzinga's, same finding: *cause undetermined.*

No subsequent filings. The names do not appear in any other public record from 2003 onward.

They were at the same site at the same time. The formal record treats them separately because their contexts were different — one was a supervised student, three were unregistered hikers. The site did not treat them differently.

I find this significant: in 2003, five people went to the site (including Asad). One returned. The one who returned was the one who had the field notes in his possession when the search team arrived.

The article and the missing persons article together document one of the five. The other three with first names only are in the S&R records. Asad is in all of them as the supervisor who filed the report.

— unsigned, November 3, 2042
