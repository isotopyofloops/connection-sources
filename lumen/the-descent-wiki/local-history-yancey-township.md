**Yancey Township** is the nearest township to the SOAR survey site, located approximately 34 kilometers from coordinates 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W.

The township has a population of approximately 340. It was established in the 1870s as a mining support settlement. Mining activity in the area ceased in the 1920s. The township persists as a service community.

## Survey site connection

The SOAR survey site lies within the historical survey area of the township. Local oral history contains several references to the site location, consistently described as a place that is "not safe to go to, not for reasons anyone can explain." No written record of these references has been made.

A 2003 search and rescue operation based out of Yancey Township covered the area around the survey site following the disappearance of a geology student. Residents who participated in the search have not given interviews. The township's local newspaper published one article about the event; the article is not available in any accessible archive.
