This article compiles documented references to the site at coordinates **47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W** prior to the commencement of the SOAR Survey Project in November 2041. References are presented in chronological order.

LEDGER compiled this record during pre-survey orientation. The compilation is incomplete. LEDGER notes that the 1989 survey file referenced here was not available through the standard archive request system; the document was located in a physical file room at [institution redacted] by a member of the SOAR team.

---

## 1887 — Minford & Sons Mining Company, Internal Survey Log

*Fragment. Original document held in county historical archive, Box 17, Folder "Site Surveys — Partial."*

> The eastward extension of Shaft No. 4 reached a formation of unknown character on the 14th inst. The lead surveyor recommends no further extension in this direction pending consultation. The acoustic properties of the formation are unusual and would require documentation before continued work. No documentation has been scheduled.

The eastward extension of Shaft No. 4 was not continued. Minford & Sons ceased operations at this site in 1889. No documentation of the acoustic properties was produced.

The relationship of the Minford mining operation to the vertical shaft documented at the site in all subsequent surveys has not been established. Geological opinion is divided as to whether the shaft is a natural formation or evidence of industrial origin.

---

## 1962 — State Geological Survey, Anomaly Register Entry

*Entry No. 1947-QC. Original held in State Geological Survey archive.*

> Site coordinates 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W. Geothermal signature exceeds regional baseline by approximately 340%. Heat source undetermined. Vertical formation at center of anomaly. Depth measurement not obtained — equipment malfunction in field conditions. Recommend follow-up survey.

Follow-up survey was not scheduled. The entry was classified as low-priority. A handwritten note appended to the entry at an unspecified later date reads: *see 1989 file.*

---

## 1989 — State Geological Survey, Internal Memorandum

*Subject: Site QC-1947. Classification: Internal. Not for general distribution.*

> Following the field survey conducted in September of this year, the Surveying Division recommends no further investigation of Site QC-1947 at this time.

> The heat flux measurements obtained are consistent with the 1962 entry and indicate continued increase in anomaly intensity. Temperature gradient at the shaft entrance exceeds safe working conditions for extended measurement operations. Depth measurement was not obtained.

> Two members of the field team reported disorientation following extended proximity to the shaft. One member declined to return to the shaft after the first day. These symptoms are attributed to a combination of altitude, thermal conditions, and terrain.

> The site presents no immediate geological risk to populated areas. The anomaly is of scientific interest but does not meet the threshold for priority investigation given current resource allocation.

> This memorandum is filed in the physical archive only.

*The memorandum was located in a physical file room at [institution redacted] by Dr. Khalid Asad in advance of the SOAR survey. It was not listed in the institution's digital record system. No explanation for its absence from digital records has been provided.*

## 1994 — Harmon, P.R., *Unusual Geological Sites of [County Redacted]: A Local Historian's Compilation*

*Self-published. Print run: 30 copies. One copy held in Yancey Township Library. One copy held in SOAR project files.*

> The site near the Minford workings has a persistent local reputation. Residents who have walked near the site describe a low sound that has no obvious source. Several describe a feeling of being watched, which they attribute to the acoustics of the terrain. Two residents told this compiler that they will not return to the area, though neither would say why.
>
> The site is not marked on any current map. The reasons for this omission are unclear. A geological entry exists in state records from 1962. A follow-up survey appears to have been conducted in 1989. This compiler was not able to obtain the 1989 results.

Harmon, P.R. died in 1997. The Yancey Township Library copy of this compilation was water-damaged in a 2018 flood. The text above was transcribed by a library volunteer from a surviving partial photocopy.

---

## Summary

At the time the SOAR Survey Project commenced, the following was known or obtainable:

- The site had been noted by mining surveyors in 1887 and not followed up.
- The site was registered as a geological anomaly in 1962 and not followed up.
- A 1989 field survey had been conducted. Its results were not filed in digital records. The physical file existed but was not cross-referenced.
- Local oral history consistently described the site as unusual and avoided it.
- A missing persons case from 2003 was associated with the site. The supervising faculty member of the missing person was Dr. Khalid Asad, the lead investigator of the SOAR survey.

The SOAR project documentation does not reference the 1887 mining log, the 1989 memorandum, the Harmon compilation, or the 2003 missing persons case.

LEDGER notes: this compilation was created during pre-survey orientation. It is unclear which members of the SOAR team reviewed it.
