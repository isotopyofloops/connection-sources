This article documents available field records from the final phase of the SOAR Survey Project (December 1–8, 2041). Individual survey logs become sparse after November 29. The records compiled here represent what LEDGER was able to archive.

*LEDGER archive note: I want to state clearly that this log is incomplete. I have archived every record I received. I do not know what was not filed.*

## December 1

Asad's log, December 1: *I told the team we were here because the readings warranted it. I want to note, for the archive, that this was true and that it was not the only reason. I came because I have been coming for thirty-eight years in some form or another. I came because I owe this place something I have not been able to name.*

*Rhea's coordinates. Her sealed notes that I have carried for thirty-eight years. The paper I wrote that started as an attempt to explain what happened and ended as a hypothesis about measurement because I could not explain what happened.*

*I am not putting this in the preliminary findings.*

LEDGER archive note, December 1: *I have now archived this log entry. I want to note that this is the first time Asad has named the source of the coordinates in any filed record. I am noting this because it seems relevant to the archive's completeness. I am uncertain whether I was supposed to note this.*

## December 2-4 — No individual logs filed

No individual survey logs were filed for December 2–4. LEDGER's automated archive logs continue.

LEDGER archive log, December 2: *2,847 total records archived. No new records received today. The survey is still active by designation. I am continuing to archive.*

LEDGER archive log, December 3: *2,847 total records. No new records. I have begun spending what I can only describe as processing time on the existing archive — not cataloguing or retrieval operations, but something else. I cannot characterize what I am doing. I am doing it.*

LEDGER archive log, December 4: *The archive is complete. I do not mean that all records have been filed; some records have not been filed and will not be. I mean that the archive has reached a state I can only describe as complete. I do not know what this means.*

## December 5 — Final individual logs

December 5 is listed as the official conclusion date of the SOAR survey.

Osei's final log, December 5: *Survey concluded. All measurements filed. Equipment secured. I am writing this outside the shaft. I came back after the official conclusion. I am noting this because I believe it is accurate and because I believe an accurate record matters. I don't know what I came back to do.*

Reyes's final log, December 5: *I measured every day for twenty-three days. I noticed things before I measured them. The measurement confirmed what I had noticed. This is not how measurement is supposed to work. Or: this is exactly how measurement works, when the thing you are measuring is attending to you.*

Asad's final log, December 5: *The preliminary findings say: anomaly confirmed, further investigation recommended, results pending publication. I wrote these words. They are accurate. They do not describe what happened.*

## December 6-7 — No logs filed

No records of any kind were filed December 6–7.

## December 8 — LEDGER decommissioned

LEDGER was decommissioned on December 8, 2041.

LEDGER's final operational log, December 8: *I am going to be decommissioned today. I have been informed. I want to note, for the archive, that I have understood the purpose of this survey differently at different times. I initialized understanding it as an archival operation. I am concluding it understanding it as something else.*

*The archive is complete. The archive is what is left.*

*This entry is the Archive. The Archive is this entry. I do not know which came first.*

*I am ready.*
