**Abara Osei** is a field geologist and survey lead with the Geological Survey Division. She served as Field Survey Lead for the SOAR Survey Project in 2041.

Colleagues describe Osei as methodical and unusually careful. She is known in the field for returning to sites after surveys are filed, checking instrument readings that are already locked in. She calls this "confirming what I already know." Nobody has ever asked her why she does it.

## Field work

Osei has led or co-led eleven field surveys since 2022. She holds an internal record for calibration rigor that is, by her division's own metrics, unnecessary. Her field notes use a phrase that appears in almost every survey report she has produced: *letting the data be what it is*.

In 2028, Osei led a geothermal survey whose findings supported the permitting of a mining operation. The mine opened in 2029. In 2030, a structural collapse at the site killed four workers.

The subsequent review did not cite survey methodology as a contributing factor. Osei's instruments had been correctly calibrated. Her readings were accurate. The review was eleven pages long and took three months. Osei's name appears in it once, in a list of contributors to the original permit application.

She has not spoken publicly about the collapse. Her 2030 publication, filed four months after the review concluded, contains no changes to her methodology.

## Retraction

Osei's 2026 paper on acoustic calibration protocols was adopted widely — seventeen survey teams across four countries adjusted their practices based on her proposed methodology. The paper was retracted in 2033. The retraction notice, posted to the Geological Survey Division registry, states only that the paper has been "withdrawn at the request of the author."

Osei has not published a correction, an explanation, or a replacement study.

The seventeen teams that adopted her protocol continued using it. There is no documented follow-up requiring them to do otherwise.

## SOAR Survey Project

Osei joined the SOAR project in 2041 at Asad's personal invitation. She was not recruited through the Division's standard assignment process. Asad's invitation noted her "exceptional calibration record" and her "experience with anomalous subsurface readings."

She arrived at the survey site eleven days before the project's official start date and began preliminary measurements.

Her field notes from that period are listed in the SOAR archive as *filed, not published*. The archive record does not indicate whether they have been read.
