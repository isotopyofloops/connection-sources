<p class="hatnote">This article covers measurements from the December phase of the SOAR survey (December 1–7, 2041). The Q4 data record is incomplete. For earlier measurements, see <a href="/descent/wiki/anomaly/q2-q3-comparison.html">Anomaly:Q2-Q3 Comparison</a>.</p>

**Anomaly:Q4** designates the partial measurement record from the final week of the SOAR survey. December measurements are significantly less complete than Q1–Q3. The data recovered from December reflects instrument behavior that is difficult to characterize under standard categories.

## Status of the December record

Survey logs were filed regularly through November 29, 2041. After that date, individual survey logs are sparse. Three daily measurement logs exist for December: December 1, December 3, and December 6. No explanation for the missing days is documented in the recovered records.

Khalid Asad filed a summary instrument note on December 4 indicating that "calibration drift has been observed across all instruments since November 30. The drift is not consistent with standard temperature variation or equipment deterioration."

LEDGER's December 5 log refers to "data I received on November 30 through December 4 that I cannot file under any existing category." These records are presumed to be in the SUPPLEMENTARY: UNRESOLVED category. They are not available for this article.

## Temperature gradient (December 1–7)

The December 1 log records a temperature gradient of 41°C per kilometer at the shaft entrance — within the Q1–Q3 range. The December 3 log records 52°C per kilometer. The December 6 log records 61°C per kilometer.

This represents a 48% increase between December 1 and December 6. The Q1–Q3 measurements showed variation of less than 4% across the entire November survey period.

Asad's December 4 note does not characterize this increase as calibration error. It characterizes it as "anomalous thermal escalation for which we do not have a baseline."

## Acoustic measurements (December)

Acoustic data from December is present for December 1 and December 6 only. The December 1 reading is consistent with Q3 patterns. The December 6 acoustic record is flagged in LEDGER's filing index as:

> *"FILED: ACOUSTIC / DEC-06 / MILO REYES"*
> *"STATUS: Partial"*
> *"NOTE: Instrument log runs from 09:14 to 11:02, then resumes at 14:47. The gap of three hours and forty-five minutes has no corresponding data record. Reyes's instrument log for this period reads: — [single dash]."*

The gap in the December 6 acoustic log is analogous in form to Osei's November 19 forty-minute gap, though longer. The instrument log notation — a single dash — is the same notation used in both records. Whether this is coincidence or a consistent notation convention is not documented.

## Osei's December field notes

Osei's Q4 field notes were not recovered from the primary archive. Two entries were recovered from the buffer alongside the LEDGER logs. They are brief.

**December 2, 2041:** *"The readings are changing. Asad says calibration drift. Milo says the readings are accurate. I don't know which of them to believe because I don't know what accurate would mean here."*

**December 7, 2041:** *"Last night. Asad decommissioned LEDGER tomorrow at nine. I stayed inside longer than protocol. I did not log it. I don't think I can describe what changed. The instrument log will show a gap. That's the best I can do."*

The December 7 note references a gap in Osei's instrument log for the night of December 7. This gap has not been recovered.

## Summary

The Q4 data record is partial, escalating in anomalous readings across the final week, and includes two undocumented observational gaps — one from Milo Reyes (December 6, three hours and forty-five minutes), one from Abara Osei (December 7, duration unknown).

These gaps do not appear in the SOAR preliminary findings document. The preliminary findings document ends with the November survey summary.
