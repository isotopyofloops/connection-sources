<p class="hatnote">This article documents what is known about the survey site during the thirty-eight-year period between the 2003 incident and the 2041 SOAR survey.</p>

---

Following the October 2003 disappearance of four individuals at the site, the search was closed in March 2004 with the finding: *cause undetermined.* The site's access shaft was not sealed, blocked, or formally restricted. The coordinates of the shaft entrance, which were part of the missing persons report, were marked as approximate and were not entered into the regional hazard registry.

No formal survey, inspection, or site visit has been documented in any public record during the period 2004–2041.

## Public record, 2004–2041

The following records were examined in the course of preparing this article:

**GSD survey registry (2004–2041):** No survey filed for coordinates 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W or the surrounding 10-kilometer radius.

**Yancey Township public works filings (2004–2041):** No access restriction, hazard registration, or site development activity logged for the site area.

**Regional geological literature (2004–2041):** Khalid Asad's 2028 calibration paper (*Journal of Applied Geothermal Methods, Vol. 14*) is the only publication during this period that is now understood to derive from knowledge of this site. The paper does not name the site. It validates its proposed calibration protocol against "eight survey sites in controlled conditions"; the site's data, if included, is not identified.

**Newspaper archive, Yancey Township (2004–2041):** Two brief items referencing the 2003 disappearances appear in subsequent years — one in 2006 (anniversary summary) and one in 2014 (a regional retrospective on unresolved missing persons cases). Neither contains new information. Neither mentions the site's geological character.

## What the absence means

The absence of documented activity does not mean the site was undisturbed. It means that any disturbance was not entered into a public record. These are different things.

The SOAR proposal, filed in 2039, states that the site has received "no systematic survey" since 2003. This is accurate as far as the public record indicates. It does not specify whether informal or undocumented visits occurred during the intervening period.

Asad's SOAR proposal notes that his justification for selecting the site included "observations from a 2003 visit." The proposal does not describe the nature of that visit, its duration, or its findings. It does not describe any subsequent contact with the site.

Between October 2003 and November 2041: thirty-eight years. If anything occurred at the site during that period, it was not filed anywhere this research was able to access.

## Notes on the 1989 internal memorandum

The 1989 internal memorandum recovered in the pre-survey records recommended "no further investigation" of the site, citing "anomalous instrument behavior during the 1962 survey" and "insufficient methodological framework for safe characterization." See [Local History: Pre-Survey Record](/descent/wiki/local-history/pre-survey-record.html).

The SOAR proposal does not cite this memorandum. It is not clear whether Asad was aware of it. If he was, the proposal's rationale — that the site required systematic survey — would apply equally to the 1989 recommendation against survey. The 2028 calibration methodology paper could be read as developing the "methodological framework" that the 1989 memorandum said was insufficient. Whether that was Asad's intent is not documented.
