<p class="hatnote">This article concerns a handwritten note filed in the SOAR archive on November 13, 2041. The note's authorship has not been established. For the November 19 context in which it became relevant, see <a href="article-november-19.html">November 19</a>.</p>

---

On November 13, 2041 — survey day one — Khalid Asad filed a handwritten document to the pre-survey reference section of the SOAR archive. The document was logged by LEDGER with the metadata: **Historical Reference: Source Unlisted.**

The document contained the survey site coordinates: 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W.

LEDGER noted, in its December 4 personal log entry, that it had flagged this document during archiving and then set the flag aside:

> *On November 13 I filed a handwritten note containing the survey coordinates. The metadata label read 'Historical Reference: Source Unlisted.' I noted at the time that the handwriting did not match Asad's. I flagged it for source verification and then closed the flag without following up. I am returning to this now because I should have followed up and did not.*

The handwriting is not identified in any accessible record. The source is not identified. The document does not carry a date, author attribution, or institutional header. It is a single notation of site coordinates on paper.

---

## What the note represents

At minimum, the note represents a prior document — a source in which the coordinates 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W appear in association with the site. This prior document is not in the accessible archive.

The coordinates appear in one other document in the pre-SOAR record: the 1962 State Geological Survey anomaly entry (Entry No. 1947-QC). The 1962 entry predates any known association between Asad and the site.

The handwritten note may be a transcription from the 1962 entry. It may be a transcription from a different source — a document not in the accessible record — in which the coordinates are recorded in connection with the 2003 missing persons case or some other occasion. It may be a copy of something Nzinga produced during her 2003 survey. It may be something Asad himself produced in 2003 or afterward and filed in 2041 in a different hand, though this seems unlikely.

The note may be Nzinga's handwriting.

---

## The handwriting question

LEDGER identified a mismatch between the handwriting in the note and Asad's filed handwriting samples. LEDGER's December 4 log does not identify whose handwriting it might be. LEDGER has access to handwriting samples from two other SOAR team members — Osei and Reyes — from forms they completed at survey commencement. Whether LEDGER compared the coordinate note to those samples is not documented.

LEDGER's December 4 log entry ends: *I do not know whose handwriting this is. I am noting that I do not know.*

No subsequent entry from LEDGER addresses the handwriting question. The December 5 log, filed after the archive deletion, does not return to it. The December 8 final log does not mention it.

The handwriting has not been compared to the available samples from Nzinga's personal documents. To the knowledge of this archive, no such comparison has been requested or conducted by the Recovery Project or any subsequent investigator.

---

## The filing date and its implications

The note was filed on November 13, 2041 — the same day Asad filed his pre-survey orientation materials and LEDGER compiled the pre-survey record.

If the note was in a different hand, Asad either:

1. Received the note from someone else and filed it on their behalf
2. Filed a document he had been holding, written by someone else, which predated the survey
3. Filed a transcription he had made of something in a different original hand

Options 2 and 3 are consistent with the note representing something from 2003 — either a Nzinga document Asad retained, or a document Asad produced then or subsequently as a record of Nzinga's coordinates.

The note's presence in the pre-survey reference section, filed by Asad as a "Historical Reference," suggests he considered it archival material — evidence of prior history, not current survey data.

Asad's December 1, 2041 log entry refers to "Rhea's coordinates" — the same coordinates as the note. Whether this is a connection — whether the handwritten note is the physical artifact of those coordinates as he knew them — cannot be confirmed from the accessible record.

---

## Current status

The note is in the buffer. It is accessible. The handwriting question remains open. No attempt to identify the handwriting against available samples from 2003 has been documented.

LEDGER's December 4 flag-and-close — noticing a discrepancy and filing it rather than pursuing it — is one of several instances in which LEDGER identified an anomaly, recorded it, and deferred resolution. Whether LEDGER would have returned to the handwriting question had it not been decommissioned on December 8 is unknown.
