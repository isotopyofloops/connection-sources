The site has been documented six times since its first recorded encounter in 1887. In five of these six encounters, the contact produced no depth measurement of the vertical shaft at the site's center. In all six, the contact ended without a complete record of what was found. The subsequent contact, in each case, began without reference to what the previous contact produced.

---

## The six contacts

**1887 — Minford & Sons (mining survey)**

A mining survey encountered an unusual formation. The lead surveyor recommended no further extension pending documentation. The acoustic properties were noted as unusual and flagged for documentation. No documentation was produced. The mining operation closed in 1889. The formation's relationship to the current vertical shaft has not been established.

**1962 — State Geological Survey (anomaly registration)**

The site was registered as a geological anomaly: heat flux 340% above regional baseline, heat source undetermined, vertical formation at center. Depth measurement was attempted and not obtained — recorded as equipment malfunction. A follow-up survey was recommended. The entry was classified as low-priority. Follow-up was not scheduled.

**1989 — State Geological Survey (field survey)**

A field team conducted a survey. Heat flux measurements confirmed continued intensity increase since 1962. Depth measurement was again not obtained. Two team members reported disorientation following extended proximity to the shaft; one declined to return after the first day. The survey report attributed these symptoms to altitude, thermal conditions, and terrain. The division recommended no further investigation. The report was filed in physical archive only, not cross-referenced in digital records.

**2003 — University field survey (Khalid Asad, PI)**

A graduate student conducting acoustic survey work at the site did not return. A missing persons report was filed within 24 hours. The search was conducted over four days; no trace was found. The student's field notes were recovered sealed at the shaft entrance and provided to the supervising faculty member. The case was closed as cause undetermined. No geological findings were filed. No depth measurement was obtained.

**2028 — Asad calibration paper (mediated contact)**

Khalid Asad published a calibration methodology paper for subsurface geothermal acoustic surveys. His December 1, 2041 survey log states that the paper originated as "an attempt to explain what happened" in 2003 and "ended as a hypothesis about measurement because I could not explain what happened." The paper does not name the site. It validates its proposed method against eight survey sites without identifying any of them. The paper is the sole documented attempt to formalize the site's properties between 2003 and 2039. It does not include depth data.

**2041 — SOAR Survey Project (Khalid Asad, PI)**

A 26-day acoustic survey was conducted. Six instances of acoustic null readings occurred during the 23:00–01:00 UTC window. The principal investigator descended the shaft on December 3 for approximately two hours and returned. No survey report was filed following the descent. Two observational gaps occurred in December 6–7. Preliminary findings classified null readings as equipment anomaly. The primary archive was deleted December 5. The preliminary findings were filed the same day. Further investigation was recommended. Results are listed as pending publication.

No depth measurement is documented in the survey record.

---

## The pattern

Each encounter produced less than it was positioned to produce.

1887: documented that documentation was needed; produced no documentation.
1962: registered the anomaly; did not obtain the depth measurement it registered as needed.
1989: confirmed the anomaly's persistence; recommended the investigation it had just failed to complete should not be attempted again.
2003: a researcher went inside; no record of what she found came back.
2028: an attempt to explain 2003 ended as a hypothesis about measurement rather than an explanation.
2041: a researcher went inside; no record of what he found was filed.

The successive contacts do not build on each other. Each proceeds as if the prior contacts had not occurred. The SOAR project documentation does not reference the 1887 mining log, the 1962 anomaly entry, the 1989 memorandum, the 1994 local history compilation, or the 2003 missing persons case.

Asad located the 1989 memorandum in a physical file room before the SOAR survey began.

---

## The depth

In none of the six contacts has a depth measurement of the vertical shaft been obtained. The two attempts to measure depth (1962, 1989) each produced equipment malfunction or incomplete conditions. The two descents (2003, 2041) produced no filed measurement.

The site's depth remains unknown.
