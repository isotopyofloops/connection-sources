In 1989, the State Geological Survey Division conducted a field survey of the site at 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W. The resulting internal memorandum is the most detailed record of any survey contact prior to the SOAR project. It is also the record that recommended, explicitly, that no further survey of the site should occur.

The memorandum was filed in physical archive only. It was not cross-referenced in the GSD's digital records.

---

## What the memorandum documents

The memorandum records the following findings from the 1989 survey:

- Heat flux measurements confirmed continued intensity increase since the 1962 anomaly registration. No mechanism was identified.
- Depth measurement of the vertical shaft was not obtained. The attempt is not described in detail. The memorandum notes that temperature gradient at the shaft entrance "exceeds safe working conditions for extended measurement operations."
- Two members of the field team reported disorientation following extended proximity to the shaft. One team member declined to return to the shaft after the first day.
- The team attributed the disorientation to altitude, thermal conditions, and terrain.

The memorandum's conclusion states that the site "presents insufficient methodological framework for safe characterization" and recommends no further investigation.

The identity of the field team members is not recorded in available documentation. The name of the team member who declined to return is not documented.

---

## Physical filing

The memorandum was filed in physical archive. It was not added to the GSD's digital record system or cross-referenced in digital databases.

The standard GSD filing practice at the time is described in the GSD institutional history as: internal memoranda are filed at the division level; external-facing reports and formal survey entries are entered into the shared geological record. Under this practice, an internal memorandum summarizing a field survey would not automatically appear in public or institutional database searches. Its visibility would depend on physical access to the division's file room.

Whether the 1989 physical-only filing was procedurally standard or an anomaly within standard procedure is not established in the available record.

---

## The authorization question

In November 2039, Khalid Asad submitted a proposal to the GSD for a systematic survey of the same site. The proposal requested authorization for a project involving shaft descent, acoustic instrumentation, and field team deployment.

The proposal does not cite the 1989 memorandum. It does not reference the 1989 recommendation against further investigation. It does not mention the disorientation reports.

The proposal was approved by the GSD in February 2040. The GSD authorization record is described as "currently under internal review."

Whether the GSD authorization process included any search of the 1989 physical archive is not documented in accessible records. If reviewers consulted the physical files, they approved the SOAR survey with knowledge of the prior recommendation against investigation. If they did not, the authorization proceeded without the GSD's own safety record as input.

Asad located the 1989 memorandum "in advance of the SOAR survey." This is documented in the SOAR project record. He had physical access to the GSD archive, found the memorandum, and chose not to include it in the proposal he submitted to that archive's institution.

---

## The 1989 methodology objection

The memorandum does not specify what methodological improvements would be required before further investigation. It states that the existing framework is insufficient; it does not describe a sufficient one.

Asad's 2028 paper — published thirteen years before the SOAR survey — introduced calibration methodology for subsurface geothermal acoustic surveys under high-temperature conditions. His December 1, 2041 survey log entry records that the paper "started as an attempt to explain what happened and ended as a hypothesis about measurement because I could not explain what happened."

The 1989 memorandum cites methodology as the barrier to further investigation. Asad's 2028 paper addressed methodology. Whether the paper constituted, in his assessment, a sufficient response to the 1989 objection — and whether this contributed to the SOAR proposal's omission of the memo — is not documented.
