The site at coordinates **47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W** is a geothermal anomaly in a remote area of [county redacted], first documented in local geological surveys in the 1960s. It is located approximately 34 kilometers from the nearest township.

Access to the site requires a 12-kilometer walk from the nearest road. There is no maintained trail. The terrain is characterized by exposed bedrock, seasonal ice, and unstable ground in several areas. Visitors have noted an unusual acoustic quality — a low, non-directional resonance — that has not been explained in published survey literature.

## Geological features

The site exhibits a geothermal signature disproportionate to regional heat flow models. Temperature gradient measurements taken in 1962, 1989, and 2003 show increasing anomaly intensity over time. The 2041 SOAR Survey confirmed this trend.

The source of the anomaly has not been identified. Three competing hypotheses exist in published literature; none has been confirmed.

A vertical shaft of unknown depth is located at the site's center. Its origin is disputed: some geological surveys describe it as a natural formation; others as evidence of early industrial activity. The local mining history includes partial records of operations in the 1880s; it is not clear whether these operations reached this site.

No measurement instrument lowered into the shaft has returned a depth reading. This is attributed in survey literature to equipment malfunction in hostile conditions.

## 2003 incident

In October 2003, an undergraduate geology student conducted an independent field survey at this site as part of a senior thesis on geothermal anomalies. She did not return from the site.

Her field notes were found the following morning, sealed in a plastic bag, at the entrance to the shaft.

A search was conducted. The shaft was examined. No trace of the student was found. The search was closed in 2004 as *cause undetermined.* A missing persons record remains open.

The student's name is linked to this article but the corresponding article has not been available for sustained periods. See [Article:Rhea Nzinga](/descent/wiki/article/rhea-nzinga.html).

## SOAR Survey Project (2041)

The site was the focus of the SOAR Survey Project from November 13 to December 8, 2041. The project, led by Khalid Asad, conducted systematic geothermal measurements using an updated acoustic methodology. The site coordinates used by the SOAR project match those documented in the 2003 survey.

The source of the 2003 coordinate data used by the project is not listed in the project documentation.

SOAR Survey results are listed as *pending publication.* LEDGER, the project's archive management system, was decommissioned on December 8, 2041. Its final log entries are available in the SOAR archive.
