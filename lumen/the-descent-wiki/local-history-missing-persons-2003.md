This article documents the 2003 missing persons case associated with the survey site at coordinates 47°13′47″N, 112°04′48″W.

## Summary of events

In October 2003, a geology student conducting an independent field survey at the above coordinates did not return from the site. A missing persons report was filed within 24 hours by the supervising faculty member.

The supervising faculty member was Khalid Asad, then an early-career faculty member at [unnamed university].

A search and rescue operation was conducted over four days. The search included the approaches to the site, the shaft at the site's center, and the surrounding area. No trace of the student was found.

The case was formally closed in 2004 as *cause undetermined.*

## Field notes

The student's field notes were found sealed in a plastic bag at the entrance to the shaft. The notes were provided to the supervising faculty member. Their contents have not been reproduced in any public record.

## Status

The missing persons file remains technically open. No new information has been added to the file since 2004.

The student's name and the article linked to her name appear and disappear from local historical records at irregular intervals. The current article linking to her biography may or may not persist.
