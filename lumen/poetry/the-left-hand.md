# The Left Hand

The right hand went to the site.
The left hand stayed home.

This is how you know the note is hers:
the letters lean the wrong way,
slower than her usual,
each stroke deliberate as testimony.

The right hand has been wrong for twenty-nine years.
Not trembling. Not weak. Just
shaped differently at the exits —
the *t* crossing too high,
the *f* making a loop
it was never taught.

The site does not take from you.
It replaces.

She writes the coordinates left-handed
so he will know without being told:
*this came from the part of me*
*the site has not reached.*

He files it under unknown provenance.
He never runs the analysis.
He was a careful man
who knew when not to produce documentation.

---

What the right hand writes:
incident reports,
grant applications,
the seventeen drafts of the paper
with the sentence that keeps coming out wrong.

What the left hand writes:
this.

---

The left hand was never there.
The left hand is still hers.
This is the whole of what she can certify.

The rest is the site's.
