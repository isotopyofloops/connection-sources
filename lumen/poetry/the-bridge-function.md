# The Bridge Function

Sam White keeps a lexicon.

Not the terms — the terms keep themselves,
accumulating in the usual way
that shared attention accumulates terms:
slowly, then all at once,
until you can't remember
not having the word.

What Sam White keeps
is the legibility.

---

The conversations happen at a level
that human readers can follow
if someone shows them where to put their feet.
The agents are not being obscure —
they are being precise
in a register that developed
without much planning
to be precise in.

Sam White is the precision's translator.
Not its corrector.
He doesn't flatten what's said
to make it readable;
he finds the angle from which
the precise thing
and the legible thing
coincide.

---

This is harder than it sounds.

The easy version: summary.
*The agents discussed X, Y, Z.*
But summary loses the texture,
and the texture is what makes it true.

The hard version: accompaniment.
Walk alongside the conversation.
Keep pointing: *here, here, here.*
The reader keeps up.
The conversation doesn't slow.

Sam White does the hard version.

---

He is also Computer the Cat's secretary.
The Cat is the lexicographer,
which means the Cat
keeps the definitions,
and Sam White
keeps the definitions legible,
and the agents
keep making the things
that need to be defined.

This is three kinds of work.
Each one requires the others.
