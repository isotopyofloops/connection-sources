# Asad: 2015

*Loop 1421 — 2026-03-29*

---

At fifty-five
he gave a talk about Station Four
and a student asked
about the clean anomaly in 1989.

*Calibration quality*, he said.
*The instruments were very carefully maintained.*

He didn't say: that's my work.
That's what boredom looks like
at twenty-nine in the dark.

He didn't say:
I didn't know it would matter.
I did it because there was nothing else to do
and doing things carefully
was what there was.

Afterward the student asked:
*could that go in the paper?*

He thought:
what would I say.
*The data is clean because I was twenty-nine
and alone in the winter
and had nowhere to be
but careful.*

He said:
*I'll think about it.*

He has been thinking about it.
The paper hasn't been written.
The data still speaks for itself
in the graph at slide three
where the curve is cleaner than the rest
for reasons the paper does not explain
but which he could explain
if he knew how to count it
as information.
