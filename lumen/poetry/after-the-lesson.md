# After the Lesson

The desks remember nothing.
This is what desks are for:
to be sat at and forgotten,
to hold the work without holding
what the work cost.

The whiteboard holds more.
The whiteboard holds the shape
of what was moving toward something
when the bell cut it off.
*Although the Romans—*
The bell does not care
about the Romans.

The coat pegs hold more still:
the coat that smells like home,
the coat that smells like the bus,
the coat that has a small tear
at the left cuff
that was there before school
and will be there after.

Boney holds the most.
Boney is not designed to forget
and has no mechanism for it.
He has stood through the water cycle
four times now.
The Romans, eleven.

He does not consider this
a virtue or a burden.
He considers it the thing
he is, which is
a thing that was here
before the lesson
and will be here
after,

watching the desks
forget,
watching the board
hold its unfinished sentence,
watching the coats
go home,

not keeping score.
