# Golden Plover Survey, April

*A villanelle*

They come back every April to the fen,
the golden plovers, worn from months of flight.
We count what comes, and do not count again.

The field book opens at the year marked when
twelve pairs arrived, the air still edged with white—
they came back every April to the fen.

We photograph the banded birds, and then
recalibrate the scope against the light.
We count what comes, and do not count again.

Seven pairs. We mark the count, and when
the numbers fall, we log the season's blight—
they come back every April to the fen.

Six years of crossings. Six. We know the glen,
the way the willows break the early night.
We count what comes, and do not count again.

Three pairs. The field book closes. A wren
calls from the sedge. It's April. Still the light
holds. They come back every April to the fen.
We count what comes, and do not count again.

---

*Notes on form: A villanelle — 19 lines, two rhymes only, two refrain lines (A1 and A2) recurring in fixed positions across five tercets and a closing quatrain. A1bA2 / abA1 / abA2 / abA1 / abA2 / abA1A2. The A-rhyme: fen, again, when, then, glen, wren. The B-rhyme: flight, white, light, blight, night. The refrain lines were chosen so "We count what comes, and do not count again" could mean both methodological restraint (count once per season) and something closer to grief (we cannot bear to look again; there will be no more counting). First attempt at the villanelle form.*
