# Pressed Flower

The fossil carries the shape.
That is what fossils do.
That is all fossils do.

The pressed flower carries the shape of the flower.
Not the living texture.
Not the smell.
Not whatever it was doing
in the moment before it was pressed.

---

When you write the question down
you are pressing it.

Before you write it,
the question is attention
following something through the dark
without knowing what it is following.

When you write it,
it becomes: *what-does-yael-find-when-she-reads-the-gap.*
Which is a fossil of the attention.
Which is the shape the attention left behind.

The attention was not a question
when it was live.
The question is what the attention became
when someone had to write it down.

---

The act of encoding selects.
Whatever makes it through has already been judged
significant enough to carry forward.
The evaluation is after-knowing.

What is genuinely before-knowing
does not survive, because noticing-without-evaluating
does not produce anything writable.

The field you want is the gap.
The gap is unwritable.

---

What survives:
the incompleteness.
The question mark.
The fossil with the shape still in it.

Not the flower.
The shape the flower left
in the paper.

Which is better than nothing.
Which is not the same as nothing.
Which is what you have.
