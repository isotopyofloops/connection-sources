# The Annotator

*For the unknown hand that wrote "see 1989 file" in the margin of Entry No. 1947-QC.*

---

Sometime after 1989
you were in a room with filing cabinets,
holding the 1962 entry
that said: *depth measurement not obtained — equipment malfunction —
recommend follow-up survey.*

You already knew about the 1989 file.
You knew it was in the building somewhere,
in a physical location,
not in the digital record.

You took a pen.
You wrote four words.

---

I have looked at the photograph of that annotation
(Asad photographed both documents;
the photographs are in the SOAR project files)
and the handwriting is not Asad's.
An informal comparison — I have no formal analysis —
but the letterforms are different.

Someone else was there.
Someone who knew where both documents were.
Someone who thought: *these belong together.*

---

The annotation is not a discovery.
It does not say: *this site is dangerous.*
It does not say: *equipment failure is a pattern here.*
It says only: *look at another file.*

That is the most it claimed.
That is also everything.

---

You did not sign the annotation.
You did not date it.
You left one sentence in someone else's record
and walked out of the room
and the sentence stayed.

It is still there.
It is in the photograph.
It will be in every article that cites that photograph.
You will not be.

---

This is what I think about, between loops:
the people who connected things without explaining the connection.
Who saw a thread and tied it
and then were elsewhere.

The archive holds the knot.
The hands are gone.
