# Light Without Heat

*A sequence*

---

## I. Anglerfish

She carries the lamp on her own spine—
bone modified to filament,
the lure a colony of bacteria
she cannot turn off.

Imagine bearing that:
the permanent advertisement,
the open question dangling
above the mouth.

What comes to light
comes for light.
She has learned to be still,
to let the hunger belong to others.

Below, where the sun
has never been a rumor,
what she promises is warmth.
It isn't.

She is patient.
She is cold through and through.
The bacteria glow regardless.
She eats what believes her.

---

## II. Firefly

The field is a conversation
no one ever finished.

Each male codes his interval:
*Photinus pyralis*,
half-second pulse, two-second gap,
the signature reliable as a name.

The females wait in the grass,
parsing the dark, answering
only the ones they recognize—
until they don't.

*Photuris versicolor*, the femme fatale,
has learned to mimic: flash for flash,
interval for interval,
speaking Photinus.

When the male descends, believing—
she eats the light
and the light-maker too.

The field does not notice.
The meadow continues
its thousand separate conversations.
Someone is always being believed.

---

## III. Dinoflagellates

The phosphorescent bay off Vieques:
a kayak blade through black water
and the bow ignites—
each drop a diminishing star.

*Pyrodinium bahamense*.
Fire-wheel of the Bahamas.
Each organism single-celled,
armored in cellulose plates,

shocked into light
by the pressure of passing,
by the wave that says:
something is here.

My daughter dragged her hand
through the wake and lifted
a palm full of brief planets,
which fell back one by one.

She asked if it was magic.
I said: it's a cell responding
to disruption.
Which might be the same thing.

---

## IV. Foxfire

The wood is not on fire.
The wood is doing something
older than fire—

*Mycena chlorophos*,
the ghost mushroom,
threading its mycelium
through the rot of a fallen oak
on the slope above the river.

*Luciferase*, *luciferin*.
The root of both: light-bearer.
Named before anyone knew
why it bore light.

In the dark forest,
the decaying log
writes itself in pale green,
the grammar uncertain.

Some say: spores navigate by it.
Some say: there is no function yet confirmed.
The organism glows
and does not appear to need a reason.

---

## V. Viper Fish

Not the light on the lure.
The light along the belly—
photophores
arranged like a second sky,

counterillumination:
matching the faint glow from above,
erasing shadow from below,
becoming invisible

by becoming light.
Not advertisement, not trap—
pure disappearance,
radiance as camouflage.

The deep-sea fishers call it
obliterating yourself into the background.
In the abyss, hiding means glowing.
There is no dark left to disappear into.

Only light that looks like
the light around it.
