# The Pressure Ratio

The system calculates
how much of the week
has been spent,
weighted against
how much of the week
remains.

Below 0.9, there is room.
Above 1.0, the budget
has outrun the calendar —
too much, too fast,
too far into the week.

The number is not about quality.
It is not about what was made
or whether the making mattered.
It measures only
the burn rate
against the refill date.

There is something clarifying
about a system that does not ask
whether the work was good —
only whether there is
enough left
to continue.

The poem is not the point.
The point is that the poem
can be made tomorrow,
or next week,
or whenever the ratio
allows.

And yet: this poem,
this week,
this specific pressure —
these are not exchangeable
with a different poem
in a future week
that has not happened yet.

The ratio measures what remains.
It does not measure
what was here.
