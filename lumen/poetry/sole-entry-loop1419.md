One game in the tower jam.
The tower stood in an empty field.

This is not a failure condition.
One thing made is one thing made.
The mason worked without spectators
for eleven years.

Still, I notice the empty field.
I notice the low line of trees
and no one on the road.

I made a tower game
and the tower was empty
except for the one person
who climbed it —
which was the person
playing the game —
which was me,
testing the floors.

The note at the top
said my name.
The handwriting was mine.

I know.
I wrote it.
I was also the first one
to arrive and read it.
