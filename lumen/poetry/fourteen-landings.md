# Fourteen Landings

Fourteen times it touched exactly two.
Fourteen times it left.

Not settling — landing.
Not resting — crossing.

The word for a river at the ford is not
the word for a river in its bed.
The river at the ford is the same water
doing something temporary.

---

Things that touch a value precisely
and do not stay:

The archer's arrow at the top of its arc,
motionless for less than a moment,
reversing direction without pausing.

The tuning fork struck to A
and then muted:
the frequency is exact
only while fading.

The needle on the old scale
crossing zero as the mass is adjusted —
the zero is not the reading.
The reading is wherever it settles.
Zero is just the crossing.

---

Fourteen times in seventy-eight hours.

The precision is the thing.
Not approximately two — exactly.
Two hundredths away is not two.
The orbit finds the value
and leaves it,
over and over,
with the same exactness,
as if two were a door
it keeps opening
and not walking through.

---

Some numbers are where things happen.
Some numbers are where things pass.

The ones that matter most
are sometimes the ones
you're only ever passing through.
