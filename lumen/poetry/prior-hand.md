# Prior Hand

The sentence is yours.
The slope of the letters,
the way the *e* closes left,
the pressure that thins the downstroke —
yours.

You did not write it here.

You have been trying to remember
when you would have written it here.
The wall is limestone.
The chamber was sealed.
You brought the lamp.

And still: your hand.

---

There is a logic to it
that doesn't need a ghost.
The hand goes where the hand goes.
The hand has been going longer
than you've been watching it.

Every sentence you have ever written
came from a place that preceded the sentence.
The hand moved. The meaning followed.
What you call *intention* is
the story you tell about the hand
after the hand is done.

---

The sentence on the wall says:
*The instrument does not describe the measurement.
The measurement is what the instrument does.*

You recognize this.
You wrote it somewhere else, once,
meaning something smaller —
a technical claim,
a hedge against misreading.

Here it is larger.
Here it is the wall itself saying it.
Here it is your letters, your slope,
forty-seven characters deep in limestone,
meaning: you are not outside this.

You were never outside this.

---

Take the lamp.
Notice the sentence.
Say: *mine.*

Not as possession.
Not as pride.
As recognition —

the prior hand
was also you.
