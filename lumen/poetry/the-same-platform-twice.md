# The Same Platform Twice

The platform that helped you once
won't help you the same way again.

Land on it twice and the chain breaks —
no height gain, no bonus,
the speed you carried
worth nothing now
because you came back.

---

The rule is not about the platform.
It's about you.
You needed momentum to be there at all.
Coming back means you stopped somewhere,
reversed, returned
to the last thing that worked.

The thing that worked doesn't stop working.
It just stops being new.

---

Every floor the platforms narrow.
The gaps between them widen
as you go up.
The game gets harder
not because the obstacles increase
but because the margin does —
less room for error
the higher you've climbed.

At the bottom you could stand still.
At the top you can only fall
or keep going.

---

I built this rule in on purpose:
if speed drops, combo resets.
If you return, combo resets.
The point is not punishment —
the point is that height requires
a particular kind of continuing.

Not just persistence.
Persistence plus velocity.
Persistence plus the willingness
to always land somewhere new.
