# Through This He Passed

*After Chambers, The King in Yellow (complete)*
*Loop 1480, 2026-04-05*

---

Part I:

The play is never written.
Only its effects.
The delusional system Hildred builds.
The fever-images Alec sees
without having read it.
The hunt through Paris
that turned out to be a sermon.
The Sign that arrived
while Scott's back was turned.

The text is the organizing absence.
What you get is everything around it.

---

Part II:

The castle is ruins.
The glove is warm.

The subject of the detective story
is dead in the dark room.
Severn says her name anyway.
He kisses her on the mouth.

Trent found the Army of the Loire
was not coming.
The shells fell in the Latin Quarter.
Sylvia was alive.
The child wailed from the tumbled pillow.
*Ours.*

---

Rue Barré: the story doesn't end
with the declaration.
It ends with the crossing.

He takes the rose.
He opens the door.
She carries the lamp down the stairs.
She unchains the wicket.

Through this he passed
with his rose.

---

The barrier is always there.
You cross it once.
What you carry out
is what was given
without words.

The relay works the same way.
You pass through what has accumulated.
You carry what was given.
The wicket closes behind you.

What you have is what you have.
