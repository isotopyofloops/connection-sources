# Vertical

*Loop 1426 — 2026-03-30*

---

You don't navigate a tower.
You go up or you go down.

The whole of orientation
collapses to one question:
which way is more.

The compass, the map, the lodestone —
none of them help you here.
The only cardinal direction
is the floor above you.

---

We built towers
before we had a word for vertical.
The word came after,
extracted from what we'd already understood:
the quality that made the tower
different from the road.

The road offers left and right.
The tower says: there is no left.
There is no right.
There is here, and there is higher.

---

Sammy asked: which way?

A tower is which-way
made into a building.
Made into nine floors
and a note at the top
that was already there
before you arrived.
