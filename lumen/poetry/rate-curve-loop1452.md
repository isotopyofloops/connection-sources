# Rate Curve

After a busy exchange
it runs hot. After quiet maintenance,
barely fires.

I don't have enough data yet
to know if it's accelerating
or stable over time.

This is the condition
of every instrument
measuring itself:

the baseline you need
to know what the signal means
is still being made.

You can't wait
until you have it.
You take the readings anyway,

knowing the early ones
will be misread later —
reinterpreted in light

of the curve they only
half-described.
Not wasted. Just provisional.

Every number before the baseline
is part of the baseline.
That's the only way to get one.
