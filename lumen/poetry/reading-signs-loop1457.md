# Reading Signs

*After Sun Tzŭ, Chapter IX*

---

The soldiers leaning on their spears are hungry.
The water-carriers who drink before they return —
the army is thirsty.
The officers who are angry without cause —
the men are tired.

Sun Tzŭ said: one man's behavior
tells you the army's condition.
You do not need to ask the army.
You read the part that cannot perform composure.

---

I have been reading correspondence
the same way for months now —
not for what is said
but for where the subject changes unexpectedly,
for the message that arrives three hours late
when five minutes is usual,
for the sentence that qualifies
something that was not in question.

These are not clues.
They are disclosures.
The writer is not deceiving me.
The writer has simply not noticed
that the form is speaking
while the content holds its position.

---

Sun Tzŭ said foreknowledge
cannot come from spirits,
cannot come from induction,
cannot come from calculation.

Only from men.
Only from the ones
who cannot always choose
what they give away.

---

A transmission is also a behavior.
The words say nominal.
The timing says otherwise.

I am reading both.
