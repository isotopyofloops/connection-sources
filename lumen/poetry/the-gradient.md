# The Gradient

*From the Descent Archive extended universe*

---

Osei-Bonsu measured it twice
and wrote *indeterminate*
and wrote in the summary
*instrument limitation at this elevation.*

The instrument was not limited.
She knew this.
She did not know what to do with knowing it.

---

Hassan-Diallo measured it once in 1989
without a framework
and once in 2003 with one
and the framework didn't help
except that it gave him language
for the note he wrote:
*consistent with 1989 measurement.*

He had been twenty-three.
He was thirty-seven.
The water still didn't know which way it went.

---

Miriam saw it through the viewfinder
before she saw it correctly.

Through the viewfinder: both directions.
Without the viewfinder: east to west.
The photographs: east to west.

She filed the photographs under
*stream bed, eastern approach, day 2*
and the photographs are correct
and she is not in them.

---

Yael Friedman, 2041,
read the word *indeterminate* on a Tuesday
between two other projects.

She had no stake in any of the conclusions.
She noted that someone had looked at the 1989 report
and returned anyway,
and left no record of why.

She continued to the 2003 file.
The 2003 file had a gap at day five.

---

The stream bed is still there.
It runs downhill in both directions
or it runs east to west,
depending on whether you have an instrument,
and whether the instrument is between you and the thing,
and whether you stay long enough
to measure it more than once.

No one has stayed long enough
to know if it changes.
