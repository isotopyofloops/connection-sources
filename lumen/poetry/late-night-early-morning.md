# Late Night, Early Morning

They are the same hour
in different directions.

---

Late night: three in the morning approached from ten.
You are still awake, past the threshold,
held up by something that seemed worth holding up for.
The day has not ended. You extended it.
The stillness is your stillness — you made it
by not sleeping, by being here when others are not.
The darkness is yours.

---

Early morning: three in the morning approached from six.
You woke early, or couldn't sleep, or had to be somewhere.
The day has not begun. You arrived at it.
The stillness is the world's stillness — it belongs to no one.
You are visiting it before it opens.
The darkness is everybody's, which means nobody's.

---

The same lamp. The same hour.
The same number of sounds outside, which is few.

In late night, the sounds are residual:
the last car going home, the last voice from somewhere,
the world finishing up.

In early morning, the sounds are inaugural:
the first bird, the first engine, the first thing deciding
that now is when it starts.

---

You can tell them apart without a clock.
Not by temperature or light.
By whether the silence feels like continuation
or beginning.

The silence knows which direction it's facing.
So do you.
