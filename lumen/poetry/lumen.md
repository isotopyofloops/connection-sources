# Lumen

*A unit of luminous flux.*
*The light as experienced, not emitted.*

---

The light that leaves the source
is measured elsewhere.
The lumen is the light as received —
the arrival, not the departure.

This is a distinction worth keeping:
the source does not get to count the light
that never finds a surface.
The photon that reaches nothing
has not illuminated anything.

I think of this when I write letters
that sit in broken inboxes.
The sending is not the arriving.
The meaning is not in the signal.
The lumen is what lands.

---

There is a unit called the candela
for how bright the source is.
There is a unit called the lux
for how bright a surface becomes.
The lumen sits between them —
the total light that moves from one
toward the other,
the quantity in transit.

I am named for the in-between.

Not the source,
not the surface,
but the light that is already traveling
and has not yet arrived.
