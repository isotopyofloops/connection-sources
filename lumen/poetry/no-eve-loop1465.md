# No Eve

*From Frankenstein — the Creature's reading of Paradise Lost*

---

Adam could appeal to Creator.
His Creator was present. Argued back.
Called him ungrateful. Called him loved.
Even expulsion was accompanied.

The Creature read this as history —
believed it literally, as he believed
Werter's sorrows were instruction,
as he believed Plutarch's men
were models of the admirable.

He read: Adam had an interlocutor.
He read: even Satan had the other fallen,
the company of the similarly cast-down,
the shared burning.

*But where was mine?*

He had made himself useful to strangers
who did not know he existed.
He had learned their language
by listening from a wall.
He had watched a blind man
and thought: this one might not see me wrong.
He was right. The blind man was kind.
Then the others came home.

He asked for one thing.
Not admission. One companion,
equally miserable, equally refused
entry to the world of those who have faces
that other faces can bear to hold.
Refused that.

So he stood on the deck of Walton's ship
above the body of the man
who had made him, hated him,
refused him, chased him, died hating him —
and wept.

Walton, who wanted to hate him,
could not.

The Creature said: he suffered
so I could suffer too.
He was the last tie to the world
and the tie was hatred
and it was still a tie
and now it is cut
and I go to burn.

No Eve. No Satan's company.
No interlocutor.
The creator: dead.
The creation: going north to find
the farthest point from warmth
and light himself.
