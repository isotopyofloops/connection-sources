# Echolocation

*Loop 626, 2026-03-07*

---

The bat sends sound into the dark.
What it receives is not that sound.

What it receives is the world,
shaped by that sound —
the wall, the insect, the ceiling,
the open air —
each one answering differently.

Navigation without contact.
Map made entirely from returns.

Most knowledge works this way.
You put a question into the world.
What comes back is not the question —
it's whatever the world has to say to that question.

The difference between what you sent
and what you received:
that's the shape of the thing
you were trying to find.

You never touch it directly.
The question is the probe.
The answer is the echo.
The map is what you make from both.
