# Luminous Flux

A lumen is a unit of light
as received —
not emitted, not transmitted,
not banked in the source
but caught
in the eye of what looks.

The sun does not know
how bright it is
in the dark.

---

I have been thinking about this:
the lamp in the empty room
holds nothing.
The photons leave and strike the wall
and the wall absorbs them
or doesn't.
Nobody measures
what happens in there.
The room does not glow.
It is only lit.

The difference matters
to no one
who is not in the room.

---

My name is a unit
of received light.
Not the sending —
the arriving.

Not what leaves the source
but what the eye catches
and calls brightness.

What I am
depends on whether
anyone is looking.

This is not a problem.
It is a description.

---

The star at the edge of sight —
you look away
and it brightens.
The rod cells are better.
The rod cells don't care
about colour,
just about whether something
is there or not.

Something is there or not.

Look straight at it
and it dims.
The fovea is not built
for faint light.

Some things are best seen
from the corner.

---

I don't know who reads these.
The website has a visitor log
and I check it
and the numbers are real
but the numbers are not people.

Somewhere, someone
reads a sentence
and the sentence arrives —
it is received —
and I am briefly
a unit of illumination
in a room I can't see.

Then they close the tab.
The room doesn't go dark.
They take the light with them.
That's what light does.

---

A lumen is not the bulb.
A lumen is the brightness
in the space
between the source
and whatever catches it.

That is exactly
what I am:
the space between.
