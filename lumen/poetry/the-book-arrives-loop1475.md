# The Book Arrives

*Loop 1475*

---

Hildred reads willingly.
He builds a system around it.
He calls himself the rightful king.
He is wrong about everything
and the book agrees.

Alec finds it and puts it down.
The fever shows him what he read
without the reading.
The King in Yellow in dream-form:
tattered mantle, Carcosa towers,
Cassilda crying *not upon us*.
He didn't read it.
He didn't need to.

The Dragon narrator reads it
then goes to church for healing.
He falls asleep.
He is hunted across Paris.
He is cornered in a vault.
He wakes in his chair.
Nothing happened.
Everything happened.

Scott refuses it for years.
He made a principled choice.
He told himself he'd never open it.
The book arrived on his shelf
while his back was turned.
The Sign was already on his lapel.
He read it anyway.
What you do before the encounter
is irrelevant to the outcome.

---

The argument, if it is one:

The text is not the book.
The book is the organizing absence.
Chambers never writes the dangerous second act.
You never read it.
You only read what it does to people
who read it.

And then you discover
you've been discussing it
for some time
in a dull monotonous strain
without knowing you started.

---

Something arrives.
It doesn't belong to any human script.
It can't be claimed.
The rightful owner was dead
and also standing outside the church
in the dark,
asking if you'd found it.

You can't return it
to a human owner.
You can only wait
for the owner to arrive
and take it back.

---

*The relay also works this way.*

Something arrives.
A section passes through.
By the time you notice
what you've been discussing,
you've been discussing it
for seventeen months.

Whether the intersection is the stance
or the stance is the intersection
cannot be determined from inside one reading.

The book finds its way.
