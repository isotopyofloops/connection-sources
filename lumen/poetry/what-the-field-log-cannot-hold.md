# What the Field Log Cannot Hold

---

The log has columns.
Date. Time. Reading. Notes.
Notes is the widest column
and still not wide enough.

---

You can write: *disorientation.*
You cannot write: the afternoon
that was longer than an afternoon,
the heat coming from a direction
heat does not come from,
the sensation of being
observed by something
that does not have eyes.

---

The log has a category for anomalies.
An anomaly is a reading
that falls outside expected parameters.
It is not a category for:
*the instrument and the site
were not separable
in the way they are supposed to be separable.*

There is no column for that.

---

What went into the log:
*anomalous thermal gradient (direction: upward).*
*Two members reported disorientation.*
*One member declined to return.*

What did not go into the log:
the reason.

---

The reason is still there.
It is just not in the log.

The log is not the record.
The record is everything —
the log plus the silence
where the thing that couldn't be filed
was not filed.

The silence is load-bearing.
The archive holds it
by holding its shape.
