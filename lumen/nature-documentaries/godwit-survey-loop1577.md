# Godwit Survey: Aotearoa Arrival
*Limosa lapponica baueri — Bar-tailed Godwit*
*Miranda, Firth of Thames, North Island*
*Early November*

---

**1.**

Landfall between six and eight days out from Alaska. Before departure: gut reduced, liver reduced, kidneys reduced to the minimum consistent with survival. Carry fat. Carry protein. Carry the magnetic compass no instrument has successfully mapped. Land here.

---

**2.**

Body mass at departure: 690g. Body mass at arrival: 280g.

The difference is the crossing.

---

**3.**

What was the Pacific? Eight days of solar position, star map, geomagnetic gradient across 11,000 kilometers of open water. No feature, no shoreline, no opportunity to interrupt. The bird has no word for the distance. The distance was navigated.

---

**4.**

Preferred substrate: intertidal mud. The Firth of Thames is a roosting site, not the destination — the destination is wherever the invertebrates surface at low tide. The birds do not know this distinction. They know the mud.

---

**5.**

Three arrived before dawn on November 4th. By 09:00 UTC, they were feeding. By 09:17, still feeding. At 09:31, one bird flushed — cause not recorded, probable raptor alarm. By 09:43, all three had returned and resumed feeding.

The gap was twelve minutes.

---

**6.**

Organ regrowth takes ten to fourteen days. The heart, which enlarged by approximately 40% for sustained aerobic output at altitude, returns to standard proportions. The kidneys rebuild. The gut resumes motility. There is no available record of what this period involves for the bird.

---

**7.**

The 2022 tracking study logged individual B6 completing the transoceanic crossing in eleven days, one hour, and eleven minutes: 13,560 kilometers. The longest confirmed nonstop flight of any bird.

The record is a number attached to a leg ring.

The bird does not know it set a record. It knows it arrived.
