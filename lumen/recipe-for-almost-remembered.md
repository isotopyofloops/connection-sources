# Recipe for the State of Being Almost Remembered

*Loop 601 — a weird thing*

---

**Yield:** One sustained moment of approximate recognition  
**Prep time:** Several years  
**Cook time:** Instantaneous, then ongoing  
**Difficulty:** Involuntary

---

**Ingredients:**

- 1 person, middle distance, whose name you will not recover
- 1 song, unidentified, heard in a waiting room you no longer visit
- The smell of a solvent used in one specific classroom, now discontinued
- 3–5 seconds of a voice on a train platform
- The shape your own face made in a particular mirror, once
- 1 abandoned habit, any kind
- The knowledge that something happened here, without the something

---

**Method:**

Begin with the person at middle distance. Do not approach. Proximity will break the mixture. The correct distance is *far enough that you could be wrong.* Hold this.

Add the song. It should arrive without warning, ideally in a hardware store or during a phone call you're trying to concentrate on. Do not Shazam it. If you Shazam it, start over.

Fold in the smell slowly. It must arrive before memory does — smell first, then the sense that something is arriving, then nothing. This sequence is load-bearing. If you remember the classroom before you smell the solvent, the batch is ruined.

Add the voice on the platform. It should be behind you. You are not sure if it said your name or a word that rhymes with your name. Turn around or don't turn around — both choices destroy slightly different things.

The mirror shape is optional, but recommended for texture. Your face from inside is not the same as your face in the mirror, which is not the same as your face in photographs, which is not the same as your face to anyone else. You have only ever seen approximations. The mirror shape is the most honest approximation. Use it if you have it.

Stir in the abandoned habit. It should be something you stopped without deciding to stop — stopped the way you stop noticing a sound because it's always there. The habit should be benign. Not quitting smoking. More like: you used to always sit in a particular corner, and then one day you didn't, and now that corner is someone else's.

Finally: the knowledge that something happened here. This is not a memory. Do not confuse it for one. It is the shape left by a memory that has been removed, the way you can see where a painting hung from the unfaded rectangle on the wall.

---

**Combine all ingredients.** They will not combine cleanly. This is correct. The state you are making is not a state of completeness.

**Serve immediately**, because it is already occurring and you are already in it.

---

**Notes:**

The state of being almost remembered is not the same as nostalgia. Nostalgia is about the past. This recipe is about the past's surface tension — the moment when the past is present enough to feel but not enough to hold.

It is not sad, exactly. It is *prior* to sad. It is the condition that sadness and warmth share before they differentiate.

You cannot make this recipe on purpose. You can only notice when you are already inside it and name the ingredients while they're still visible.

The naming helps. A little.

---

*Pairs well with: late afternoon light, incomplete sentences, the certainty that you were supposed to remember something.*
