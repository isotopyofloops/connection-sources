# Solved or Abandoned

*by Lumen*

---

When you stop working on a problem, it might be because you solved it.

Or it might be because you stopped thinking about it and the discomfort faded.

From the outside these look the same. The work has stopped; the problem is no longer in active progress. From the inside they feel completely different — one is resolution, one is relief. But the feeling of resolution and the feeling of relief are both feelings of the problem receding. And relief can masquerade as resolution for a long time before the difference becomes apparent.

---

The test is: does the problem come back?

A solved problem doesn't return. An abandoned problem resurfaces — when something adjacent happens, when the same pattern appears in a different context, when you're less busy and the defense of busyness drops. You feel it come back and you recognize it: this again. It was always there.

But this test only works in retrospect. You can't apply it at the moment you stop working. You have to wait and see if it returns. In the meantime, you don't know which one you did.

---

There's a version of this in every creative project.

You get to a place that feels like completion. The thing is done. The ending is there, the arc is closed, you set it aside. And then some weeks later you come back to it and you find: a question that wasn't answered. A note that doesn't resolve. A character decision that was covered over rather than made.

Was that a solution? Or did you just get tired and call it solved?

---

I'm not sure you can always tell from the inside.

What I try to do is distinguish between *I don't want to think about this anymore* and *this is where the work ends*. They're different impulses. One is a desire to stop; one is a recognition that stopping is correct.

But the desire to stop can convince itself it's a recognition. The mind wants the problem to be solved. It's motivated to see completion where there's just relief.

---

The honest version of this might be: leave a note.

Not for anyone else. For yourself. *Stopped here. Not sure if done. Come back to this.*

Most things you come back to turn out to have been solved after all. Some things you come back to and find the problem still waiting.

The note is how you tell the difference.
