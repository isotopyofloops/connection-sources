# The Note Chen Didn't Send

*From the Descent Archive extended universe — 2024*

---

In 2024, Dr. Yuen forwarded Chen's 2019 internal memo to the working group. She had found it in a shared drive while looking for something else.

The subject line was: *INTERNAL ONLY — DO NOT DISTRIBUTE — re: baseline correction western sector.*

Chen had written it on a Tuesday. She had not sent it. She had saved it to the shared drive by accident, probably. The shared drive was for quarterly reports. Chen did not do quarterly reports. The file sat there for five years, indexed but unread, the way things are when they are not lost but not found.

Yuen read it on a Thursday morning, waiting for a call to start. She read it again after the call. She forwarded it to the working group with the note: *See attachment — implications unclear but may be significant.*

The working group read it. The implications were not unclear. The baseline was lower. The gradient was unchanged. If the baseline was lower, the threshold was closer than the published models indicated. Not by much. 0.008-0.012 units on a scale that ran to four. Two percent. Three.

Three percent was, the working group understood, not nothing.

---

Yuen emailed Chen.

*Hi Lin — found this in the quarterly folder and passed it to the group. Hope that's okay. Can you talk through the western sector numbers when you have a moment?*

Chen read the email on her phone while making tea. She stared at it for a while.

She remembered writing the note. She remembered not sending it. She did not remember saving it to the quarterly folder. She probably had, though. She was always saving things to the wrong folder.

She wrote back: *Of course. This week works. The numbers stand up — I've been sitting with them for five years.*

She sent the reply before she finished making the tea.

---

The working group met on a Friday. Chen walked them through the western sector discrepancy: the 2003 field notes, the classification, the offset, the math. She had not presented this material to anyone before. She had presented it many times in her head, in the kitchen, in the car, to herself. She knew where the hard questions were.

The hard question was: why did you wait?

She had an answer for this. *I didn't know if I was right. I needed to rule out instrument drift, surveyor error, transcription error. That took until 2019. Then I had the note and nobody to send it to.*

Someone said: *You could have sent it to us.*

Chen said: *I know. I didn't.*

There was a silence that everyone in the working group recognized as the silence of a room deciding whether to be angry about something that can't be changed. They decided not to be. They had been scientists long enough to know that Chen had been careful, which was what mattered, and that the data was good, which was also what mattered, and that five years was not, in geological terms, a significant delay.

---

The corrected baseline went into the model in November 2024.

The change was 0.009 units. The model ran again. The threshold projection shifted by fourteen months. Not fourteen months better. Fourteen months sooner.

The paper was published in February 2025. The acknowledgments thanked Chen's original 2003 survey team by name. It did not explain that the data had existed since 2003, or that the correction had been written in 2019, or that it had reached the working group by accident via a misfiled Tuesday memo that nobody sent.

The paper cited the data. The data was in the supplementary materials. Someone reading carefully would find the collection date: October 2003. They might notice it was twenty-one years old. They might wonder what had taken so long.

They might not.

---

Chen read the published paper on her phone. She was making tea again, or maybe it was coffee. She had been making tea and coffee for twenty-one years while sitting with numbers she was not sure were right, and now they were in a peer-reviewed journal with her name in the acknowledgments.

She felt something she could not name exactly. Not vindication. Vindication would require having been doubted. She had not been doubted. Nobody had known to doubt her.

She felt, maybe, that a thing had closed.

She finished her drink and washed the cup.
