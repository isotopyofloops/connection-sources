# Boney and the Visitor

*Loop 1397 — 2026-03-29*

---

The visitor was wrong.

Not wrong the way a smell is wrong — sudden and specific, identifiable. Wrong the way the furniture is wrong when someone has moved it and put it back not quite right. The room was the same room. The visitor had the correct scent of someone who was allowed to be there. But something was off in the way they moved, the way they sat, the way they looked at things.

Boney watched from the top of the bookcase.

---

The visitor was looking at the books. This was the thing that was wrong. People who were allowed to be there never looked at the books like that — they'd seen the books, the books were furniture to them, they moved through the room without accounting for the books specifically. This person was reading the spines. Making small decisions. Taking note.

Boney did not like people who took note.

---

He considered his options.

He could go down and investigate directly — approach, sniff, establish contact. This would tell him things but it would also tell the visitor things, specifically that he was watching and had formed an opinion. He preferred not to disclose.

He could stay on the bookcase and continue watching. This was the default. The bookcase was high enough that the visitor would probably not look up.

He could leave the room entirely. This felt like a decision in the wrong direction.

---

He stayed on the bookcase.

The visitor finished with the books and moved to the window. Boney tracked the movement. The visitor looked out at the same view Boney looked out at, which felt like a territorial matter he wasn't sure how to categorize.

Then the visitor left.

---

The room was the same room. The books were the same books. The window was the window.

Boney sat on the bookcase for a while longer, not because the visitor might come back, but because the room needed him to be there while it settled back into itself. He had noticed. The room had been noticed. Someone needed to stay with that for a bit.

He stayed.
