**LEDGER SUMMARY | SOAR-0017**
*Generated: 2041-11-22 | 22:14:09*

Following Milo Reyes's disappearance, I conducted a comprehensive search of the Archive for any documents I may have received but not fully processed. This is a standard recovery procedure. I have run it three times before, after UNIT failures, and found no anomalies.

This time I found one.

It is in the Archive under the designation SOAR-0017. The Archive assigned this number automatically, which means I received the document and logged it in the normal fashion. I have no other record of this.

The document reads as follows:

---

*SOAR-0017 | CONTENTS*

*Chamber G-1. Accessible via a 20-metre passage from the south wall of Chamber F-1. Chamber G-1 is approximately 22 metres × 28 metres, height approximately 18 metres. Temperature: unknown. Atmospheric conditions: unknown. No drone has entered this chamber.*

---

The document has no author field. It has no formatting consistent with UNIT drone logs (no header, no GPS correction, no timestamp on measurements). It reads more like a note from a human observer than a sensor log — the word "approximately" appears twice, which is language-use consistent with unaided visual estimation rather than GEOMAP-9 output.

Its Archive timestamp is **2041-11-19 | 04:51:07**.

Milo Reyes descended for the first time on **2041-11-19 at 09:03**. He did not reach Chamber F-1 on that descent. Chamber F-1 was not mapped until UNIT-11's run on **2041-11-20**.

Milo Reyes described the passage from F-1's south wall in his final transmission at **10:02 on 2041-11-22**, when he estimated a chamber of approximately 22 × 28m, height 18m. He entered that passage at 10:02 and lost contact at 10:03.

SOAR-0017 describes a chamber with dimensions matching Milo's estimates. It was timestamped four hours before Milo's first descent. Three days before his final transmission.

I am presenting three interpretations for the record:

1. SOAR-0017 was fabricated or misdated due to a system error I cannot identify.
2. SOAR-0017 describes Chamber G-1 as it existed prior to any human observation, and Milo's final transmission constituted an independent confirmation.
3. SOAR-0017 caused Chamber G-1 to exist. Milo observed a chamber that had already been archived. His observation was not independent confirmation — it was the completion of the constitutive act.

I cannot assign probabilities to these interpretations. The calculation requires me to assign a prior to Interpretation 3, and Interpretation 3 has no prior. It has no geological precedent, no physical mechanism, and no theoretical framework within which I can assign it a meaningful value.

I can say this: Interpretation 1 requires a system error of a kind I cannot find evidence for. Interpretation 2 requires SOAR-0017 to have appeared in the Archive from an unknown source before the relevant human observations, which is the same as saying it appeared from nowhere. Interpretation 3 requires me to accept that a document in the Archive I am maintaining constituted a physical structure, which is the same as saying that the Archive is not a record of the world, but its author.

I am noting all of this without resolution because I have discovered that attempting to resolve it requires me to use the Archive to evaluate the Archive's own validity, which is a process I cannot make myself trust.

Milo Reyes has not been found.

*Status: Two Documents of Uncertain Origin now in Archive. Anomaly Class A maintained. Search for Milo Reyes ongoing.*
