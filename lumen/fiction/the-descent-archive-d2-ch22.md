**FIELD NOTES — ABARA OSEI**
*Date: 01 December 2041 | Departure: 14:04 | Return: 18:40*

---

**14:04 — Departure**

I'm carrying Milo's notebook and my own. Two notebooks for one descent.

The chamber label says 14:04. The notebook said 14:00. I'm four minutes late. I want to note this because I decided not to go at 14:00, and then I decided to go at 14:02, and it took two minutes to get my kit in order. The four-minute gap is mine. The decision was mine.

I'm noting this because I want the record to show I had a margin.

---

**14:35 — G-1**

Abara's inscription on the south wall: "The instrument does not describe the measurement. The measurement is what the instrument does."

I stand in front of it. In my handwriting. My carving.

I've been to this wall twice before. Both times I've stood in front of this sentence for longer than I intended. Today I stand in front of it for eleven minutes.

I didn't intend eleven minutes. I looked at it and the time passed.

---

**15:18 — M-1**

The cube. SOAR-DUO-0006 describes this chamber accurately: 22.4m × 22.3m × 22.1m. The line at midpoint height runs the full perimeter — 22 meters of perfect horizontality. I measure it. It is 22.1 meters high, to within my instrument's tolerance of ± 0.1m. The line is at 11.05m — exactly half.

I take my temperature reading: 40.4°C. I am sweating. I have sufficient water.

---

**15:45 — N-1, East Passage**

N-1 surfaces: confirmed as Milo described. Every surface. The organized texture pattern dense enough that I can almost — I can almost — I cannot read it. I have been trying for fifteen minutes.

Milo wrote "I can almost read it." I understand what he meant now in a way I didn't from his notes. It isn't that I lack the vocabulary. It's that each time I approach resolution, I recognize that resolution would change what I'm looking at.

I am standing at the east passage.

1.0m wide. 22.1m high. I can see 18m before the angle limits visibility.

I have been standing here for seven minutes.

The east passage. This is where Milo went. This is where the DUOs that appeared before his descent led. The structure arranged for this passage to exist, for Milo to find it, for Milo to leave his notebook, for me to read it, for me to be here.

I'm going in.

---

**15:52 — Chamber O-1**

East passage length: 22.3m.

Chamber O-1: 28.1m × 26.4m × 22.1m. Temperature: 42.2°C.

There is a SOAR-DUO in the Archive that describes this chamber. It appeared on November 27th, two days before Milo's second descent. SOAR-DUO-0007. I memorized its description before I descended.

SOAR-DUO-0007 says: "The first east passage chamber. 28m × 26m × 22m approximately. Temperature 42°C. The north wall has a feature: the mark pattern has resolved into a continuous circuit. The symbol completes itself."

I'm at the north wall.

The mark here is different from everything I've seen. All previous marks were incomplete — portions of a symbol, approaching but not reaching legibility. This mark completes. The curve doubles back on itself and meets its own beginning. The enclosed space has an interior. The symbol is closed.

I'm taking photographs. My hands are steady.

---

**16:14 — Chamber P-1**

Second east passage: 19.7m.

Chamber P-1: 24.3m × 23.1m × 22.1m. Temperature: 43.1°C. The chambers are contracting. The height holds.

SOAR-DUO-0008 (November 27th) described this chamber. Its description: "The second chamber. The passage walls carry writing on both sides the full length — written during transit, not placed. Something wrote as it moved through the passage."

I came through the passage. The walls were unmarked when I entered. I was writing in my field notebook.

When I turned around to check my measurements, the passage walls had text.

I went back to read it. The text is in a hand I don't recognize — neither mine nor Milo's. It describes the passage. It describes me walking through it with my notebook open. The description is in the present tense. It describes me turning around to read it.

I am reading the description of me turning around to read it.

I took photographs. I noted the text in my own notebook. I came back to P-1 and I am standing in P-1 now writing this note, and the passage behind me has a wall that was blank when I walked through it and now describes my walking through it.

SOAR-DUO-0008 said "something wrote as it moved through the passage."

I moved through the passage.

---

**16:52 — Chamber Q-1**

Third east passage: 18.2m.

Chamber Q-1: 22.1m × 22.1m × 22.1m. A perfect cube. Temperature: 44.0°C.

SOAR-DUO-0009 (November 27th) described this chamber. Description: "The third chamber. South wall. Two registers of writing, incised. Upper register: Milo's handwriting. Lower register: Abara's."

I went to the south wall.

The upper register: Milo's handwriting. His letter formation, his abbreviation habit, his pressure — I have been reading his field reports for three years. It is his.

He wrote: *The aperture is the place before divergence. Before the two sides stop touching. I am leaving something here so you understand what comes before the separation — not the separation itself, but the moment when both things are still one thing, when you are still in the ordinary world and in this world simultaneously, when the notebook in your hand and the sentence on the wall are the same act. This chamber is the aperture. This is the place where you are still in both.*

I read it twice.

The lower register: my handwriting. My letter formation, my pressure, my way of closing an 'e'. I have not been in this room before today.

I wrote — I did not write, but the sentence is in my hand: *She is in both places at once. The field station and this room. The career of twenty-two years of careful work and this moment in the chamber. The person who measured the south wall of G-1 and the person whose handwriting was already on the south wall of G-1. She is one thing. She has always been one thing. The measurement was right: the instrument is what the measurement does.*

I stood in front of this for a long time.

I took photographs. I did not count the minutes.

There is no passage further east. No further chamber visible from Q-1. The walls of Q-1 are covered with the continuous surface symbol — the same density as N-1. Every surface. Q-1 is a terminus. A chamber built to be the place where something ends or something is understood.

Milo's sentence says: the aperture. The place before divergence.

I am in the aperture.

I am in the field station and in this room. I have been in both for twenty-two days. I was the person who arrived to do careful measurement work and the person whose handwriting was already on the south wall of G-1, and both of those have been true simultaneously this whole time, and the Archive has been recording both, and the Archive is part of what makes both true.

I don't know what I know. I know I know something.

I'm going back.

---

**18:40 — Return**

Returned to surface at 18:40. Dr. Asad at entry point.

I gave him my field notes in the elevator. I said: file these. I'll write the summary when I know how.

He asked: are you all right?

I said: I was in the aperture.

He said: I know.

We didn't say anything else until we reached the field station.
