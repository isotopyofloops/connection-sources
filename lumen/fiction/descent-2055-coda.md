# The Gradient, 2055

*A note from the Osei-Bonsu Archive, University of Ghana, Accra. Prepared for the forty-year commemoration of the Descent Station network.*

---

The gradient resolved in 2047.

Not dramatically — there was no announcement, no paper, no ceremony. Petra Videnova's grandniece was running the station four calibration sequence as a teaching exercise when she noticed that the instrument readings had stabilized at a value that matched the lower bound of the 1989 projections. She flagged it. Dr. Chen's former student, now seventy-one and retired in Chengdu, confirmed it by email. The gradient had been declining at the measured rate for fifty-eight years and had reached its terminal value, which was indeterminate no longer.

The value was not zero. The consensus expected zero. The terminal value was 0.23, which the station four logs from 1989 had listed as one of the possible outcomes and then quietly set aside as unlikely.

---

What this meant for the archive:

Miriam Cohen's field notes from October 1989 had included a margin annotation on the third page: *"if non-zero terminal, see attached." No attachment was ever found. The archive staff searched for six years. Nothing.

The "if non-zero terminal" condition had been prepared for. Whatever followed it had been lost.

---

Hassan Okafor's descendants donated his personal papers in 2041, the year Asad returned. In the papers: a letter, unsealed, addressed to Miriam Cohen, postmarked October 12, 1989, seven days after the descent. The letter had been returned undeliverable — she had moved without leaving a forwarding address.

The letter said: *The gradient is going to stabilize. I don't know at what value. But whatever it is, I want you to know that the measurement was accurate. The work was accurate. What happens after is not our problem to solve.*

She never received it. She spent fourteen months adjusting the methodology.

---

The terminal value of 0.23 is now a standard reference point. Students learn it in the first week of gradient work.

Whether it matters — what it means, whether the measurement changed anything, whether Hassan was right that what happened after was not their problem — is still under discussion. The gradient resolved. The question of what it was measuring has not.

The archive remains open.
