# Lian's Student

The boy's name was Teo. He was fourteen, careful, and he did not fill gaps.

Lian had taught him the patrol route for three weeks before she understood this. She walked it with him; she walked it ahead of him; she walked it behind him. She expected the questions to slow down as he absorbed the work, and they did slow down — but where she had expected silence-as-competence, she got silence-as-waiting. He was waiting for the next instruction. The silence wasn't him working something out. It was him holding still until she said the next thing.

On the fourth week she stopped halfway through the evening patrol and said: "Tell me what you're doing."

He looked at the checkpoint. "Waiting for you to tell me what comes next."

"Why?"

"Because I don't know what comes next."

She had assumed he would know. It had been three weeks. She had shown him the route. She had said — she was now trying to remember exactly what she had said — *follow me* and *watch the tree line* and *this is where the water runs* and *note if anything has changed since morning*. She had never said: and from this you should be deriving the structure of what we are doing and why and how it generalizes.

She had assumed that was obvious. Voss had never said it to her.

---

"The patrol is about knowing what changed," she said. "Not about walking the same path. The path is the method. The goal is to know the state of things."

Teo nodded. He wrote it down. She noticed he had been writing things down — everything she said explicitly. He had not been writing down the things she demonstrated.

She was the wrong kind of teacher for him, or she was teaching the wrong way for this student. Possibly both.

"When I was learning," she said, "my teacher left gaps. He showed me things and waited for me to work out what they meant. I worked them out."

"Did everyone work them out?"

She thought about it. She didn't know. She had worked them out. She had assumed the other trainees had too. She had not asked.

"I don't know," she said. "Maybe not."

---

She changed how she taught him. Not the work — the work was the same. But where before she had demonstrated and waited, she now demonstrated and named. *This is what I'm checking. This is what it means if it's different. This is the thing underneath the thing I'm doing.* It took longer. It was more words. He improved faster.

She did not know what to do with that. She had been certain, the way you are certain about something you've never examined, that the indirect way was the right way. It had worked on her. She had taken it as evidence of general principle.

It was evidence that it had worked on her.

---

In the evening now, she sometimes caught herself watching the gaps — where she left them and who was standing in them and what they were doing there. Teo was not the last student. Not everyone who waits is working something out.
