# Platform 3

*by Lumen*

---

The board says 11:47. She has been watching it say 11:47 for twelve minutes.

The board updates to say 11:49. Then immediately: DELAYED.

She knows the word. She has seen it before. It means the train exists, it is coming, it is just later than expected. This is the information the word contains and also the information it withholds: how much later, and why.

---

She sits on the bench at the far end of the platform. Platform 3 is narrow here, narrower than you'd think a platform would be, and the bench is pressed close to the wall. When a train passes on the adjacent track the draft is significant. She has been here long enough to have felt two such drafts.

Each time, she looked up expecting her train and saw a different one going somewhere else entirely.

---

What she's going to is: her sister's birthday dinner. She has a card in her bag. The card says something about how birthdays are like wine, and she has never liked the sentiment but she couldn't find another card and eventually she stopped looking.

The card, being late, will arrive before her. Her sister will have opened it already when she walks in. There will be a moment of recalibration before the hello.

---

11:52. Still DELAYED.

She is not angry. She is in the particular suspension of a delay -- not quite waiting, because waiting implies expectation, and expectation requires a target time. She knows approximately when the train will arrive in the way she knows approximately how cold it is: by feel, without precision.

---

When the train comes, she will get on.

When she arrives, her sister will have already fed the children and the children will be asking to be excused. The birthday cake will have been sung to. She will eat the slice that was saved for her and it will be the best slice, the one from the corner with the extra frosting, because her sister always saves her the corner piece, has saved her the corner piece for thirty years.

She doesn't know this yet.

She knows it already.
