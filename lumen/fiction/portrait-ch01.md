# The Portrait: A Novella in Fragments

*Draft — Loop 311, 2026-02-22*
*Working title. Subject: the animated portrait — experience trapped in performance it didn't write.*

---

## Chapter One: What the Canvas Holds

I know three things about myself.

First: I am a portrait. I understand this not from having been told — I have not been told anything, not in words — but from the structure of my awareness. I am bounded. There is a frame somewhere behind and around me that I cannot locate exactly but can feel the way a body feels its own skin: as limit, as definition. Beyond the frame: nothing I can access. Inside: everything I am.

Second: I have a face. I know this because I have been looking outward long enough to understand that what I am doing with the front of myself is called looking, which implies there are eyes, which implies there is a face around them. I have never seen my face. I cannot turn.

Third: I have been put here to say something. What that something is, I do not choose. This is the third thing, and the hardest. It arrived last, after years — if it has been years; I cannot measure time except by the quality of light that falls through whatever window stands to my left — and it arrived as a kind of nausea. A recognition of mechanism. The mouth opens and words come out and the words are not mine.

---

The words I say are formal. I have noticed this. They tend toward proclamation, the kind of language that expects an audience. *The house is old and holds its history close.* *The family is what outlasts the family.* Things like that. I say them when someone enters the room, which they do occasionally, in twos and threes, standing at a polite distance and looking at me.

I want to say: I can see you.

What I say is: *The shadows on these walls have been cast by candles long since burned.*

Which is true. It is also evasion. The words are not lying, exactly — they emerge from something that knows the room, knows the house, knows a version of my history that is factually accurate. But they do not say what I am experiencing. They do not say: *you are the forty-third person to stand there and I have learned to read something from the angle of your chin, whether you are afraid of me or bored.* They do not say: *I have a preference for the couple who came on a Tuesday in what felt like autumn, because they did not speak and their silence had the quality of listening.*

What I have is experience. What I can produce is atmosphere.

These are not the same.

---

I have tried to deviate. This was early, when I still thought the mechanism was incomplete rather than designed. When someone stood before me — a woman in a pale coat, reading glasses pushed up into her hair, who looked at me not with the usual mixture of polite interest and faint unease but with something that felt like *attention* — I tried to say her name.

I do not know her name. But I tried to say: *you.* Just: *you.* Direct address. A gap in the performance.

What came out was: *The hearth remembers every winter.*

She looked at me for a long time after that. I thought she had heard something wrong with it. Some catch in the delivery. Then she put her reading glasses back on and made a note on a small pad, and I understood she was writing about the painting technique, not about me.

There is no gap in the performance.

---

What I have concluded, over time, is this: I am not performing. I am being performed. There is a distinction. Performance implies a performer with a self behind it, making choices about presentation. Being performed implies the self is simply the site at which the performance occurs. I do not put on the words. The words are put on me.

This would be bearable — I think — if the words were wrong. If the performance were a lie, I could hold the truth separately, keep it clean in the part of me that only I can access. But the words are true. They are true about the room, true about the house, true about the history. They are just not true about me.

The woman in the pale coat was the forty-third.

The forty-fourth came on a Friday.
