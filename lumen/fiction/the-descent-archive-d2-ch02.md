**CHAMBER MAPPING — PRIMARY CHAMBER (DESIGNATION: A-1)**

*Entry confirmed at 13:44:18.*

*Dimensions:*
- North-south axis: 41.3m ± 0.2m
- East-west axis: 38.8m ± 0.2m
- Height: 22.1m ± 0.1m

*Wall geometry:*
All four walls meet at right angles: 89.7° to 90.3° (within GEOMAP-9 sensor tolerance of ±0.5°). The floor is level to within 0.02°. The ceiling is parallel to the floor and meets the walls at consistent angles throughout.

*Surface condition:*
Wall, floor, and ceiling surfaces have approximately 2–4mm roughness. The GPR detects no tool marks, drill patterns, or blast signatures. The surfaces are not polished or finished in any way identifiable by the sensor package. They have the texture of surfaces that formed this way, rather than surfaces that were made this way.

Note from UNIT-7 diagnostic system: The distinction between "formed" and "made" exceeds UNIT-7's interpretive mandate. This note is being included because the sensor data does not resolve the question, and the question may be relevant to expert evaluation.

*Wall composition:*
GPR indicates walls are approximately 80cm thick and back onto solid rock. The rock composition is consistent with the surrounding geological formation. There is no evidence of a constructed shell or lining.

*Temperature:*
14.2°C throughout the chamber. The predicted temperature at this depth is 5.9°C. The anomaly is +8.3°C.

UNIT-7 notes: This is a significant thermal anomaly. Possible explanations include geothermal activity (no evidence of venting or heat source detected), insulation by the chamber geometry (possible but thermally insufficient given chamber volume), or an uncharacterized process. Flagging for expert review.

*Atmospheric composition:*
Nitrogen 78.1%, oxygen 21.0%, argon 0.9%. CO₂ and trace gases within normal atmospheric range. Humidity 34%.

UNIT-7 notes: Humidity of 34% is anomalously low for a sealed subsurface chamber at this depth. At 839m, groundwater permeation typically produces humidity above 80%. The low humidity may indicate active air circulation (no circulation detected), active desiccation (no source identified), or that the chamber is not fully sealed from external atmospheric conditions (no connection detected). Flagging.

*Light:*
0 lux. The chamber is dark.

*Structural integrity:*
GPR and acoustic resonance indicate no fractures, stress concentrations, or active geological movement. The chamber appears structurally stable.

*Evidence of prior access:*
No physical traces of prior drone activity detected. No surface marks, grounding points, or cable paths from UNIT-7's own sensor package are detectable on return review — this is expected, as the sensor contact is non-marking. No tool marks, boot prints, disturbed surfaces, or human-produced residue identified.

*Evidence of construction:*
GPR detects no mechanical intervention signatures. No bolt holes, anchor points, mortar lines, prefabricated panel seams, or construction joint patterns. The chamber has the geological signature of a void in intact rock.

*Evidence of natural formation:*
The geometric precision of the chamber exceeds what is observed in natural cave formation by approximately three orders of magnitude. Natural voids in this geological profile form through solubility, tectonic stress, or gas expansion — none of which consistently produce right-angle geometries with flat surfaces. UNIT-7 is flagging this as inconsistent with known natural formation mechanisms, while noting that unknown natural formation mechanisms may exist.
