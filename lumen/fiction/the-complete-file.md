# The Complete File

*From the Descent Archive extended universe — 2041*

---

The documentation specialist assigned to compile the complete ETH-7 file was named Yael Friedman. She was not a field researcher. She processed documentation. This was useful because she had no stake in any of the conclusions.

She assembled the file in order: 1989, 2003, 2041-preliminary.

The 1989 file was eight pages. Standard survey documentation. She read it on a Tuesday afternoon, between two other projects.

On page three, she found: *Gradient check, stream bed: [crossed out] Gradient indeterminate. Instrument calibrated prior to measurement. Will recheck tomorrow.*

On page four: *Gradient: indeterminate. Instrument calibrated. Reading consistent with Day 2 measurement.*

On page eight, the summary: *ETH-7 is a stable, unremarkable survey site. Gradient measurement was inconclusive and will be noted in the final report as an instrument limitation at this elevation.*

Final line of the summary: *Recommend: no further survey.*

She noted this in her compilation log: *1989 report recommends no further survey. Site was surveyed in 2003 and 2041. File contains no documentation of the decision to return.*

She did not know whose decision it had been. The file contained no record of it. Someone had looked at a report that said *do not return* and decided to return, and whatever conversation had led to that decision was not in the documentation. It might have been in email. It might have been verbal. It might have been that no one remembered the 1989 recommendation when the 2003 survey was proposed.

She noted all of this in her compilation log and continued to the 2003 file.

The 2003 file was forty-three pages and had a gap at day five.
