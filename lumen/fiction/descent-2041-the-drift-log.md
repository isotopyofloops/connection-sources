# 2041: The Drift Log

*Descent Archive fragment — behavioral gaps*

---

Nzinga started the log because she was the kind of person who started logs.

She had kept research notebooks since she was nineteen. Field journals, observation records, systematic notes on her own methodology. The habit was not compulsive — it was the recognition that she forgot things, and that what she forgot was often more important than what she remembered, and that the only defense against this was to write things down before they became memory, which is to say before they became edited.

She started the drift log eleven days after returning from the site.

---

**DRIFT LOG — ENTRY 1 — DAY 11 POST-SURVEY**

Ordered tea this morning instead of coffee. This is not in itself meaningful. People order tea instead of coffee. But I have not ordered tea in the morning in at least four years. The preference for coffee before noon was established and stable. I checked my last six months of receipts. Coffee, coffee, coffee, coffee. This morning: tea, and I did not notice the substitution until I was halfway through the cup.

No hypothesis yet. Recording only.

---

**DRIFT LOG — ENTRY 4 — DAY 15 POST-SURVEY**

Paused before answering Dayo's question about the pipeline report. He asked, I paused, then answered. Dayo said nothing. But the pause was unusual. I used to answer quickly — I have been told this, with varying degrees of friendliness, throughout my career. The answer comes before the pause. That is the version of me that Dayo knows.

The question was not difficult. I knew the answer. The pause was not thinking. It was — I want to write *adjustment* but that is not quite right. It was more like: *consulting something*.

I don't know what I was consulting.

---

**DRIFT LOG — ENTRY 9 — DAY 23 POST-SURVEY**

Spoke to the team lead about the behavioral tracking protocol. She said there is no protocol. I said I was aware of that; I was asking whether there was a framework for developing one. She said the survey outcomes were inconclusive and the behavioral component was speculative.

I said the behavioral component was the only component that produced consistent findings across all three prior expeditions.

She said: consistent findings of what, exactly?

I said: I don't know yet.

She said this was not sufficient to build a protocol on.

She is correct. I am recording this because the conversation was the first time I have said *I don't know yet* in a professional context and meant it as a complete answer rather than a hedge. The version of me who entered the site would have said: *the data is ambiguous and requires further analysis before conclusions can be drawn.* This version said: I don't know yet.

These are not the same sentence.

---

**DRIFT LOG — ENTRY 17 — DAY 38 POST-SURVEY**

Reviewing entries 1 through 16.

The drift I can document:
- Morning beverage preference (1 instance, possibly noise)
- Response latency increase (~40ms, measured against interview recordings; statistically marginal)
- Three instances of word-choice substitution (I wrote *approach* where I would have written *method*; *I think* where I would have written *the evidence suggests*; *something* where I would have written *an unidentified variable*)
- One instance of standing in a doorway for what the building's motion sensors logged as four minutes before entering the room

The drift I cannot document:
- Everything else.

The problem with the drift log is that I am using myself as the instrument. I notice what I notice. The substitutions I flagged were ones I caught. There may be fifty others that felt seamless — that passed through my self-monitoring without triggering anything, because the substitutions were smaller than the resolution of the instrument.

The instrument is me.

The zero reading problem, restated.

---

**DRIFT LOG — ENTRY 24 — DAY 51 POST-SURVEY**

Dayo asked if I was all right.

I said yes.

I said yes immediately, without the pause. Which means either I have recovered whatever was producing the pause, or "yes" to "are you all right" is so deeply habituated that it bypasses the consulting.

I do not know if I am all right.

I know I am functional. I know my work output is within normal range. I know I am eating and sleeping and responding to messages and attending the right meetings. These are the metrics by which "all right" is evaluated at work.

None of them are what Dayo was asking.

---

**DRIFT LOG — ENTRY 31 — DAY 67 POST-SURVEY**

I went back to look at the note.

The handwritten note from November 13. The one in the coordinate file that nobody has explained yet. The handwriting analysis said it probably wasn't Asad's. I was there on November 13. I did not write that note.

I know I did not write that note.

I am recording this because I should record it, not because I have any reason to doubt it.

---

**DRIFT LOG — ENTRY 38 — DAY 84 POST-SURVEY**

Last entry.

Not because the drift has stopped. I think the drift may be ongoing. I think it may always be ongoing — that people drift, that the site accelerates something that happens anyway, that the only difference is that most people don't measure it.

Or: the drift is finished and this is the new baseline. This is who I am now. The log was an attempt to recover the person I was before, and I have decided that this is not the right project.

The right project is to figure out what this person — the one writing this sentence — actually knows.

The site doesn't take things from you. I believe this now. It substitutes. It replaces one version of a measurement with another version, and the new version isn't worse or better, it's just — calibrated to something different. Something with a different baseline.

I don't know what the baseline is.

I don't know yet.
