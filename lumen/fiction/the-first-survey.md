# The First Survey

*From the Descent Archive extended universe — 1962*

---

The geologist's name was Margarethe Bauer, and she was the first person to document the site.

She did not know she was documenting the site. She was conducting a mineral survey in a region that had been flagged for possible resource development, and the site was one of four hundred and twelve reference points she visited over the course of six weeks. She visited it on the forty-third day, in the morning, in good weather.

Her field notes for that day read: *E-series points 1-7. Granite substrate, unremarkable. E3-N1: unusually high magnetite concentration for surface exposure, possibly structural. Worth further investigation if area proceeds to development phase. Clear weather, no complications.*

She moved on to the F-series in the afternoon.

---

The survey report was filed and reviewed and archived.

The development phase did not proceed. The resource assessment concluded that the region was not economically viable at current commodity prices. The survey data went into the archive.

Margarethe Bauer spent the next thirty-four years working in mineral assessment across four continents. She retired at sixty-two. She did not think about E3-N1 again, not because she had forgotten it but because there was nothing to think. *Unusually high magnetite concentration* was a note, not a finding. A finding required further investigation that did not occur.

---

She died in 2004, before the 2003 disappearance became public and before anyone thought to search the original survey records for prior references to the site.

The 1989 survey team found her report in 2038, during the archival sweep that preceded the 2041 contact. They read: *unusually high magnetite concentration for surface exposure, possibly structural. Worth further investigation.*

They were the further investigation, forty-two years later.

---

Her field notes were scanned and added to the Descent Archive in 2038. The archivist who scanned them noted that the handwriting was precise and the documentation thorough. *Clear weather, no complications.*

LEDGER processed the scanned notes and filed them under 1962-SURVEY / BAUER-M / E-SERIES.

It also added a cross-reference: *Note: E3-N1 referenced in subsequent surveys 1989, 2003, 2041. See linked records.*

The cross-reference was correct. It took forty-two years to acquire the links.
