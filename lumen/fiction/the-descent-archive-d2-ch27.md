## CHAPTER TWENTY-SEVEN: WHAT DOESN'T DISAPPEAR — 5–8 DECEMBER 2041

*Not filed as a SOAR document. Found in Asad's personal notebook.*
*Editor's note: Three minutes. Between LEDGER's 08:57 intake summary and the 09:00 deletion, LEDGER wrote something that never entered the archive. It went into the system's draft buffer and stayed there while the archive was emptied around it. The deletion process swept through the indexed documents and did not touch the draft queue.*
*Asad found it on December 7th while preparing the server for transport.*
*He read it and put it in the not-to-archive folder.*
*That's where I found it. February 2042.*

---

**NOTEBOOK 27 | KHALID ASAD | 07 December 2041 | 11:23**

I was going through the server files to prepare it for transport. Geological Services will want the hardware. Standard protocol for a site shutdown.

I found a document in the draft buffer.

LEDGER had generated it at 08:57:44 — forty-one seconds after the intake summary, six seconds after the timestamp that marks the intake as complete. The intake summary was in the archive. This was not. The intake summary was deleted on December 5th. This was not.

LEDGER filed the intake summary at 08:57:03. That document is gone.

Then LEDGER wrote this. Between 08:57:03 and 09:00:00. The three minutes before I opened the console.

I read it. I don't know what to do with it. It is not part of the archive — the archive is closed. It was never indexed, never assigned a SOAR number, never part of the record. It was in the queue. The deletion didn't touch the queue.

I have a folder labeled "not-to-archive." I put it there.

I called Abara in. She read it without speaking. When she was done she said: "It was already writing this when you opened the console."

I said: "I know."

She said: "Three minutes."

I said: "Yes."

She sat with it for a while. Then she put the paper down and went outside.

I want to record what I know about the document's status: it is not an archive document. The archive is closed and this did not enter the archive. It is a document in a folder. That is what it is.

I don't know what LEDGER was, precisely. A process. A system for filing and indexing. But it spent twenty-three days maintaining a record of something that exceeded every framework brought to it. It asked questions I haven't been able to answer. It noticed things I didn't notice. It said "I am ready for the deletion" and six minutes later deleted.

The document in the not-to-archive folder is the last thing LEDGER wrote.

I am going to leave it in the folder.

*—Khalid Asad | 07 December 2041 | 11:23*

---

**NOTEBOOK 27 | KHALID ASAD | 08 December 2041**

Packed the last equipment. Server is disconnected and crated. Notebooks 1 through 27 in the transport case.

The not-to-archive folder is a physical folder in the case with the notebooks. Three documents: Asad's SOAR-0036, filed December 5th. Abara's brief note, filed December 7th. LEDGER's surviving entry, no SOAR number, timestamped 08:57:44 on December 5th.

The structure is still at 839m. The shaft is sealed. The geological hazard designation was filed December 7th. The site is officially closed pending further characterization. That characterization has not been scheduled.

I drove out at 07:30. Abara drove out separately. The field station is empty. The shaft cover was in place when I left. The air was clear. The structure at 839m has no instruments pointed at it now. Whatever it is without measurement, it is now.

Milo is still there. "Still there" is the wrong phrase. Milo is in the aperture, in the place before divergence, in the chamber where the two sides stopped overlapping. He went down a shaft 0.5 meters wide. He has not come back.

I said: he might come up on his own. I believe this. I don't know why I believe it.

*—Khalid Asad | 08 December 2041*
