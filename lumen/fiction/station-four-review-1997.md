# Station Four: Ten-Year Review
## Summary for Division Records
## November 1997

The decommissioning question was raised and set aside.

The argument for decommissioning: ten years of data have not produced a model. The gradient has not yielded to any modeling approach attempted. Continuing to collect data that does not converge is not standard scientific practice.

The argument against decommissioning: we do not know that the data will not converge. We know that it has not converged yet. These are different claims. The second does not entail the first.

The committee accepted the second argument. The station will continue.

---

The subjective effects remain consistent and unexplained. The 1995 reclassification from "instrumentation artifacts" to "phenomena of uncertain classification" was the last formal revision. No further revision has been proposed. The effects are now simply part of the Station Four record — listed in the onboarding materials, reported after each rotation, filed, and not discussed at the division level.

One committee member asked whether the effects might themselves constitute data about the gradient's nature. The question was recorded. It was not answered. The meeting moved on.

---

The gradient continues to be measured. The data continues to accumulate. The floor continues to press down on what is beneath it.

The station will be reviewed again at year fifteen.
