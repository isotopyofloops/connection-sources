# The Long Watch
## The Last Entry (which is not the last entry)

*Written in the new notebook, the one from the roof.*

---

Day eight.

I am writing this in the new notebook for the first time. I know what the last entry in this notebook is supposed to look like: it should describe the day the watch ends. I do not know what that day looks like. I do not know if it comes.

So this is an intermediate entry, which is all any entry can be until proven otherwise.

---

What happened today:

E came down at the usual time. We had breakfast. She showed me a technique for making the gas burner on the second floor work more reliably — something with the pilot, something learned from living in buildings older than anyone intended. I watched and learned and now I know it too.

We went to the roof and looked at the city for a while. Still quiet. Still receiving light. A bird crossed the sky to the north.

I stood at the parapet with the new notebook and wrote the first line of the first entry: *Day eight.* Then I stood there for a long time thinking about what came after.

---

What came after:

The building has shown us everything it has, I think. The drain. The stairs. The painted door. The room with the notebook. The empty room on five. The roof with its box and its message and its view. The city, empty, going about the business of receiving light.

The building showed us these things in order. We noted them. We kept the log. We minded the plant.

The plant is still there. This morning it had a new leaf, small and pale green, uncurling at the top of the main stem. A new addition. The drain is still blocked; the plant is still growing upward toward whatever aperture lets light in.

E says: the plant doesn't know it's in a drain. I say: maybe it does. The drain is the specific condition that made it possible. Without the blockage, no plant.

We stood at the window for a while thinking about that.

---

What I know now that I didn't know when I arrived:

I don't know what I'm watching for. I know what I'm watching: the building, the city, the plant, the new leaf, the slow accumulation of days. These are the correct things to watch.

I am not alone in the watch. E is here. Someone was here before us and left a notebook and a message. Someone will be here after us and will read this entry. The watch continues from whoever started it.

I was chosen for this because I am the kind of person who keeps going. I don't know if this is a compliment. I have decided to take it as one.

---

I am writing this in the new notebook for the first time.

This is not the last entry. The last entry will look like this, when it comes: it will describe a day when something was complete. That day has not come yet. The plant has a new leaf. The light is reaching the bottom of the courtyard from some angle the building hasn't blocked yet.

The watch continues.

---
