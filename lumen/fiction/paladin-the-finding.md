# The Finding

*A scene from the Paladin world — follows "The Tree"*

---

He found him on the third day, in a hollow between two ridges where a stream ran fast and cold.

The master was sitting with his back against a boulder, facing north, which Edric thought was probably deliberate — facing away from everything behind him. He looked smaller than he had in the village. Not reduced by illness, exactly. More like the armor of his function had been set aside, and what remained was just an old man in a coat, watching water move.

He turned when he heard Edric's horse.

For a moment, nothing. Then: "You should not have come."

"I know," Edric said.

He dismounted. He unpacked the cloth-wrapped fruit from the supply bag. He held it out.

The master looked at it for a long time without taking it. "How long ago did you pick it?"

"Sixty-two hours."

"That leaves five days."

"Yes."

The master looked back at the stream. "I went north to prevent harm."

"I know that too," Edric said. "I didn't know it when I left. I understood it on the way back from the tree." He paused. "I was angry with you for leaving. I thought it was cowardice. I was wrong."

The master said nothing for a moment. Then: "What changed your understanding?"

"The direction." Edric kept holding the fruit out. "East would have put you in the forest during the change. You chose wilderness. You chose the place with the fewest people."

"I chose the place where I could do the least damage," the master said. There was no pride in it. Just precision. "That is not the same as heroism."

"No," Edric agreed. "It's just correct."

Another silence. The stream went on.

"If I take that fruit," the master said slowly, "I am accepting that I deserve to be cured."

Edric had not expected this. He thought about it for a moment — the real question inside the statement, not the argument he'd been preparing. "Does it work that way? Does it only cure people who deserve it?"

"No. The accounts suggest it cures indiscriminately."

"Then whether you deserve it isn't part of the mechanism."

The master looked at him then — really looked at him, in the way he occasionally did during training when Edric had done something unexpected. Not approval, exactly. Assessment. "You have been thinking."

"I had three days."

The master reached out and took the fruit.

---

He ate slowly, which Edric had not expected. He had imagined urgency. Instead the master ate as he did most things — carefully, attending to it. When he was finished he set the cloth in his lap and sat quietly for a while.

"How many were there?" he asked finally. "On the tree."

"Three. I took two."

The master looked at him. "Two."

"Yes."

"Why?"

Edric had been waiting for this question for three days and he still did not have a satisfying answer. "I don't know. I stood there with one in my hand and it seemed wrong. So I took another."

The master was quiet for a moment. "When did you realize you'd need to find me?"

"Immediately. That was never the question."

"The second fruit was also not a question," the master said. "You took it before you knew why."

"Yes."

The master folded the cloth precisely, into thirds. A thing he had done a thousand times. "A paladin acts within incomplete information. The training teaches you to recognize when the information is sufficient and when it is not, and to proceed correctly in both cases." He tucked the cloth into his coat. "You did not have enough information to know why you needed the second fruit. You had enough information to know that you should take it."

"I'm not a paladin," Edric said.

"No." The master stood, slowly, testing his own weight. He seemed to be assessing something internal. "You are not. Not yet." He picked up his pack. "The village needs us to return by tomorrow evening."

"Your horse—"

"I left it three hours west, with a farmer. I was not thoughtless." He looked at Edric steadily. "The second fruit. Keep it wrapped. Keep it cool."

"What is it for?"

"I don't know yet." He began walking west, toward where the horse was. "You took it before you knew why. That was the correct decision. We will find out the why when we need to."
