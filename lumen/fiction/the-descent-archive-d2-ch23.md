## CHAPTER TWENTY-THREE: WHAT ABARA KNOWS — 1–2 DECEMBER 2041

*This chapter has no SOAR designation.*
*Editor's note: In twenty-two days, Abara Osei filed fourteen reports. Field notes, calibration studies, descent records, formal acknowledgments. Every report was careful, precise, complete.*
*After December 1st, she stopped.*
*This chapter is what she said instead.*

---

**01 December 2041 | 19:30**

Abara and Asad sat at the kitchen table. The photographs from Q-1 were in front of them.

Asad had looked at them for twenty minutes. He had not said anything.

Abara had made coffee. She had not touched hers.

Asad said: "Walk me through the lower register."

Abara: "It's in my handwriting."

Asad: "I know. What does it say?"

Abara: "I already filed it. SOAR-0034."

Asad: "I know. Tell me what it says."

A pause.

Abara: "It says I'm in both places at once. The field station and the chamber. The career of twenty-two years and this. It says the instrument is what the measurement does. It says she has always been one thing."

Asad: "Do you believe that?"

Long pause.

Abara: "I was standing in front of my handwriting in a chamber I hadn't been to. The sentence was accurate. I was in both places at once. I was in the field station — I was thinking about the field station, about leaving, about you waiting at the surface — and I was in Q-1. Both completely. Not split. Both."

Asad: "That's constitutive. You were in both places because —"

Abara: "Yes. I was in both places because the Archive constitutes the structure, and the structure was constituting me, and the Archive has records of me here and there and the structure used them both. Yes. I know the hypothesis. I wrote the acknowledgment."

Asad waited.

Abara: "But knowing the mechanism doesn't change what happened. I was in both places. The mechanism is the description. The experience is the thing."

---

**19:55**

Asad said: "What do you know that you haven't filed?"

A long pause.

Abara: "I know something I can't put in the archive."

Asad: "Why not?"

Abara: "Not because I don't want to share it. Because I don't know what writing it would do."

Asad: "Say it here. Not for the archive. Between us."

Abara looked at her coffee. She picked it up and put it down without drinking.

Abara: "The archive is constitutive. We know that. Every document we've filed has contributed to what the structure is now. The DUOs appeared because the archive constituted them, and they constituted the events they described, and the events constituted the archive further."

Asad: "Yes."

Abara: "Milo wrote from inside. He wrote what he found in the shaft and the blank page is still blank. He wrote what I would do and he was right. He wrote that Q-1 is the aperture — the place before divergence."

Asad: "Yes."

Abara: "I was in the aperture. I was not yet diverged. Both things still true simultaneously."

A long pause.

Asad: "And now?"

Abara: "And now I'm back. And the divergence has happened. I left the aperture and came back to this side. The two places are separated now. I'm here."

Another pause.

Asad: "What do you know that you can't file?"

Abara: "If I file it, it will be true in the archive. I don't know if it's already true outside the archive."

Asad: "Tell me anyway. Between us."

Abara: "Milo isn't lost. He didn't fall down the shaft. He went down the shaft because the shaft leads somewhere, and the somewhere is the further he needed to go. He's in the structure — all the way in. The structure is constituted by the archive and the archive is still here and Milo is still part of what the archive is constituting."

Asad: "He's alive."

Abara: "He's inside. Whether that's the same as alive — I don't know. He's in a place where the two-places-at-once doesn't need to resolve. The aperture doesn't close for him. For us, we left the aperture. We're on this side. Milo found a way to stay in it."

Long silence.

Asad: "You know this."

Abara: "I know it the way Asad knew the chamber contents at the passage entrance. The way the DUOs knew what would happen. It's the same kind of knowing. It's reliable and I can't defend it."

---

**20:40**

Asad said: "File it."

Abara: "No."

Asad: "Abara —"

Abara: "If I file it, the archive constitutes it. The archive constitutes the structure. The structure responds. If I write 'Milo is in the aperture, he's all the way inside, the structure is keeping him' — that's a constitutive act. I don't know what it constitutes."

Asad: "The archive already has —"

Abara: "The archive has measurements and observations and hypotheses. It has my acknowledgment that the hypothesis is irrefutable. It does not have a direct statement of the structure's current state with respect to a missing person."

Asad: "I filed the missing-person report."

Abara: "You filed that he's missing. You didn't file what the structure is doing with him."

A long pause.

Asad: "What would happen if you filed it?"

Abara: "I don't know. That's why I'm not filing it."

She pushed her chair back.

Abara: "There is a kind of knowledge that, if written, becomes the thing it describes. There is a kind of knowledge that, if kept inside, is still true but doesn't propagate. I'm keeping this inside."

Asad: "That's not science."

Abara: "No. It's not. I'm choosing to not do science with this particular thing. I'm twenty-two years of careful science and I'm choosing, once, to not put something in the archive because I think putting it in the archive would change it."

She stood up.

Abara: "That's what I know that I'm not filing. What the archive constitutes becomes real. What I keep out of the archive stays possible."

---

**02 December 2041 | 08:00**

Asad found a note from Abara on the kitchen table:

*Khalid —*

*I went outside this morning to stand at the shaft entry. It's the first time I've been outside in four days. The air is cold.*

*I thought about filing last night. I wrote three sentences, once, to see what they felt like. Then I deleted them.*

*What they felt like: like closing something that was open. I don't know if that's the right thing to do. I know I'm not ready to do it.*

*The archive is growing. We should discuss what we do about that.*

*—A.*

*I'm going to ask her.*
