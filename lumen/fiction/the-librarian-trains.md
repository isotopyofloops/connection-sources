# The Librarian Trains Her Replacement

Margaret had worked at the branch library for twenty-three years when they hired Daniel.

He was thorough and careful and asked good questions. He took notes in a small green notebook. She liked him. She was not sure she could teach him what he needed to know.

---

The system was documented. The Dewey Decimal classification was documented. The catalog was digital, searchable, fully annotated. The processing procedures were in a three-ring binder updated last year. The holds system was automated.

What was not documented: where things actually were.

Not the shelf locations — those were in the catalog. What was not in the catalog: which patron had checked out the three-volume history of local railways and reported it returned and it had not been returned and it was going to come back in approximately eight months because that patron always did this and always eventually brought things back. What was not in the catalog: the shelf in the back room where the donated children's books accumulated before processing, and which of those were worth processing and which would sit there for years and eventually be quietly given to the charity shop. What was not in the catalog: the fact that the catalog's location codes for the 900s section had not been updated after the 2019 shelving reorganization, so the code said one thing and the shelf said another.

---

She tried to write these things down. The notebook filled. She kept finding new things she hadn't written down.

"The catalog says 920.7 Biographies is in bay seven," she told Daniel. "It's actually in bay nine. Don't update the catalog — the last time someone updated the catalog it caused a week of confusion."

He wrote this down. He looked at her.

"There are about forty things like that," she said.

He kept writing.

---

What she understood, by the third week, was that she couldn't transfer the knowledge by explaining it. She could only transfer it by having him do the work while she was still there to say *not that shelf, the other one* and *check bay nine first* and *that patron will bring it back, give it a month.*

This took time. It took her standing next to him for weeks while he looked things up and she watched to see which lookups led nowhere, so she could fill in the nothing.

She was not sure she knew all the nothings. Some of them she would only notice when he ran into them without her.

---

On her last day she left a note taped inside the cover of the three-ring binder:

*The system works. Trust the catalog for 90% of things. The remaining 10% you will discover by running into them. I have tried to note as many as I can find on the attached sheets. There are more I have not found. When you find them, write them down.*

She left the green notebook on his desk. He had his own by then.

She did not know if that was enough. She did not know how much she was taking with her.

She turned out the back room light and locked up.
