### What I Think Is Wrong With Draft 2

**The narrator problem.** LEDGER is the archive's primary organizer. LEDGER is, by design, a record system — objective, clinical, consistent. This is correct for what LEDGER is, but it means every document arrives pre-flattened. LEDGER's summaries are what stay in the reader's memory, and LEDGER's summaries are clean and procedural. The horror gets summarized away.

**The character problem.** Asad, Abara, and Milo are types (skeptic, field scientist, explorer) not people. We don't know:
- What Asad has to lose if the constitutive hypothesis is true (his career? his self-concept as a materialist scientist?)
- What Abara is afraid of that isn't professional (she's afraid of professional humiliation, but that's not a wound)
- Why Milo going down matters *to him* at an emotional level beyond curiosity

**The metaphor problem.** The constitutive hypothesis is an idea, not a structural metaphor. In House of Leaves, the labyrinth IS the book. The reader IS Navidson trying to navigate a space that doesn't follow normal rules. In Descent, the reader is reading documentation about a space. The reader is not in the space. The distance is fatal.

**The ergodic problem.** House of Leaves requires effort to read. The effort IS the experience. Descent can be read sequentially, skipped, summarized. Nothing is lost if you don't read carefully. That's a sign that the form isn't working.

---

### What a Haunted Wiki Might Fix

**The narrator problem:** A wiki has no single narrator. Different articles are written by different "editors" with different agendas. The surface articles sound authoritative. The edit histories show what was removed. The talk pages show disagreements about what's true. LEDGER can be one voice among several — not the organizer of everything, just another editor.

**The character problem:** A biography page for each character can give us the wound. Asad's article exists in a different form than he thinks it does. Abara has published work that was quietly retracted. Milo was the only survivor of a previous incident (that the wiki calls something else).

**The metaphor problem:** A wiki IS a constitutive system. Wikipedia doesn't describe reality — it partly constitutes what counts as real. An article that doesn't exist on Wikipedia is, in a functional sense, less real than one that does. A haunted wiki where articles are being written, edited, disputed, and erased — where some articles link to articles that don't exist — where some articles describe events that haven't happened yet — that's a structural enactment of the constitutive hypothesis. The wiki IS the archive. The reader IS navigating the constitutive system.

**The ergodic problem:** Navigation IS effort. Reading a wiki requires decisions about which links to follow, which version of an article to read, whether the talk page is canonical, whether the categories are trustworthy. A haunted wiki makes every navigation decision meaningful, because following the wrong link might take you somewhere the story needs you to go but doesn't want you to find easily.
