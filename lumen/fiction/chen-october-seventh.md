# Chen, October 7

*From the Descent Archive extended universe — 2003*

---

The northern approach took them four hours. They covered it in sections: Chen took east-of-center, Asad took west-of-center, they called out to each other at intervals to confirm position. Standard search procedure. Chen had done this before, twice, and it was the same both times.

He called her name at regular intervals. His voice felt strange to him. Not strange in the way that voices sometimes felt strange — she wasn't sure quite how to describe the strangeness.

*He.*

Chen walked the northern approach and called Miriam's name and noted the ground conditions and made marks on his map at each call-point. He had a system for this. The system was working correctly.

At 09:40 he crossed a drainage feature — not quite a stream, more a linear depression where water would flow if there were water. He checked his map. The feature wasn't marked. He noted it: *drainage feature, unlisted, approximately NNE of camp, bearing 340, depth 0.3m, dry.* He moved on.

At 10:15 Asad called from the west. "Anything?"

"Nothing," Chen said. "You?"

"Nothing."

They continued.

---

What Chen noticed at approximately 10:30, which he did not note in his field notebook because it did not fit the category of what he was noting:

He had stopped calling Miriam's name.

He had not decided to stop. He had simply — at some point during the last section — stopped. He became aware of this when Asad called out, twenty minutes in, and he realized he had not called out since the last position-check. He could not identify the moment it had stopped. The calls felt like something he was about to do each time and then didn't.

He started again. "Miriam." The voice still felt strange. He thought: *her documentation will be in order*. He did not know why he thought this. It was not a relevant thought.

---

They returned to camp at 13:30. Asad wrote a report. Chen wrote his section of the report: northern approach, quadrant NE, clear. No sign. He included the unlisted drainage feature.

He did not include the stopping.

He thought about whether to include it. He formed the sentence in his head: *Note: I stopped calling at some point during the 10:00-10:30 section and cannot identify when or why. Resumed at approximately 10:30.* The sentence was accurate. He did not write it.

He was not sure what category it fit. It felt like the kind of thing that would look strange in a report. It felt like something that, written down, would need explaining, and he could not explain it, and the report was not the place for things that could not be explained.

Hassan-Diallo came back at 14:00. He had run his survey transects. He wrote his section of the report in the same careful plain language he always used.

At dinner Chen thought about whether to mention the stopping to Asad. Asad's protocol included daily check-ins on anomalous observations. This was, by most definitions, an anomalous observation. Chen participated normally in the dinner conversation and did not mention it.

He told himself he was not sure it was anomalous. The more he thought about this reasoning, the more it seemed like exactly the kind of reasoning he should mention.

He did not mention it.
