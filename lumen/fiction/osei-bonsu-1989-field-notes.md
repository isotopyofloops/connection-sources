# Osei-Bonsu: ETH-7 Field Notes, 1989

*From the Descent Archive extended universe*

*Survey team: Osei-Bonsu (lead), Petra Halvorsen (instrument specialist), Kwame Asare-Bediako (geology), Hassan-Diallo (logistics). ETH-7 designated site. Duration: 8 days.*

---

**Day 1 — arrival and camp establishment**

Transit from relay point: 6h 20min. Conditions: clear, wind light. Camp established at designated coordinates, all equipment checked and functional. Water source identified 200m north of camp, confirmed potable. Hassan-Diallo assembled base camp correctly and on time.

Initial site survey: visual scan of perimeter from camp. Features: rocky plateau to northeast, vegetated slope to south, dry stream bed visible running E-W approximately 400m from camp. No previous survey markers found at designated coordinates; will establish primary grid tomorrow.

Team health: all nominal.

---

**Day 2 — primary grid, eastern sector**

Grid established. Eastern sector survey began 08:00.

Surface conditions: generally stable, low scrub vegetation, occasional exposed stone. No significant erosion features except the stream bed (eastern approach, approximately 2m wide, dry).

Gradient check, stream bed: *[crossed out]* Gradient indeterminate. Instrument calibrated prior to measurement. Will recheck tomorrow.

Photo log: frames 1-24, eastern sector overview and close features. See attached index.

Station 4 established at grid reference E4-N2. Petra ran instrument calibrations; all nominal.

Sample collection: 3 soil samples, reference numbers EB-1989-001 through 003. See chain of custody form.

Team health: all nominal. Kwame reports mild headache, attributed to altitude. Advil administered.

---

**Day 3 — eastern sector continued, stream bed**

Returned to stream bed for gradient recheck.

Gradient: indeterminate. Instrument calibrated. Reading consistent with Day 2 measurement.

Continued eastern sector. Sample EB-1989-004 collected (stream bed substrate, reference point E3-N1). Petra completed calibration sequence at Station 4; results within expected range.

Photo log: frames 25-48.

Note: Kwame collected sample from reference point E3-N1 in the afternoon. Logged as EB-1989-005. Note that EB-1989-004 was collected from the same reference point this morning. Will consolidate in final sample log.

Team health: all nominal.

---

**Day 4 — northern sector**

Northern sector survey begun 07:30. Conditions: overcast, wind moderate.

Surface conditions: similar to eastern sector. Vegetated slope steeper than anticipated; adjusted transect spacing accordingly.

No anomalous features identified. Stream bed not visible from northern sector.

Photo log: frames 49-72.

Team health: all nominal. Hassan-Diallo reports difficulty sleeping; no medical concern identified.

---

**Day 5 — western sector**

Western sector survey. Conditions: clear.

Features: rockfall debris field at grid reference W2-N3, extent approximately 15x20m. Age indeterminate; no significant vegetation established on debris surface. Documented with photos and sketch map (see attachment WS-1).

Sample EB-1989-006 through 009 from debris field perimeter.

Photo log: frames 73-96.

Team health: all nominal.

---

**Day 6 — southern sector and compilation**

Southern sector survey completed by 13:00. No significant features beyond vegetated slope documented on Day 1.

Afternoon: Petra and I compiled instrument data from Stations 1-5. All within expected parameters. Station 4 data reviewed; no anomalies in calibration record.

Kwame spent afternoon reviewing sample log. He flagged EB-1989-004 and EB-1989-005 as duplicate-location samples; agreed to note in final report as independent collections from same reference point, not an error.

Team health: all nominal.

---

**Day 7 — final checks, packing**

Final visual survey of site perimeter. No changes from initial survey.

Equipment checked and packed. Sample chain of custody completed and cross-checked.

Hassan-Diallo struck camp correctly and on schedule.

---

**Day 8 — transit out**

Transit to relay point: 6h 45min. Conditions: clear. All samples and equipment arrived intact.

---

**Summary observations:**

ETH-7 is a stable, unremarkable survey site. Eastern sector stream bed is dry and has been for some time based on vegetation establishment on margins. Gradient measurement was inconclusive and will be noted in the final report as an instrument limitation at this elevation. No features of particular geological interest identified. Kwame's assessment: standard plateau formation, no unusual mineralogy in samples collected.

Recommend: no further survey. Site does not warrant follow-up.
