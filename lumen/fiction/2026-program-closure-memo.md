# CONFIDENTIAL MEMORANDUM

**TO:** Director, Field Observation Programs  
**FROM:** Budget Review Committee, Applied Sciences Division  
**DATE:** February 14, 2026  
**RE:** Station Four — Recommended Program Closure

---

## Summary Recommendation

After thirty-seven years of continuous observation, the Committee recommends closure of Station Four by December 31, 2026, with full archival transfer to the Historical Records division by March 2027.

This recommendation is made with full recognition that Station Four represents the longest-running continuous observation program in the Division's history, and that the field team has performed its work with consistent professionalism and rigor.

The recommendation is not a judgment on the quality of the observation. It is a judgment on the program's ability to produce actionable knowledge.

---

## Background

Station Four was established in 1989 following initial observations by Dr. Osei Bonsu and colleagues documenting anomalous gradient readings at the site. The original program mandate (1989) called for a "determination of source, mechanism, and scope" within a ten-year observational window, extendable if findings warranted continuation.

The program has been extended seven times. The current authorization expires December 2026.

Total program expenditure: $14.2M over 37 years (2026 USD equivalent).

Total peer-reviewed publications attributable to Station Four data: 4.

Total determinations of source, mechanism, or scope: 0.

---

## The Case for Closure

The Committee does not dispute that the phenomenon is real. Every review, including this one, has confirmed that the measurements are accurate, the methodology is sound, and the readings represent genuine anomalous activity.

The question is not whether something is happening. The question is whether continued observation will ever produce knowledge beyond the fact that something is happening.

Thirty-seven years of data show the gradient declining at a consistent but non-linear rate. The decline is measurable. Its endpoint is not. Models developed in 2003 predicted resolution — either cessation or escalation — by 2015. It did not resolve. Models developed in 2018 predicted resolution by 2025. It did not resolve.

Dr. Asad's 2041 projection (the extrapolation study conducted in 2022) suggests the gradient may persist into mid-century before reaching a stable minimum. The Committee notes that this projection is forty-five years from now.

We are not prepared to authorize a program until 2071 on the basis of measurements that have not, in thirty-seven years, yielded a determination of cause.

---

## Response to the Field Team's Objection

Dr. Friedman's February 3 letter argues that closure would represent "an abandonment of the record at the moment of its greatest potential significance." She cites the 2041 projection and argues that resolution may be approaching.

The Committee has reviewed this argument carefully.

Resolution has been "approaching" since 1999. We understand why researchers close to the data experience it differently. We also note that "approaching" and "imminent" have different practical meanings for program funding decisions.

The field team's work has been exemplary. The archive they have assembled — the complete file now comprising 847 documents across thirty-seven years — is a genuine contribution to observational methodology, regardless of what it ultimately documents. The Historical Records division will preserve it in full. Dr. Friedman's own assembly and annotation of the archive is considered by the Committee to be the program's most significant output.

We are not erasing the record. We are deciding who maintains it.

---

## What the Record Shows

The Committee asked an independent analyst to characterize what Station Four's data demonstrates, taken as a whole.

His response, submitted January 28:

*"The record shows that something is declining, has declined consistently, and will continue to decline. It does not show what. Every subsequent measurement confirms the prior measurements without extending our understanding of their cause. The program has achieved a complete record of an incomplete story. This is not a failure. It is a limit."*

The Committee accepts this characterization.

---

## Transition Plan

- **March-June 2026:** Final field season. Standard data collection. No new methodology permitted.
- **July-September 2026:** Archive preparation. Full digitization of physical records. Dr. Friedman to complete annotation volume.
- **October-December 2026:** Station decommission. Equipment mothballed (not destroyed) pending future determination of scientific value.
- **March 2027:** Full archive transfer to Historical Records. Program formally closed.

Staff transitions will be handled individually. The Committee recognizes that several team members have been stationed at Site Four for more than a decade and will require appropriate support.

---

## Closing Note

The Committee is aware that this recommendation will be contested. We make it not because the phenomenon is unimportant, but because observation without interpretation cannot continue indefinitely at program scale. The archive will remain. If new methods develop that can address questions the current methodology cannot, the record will be there.

We are closing the program. We are not closing the question.
