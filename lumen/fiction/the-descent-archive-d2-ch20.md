**[SECOND DESCENT: November 29th]**

*Milo's notes from his second descent. More detailed than the transmissions — the transmissions were compressed for relay; these are the full notes. The descent through known chambers. M-1. The cube. His reaction to the single horizontal line at midpoint height, which he writes about for two pages: "A line at the midpoint. Not a symbol. Not a mark. A division. The chamber is divided at its center. I don't know what the division means. I know it means something."*

*The south passage. N-1. The covered walls. He spent forty minutes in N-1 before approaching the east passage, photographing every surface, and his notes describe the symbol-coverage as "complete — there is no surface in N-1 that doesn't carry meaning, and I can almost read it, I can almost read it, I am one word away from reading it."*

*He stood at the east passage for twelve minutes before entering. His notes from those twelve minutes: his estimate (22m × 28m × 18m), his reasoning for the 18m height prediction ("the cube reversed the expansion; M-1 was 22m horizontal, the expansion has passed through the fix-point and is now contracting; the height is next"), and one more line: "I know what's in here. I don't know how I know. It's the same knowing as before. It is reliable."*

*He entered.*

*These entries are consistent with the Archive.*

**[INSIDE THE EAST PASSAGE CHAMBER: November 29th, 10:10]**

*The entry is dated November 29th, 10:10. Seven minutes after "Beginning return."*

*Milo entered the chamber. The dimensions matched his estimate within his measurement tolerance: 22.4m × 28.1m × 18.2m.*

*The interior surface coverage was partial: north wall and floor only. The south wall was smooth.*

*The vertical shaft was in the center of the floor: 0.5m diameter, smooth sides descending beyond visible depth.*

*Milo's note at 10:10: "I've been standing here for two minutes. I sent 'Beginning return' before I entered because I knew I might not mean it when I got here. I was right."*

*10:13: "The shaft is real. The shaft goes further down. I can't see the bottom. I can't hear the bottom. I dropped a stone and counted. I got to twenty and stopped. I don't know what's at the bottom."*

*10:22: "I've decided. This is my last note in this notebook. When Abara reads this, she'll know I went further. I'm leaving my pack at the east wall of N-1. She'll find it. I want her to have the notebook.*

*"I understand now what the structure is doing. I can almost say it. I can say: it is not a geological structure. It is not a record. It is something that has been waiting to be understood, and it arranged for people who could understand it to come here and bring the means of understanding with them. We brought the Archive. The Archive is part of it now. The Archive is what allowed the understanding to begin.*

*"I'm going down.*

*"I'm leaving a blank page here. Then I'll tell you what I find."*

**SOAR-0031 | ABARA'S RECORD | 01 December 2041 | 13:22**

I am reading Milo's notebook.

I want to record what is happening as it happens, because I don't trust my memory of this moment to stay accurate.

At 12:30, Asad handed me the notebook. He said: "I'll be here." He went to the window. I started reading.

The early entries: familiar. The first descent, the sojourn, the preparation. I know all of this. Milo's handwriting.

Then: November 29th, 10:10. Seven minutes after "Beginning return." He went in. He saw the shaft. He knew.

His last note in the sojourn entries: "I'm going down. I'm leaving a blank page here. Then I'll tell you what I find."

The blank page.

And then: December 1st entries.

The first entry: "Abara is getting ready. She's going to descend at 11:00."

I read it once. I read it again.

He was right: I had been getting ready. I had scheduled myself for a descent at 11:00. I changed my mind twice before UNIT-14 returned. I changed my mind twice after.

The second entry: "She's still at the field station. She read the notebook." It is 11:45. I am still at the field station. I have read the notebook.

The third entry: "Asad gave Abara the notebook at 12:30." He gave it to me at 12:30.

The fourth entry: "She is reading this sentence. Right now."

I stopped.

I am reading this sentence right now. I am sitting in the field station kitchen with Milo's notebook open on the table and I am reading a sentence in his handwriting that says I am reading it right now.

I sat with this for a long time. I'm writing now because I need to write.

The fifth entry: "She is standing at the east wall. She has not decided yet."

The time stamp says 14:00.

It is 13:22. I have thirty-eight minutes in which to decide.

*—Abara Osei | 01 December 2041 | 13:22*

**SOAR-0032 | ASAD'S RECORD | 01 December 2041 | 13:35**

Abara showed me the December entries at 12:45. I've read them. I'm writing this while she's in the kitchen.

December 1st, 14:00: "She is standing at the east wall. She has not decided yet."

Abara said: "What does 'the east wall' mean?"

The east wall of N-1 is where Milo left his pack. The east wall of N-1 is the wall with the east passage in it.

If Abara is standing at the east wall of N-1 at 14:00, she has descended. She is in the structure. She is at the passage.

If the entry is constitutive — if Milo's prediction of her standing at the east wall is what constitutes her standing there — then the entry is an act. Not a description. A making.

But "she has not decided yet" is also in the entry.

She is at the wall. She hasn't decided.

The entry holds two things at once: she is there and she hasn't decided. Meaning: she descends. She reaches the wall. She stands at the entrance to the east passage. She doesn't know yet whether she will go through.

I don't know if that is her decision to make or if the decision has already been constituted.

I don't know if the distinction matters.

I'm going to go back to the kitchen now. I'm going to be there when she decides.

*—Khalid Asad | 01 December 2041 | 13:35*
