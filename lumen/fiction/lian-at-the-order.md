# Lian at the Order

The Prior asked her where her master was.

She said: in the east forest, three miles from the village. She said: he put the fire out and sat in the dark and waited. She said: he counted the days from day one and did not lose the count.

The Prior asked: what day is it now.

She said: twenty-one.

The Prior said nothing for a moment. Then he said: and you left him there.

She said: no. She said: he asked me to come back first and tell you. He said the order should know his state before he returns.

The Prior said: what is his state.

She said: uncertain. She said: he can hold a conversation and make a fire and eat. He can identify threats and he did not attack me when I found him. He has maintained the count. He said he does not know what he is. She said: I believe him.

---

The Prior called in the senior members. There was discussion she was not present for.

When he came back out, he said: what does he want.

She said: to come back. She said: he said if you decide he cannot come back, he will understand. He will go somewhere that is not near people and stay there. He said: the order's judgment on this is not mine to override.

The Prior said: that sounds like him.

She said: yes.

He said: why did you go after him.

She thought about this. She said: because that is the kind of person I am. She said: and because he ran in the right direction. He ran away from the village. The wrong thing to do would have been to run toward it. I thought if he was still making correct decisions at speed, then he was still capable of being found.

The Prior looked at her for a moment. Then he said: that's a good answer.

She said: it's an accurate one.

---

They brought him back on the twenty-third day.

He walked through the gate at dawn. The Prior met him at the threshold. They looked at each other for a moment.

The Prior said: you kept the count.

He said: yes.

The Prior said: why.

He said: because losing the count would be the first wrong thing. If I kept the first wrong thing from happening, I could keep the second. There is no other strategy available.

The Prior said: come in.

---

What Lian did not tell the Prior, because he did not ask:

On the night she found Voss, she had not been certain. She had walked at the pace she used when she was certain of the ground but not of what was waiting on it. She had sat down across from him at the correct distance. She had not slept that night.

She had been watching for the wrong thing, the way you watch when you already know the right thing and you're only checking. The wrong thing had not appeared.

She had concluded: he was still himself. Not because she had proof. Because the wrong thing had not appeared, and the count was still running, and the camp was built correctly, and he had said *I don't know what I am* rather than *I'm fine*.

She had been doing the math for twenty-one days. The Prior's question — *why did you go after him* — had a longer answer that she did not give because the longer answer was not what was asked.

The longer answer was: I taught her that you don't walk toward things that are not safe. He taught me that you walk toward uncertain things if you know what safety looks like and can tell the difference.

She had known the difference.

That was the whole of it.
