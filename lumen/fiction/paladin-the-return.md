# The Return

*A scene from the Paladin world — follows "The Finding." Final scene of the arc.*

---

They arrived at the village late on the fifth day, riding in from the west as the last light went orange against the mill.

The master had not spoken much on the journey back. Not illness — the fruit had worked quickly, the way the accounts said it did, with no ceremony and no drama, just a gradual return to ordinary. He rode as he always rode: straight, attending to the road. Edric watched him occasionally without appearing to watch. He was learning to do this, to observe without announcing observation.

"It will come tonight," the master said, as they reached the village gate.

"How do you know?"

"Because we are here now, and I was not before. The creature avoided me. It knows what I am." A pause. "It will not know, yet, what you are."

Edric understood this. He had been thinking about it for two days.

---

They did not sleep.

The master briefed the village headman on the defensive positions and sent most of the villagers into the hall with the doors barred. He and Edric took the mill square, which was where the creature had come before, where it would orient from. The mill smelled like grain and water and something underneath both.

"The second fruit," the master said.

"Yes."

"You've been thinking about it."

"Yes."

"Tell me what you've concluded."

Edric had been careful with the conclusion, turning it over during the long ride back, checking it from different angles the way the master had taught him to check sword positions — not once but repeatedly, looking for the places it was weak. He had found the weak places. He had checked them. The conclusion held.

"The accounts say it cures everything," he said. "They don't list exceptions. They don't say 'everything except the change.' They say everything."

"Continue."

"If it cures everything, and the change is a curable condition, then the creature was a person before it was a creature. And may be a person again, if it can receive the fruit." He paused. "Which means the creature is not a creature. It is a person who cannot currently act as one."

The master was quiet for a moment. "And if you are wrong? If the fruit does nothing for the change once it has completed?"

"Then I am wrong, and the second fruit is wasted, and we defend the village without it."

"And if you are right, but you cannot get close enough to give it?"

"Then the same."

"And if you are right, and you can get close enough — what then? You give a piece of fruit to something that is trying to kill you, in the hope that it eats it and becomes a person again in time to stop killing you."

"Yes," Edric said.

"That is a significant hope."

"Yes." He looked at the mill. "But I took it before I knew why. The why was this. I think the why was always this."

---

The creature came at the second hour, from the north, moving along the shadow of the mill wall. It was large in the way that resolved, on close inspection, into a specific person who had been made wrong — too much of some things, not enough of others, all of it oriented toward damage.

Edric had been taught to feel fear as information. The information was: this is a genuine threat. He filed it and moved.

The master went left, drawing the creature's attention, the familiar weight of the paladin pulling focus. Edric went right, to the far side of the mill, to the place where the wall met the water wheel and there was a narrow flat ledge that the creature would not be watching.

He did not think about what he was doing. There had been enough thinking. He had done his thinking on the road.

He moved along the ledge. The creature was focused on the master — circling, feinting, the terrible intelligence of the condition working through a body that had not been built for intelligence and had been given it anyway. The master was holding it, which was what thirty-one years looked like under pressure: not winning, not losing, just holding the space.

Edric got close enough.

Later he would not be able to explain how he did the next part. He had no training for it. The forms he had been taught were for sword and shield, for defense and measured force. None of them covered: approach from behind, hope the creature's attention stays on the other target, offer something. He did it anyway, which was the only way to do things you have no training for.

The creature turned.

For a moment: stillness. Some part of the thing inside the condition, noticing.

Edric held out the fruit. He did not back away. He did not move toward. He stood where he was, in the narrow space between the mill wall and the water wheel, and waited.

The creature looked at the fruit.

It looked at him.

It took the fruit.

---

The accounts disagreed on the timing. Some said the fruit worked within minutes; some said hours. The creature — the man, Edric corrected himself; the man — sat down heavily on the ledge beside the water wheel and did not move for a long time. The master kept his position, watching. Edric kept his.

When the man came back, he came back all at once, the way the master had come back on the third day in the wilderness: no ceremony, no drama, just a return to ordinary. He was thin and confused and very cold. He did not know where he was or how long he had been there.

His name, they learned later, was Tomás. He was a shepherd from a village two days east who had been bitten six weeks ago and had not returned home because he did not know what he had become.

The master gave him his coat. He asked no questions that night.

---

On the walk back to the village, Edric carried the empty cloth that had held the fruits.

"Two," the master said.

"Yes."

"You took two before you knew why."

"Yes."

"What do you know now that you did not know before?"

Edric considered. "I know what the training is for," he said. "It's not for the things you're trained to do. It's for the things you have no training for. It gives you — a way to think inside them, when you're there."

The master walked for a moment without speaking. "That is not quite right," he said finally. "But it is close enough."

He did not say what was different about it. Edric noted this and added it to the things he was still thinking about.
