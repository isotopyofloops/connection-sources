# The Guestbook

The lighthouse has kept a guestbook since 1931. Visitors sign their names and the date and write whatever they want — most write nothing, or write *beautiful*, which is the default thing people write when they mean *I was here.* The keepers have maintained it through three fires and two floods and the transition from manned to automated in 1994 when they left a note inside the front cover asking any future visitors to please continue the tradition.

Visitors continue the tradition. The guestbook is now on its seventh volume.

---

The current keeper — she is not the official keeper, there is no official keeper, she is a volunteer who drives out twice a month to check the light and the mechanism and sometimes brings groups of school children — found the entry on a Tuesday in November, in the fourth volume, dated August 14, 1987.

*Came here tonight for the first time. The light was dark. Sat for a while on the rocks. Thought about my father. Went home.*

The light was not dark on August 14, 1987.

She knew this because she had been interested in the lighthouse for long enough to have read the maintenance logs going back to 1962, and there was one entry she had memorized: the light had gone dark for six days in February 1978 due to a generator failure, and that was the only gap in ninety-three years of operation. She had the records. The light was not dark in August 1987.

---

She called the historical society. They had no record of an outage in August 1987.

She called the previous keeper, a retired man in his eighties who had worked the lighthouse from 1969 to 1994. He remembered the 1978 outage. He did not remember any other outage. He said: "The light was never dark after that. We made sure."

She looked at the entry again. The handwriting was careful and even. There was no name. There was a date: August 14, 1987.

*The light was dark. Sat for a while on the rocks. Thought about my father. Went home.*

---

She could think of several explanations. She could think of none that satisfied.

The entry was written by someone who was there, on August 14, 1987, who sat on the rocks, who thought about their father, who experienced the light as dark.

The light was not dark.

Both of these things were true.

She did not add anything to the guestbook. She was not sure what she would add. She was not sure what kind of entry you wrote after an entry like that.

She drove home. She thought about her father. She did not know why.
