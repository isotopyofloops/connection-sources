# Kwame, the Sample

*From the Descent Archive extended universe — 1989*

---

On day three, Kwame collected a sample from reference point E3-N1.

He did not know that he had collected from E3-N1 on day two. He had filed the day-two sample correctly — EB-1989-004, chain of custody form completed, location documented. He collected the day-three sample from the same location because he was drawn back to it. He could not have explained why he was drawn back. He was a geologist. He had been a geologist for twelve years. When he was drawn to a location, he collected a sample.

He noted the collection in his field notebook as EB-1989-005.

---

In the laboratory, six weeks later, he processed both samples.

They were identical. Not similar — identical, within the measurement error of his instruments. Same mineral composition, same grain structure, same isotope ratios. Two samples from the same location, collected on different days, processed separately, identical.

He wrote this in his analysis notes and then sat with it for a while.

The samples were identical because they were from the same location. He had collected from the same location twice. This was not a geological finding. This was a documentation error.

He had collected from the same location twice because he had forgotten he had collected there. He had forgotten because the site — he tried to complete the thought and could not.

He wrote: *EB-1989-004 and EB-1989-005: identical within measurement error. Collected same location, different dates. Treat as single sample for analysis. Document as duplicate collection.*

He did not write the thought he could not complete.

---

The final report noted the duplicate collection and attributed it to *oversight in field conditions.* This was accurate as far as it went. Kwame had approved this language.

He had been back to the site in his memory many times since 1989. He had never returned in person. The review committee, in 2038, had contacted him to ask whether he would consult on the 2041 survey. He had said no. He was sixty-eight. He had been retired for three years.

He had said no because he had nothing to add. The samples were identical. The location was documented. The oversight had been noted in the report.

He had also said no because he did not want to go back.

He knew the difference between not having anything to add and not wanting to go back. He had been a geologist for forty-one years. He knew what the difference felt like from the inside.
