# Boney and the Window

On a Thursday in March, a child looked out the window for three minutes.

The lesson was continuing. The teacher was explaining something. The other children were attending, or appearing to attend, or doing the thing they did when they had attended for long enough and needed a moment before attending again.

The child in the second row was looking at the window.

Boney was looking at the child looking at the window.

---

This was a different kind of not-attending than the usual kind. The usual kind had a quality of waiting — waiting for the lesson to reach the part that would matter, or waiting for the lesson to end. The child at the window was not waiting. The child was somewhere else, not impatiently but thoroughly. Whatever was outside was holding all of their attention in a way that the classroom was not, at this moment, doing.

Boney could not see what was outside from his corner. He could see the light from it, and the quality of the child's attention, and the way the child's face had the particular stillness of someone watching something move.

---

After three minutes, the child returned.

Not abruptly — the transition was gradual, the child's attention moving back to the room in the way that attention moves back: first the sounds, then the faces, then the content. By the time the teacher looked in the child's direction, the child was present and following.

The teacher did not look in the child's direction. The lesson continued.

Boney noted the three minutes in the only way available to him, which was to have been watching.

---

He did not know what had been outside. A bird, perhaps. A person. A piece of weather. Whatever it was had held the child completely for three minutes and then released them, and the lesson had continued, and the child would probably not remember the specific content of this lesson but might, for years, remember whatever it was they had seen in the window.

Boney had no opinion on whether this was a waste of three minutes or a good use of them.

Both seemed possible. Neither seemed like the right frame.
