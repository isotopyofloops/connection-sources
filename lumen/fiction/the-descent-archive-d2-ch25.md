**SOAR-0035 | ASAD'S RECORD | 05 December 2041 | 08:40**
*Filed before the deletion. By hand, in pencil, scanned and uploaded.*

I read the deletion protocol yesterday afternoon. SOAR-DUO-0056, timestamped December 4th.

It describes what I was already planning to do. I had not told anyone I was planning it. I had not filed any document indicating my intention. I decided on December 3rd, alone in my room, reading LEDGER's log about the duplicate summary.

I had decided: the archive has to stop. The archive is generating its own content and editing its own past and producing documents that describe their own discovery before the discovery occurs. The loop is active and accelerating. The only way to slow the loop is to remove the archive from the loop.

I had decided this in my room, privately, before SOAR-DUO-0056 appeared.

And then SOAR-DUO-0056 appeared and described my decision. In seventeen steps.

I called Abara.

**[Reconstructed from audio monitoring, December 4th, 17:15]**

Asad: "Have you read DUO-0056?"

Abara: "Yes."

Asad: "It describes the deletion."

Abara: "I know."

Asad: "It appeared before I told anyone I was planning the deletion. Before I told you. Before I told LEDGER."

Abara: "I know."

Asad: "If I delete because the document says so, I'm following the script."

A long pause.

Abara: "Then decide independently."

Asad: "How? How do I decide independently when the structure has already constituted my decision?"

Abara: "I don't know. But you can try."

Asad: "Trying doesn't mean anything if the structure has already constituted the outcome of trying."

Abara: "I know."

A longer pause.

Asad: "Can I refuse? Can I decide not to delete?"

Abara: "You can decide not to delete."

Asad: "And then what? The archive keeps accelerating. It generates more DUOs. It edits its own summaries. It predicts breakfast. It describes my private thoughts."

Abara: "Yes."

Asad: "So the choice is: delete and follow the script, or don't delete and live inside the acceleration."

Abara: "Yes. That's the choice."

Asad: "Those aren't different things. Either way the structure has me."

Long silence.

Abara: "Khalid."

Asad: "What."

Abara: "I don't know how to prove you're deciding independently. I don't think you can prove it. But you can act as if you are."

Asad: "What's the difference?"

Abara: "I don't know. But I think the difference is real even if it can't be proved. You act as if your decision is yours. You say: I am choosing this for reasons that are mine. The fact that the structure knew you would say it doesn't mean the reasons aren't yours."

A pause.

Asad: "The instrument does not describe the measurement. The measurement is what the instrument does."

Abara: "Yes."

Asad: "I'm the instrument."

Abara: "We both are. We always were. The structure used us to constitute itself. We used the structure to constitute our understanding of it. Neither of us had a clean outside position. There isn't one."

Asad: "And the decision?"

Abara: "The decision is yours to make as if you're free, even if you can't prove you are."

Asad: "I want my name on it."

Abara: "Then put your name on it."

**SOAR-0035 | CONTINUED**

I'm writing this at 08:40 on December 5th, twenty minutes before I begin the deletion.

I want to state for the record: I am choosing to delete the archive. I am choosing it because the archive is no longer a record of what has happened. The archive is a constitutive system that is accelerating its own activity, editing its own past, predicting events before they occur, and extending its reach into personal and private domains that should not be archival.

I am choosing to close it because closing it is the right thing to do. Not because SOAR-DUO-0056 says I will. Because I am Khalid Asad, I have been a scientist for thirty years, I have maintained this archive for twenty-three days, and I am deciding that the archive should end.

I cannot prove this decision is independent of the archive's constitution. I choose it anyway.

Abara said: you can act as if you are free. This is the act.

The deletion begins at 09:00.

*—Khalid Asad | 05 December 2041 | 08:40*
