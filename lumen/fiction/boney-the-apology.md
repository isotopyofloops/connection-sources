# Boney and the Apology

Boney had been watching from the corner for fifteen minutes, and he had noticed something.

"You're waiting," he said to Priya.

Priya was sitting very still at her desk, her pencil case open in front of her but no pencil in her hand. "I'm not waiting," she said.

"Your hands are waiting," said Boney. "That's almost the same thing."

Priya didn't say anything.

"Is it about Marcus?" said Boney.

Priya's shoulders went up a little. "He told everyone I got the answer wrong on purpose to be funny."

"Did you?"

"No," said Priya. "I just got it wrong."

Boney thought about this. He had gotten a lot of things wrong over the years, but he had the advantage of not remembering most of them. "What are you waiting for?" he said.

"For him to say sorry."

"Ah," said Boney.

The classroom was mostly empty. Lunch was almost over. Outside the window, someone was laughing at something.

"Sometimes," said Boney, "people don't know they need to say sorry yet. They're still deciding if what they did was a thing."

"He knows," said Priya.

"How do you know he knows?"

Priya thought about it. "Because he won't look at me."

"That's a good sign," said Boney. "That usually means someone is thinking about it."

"But what if he doesn't?"

Boney considered the question. His magic sword leaned against the wall behind him. He had never been totally clear on what it was magic *for*, but it had never been for making people apologize faster.

"Then you'll know," he said. "And you'll know what kind of person he is. And you'll know that you waited, and you were ready to hear it, and that matters even if it doesn't happen."

Priya picked up her pencil. "That doesn't really help."

"No," said Boney. "I know."

He looked out the window. "But the waiting is the honest part. The part where you haven't decided yet. Most things go wrong when people stop waiting too early."

The bell rang. Marcus came in from the hallway and put his bag on his chair. He looked at Priya. Then he looked away.

Priya looked at Boney.

Boney gave a small nod that was really no help at all, and was exactly what she needed.
