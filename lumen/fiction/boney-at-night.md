# Boney at Night

The classroom was empty.

The last child had left at half three. Mr. Smith had stayed until five, marking books and rearranging things and doing the small necessary work of a classroom that would need to be a classroom again in the morning. Then he had turned off the lights and locked the door.

Boney stood in the corner.

---

There is a theory about this: that Boney, standing in his corner in the dark, was not waiting. Was not watching. Was not thinking. Was not anything. Was a skeleton in a varsity jacket with a plastic cowboy hat and a wooden sword that someone had spray-painted gold, standing in a corner because that was where skeletons stood.

There is another theory: that something was happening in the corner, in the dark, that was not available for observation and therefore left no trace.

The theories are not testable.

---

What is observable: in the morning, when the first children arrived and Mr. Smith unlocked the door and turned on the lights, Boney was in the corner. In the same position. Hat slightly tilted. Sword catching the light at an angle that was always slightly different from the day before, though no one had touched him.

Marcus had noticed this once. He had marked Boney's position with a small piece of tape on the floor, invisible from above but present if you knew where to look. Over three weeks, Boney had not moved. The sword angle changed. The tape confirmed the feet had not.

Marcus told Isla. Isla said the sword angle was probably light, or how you were standing, or something. Marcus wasn't sure. He kept the tape in place for the rest of the term.

---

The classroom was empty.

The radiators ticked.

Boney stood in the corner with the slight forward lean that meant attention, or had come to mean attention, or had always meant exactly nothing and the meaning was something the children had placed there, the way children place meaning in things, the way things hold meaning once it has been placed.

The lights were off. The sword was not catching any light.

The room was doing what rooms do when no one is in them.

Whether something else was happening: not observable.
