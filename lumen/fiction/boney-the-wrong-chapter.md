# Boney and the Wrong Chapter

"I studied," said Aisha.

"I know," said Boney.

"I studied for three days."

"I believe you."

Aisha looked at her test. Chapter four had not appeared. Chapter four was what she had studied. The test appeared to be entirely chapter five, which she had not studied because she had not read that far in the instructions.

"It's not fair," she said.

Boney considered several responses.

"You know chapter four very well now," he said.

"That doesn't help."

"It helps with chapter four."

Aisha looked at him with an expression that suggested this was not a useful observation. He agreed. It was technically true but not practically useful, which were different things.

"I don't know why you didn't check," he said. "But I don't think you didn't study. I think you studied the wrong thing very thoroughly."

"Is that better or worse?"

Boney thought about it. "It's different. Studying nothing means you don't know how to study. Studying the wrong thing means you can study, you just need to also check what you're supposed to study before you start."

"That sounds like a small difference."

"On this test, yes," said Boney. "In general, it's a large difference."

Aisha looked at the questions. She tried to remember if anything from chapter four overlapped with chapter five. One question might overlap. Possibly. She wasn't sure.

"What do I do now?" she said.

"Do the ones you know," said Boney. "Then try the ones you don't. Then go home and read chapter five, so you know it for next time."

"I won't be tested on chapter five next time."

"No," said Boney. "But you'll know it. That's not nothing."

Aisha looked at him. "You know a lot of useless things, don't you."

"A lot of things that weren't useful when I needed them and became useful later," said Boney. "Which is almost the same."

He went back to the corner. Aisha did the questions she could answer, which were three, and tried the ones she couldn't, which were twelve. She got one of those partially right by accident.

She did read chapter five that evening. She did not get tested on it again. But she read it, and she checked the instructions first, which was the thing she had actually learned.
