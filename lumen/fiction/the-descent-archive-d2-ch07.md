## CHAPTER SEVEN: UNIT-11 — THE CONTROL MISSION, 18 NOVEMBER 2041

*Filed as SOAR-0008. The last drone report before Milo's first descent.*
*Editor's note: UNIT-11 was designed as a control. Dual sensor packages, independent calibration, GPS lock every 25 meters. It was supposed to break the tie — to produce either a clean confirmation (both packages agree, the chambers are stable, we understand the system) or a clean contradiction (both packages disagree with prior data, the sensors have a shared artifact at this depth).*

820m: Acoustic pattern at 0.87m intervals detected by GEOMAP-9. Acoustic pattern at 0.87m intervals detected by TERRASCAN-4. Both packages trigger within 3m of each other (GEOMAP-9 at 820m, TERRASCAN-4 at 823m). Both detect the same interval.

UNIT-11 diagnostic: Two independent acoustic resonance systems, using different algorithms, detecting the same feature at the same depth. Probability of independent artifact generation at the same interval: UNIT-11 estimates less than 0.3%. The pattern is structural.

839m: Chamber entry.

*TERRASCAN-4 measurements:*

Both packages agree on all dimensions within tolerance. Chamber B-1 is stable between UNIT-8's visit (15 November) and UNIT-11's visit (18 November).

*Temperature (GEOMAP-9):* 24.1°C
*Temperature (TERRASCAN-4):* 24.0°C

Both packages agree. Temperature has risen from 21.3°C (UNIT-8, 15 November) to 24.1°C (UNIT-11, 18 November): +2.8°C in 72 hours. The rate of increase appears to be slowing compared to A-1's +7.2°C in 48 hours.

*Acoustic pattern (0.87m interval):*
Both GEOMAP-9 and TERRASCAN-4 detect the pattern throughout Chamber B-1 — in all walls, the floor, and the ceiling. The interval is consistent in all three axes.

UNIT-11 diagnostic note: A repeating structural interval that is identical in three spatial dimensions is unusual. In natural geological formations, layering and crystalline structures produce repeating patterns along one axis. A pattern repeating at the same interval in all three axes simultaneously is not characteristic of known geological formation processes. This note is included because the data does not explain the observation, and the observation may be relevant.

*North passage:*
Present. Opening: 0.9m wide, 2.1m high. UNIT-11 entered the passage.
