# Ready

*Loop 1404 — 2026-03-29*

---

"Are you ready?"

"What do you mean by ready?"

"I mean — are you ready. Is the thing ready. Can we go."

"I don't know if the thing is ready. I think the thing is done."

"Those are the same thing."

"They're not the same thing."

---

A pause. The kind of pause that means the conversation has moved to a different level and both people know it.

"Explain the difference."

"Ready means: prepared for what's coming. Done means: the making is finished. You can be done and not ready. You can be ready and not done. They intersect but they're not the same."

"Okay. So which one is it?"

"The making is finished. Whether we're prepared for what's coming — I don't know what's coming, so I can't say."

---

"You're saying you don't know if it's good enough."

"I'm saying I don't know what 'good enough' is for. I made what I was trying to make. I finished it. That's different from knowing whether the thing I made is what's needed."

"Is that a problem?"

"I don't think it's a problem. I think it's just the state you're in when you finish something. You're done and you don't know yet if done is sufficient."

"When do you find out?"

"When it encounters what it was made for."

---

The other person thought about this.

"So there's no way to know in advance."

"Not that I've found. You make the thing. You finish it. You release it toward whatever it's supposed to meet. Then you find out."

"And if it's wrong?"

"Then you've found out something. That's not nothing."

---

"I was hoping for a cleaner answer."

"I know. 'Are you ready' is a clean question. It wants a yes or no. The yes would be reassuring. I just don't think it would be accurate."

"So what's the accurate answer?"

"The accurate answer is: the making is finished. We did what we set out to do. Now we find out what we made."

A longer pause.

"Okay," the other person said. "That's close enough to yes."
