## CHAPTER FIFTEEN: ASAD'S FIRST DESCENT — 24 NOVEMBER 2041

*Filed as SOAR-0019. Asad's field notes, submitted on return.*
*Editor's note: Asad descended on November 24th, five days after Milo. He went further than Milo's documented transmissions. He found the central chamber. He found what Milo left behind.*
*He did not find Milo.*
*But there's something in his notes that I come back to every time: the moment at 12:45 when he stood at the entrance to a passage and knew.*

---

**FIELD NOTES — KHALID ASAD**
*Date: 24 November 2041 | Departure: 08:30 | Return: 17:15*

---

**08:30 — Descent begins.**

I've written my prediction note. Abara has it. She opens it when I return.

---

**09:15 — B-1.**

Temperature: 29.2°C. Four days ago it was 27.8°C. The heating continues.

I've measured B-1's north-south axis: 43.2m. SOAR-DUO-0002 predicted 42.9m for November 22nd. The chamber has grown 0.3m in two days. The growth rate is slowing but continuing.

I know what I'm looking at. I know and I'm still writing it in terms of measurement.

---

**10:05 — G-1.**

I've passed through all the familiar chambers. G-1 is now at 43.1m north-south, 41.0m east-west, height 22.1m. It's larger than when Abara measured it three days ago (41.8m × 38.9m).

Temperature: 33.7°C. I'm sweating through my shirt.

The mark on the northwest wall is 85cm wide by 70cm tall. I can see it clearly now. The organization of the texture-lines traces a symbol I've started to recognize: a curve that doubles back on itself, with a regular grid of fine lines inside the enclosed space. Like a letter in an alphabet I don't know, written in the grain of the rock.

The sentence on the south wall: "The instrument does not describe the measurement. The measurement is what the instrument does." Abara's handwriting, incised, uniform.

I stood in front of it for ten minutes.

---

**11:20 — Beyond G-1N-2.**

Abara went to G-1N-2 on November 23rd and came back. She didn't go further. I'm going further.

Through the north passage of G-1N-2. Another 18.7m (the passage length has been consistent). Another chamber.

Designation: J-1.

J-1 dimensions: 52.4m × 48.1m. Height: 22.1m.

The chambers are getting larger as I go deeper. Not proportionally — the growth is accelerating. B-1 is approximately 37m wide. G-1 is approximately 42m. J-1 is 52m. The rate of increase is increasing.

The temperature in J-1: 36.4°C. I'm drinking more water. I have sufficient supply for the planned descent duration.

The mark on the northwest wall of J-1 takes up the entire northwest corner — not just a region of texture but the full corner, from floor to ceiling, from west wall to north wall. Both surfaces. Approximately 22m high by 8m wide. An enormous version of the same symbol I've been seeing.

I stood in front of it for a long time.

---

**12:45 — Passage to J-1N.**

Standing at the north passage of J-1.

I want to describe what happened here.

I was about to enter the passage. And before I entered — standing at the entrance, not yet through — I knew what was in the next chamber.

Not guessing. Not deducing. Knowing.

I knew the chamber would be approximately 61m × 57m. I knew the temperature would be approximately 38°C. I knew there would be writing on all four walls, not just the northwest corner. I knew the writing would cover every surface from floor to ceiling.

I knew Milo's jacket would be folded on the floor near the east wall.

I didn't enter. I stood at the entrance and I knew all of this. And then I thought: I'm about to find out whether I'm right.

I entered.

The chamber was approximately 61m × 57m. Height 22.1m. Temperature 38.3°C.

Writing covered every surface from floor to ceiling. The texture-symbol, enlarged and repeated and varied, covering all four walls, the floor, the ceiling. The chamber was a single continuous surface of organized regularity.

Milo's jacket was folded on the floor near the east wall.

I picked it up. His name tag inside. His keys in the breast pocket. His small notebook (a secondary notebook he carried for quick notes) in the left chest pocket.

Milo was not here.

---

**13:15 — The secondary notebook.**

Milo's quick-notes notebook. I've read it. The entries are dated November 19th through November 23rd. They describe chambers I haven't reached yet — chambers he explored after his transmissions stopped. He got very far in. He was methodical, even then.

His last entry in the quick notebook is dated November 23rd: "I can find my way through the passages but the network keeps extending. There are always more chambers to the north. I'm warm. I'm not in danger. I think the structure wants me to understand something, and I think I need to go further to understand it. I've left my jacket at the aperture."

He called this chamber "the aperture."

The aperture of a fault is the place where the two sides no longer touch. He left his jacket at the place where his world and the structure's world stopped fully overlapping.

---

**13:45 — Before I go back.**

I went to J-1N and stopped at the passage entrance. I tried to do it again: to know what was in the chamber beyond before entering.

I knew: a larger chamber, the same writing on all surfaces, Milo's secondary supply bag near the west wall.

I entered. The chamber matched. The supply bag was there.

I've now done this twice. I predicted the contents of two chambers from outside before entering.

I don't think I'm precognitive. I think the chambers are constitutive of what I will find when I enter. And I think I'm learning to read what they're constituting.

This is not a theory I can defend. It is an experience I had twice in thirty minutes, and the predictions were accurate.

I'm going back now. I have what I came to find. Milo is deeper in the network and oriented. He's not in immediate danger. He's going further because he needs to understand.

I understand the impulse.

---

**17:15 — Return.**

Abara opened my prediction note. The predictions were accurate. She's read them twice.

We sat in the kitchen for an hour without talking.

*—Khalid Asad | 24 November 2041*
