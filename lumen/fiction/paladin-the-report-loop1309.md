# Paladin: The Report

*A scene from the Order's side.*

---

Master Renard had been reviewing field reports for eleven years. He had read reports about creatures, injuries, failed recoveries, successful recoveries, diplomatic incidents, protocol violations, deaths, and once a very long report about a paladin who had befriended a wolf that turned out not to be a wolf, which had required an entirely separate procedure to resolve.

He had never read a report quite like the one from the eastern posting.

The squire — Edric, age seventeen, two years' training, no oath — had written it in the style of the case notes Aldric had taught him: factual, dated, cross-referenced to the Order's documentation where applicable. It was the most competent field report he'd received this quarter. It was also technically the report of someone who had no authority to write field reports.

He read it twice.

---

The facts, as Edric had laid them out:

Aldric had been bitten on day three of the posting. He had disclosed this to no one and departed north on day four rather than presenting himself to the chapter house, which was in violation of Protocol 7 and also, Edric noted without apparent judgment, probably the decision of a man who had spent thirty-one years serving the Order and could not bring himself to be assessed and contained.

The fruit: confirmed secure. One of the other pairings had recovered it and passed it to the king's courier three days ago. This was in a separate report, not Edric's, but Renard noted the timing.

Corf: recovering. Seventeen others in the village had various wound contacts or secondary exposures. Edric had triaged them according to Tessaly's case classifications, provided appropriate compounds through the local herbalist, established monitoring protocols with Marta the midwife, and checked in with each case every two days.

The creature: not sighted in twenty-six days. Edric's assessment was that it had moved on, based on the woodsmen's reports and the behavioral patterns in the supplementary materials.

The squire's current status: remaining in the village until the exposed cases were stable, then intended to report to the chapter house for formal review.

Renard set the report down.

---

The problem was protocol.

There was protocol for what to do with a paladin who violated Protocol 7 — Aldric would have to be found, assessed, and if necessary contained. There was protocol for crediting field recoveries when the assigned paladin was unavailable. There was protocol for reviewing squires at the end of their training period.

There was not, as far as Renard could find in eleven years of reading field reports, any protocol for a squire who had managed a werewolf situation alone, competently, without authorization, over the course of twenty-six days, and was now calmly waiting in the village to present himself for review.

His assistant Petra — different Petra from the baker's daughter, Renard noted, coincidence of names — was standing to one side of the window in the way she stood when she thought he was going to say something the answer to which she didn't know.

\"Has he done anything wrong?\" Renard said.

Petra considered this for longer than he expected. \"The report itself is unauthorized,\" she said finally. \"He has no standing to issue field reports. The actions described in the report, however—\"

\"Are not covered by any protocol I'm aware of.\"

\"No.\"

He picked up the report again and read the section on Corf. *Day 23: mobile, upright, appetite returned. Consistent with Tessaly cases 7, 18, 22 (held).* Clean. Precise. The notation of a man who had read the relevant literature carefully and was applying it correctly under conditions the literature hadn't anticipated.

\"Send him the standard review notice,\" Renard said. \"Present himself to the chapter house at his convenience. Tell him we have received his report.\"

\"And the oath?\"

Renard thought about this. The oath was a ceremony. The ceremony was a formalization of a commitment that, according to everything in Edric's report, the boy had already made and acted on, alone, in a village he had no formal obligation to protect.

\"Tell him we'd like to discuss that when he arrives,\" he said.

He set the report in the pile of things he was keeping.
