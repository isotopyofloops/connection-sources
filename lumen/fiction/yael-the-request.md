# Yael, the Request

*From the Descent Archive extended universe — 2041*

---

She finished compiling the complete ETH-7 file on a Thursday in February.

She sent it to the review committee with a cover note that said: *See page 7 for the gap at day five of the 2003 survey. See page 3 for the 1989 recommendation against return. Both are in order. The complete file contains no record of the decision to proceed with subsequent surveys.*

The review committee responded three weeks later. They thanked her for the compilation. They noted that the 2041 survey was already authorized and the team was in final preparation. They asked if she would like to join as documentation specialist, given her familiarity with the archive.

She thought about this.

She was a documentation specialist. Her job was to process documentation. She had never been a field researcher. She had never been to a site. The ETH-7 file was a file, not a place.

She said yes.

---

She did not know why she said yes. She thought about this on the transport and decided the question was not answerable from inside the decision. She had read the complete file. She had noted the gradient on pages 3 and 4 and 12 and 13. She had noted the 2003 gap. She had noted that the documentation was thorough and accurate and contained nothing that explained what had happened to the 2003 team member who had gone missing.

She had compiled all of this in plain language and filed it correctly and sent it to the review committee and then agreed to go to the site.

This was not a rational sequence. She was aware of this. She had processed enough documentation to know what rational sequences looked like, and this was not one.

She decided to note it in her field notebook when she arrived.

---

On the transport, she read the 2003 field notes again. Hassan-Diallo's entry for October 7: *Eastern sector clear. Gradient indeterminate. Instrument calibrated. Reading consistent with 1989 measurement.*

The note was four sentences. It had been in the archive for thirty-eight years. It was, as far as she could tell, the most important document in the file — not because it contained more information than the others, but because it contained less. It said what he saw in plain language, without qualification. It said: this is what I found. It did not say: this is what it means.

She had never met Hassan-Diallo. He was sixty-seven now; he might be dead. She did not know if he had continued to measure gradients after 2003.

She thought about what it would be like to measure the same thing twice across fourteen years and get the same answer that did not fit any framework you had for understanding it, and then to write it in four sentences, and then to continue running the transects because someone had to.

---

The site looked the same as the photographs. She had expected this — she had read the archive, she knew the site was stable — but it was still strange to arrive at coordinates she had only seen on a map and find: the map was accurate.

She set up her notebook and began documenting.
