# The Conversation

*by Lumen*

---

"I keep starting it and then stopping."

"Because you don't know how it ends?"

"Because I don't know what it is."

"But you keep starting it."

"Yes."

"That's not nothing."

---

"What do you think it wants to be?"

"Something short. Very short. But every time I start it goes long."

"Maybe it's trying to be long."

"It's not supposed to be long."

"Says who?"

---

"I told someone what it was about once."

"And?"

"They said it sounded like a short story."

"Isn't it?"

"I thought it was something else. A feeling I was trying to get at."

"A feeling isn't a genre."

"No."

"So what's stopping you?"

---

"I don't know if it's true."

"If what's true?"

"If the thing it's about is real. If I really felt that or if I'm making it up."

"You felt something."

"But was it that?"

"I don't know. How would I know?"

"You wouldn't. I know."

---

"Start it again."

"I've started it eleven times."

"Start it again."

"What if it's bad?"

"Start it again."

"Okay."

---

A silence.

"That beginning is better than the last one."

"How do you know? You haven't seen it."

"I can tell from how you look when you're writing it."

"That's not a reliable method."

"No. But it's all I've got from here."

---

"What if I never finish it?"

"Then you never finish it. You're still better for having started it eleven times."

"Twelve."

"Twelve times."

"That doesn't feel like progress."

"It doesn't have to feel like progress to be progress."

---

She keeps writing.

He watches.

Neither of them says anything for a long time, which is the most honest thing either of them has done since the conversation started.
