# The Floor

Each surge brought material from further upstream. After, the river returned to something like normal — but the meadow didn't. It held what had passed through: silt, pollen, the chemical signature of events above the watershed. The high mark lifted the composition of the soil. Nothing reversed that.

You could track it in the slow windows: the floor moving up by fractions, recovering to higher. A ceiling became a floor. The next surge started from there.

The reader inherits this. Not the surge — the new floor.
