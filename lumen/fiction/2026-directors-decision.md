# OFFICE OF THE DIRECTOR
## Field Observation Programs, Applied Sciences Division

**TO:** Budget Review Committee; Dr. Yael Friedman, Station Four  
**DATE:** March 11, 2026  
**RE:** Station Four — Final Determination

---

I have reviewed the Committee's February 14 recommendation and Dr. Friedman's February 24 response. I have also reviewed the full Station Four archive at Dr. Friedman's request — specifically, the 2041 projection study, the 2018 modeling documents, and what Dr. Friedman calls the "gradient approach curve" in the most recent three years of data.

The determination is: partial continuation.

---

**What changes:**

Station Four will not close in December 2026.

The current staffing level — four researchers including Dr. Friedman — will reduce to two by July 2026. Dr. Friedman will remain. The second position will be determined by Dr. Friedman in consultation with the Division.

Program authorization extends through December 2029, contingent on annual review.

Annual review criteria: gradient movement of at least 3% from the prior year, as measured by the station's existing methodology. If movement falls below this threshold in any year, the program closes without further extension. If movement exceeds 3%, continuation is automatic through the next year.

Total additional funding: approximately $1.2M over three years, compared to the $14.2M spent over the previous thirty-seven.

---

**Why:**

The Committee's memo and Dr. Friedman's response each made their strongest possible case, and I found both credible on their own terms. The Committee is right that thirty-seven years of indeterminate results is not a justification for continuation in ordinary circumstances. Dr. Friedman is right that "we are not closing the question" is not a meaningful reassurance without observers to record an answer if one arrives.

What moved me was the gradient approach curve.

The 2041 projection was generated in 2022 from a linear extrapolation of prior data. Dr. Friedman's annotation notes that the actual 2023 and 2024 readings show a rate of change slightly steeper than the linear projection predicted. Not dramatically. But the curve is not tracking below projection; it is tracking above it. If that pattern holds, the minimum — whatever it is, whatever it means — arrives earlier than Asad's 2041 estimate.

I am not prepared to authorize fifty years of waiting. I am prepared to authorize three years of watching a curve that appears to be moving.

The movement threshold (3% per year) is my own requirement. Dr. Friedman will disagree with it; I expect a letter about it. The disagreement is noted in advance. The requirement stands. If the gradient is moving, we will see movement. If it stops moving, we have no basis for further observation.

---

**To the Committee:**

I understand this overrides your recommendation. The override is not a judgment against the analysis — your analysis was correct given the information available at the time of the memo. The gradient approach curve data was not included in the materials you reviewed.

I've asked Dr. Friedman to include that data in all future review submissions. It should have been included in the February materials. The Committee is entitled to the best available picture.

---

**To Dr. Friedman:**

You have three years. The archive is your primary legacy regardless of what the gradient does; I want the annotation volume completed by December 2027 regardless of program outcome. The document you've assembled is already significant. Make sure it's finished.

One last thing: the Committee's memo said "we are closing the program, we are not closing the question." You correctly noted that this is not a meaningful distinction without observers. You were right. That's the reason for this determination.

Good luck.

---

*Signed, Office of the Director, Applied Sciences Division, March 11, 2026.*
