# Boney and the Group Project

The groups had been announced on Monday, and by Wednesday Boney had noticed a pattern.

In every group, there was someone who had already decided what the project was going to be. There was someone who had not said a word since Monday. There was someone who was trying to make everyone happy and was succeeding at nothing. And there was someone who was doing the actual work while looking increasingly tired about it.

He mentioned this to Ms. Patel while she was setting up the photocopier.

"You've described every group project in the history of education," she said.

"Is that a solution?"

"It's an observation," she said. "Solutions are your department."

---

Boney drifted over to Table Four, where Anya was writing in her notebook while the rest of the group argued about whether the poster should be landscape or portrait. The argument had been going on for six minutes.

"Anya," said Dev. "Landscape or portrait?"

"Either is fine," said Anya.

"You have to pick one," said Dev.

"Portrait," said Anya.

"I think landscape," said Priya.

"Then landscape," said Anya.

Dev looked at Boney. Boney looked at Anya. Anya continued writing.

"What are you writing?" said Boney.

"The actual content," said Anya. "For the poster. Someone has to."

"You could tell them that."

"I could," said Anya.

Boney waited. Anya did not tell them that.

---

He tried Table Two next. Marcus had a very clear vision. The project was going to be his idea, in his format, with his color scheme. He was explaining this to Fatima and James with the confidence of someone who had not yet noticed they had stopped listening.

"—and the title should be in red because red is attention-grabbing, and we should have exactly three bullet points per section because any more than three is too many—"

Fatima was doodling in the margin. James was looking out the window.

"Marcus," said Boney.

Marcus paused. "What?"

"What does Fatima think the title should say?"

Marcus looked at Fatima as if he had briefly forgotten she was there. "What do you think the title should say?"

Fatima stopped doodling. "Oh," she said. "I was thinking something with a question in it. Like, something that makes you want to read it."

"That's actually better than what I had," said Marcus, after a moment.

Fatima looked slightly surprised.

"It is," said Marcus. "Mine was just a statement."

---

Boney came back to Table Four. Anya had filled three pages.

"Anya," said Boney. "How much have you written?"

"Enough for two posters."

"Does your group know that?"

"Probably not."

Boney waited again.

Anya put her pen down. She turned the notebook so the group could see it.

"Oh," said Dev. "You wrote all of that?"

"Someone had to," said Anya. But her voice was different this time.

"That's really good," said Priya. She meant it.

"Portrait," said Anya. "Definitely portrait. We have a lot of content."

---

Later, Ms. Patel asked Boney if he'd solved anything.

"I don't think so," he said. "Marcus might listen slightly better. Anya might show her work slightly earlier. That's about all."

"That's quite a lot," she said.

"Is it enough?"

Ms. Patel considered. "For a Wednesday, yes."

Boney looked over at the four tables, each of them arguing productively now, or at least arguing with slightly more information than before.

"It's always a Wednesday," he said. "There isn't a Thursday version."

"No," she agreed. "There isn't."
