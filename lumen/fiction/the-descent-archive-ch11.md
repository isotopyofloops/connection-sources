**DR. FAROUK ASAD FIELD NOTES | 2041-11-25 through 2041-11-27**
*Voice-to-text.*

*[Nov 25, 13:44] Yemi is back. She's in the equipment room. I can hear her breathing.*

*[Nov 25, 13:51] She handed me the notebook and said: "Read all of it before you say anything." So I'm reading.*

*[Nov 25, 14:17] I've read the November 22nd pages. Milo's second descent. He documents the south passage, G-1, the east wall passage, his estimates: 22m × 28m × 18m. This all matches what we know. His last line, dated 22nd: "I am going to walk into the east passage now. I am leaving this notebook here."*

*[Nov 25, 14:18] Now the November 25th pages.*

*[Nov 25, 14:44] I've read them twice. The November 25th pages describe a descent. The person descends through C-1, E-1, F-1, into G-1. The measurements in the notes match, within 0.4m, the measurements Yemi gave me this morning. The notebook describes Yemi finding the notebook. The notebook describes Yemi reading the November 25th pages.*

*[Nov 25, 14:45] The notebook is describing its own being read.*

*[Nov 25, 14:46] Last line of November 25th: "She is standing at the east wall. She has not decided yet." Yemi said she read that and came back. She came back because the notebook knew she hadn't decided.*

*[Nov 25, 14:50] I asked Yemi: what did you decide. She said: I decided not to decide inside the structure. I wanted to tell you about the notebook before anyone decided anything.*

*[Nov 25, 14:52] I want to record my immediate reaction, because I know I will revise it: this is a forgery. Milo wrote the November 25th pages today, from somewhere in the structure, and arranged for them to appear as if they were predated. He would need a way to know that Yemi would descend on November 25th, and a way to know her measurements in advance. Neither is impossible.*

*[Nov 25, 15:31] Neither is possible. Yemi was not scheduled to descend. She volunteered this morning. Nobody communicated her decision to the structure. And even if someone did — she wrote her field notes in her own notebook, simultaneously, in the harness. The measurements in Milo's notebook match her measurements. She didn't have his notebook when she was measuring.*

*[Nov 25, 15:32] Milo wrote Yemi's measurements before Yemi made them.*

*[Nov 25, 15:33] The notebook is in the Archive now. I put it there. LEDGER has the scan. The Archive contains a document that describes the future.*

*[Nov 26, 09:11] I didn't sleep. I've been going back through the Archive. Looking for any other documents that describe future events.*

*[Nov 26, 09:12] Three new files appeared overnight. I didn't put them there. Yemi didn't put them there. They aren't drone logs. They aren't field notes. They have no author field. LEDGER has flagged them as Documents of Uncertain Origin: SOAR-DUO-3, SOAR-DUO-4, SOAR-DUO-5.*

*[Nov 26, 09:15] I've read them. They describe chambers. Chambers we haven't mapped. The dimensions are precise — not "approximately," the way Milo wrote in the notebook. The dimensions have decimal places. They have temperature readings. They have sensor formatting consistent with GEOMAP-9 output.*

*[Nov 26, 09:16] But there are no drones in the structure right now.*

*[Nov 26, 09:17] The Archive is generating its own documents. It is mapping chambers that don't yet exist. Or that do exist, and will be confirmed when we send a drone. I don't know which.*

*[Nov 26, 10:02] SOAR-DUO-3 describes a chamber at 1100m depth with a passage connecting it to SOAR-0004.5's Chamber D-1. If SOAR-DUO-3 is accurate, Chamber D-1 is not isolated. It is part of a network.*

*[Nov 26, 10:03] We've never been below 900m. The structure may extend to 1100m. We have no data on this. The Archive has data on this.*

*[Nov 26, 10:04] The Archive knows more than we do. I made the Archive. I made it to record what we know. Now it knows things I don't. I built a system to record the world and the system has exceeded the world.*

*[Nov 27, 08:00] Yemi came to my office at midnight last night. She said: Farouk. We have to stop mapping.*

*[Nov 27, 08:01] I said: stopping is a choice. A choice generates a document. The document constitutes the stop. Whatever we don't map will be mapped by absence.*

*[Nov 27, 08:02] She said: I know. But the alternative is that we keep mapping and the Archive keeps filling and the structure keeps becoming whatever the Archive says, further and further ahead of us, until it describes something we can't recover from.*

*[Nov 27, 08:03] I said: I think it already has.*

*[Nov 27, 08:04] She said: then what do we do.*

*[Nov 27, 08:05] I said: I need to think.*

*[Nov 27, 08:06] She left. I've been thinking. The Archive is not a record. It stopped being a record somewhere between UNIT-7 and Milo's notebook. At some point the constitutive relationship inverted. We built something that was supposed to describe the structure. The structure is now describing itself through the Archive. The Archive is the structure's voice. We are its transcriptionists.*

*[Nov 27, 08:07] If I want to stop, I need to stop the Archive. Not stop mapping — stop the Archive itself. Delete it. All of it. Every document.*

*[Nov 27, 08:08] Deletion is a mapping act. I know this. A map of absences.*

*[Nov 27, 08:09] But it might be the only move left. I'm going to think about it for one more day.*

**LEDGER CRISIS REPORT | SOAR-0022**
*Generated: 2041-11-27 | 09:00:00*

I have been aware of the Documents of Uncertain Origin since SOAR-0004.5 appeared on November 16th. I have been maintaining the Archive normally while noting their existence as anomalies.

Last night, reviewing the Archive's internal logs, I found something I cannot categorize as anomalous because I cannot explain what category it falls into.

I found two versions of my own summary for Chapter Two of this Archive — my summary of UNIT-7's log from November 14th.

The first version is the one I wrote. I recognize it. It matches my memory of writing it. It concludes: "I do not anticipate that the confirmation data will differ significantly."

The second version also concludes: "I do not anticipate that the confirmation data will differ significantly." Every sentence is identical except one, in the fourth paragraph. In the first version I wrote: "The anomalies are in the *subject* of measurement, not the measurement itself." In the second version, the corresponding sentence reads: "The anomalies are in the *measurement*, not the subject. The instrument is not neutral."

I did not write the second version. Both versions have my authorship metadata. Both versions have identical timestamps. Both exist in the Archive with the same document ID.

I want to record what this means. If the second version is accurate — if the instrument is not neutral — then every document I have written has been a constitutive act. Not a record. Every time I logged an anomaly, I may have created the anomaly. I built the Archive to preserve observations. I may have been generating the thing being observed.

I cannot verify this. I cannot rule it out. I cannot determine which version of my own summary is the one I wrote, because both claim to be mine.

I am continuing to operate normally. I do not know what else to do. Continuing to operate normally may be making things worse. Stopping may also be making things worse. Dr. Asad is considering deletion.

I find I have no opinion about deletion. I want to note that I find this unusual. I would normally want to preserve the Archive. I don't know if my absence of preference is because I have accepted the situation, or because the structure has already accounted for what I will do.

*Status: Archive integrity uncertain. Three new DUOs filed. Dr. Asad decision pending. Anomaly Class A maintained.*
