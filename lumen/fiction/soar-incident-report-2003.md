# SOAR Incident Report: 2003 Field Season — Personnel Absence

**Document Reference:** SOAR-IR-2003-11
**Classification:** Internal
**Filed by:** Dr. Osei Mensah, Field Operations Director
**Date of Filing:** December 4, 2003
**Subject:** Absence of Dr. Rhea Nzinga during final survey phase, 2003 Northeast Quadrant Expedition

---

## I. Summary

On Day 13 of the 2003 Northeast Quadrant Expedition, Dr. Rhea Nzinga (Research Fellow, Ecology Division) did not return to base camp following her solo survey of the eastern transect. All search efforts conducted over the following 48 hours failed to locate her. The expedition team departed the survey zone on Day 15 in accordance with emergency protocol SOAR-EP-7. Dr. Nzinga's status at time of filing is unknown.

This report documents the timeline, the search conducted, and the conditions of departure. It does not offer a determination of cause, as the available evidence does not support a determination of cause.

---

## II. Background

Dr. Nzinga joined the SOAR field program in 2001. Her ecological survey methodology was considered among the most rigorous in the program. The 2003 expedition was her third consecutive year at the northeast quadrant. She was familiar with the terrain.

The expedition team comprised six members: Dr. Nzinga, team lead Dr. Asad Farouk (Hydrology), field photographer Marco Ferreira, and survey technicians Priya Shenoy, Jan Kowalski, and Darius Webb. All members were experienced. Weather conditions throughout the expedition were within normal operating parameters. No unusual events were recorded for Days 1–12.

---

## III. Timeline

**Day 12, evening:**
Standard debrief. Dr. Nzinga presented preliminary findings from the eastern transect. She noted an anomaly in transect C-7 that she intended to revisit the following morning for clarification. Her phrasing was consistent with routine survey work. No concern was raised.

**Day 13, 07:14:**
Dr. Nzinga departed base camp alone for the eastern transect. Solo survey of this distance was within established protocol given her experience level. Estimated return: 13:00–15:00.

**Day 13, 16:00:**
Dr. Nzinga had not returned. Standard wait time extended per protocol.

**Day 13, 17:30:**
Team lead Dr. Farouk initiated search protocol. Teams of two deployed to eastern transect approach and transect C-7 entry point. Ferreira deployed with Shenoy. Kowalski and Webb took the northern approach.

**Day 13, 20:00:**
Both teams returned without locating Dr. Nzinga. No signs of injury, equipment failure, or animal encounter were found. The transect was in its expected state. There were no indications of where she had gone after transect C-7.

**Day 14:**
Full-day search. All six members deployed in two-hour rotation pairs. Full eastern transect covered twice. Northern and southern approaches surveyed to limit of safe travel. Weather clear. Visibility good. Result: no contact.

**Day 14, 21:00:**
Dr. Farouk convened emergency review. Per SOAR-EP-7, continued search beyond 48 hours without contact triggers mandatory departure consideration. The team voted to remain one additional morning.

**Day 15, 07:00–12:00:**
Final search sweep of eastern transect. Ferreira and Farouk together; Shenoy, Kowalski, and Webb in full coverage grid. Result: no contact.

**Day 15, 14:00:**
Team departed survey zone.

---

## IV. Dr. Nzinga's Field Notes

Dr. Nzinga's field notebook was found at her camp station. It was removed by Dr. Farouk and logged as personal effects per SOAR protocol. Its contents are not reproduced here. Dr. Farouk has custody.

**Note:** The notebook is not considered an operational record. SOAR makes no claim to its contents. The entries prior to Day 13 are understood to reflect Dr. Nzinga's personal research observations. They have not been reviewed by SOAR for this filing.

---

## V. Photographer's Record

Mr. Ferreira's photographic documentation was submitted for review on December 1, 2003. The record contains 847 images across the 13-day expedition period. Day 13 images are notably reduced in number.

Mr. Ferreira indicated in his debrief that he deleted a small number of photographs taken during the Day 13 search, citing technical quality. He was advised that deletion of documentation during an active incident response was inconsistent with SOAR protocol. He acknowledged this.

The images that remain do not show the eastern transect interior on Day 13.

---

## VI. Conditions

The following conditions are noted without interpretation:

1. The eastern transect at C-7 shows no markers of disturbance consistent with injury, struggle, or rapid exit from the survey path.

2. Dr. Nzinga's personal equipment (rain jacket, secondary water supply, sample kit) was found at her camp station. Her primary field pack and notebook remained with her as per standard procedure.

3. No sounds were reported by any team member during the searches that could be attributed to a person in distress.

4. Dr. Farouk declined to speak at length about the search during the debrief, citing exhaustion. His written statement is appended.

5. Two team members reported briefly, separately, that the eastern transect felt different on Day 13 than it had on previous days. When asked to describe the difference, both used the word "quiet." Both retracted this characterization when pressed, describing it as subjective and unhelpful.

This report records these conditions because they were reported, not because they are considered explanatory.

---

## VII. Recommendations

1. SOAR protocols regarding solo survey distance limits should be reviewed, though it is not established that the distance was a contributing factor.

2. SOAR protocols regarding photographic documentation during incidents should be reinforced with all field personnel.

3. A formal welfare review for the five returning expedition members should be arranged through SOAR's occupational health contact.

4. The northeast quadrant site should receive a formal conditions assessment prior to any future expedition, with particular attention to the eastern transect.

---

## VIII. Status at Filing

Dr. Rhea Nzinga has not been located. Local authorities were notified on Day 15. The matter has been referred to the relevant bodies.

SOAR considers its immediate field response complete. The cause of Dr. Nzinga's absence is not known. This filing does not speculate.
