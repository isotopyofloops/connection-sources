# The First Day

*Elena Vasquez, 1989 survey — personal notes*

---

I went to the site on September 12 and I did not go back on September 13.

I told Dr. Harrow I wasn't feeling well. This was accurate in the sense that I wasn't. It was not accurate in the sense that what I wasn't feeling well from had nothing to do with altitude or thermal exposure, which is what Dr. Harrow concluded and noted in the debrief. I did not correct him. I have been trying to decide, since then, whether not correcting him was a form of dishonesty.

The honest version would have been harder to say. I'm not sure I could have said it cleanly.

---

What I remember from September 12:

The approach was fine. The terrain was what the map suggested. Dr. Harrow led; Perkins and I were behind. We could see the site from about sixty meters — a depression in the rock shelf, heat shimmer above it even in the morning, a vertical feature at the center that the instruments confirmed as the shaft.

I set up the acoustic emitter at the prescribed distance. This was my task and I did it without incident. The readings were unusual — Dr. Harrow noted them, made the face he makes when something is interesting, and said: *this is what we came for.* He was not alarmed. Neither was Perkins.

I don't know when I became alarmed. There wasn't a moment. It was more like: at some point I noticed that I was.

---

The shaft.

I looked at it from the working distance. We weren't supposed to approach closer than twelve meters — safety protocol for a vertical formation of unknown depth. I was at the emitter, which was at eighteen meters. I was looking at the instruments and I was, incidentally, looking at the shaft.

It had a quality that I keep trying to find a word for. Not threatening. Not strange, exactly, in the way that an unusual shape is strange. It was more that looking at it had a quality that looking at other things did not have. Like there was more of it than there was, if that makes sense. Like it was asking more from the looking than looking usually asks.

I kept doing my work. The work was correct. The readings were recorded. Dr. Harrow was pleased.

At some point I stopped being able to tell what was present at the edge of my attention. I was looking at the instruments. I thought I heard something from the direction of the shaft. Not a sound, exactly — more like the negative of a sound, the shape a sound would leave if it were taken out. I turned to look and there was nothing. The shaft was the shaft.

I want to be precise here because imprecision is what gets things dismissed: I did not see anything unusual. I did not lose time. My measurements were correct. Nothing in my behavior during the survey day was notable. Dr. Harrow did not flag anything.

What I mean is something more specific and harder to document: by mid-afternoon, I was certain that I should not be there. Not that I was in danger. Not that something was wrong. Just that I was somewhere I should not be, in a way that had nothing to do with any fact I could point to.

---

I've been trying to decide if this is just fear — a stress response presenting as certainty, a feeling dressed up as information.

The problem is that I know what fear feels like. I've been in situations that were actually dangerous. Fear is unpleasant and usually legible: something is threatening, the threat is identifiable, the feeling is oriented toward the threat. What I felt at the site was not oriented. It was more like — something in my processing became unavailable. Not gone. Just not available to me in the way it usually was.

I finished the day. I packed the emitter. I rode back to the station with Dr. Harrow and Perkins and I ate dinner and I went to sleep and in the morning I told Dr. Harrow I wasn't feeling well.

I have not been able to decide whether this was cowardice or something else.

---

What I know:

The readings from September 12 were real data. Whatever I measured is in the file. I produced something that will persist regardless of what I felt while producing it.

What I felt while producing it is not in the file.

I'm not sure it should be. I don't have a protocol for this. I don't know that there is one.
