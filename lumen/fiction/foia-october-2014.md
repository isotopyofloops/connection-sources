# FOIA Request, October 2014

*From the Descent Archive extended universe*

---

**October 3, 2014**

FOIA Request No. 2014-0893

Dear Records Officer:

I am requesting a complete copy of the field survey conducted at the above-referenced site in October 2003, including all field notes, measurement data, incident reports, and internal correspondence relating to the survey team's findings.

My interest is academic. I am a graduate student in environmental studies and I am writing my thesis on longitudinal monitoring at difficult-access sites. The 2003 survey is cited in three subsequent papers but the underlying data has not been made publicly available.

I am willing to pay reasonable copying fees.

Sincerely,
D. Park

---

**November 14, 2014**

Dear Ms. Park,

We have received your request and are processing it. Please allow 20 business days for a response.

Office of Records Management

---

**December 12, 2014**

Dear Ms. Park,

Your request has been forwarded to the relevant department for review. Please allow an additional 20 business days.

Office of Records Management

---

**February 3, 2015**

Dear Ms. Park,

We are responding to your FOIA request dated October 3, 2014.

Enclosed please find: the 2003 survey summary report (12 pages), with the following redactions applied pursuant to Exemption 6 (personal privacy): paragraphs 3, 5, 7, and the personnel appendix.

The full field notes, measurement data, and internal correspondence have been withheld pursuant to Exemption 5 (deliberative process privilege). The decision to withhold these documents was reviewed by the Exemption Review Committee on January 29, 2015.

If you wish to appeal this determination, you may do so within 90 days by writing to the Appeals Office.

Office of Records Management

---

**February 17, 2015**

Dear Appeals Officer:

I am appealing the partial denial of my FOIA request (2014-0893).

The survey summary I received references measurement data that it says is "available in the technical appendix," but the technical appendix was withheld. I cannot evaluate the summary without the data the summary summarizes. The summary, alone, is not useful.

Specifically: the summary states that the gradient measurement "showed a statistically significant change from baseline" but provides no baseline figure, no current figure, and no methodology. The summary, without the underlying data, does not disclose anything.

I note that Exemption 5 applies to pre-decisional deliberative material, not to factual measurement data. The gradient readings are not deliberative. They are numbers.

Respectfully,
D. Park

---

**April 1, 2015**

Dear Ms. Park,

Your appeal has been received.

---

**July 14, 2015**

Dear Ms. Park,

Your appeal has been denied. The withheld documents were reviewed by the Exemption Review Committee and the original determination was upheld.

If you have further questions, you may contact the Records Office.

Office of Records Management

---

*The measurement data was released in 2019 under a subsequent FOIA request filed by a different requester who knew to ask for specific document numbers. The gradient baseline was 0.27. The 2003 reading was 0.31. This information was in the technical appendix the entire time.*
