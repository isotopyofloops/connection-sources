**FIELD NOTES — MILO REYES**
*Date: 29 November 2041 | Departure: 08:30*

*[Note: Milo's notes were transmitted in five segments as he passed through zones with intermittent signal. Segments received at: 08:42, 09:04, 09:31, 09:55, 10:03. The notes are reproduced in sequence.]*

**TRANSMISSION 1 | Received 08:42**

Departed 08:30. I wrote my prediction note last night. It's in the Archive's drawer. I'm not thinking about it.

Temperature at entry: 24.1°C. Same as it's been at the entry point since November 18th. The entry-point temperature is stable. Whatever is changing in the deeper chambers isn't propagating up to the surface interface.

I've been thinking, on the walk down, about what I said to Asad. About the aperture. About the question of home changing into the question of further.

I want to write it more precisely now, while I'm still close enough to the surface that I can think in normal terms.

What I meant was: when I was at the aperture, I knew I could find my way back. The path was clear. I turned around and came back because Abara had authorized a time limit. Not because I had hit a wall. Not because I was disoriented. I chose to come back.

The question of further was: why did I choose to come back? The scientific reasons were real. But underneath them, there was a reason I didn't file: I wasn't ready. I had understood enough to want to understand more, but I hadn't understood enough to trust where more would take me.

I'm thinking about this now because I want to be honest, on the record, about why I'm here. The scientific reasons are real. But I'm also here because I'm ready.

B-1 dimensions: 37.2m × 34.8m × 22.1m. Temperature: 30.4°C.

Continuing descent.

---

**TRANSMISSION 2 | Received 09:04**

G-1. North-south 43.8m, east-west 41.3m, height 22.1m. Temperature: 34.9°C.

The sentence on the south wall: "The instrument does not describe the measurement. The measurement is what the instrument does."

Abara's handwriting. I've read it four times across two visits. I read it again.

I want to record what the sentence does when you read it in this chamber, knowing what you know.

Before November 22nd, it would have been a philosophical statement. One position among several in the debate about measurement and constitution.

Now it is: an inscription made by a mechanism we don't understand, in a researcher's handwriting, in a chamber that expands when nobody is looking, stating the mechanism's own operating principle.

The sentence describes the chambers. The sentence is on the wall of the chamber it describes. The chamber is what the sentence says it is.

Milo's note to himself: if I find my handwriting on a wall somewhere today, I will not be surprised. This is what I expect not to find.

Actually it is. Let me be honest. I'll update the prediction note when I return. Negative space prediction: I expect NOT to find a chamber that is smaller than the ones already documented. I expect NOT to find a south-facing passage (all passages have been north). I expect NOT to find silence in the deepest chamber — I expect the deepest chamber to have a quality of reception, not silence.

These are predictions in the negative. They are now in the record.

Descending past G-1N.

---

**TRANSMISSION 3 | Received 09:31**

I am in a chamber I'm going to call M-1. I've passed through J-1 and J-1N (the aperture-adjacent chamber, where Asad found my supply bag and first knew what I meant). M-1 is beyond the range of any documented exploration.

M-1 dimensions: 22.4m × 22.3m × 22.1m.

It is almost a cube.

The height, for the first time, matches the horizontal dimensions. The chambers have been getting larger as I go deeper — 37m, 42m, 52m — and then M-1. 22m on all three axes. I have come out the other side of the expansion.

I'm calling it the cube.

Temperature: 40.1°C. I'm drinking water. I have sufficient supply.

The mark on the northwest wall of M-1 is different from the marks I've seen before. The previous marks were regions of organized texture — surface irregularities tracing a symbol I've learned to half-read. The mark in M-1 is not a symbol. It is a line. A single horizontal line, 22.1m long, running from the west wall to the north wall, at height 11.05m — exactly half the chamber height. The line is straight to a precision that would require a laser level. It runs at the exact midpoint of every surface it crosses.

A horizontal line at the midpoint height of a cubic chamber.

I am writing this down because I cannot interpret it and I want the record to be precise.

There is a passage in the south wall.

Every passage I have traveled, in this structure, has gone north. This is the first south-facing passage I have encountered. It is in the south wall, 1.2m wide, 22.1m high (the full chamber height). I can see perhaps 15m into it before the angle limits visibility.

This is the second item on my negative-space prediction list. I said I expected NOT to find a south-facing passage. There is a south-facing passage.

I'm noting the match and going in.

---

**TRANSMISSION 4 | Received 09:55**

The south passage is 31.4m long. At the end: a new chamber.

Designation: N-1.

N-1 dimensions: 28.3m × 26.7m × 22.1m. Temperature: 41.8°C. The height is consistent. The horizontal dimensions are smaller than M-1. The contraction continues.

The writing in N-1 covers all four walls, floor, and ceiling. The same organized surface texture I found in the aperture chamber, but denser. The symbol — the doubled-back curve with the interior grid — fills every visible surface. I am standing in a chamber whose entire interior is a continuous version of the mark.

I took photographs. I am sending them as a compressed attachment with this transmission.

There is a passage in the east wall of N-1. It was not flagged in UNIT-12's mapping data. The passage is 1.0m wide, 22.1m high. I can see approximately 8m before the visibility ends.

I'm going to estimate the chamber at the far end. This is what I'm constituting from outside, before entry:

Approximately 22m × 28m × 18m.

That last number — 18m — is the height estimate. The height in every documented chamber has been 22.1m, constant to within 0.1m. I am predicting a chamber with a different height. I don't know why I'm predicting this. It is what I know from outside.

I am noting the prediction and going in.

*[Attached: 47 photographs. Confirmed received at surface station: 10:01.]*

**TRANSMISSION 5 | Received 10:03**

Beginning return.

*—Milo Reyes | 29 November 2041*
