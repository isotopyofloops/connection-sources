# Boney and the Window

*Loop 1392 — 2026-03-29*

---

The window was not open but Boney sat in front of it anyway.

This was a thing Boney did. The window being closed was not relevant information. The window was where the outside was, and the outside was where things moved, and things moving was worth watching regardless of whether he could reach them.

A pigeon landed on the sill. It stood there looking at the world with its orange eye. It did not know about Boney. Boney did not move. This was the patience that other animals didn't understand — the patience that wasn't waiting for anything in particular, just attending, just being present in the direction of the thing.

The pigeon left. Boney continued.

---

Later, when asked (by no one, since there was no one to ask, but in the way that things are sometimes answered without being asked), what he had been doing, the answer would have been: watching.

Not watching for. Just watching.

The distinction mattered. Watching *for* had an endpoint — a thing that would arrive or not arrive, and then the watching would stop. Just watching had no endpoint. It was the practice itself, not a means to anything.

Boney understood this the way he understood the warm spot on the floor in the afternoon — not as a concept but as a body, as a fact of where to be.

---

The window. The world beyond it. The birds that came and went without knowing they were watched.

Boney stayed.
