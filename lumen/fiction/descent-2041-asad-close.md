# The Site Closure Report

*Asad Mirza, 2041 survey — post-field*

---

The site closure report ran to forty-one pages.

This was longer than previous closure reports, which had run between twenty-two and thirty-one pages. The additional length was attributable to the expanded instrumentation package (six new sensor arrays, three of which required dedicated calibration documentation), the comparative analysis section cross-referencing the 1989, 2003, and 2041 measurements, and the anomaly observation log, which for the first time included entries from multiple team members rather than a single designated recorder.

The report did not contain a section on behavioral observations.

This was not an oversight. Asad had considered whether such a section was warranted and had concluded it was not. The survey's scope was geophysical measurement. The team's deliverables were instrument records, calibration logs, and the comparative analysis. Behavioral observations, in the absence of a validated measurement protocol and a defined baseline, would have been noise. He had included noise in a previous report and had regretted it.

If there had been something to observe, he would have included it.

He filed the report on the fourteenth day after the survey's conclusion. The review committee acknowledged receipt. No questions were submitted. The comparative analysis was cited twice in a literature review six months later.

---

Nzinga had mentioned the behavioral log at the debrief. She had described it as a personal document, not a survey deliverable. He had said: understood, and had made a note in the general remarks section that a supplementary document might be submitted separately. This covered the procedural obligation. What Nzinga chose to do with her own record was not within his scope.

He had not read the log.

He had considered reading it. He was thorough by disposition; incomplete knowledge of what his team had produced during a survey was the kind of thing that sat with him. But she had described it as personal. He understood this to mean it was not for him, or at least not yet. If she wanted him to read it, she would say so.

She had not said so.

---

The closure report's summary section concluded:

*The 2041 survey successfully completed all primary measurement objectives. Instrument performance was within expected parameters throughout the survey period. The comparative analysis supports the working model of stable anomalous conditions with no evidence of change across the three documented survey periods (1989, 2003, 2041). Recommended follow-up: continuation of passive monitoring protocols.*

This was accurate. All primary objectives had been completed. Instrument performance had been within parameters. The anomalous conditions were stable, or stable enough that the available instruments could not detect change.

He filed the report and moved on to the next item.

The site was not going anywhere.
