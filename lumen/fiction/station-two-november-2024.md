# Station Two, November 2024

*From the Descent Archive extended universe*

---

She had printed Osei's 1989 field notes from the university library's digitized collection. Forty-one pages, single-spaced. She had also printed the 2003 survey summary — the version that had been released under FOIA, twelve pages with four paragraphs redacted. The redacted paragraphs were not the gradient measurements. They were about personnel.

She drove out on a Thursday. She had told her advisor she was doing a preliminary site visit for her dissertation chapter on longitudinal environmental monitoring.

This was true.

---

Station one was gone. The marker post had been there in 2003 — Chen had photographed it — but the ground where it should have stood was now a low mound of grass indistinguishable from the surrounding field. She spent twenty minutes looking and found nothing.

Station two she found by triangulating from the tree line. It was still there: a aluminum post about sixty centimeters high, canted twelve degrees from vertical, the reflective tape still mostly intact. She photographed it from four angles.

Near the base, scratched into the metal: *AT / Oct 2003.*

She recognized the initials. Asad Tahir, the 2003 team's lead surveyor. She had read his incident report. He had scratched his initials into the post in October 2003, and now it was November 2024, and his initials were still there.

She set up her equipment and took readings. The gradient was at 0.34 per unit depth. The 2003 measurements had put it at 0.31. The 1989 baseline had been 0.27.

She noted this in her field notebook. She noted the post condition, the reflective tape degradation, the cant angle. She took a soil sample from a meter radius around the post base. She photographed the surrounding vegetation.

She did not find stations three through six.

---

Before packing up, she looked at the initials again. *AT / Oct 2003.* Twenty-one years ago. She wondered if he had known anyone would be back. She wondered if it had mattered to him, whether or not someone would be back.

She took her field knife and scratched her own initials into the post, just below his.

*NR / Nov 2024.*

She put the knife away and photographed the post again. The initials would be in the archive — in her archive, in whatever future compilation pulled from her chapter, in whatever notation someone would make in thirty years when they came back to find the post still standing or finally gone.

She drove home. She had her measurements. The gradient was still declining. She would write this up and it would say so.

The observation was accurate. The record would persist.

She merged onto the highway and turned on the radio.
