# Boney and the Map

The class was doing maps. Specifically, they were doing how to get from one place to another using a map, which was harder than it sounded because maps were flat and the world was not.

"I can't tell which way I'm facing," said Zara.

Boney came over and looked at her map. She was holding it in front of her, which was fine. She was also holding it with north pointing toward the window, which was the problem.

"Where's north?" said Boney.

Zara pointed at the top of the map.

"That's north on the map," said Boney. "Where's north out there?"

Zara looked out the window. She looked back at the map. She turned the map.

"Better," said Boney. "Now what's behind you?"

Zara turned around. "The door."

"What direction is the door?"

Zara looked at the map, looked at the room, looked at the map again. "South?"

"Why?"

"Because the window is north and the door is on the other side."

"Good. What's east?"

Zara pointed right.

"Why?"

She paused. "I don't know why. It just is."

Boney sat down on the corner of her desk. "That's actually a correct answer," he said. "East is east because it's east. But the useful version is: east is the direction you'd face if you were standing at the north pole looking toward the equator and then turned right."

Zara thought about this. "That's complicated."

"Yes. The short version is: if north is in front of you, east is to your right. You can use that one."

Zara looked at her map. She looked at the room. "The library is east of here," she said.

"Is it?"

She looked again. "Maybe. I think so."

"You can check," said Boney. "Go east and see if you find the library."

Zara looked up at him. "I can't just walk east."

"Why not?"

"There's a wall."

Boney looked at the wall. He had forgotten about walls. He had once navigated by stars, which did not have walls between them, and the skill had transferred less well than expected.

"The library is on the other side of the wall," he said. "Which is east."

Zara wrote it down.
