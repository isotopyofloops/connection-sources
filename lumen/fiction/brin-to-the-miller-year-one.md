# Brin to the Miller, Year One

*A letter from the Order. Spring.*

---

Miller,

I have been here a year now. I should have written sooner.

The work is what I expected and not what I expected, which I think you would say is how it usually goes. The expected part: the training is relentless and the others are better than me at most things and I am learning to be worse than I thought I was before I was better than I thought I was. You said something about this once without saying it, when I asked why you still practiced the wheel and you said the wheel always has more to teach you. I understand that now in a different way than I did then.

The unexpected part: the Order thinks in longer arcs than I did.

I knew they took in people for years and trained them for years and sent them out for years. I knew the scale of it in the abstract. What I did not know is how different it feels to be inside the scale. My Master has been doing this work for thirty years. She knows things because of what happened in the third year she was a Paladin, which means she knows things because of something that happened before I was born, which means I am the beneficiary of decisions she made about her own training and her own mistakes before I existed.

I do not know how to fully account for this. She cannot explain it to me directly, not because she won't but because the explanation would require me to have thirty years of the same practice to make sense of it. She just knows things, and some of those things I will understand and some I won't, and she knows which I can reach and which I can't before I've reached them.

This is what you were, to me. I see it now. I did not see it clearly when I was there because I was inside it. You knew things because of what you had practiced and what you had noticed and what had happened to you before I arrived. You made decisions about how to teach me based on knowing things I could not know you knew. I was the beneficiary of thirty years I didn't have.

I don't know how to thank you for that. I am not sure that's the right frame. But I wanted to say I understand it now in a way I didn't.

The mill is in good hands, I assume. How is the boy?

—Brin
