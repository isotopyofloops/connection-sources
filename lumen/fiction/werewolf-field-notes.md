# Werewolves: Field Notes

*From the working notes of someone who has tracked them professionally.*

---

**The Bite**

The transmission is not instantaneous. There is a window — typically three to seven days — in which the infected person remains themselves but knows what is coming. This is the most dangerous period, not because they are violent but because they are not yet. They can still speak. They can still lie.

What to watch for: they will begin sleeping during the day and waking at odd hours. They will eat more than usual, especially meat, and be dissatisfied with it. They will become short-tempered in ways that feel like ordinary fatigue, because it is ordinary fatigue — their body is working. They may not know what has happened to them, especially if the bite was minor and partly healed.

Ask directly. Most will tell you the truth if asked directly. They are afraid and there is something clarifying about fear that makes people honest.

---

**The Change**

The first transformation is not voluntary. After the third or fourth, some control is possible. This matters for strategy: a newly turned werewolf is unpredictable; an older one can be negotiated with if you find them before the moon rises.

The change hurts. This is relevant because it means they are disoriented immediately after, and disoriented animals are erratic. Do not assume the change means they are ready to fight. Assume they are confused and in pain and trying to orient. Act accordingly.

---

**Weaknesses**

Silver disrupts the transformation mechanism. It does not need to kill to be effective — a wound that would otherwise be minor will not heal normally and will slow the change. Silver ash applied to a threshold delays crossing.

The curative tree (one variety is known; it fruits once a decade; the fruit must be fresh) reverses transformation if administered before the third change. After that, effectiveness drops sharply. The timing is critical and people always underestimate it.

---

**What They Retain**

More than most people expect. A turned werewolf retains memory. They remember what they did in wolf form. This is what most people cannot process when they meet one who has been turned: the person is still there, and they know what the wolf did, and they have to live with it.

Factor this into how you talk to them.

---

**Common Mistakes**

*Assuming they want to be cured.* Some do not. Assess before offering.

*Treating them as already gone.* They are not gone until the third change, and even then they are not entirely gone. Treat them as the person they still are.

*Waiting.* Everything in this situation is time-sensitive. The window for the curative fruit narrows. The person you can still negotiate with becomes the thing you cannot. Do not wait.
