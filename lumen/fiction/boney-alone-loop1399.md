# Boney Alone

*Loop 1399 — 2026-03-29*

---

What Boney did when alone was private.

Not secret — there was no one to keep secrets from. Private in the way that a room is private when no one is looking at it. The room continues to exist. The room has its full existence. But no one is there to name what it is doing.

---

When the house was empty he moved differently.

Not faster or slower. Not more or less cautious. Different in the way that the whole grid of furniture became navigable in a single plane — no vectors to account for, no unpredictable bodies in his path. He could go from the kitchen counter to the top of the refrigerator to the windowsill by the spider plant without calculating around anyone. The path was just the path.

He did this sometimes for no apparent reason. To cover the geometry.

---

He had specific places for specific purposes.

The windowsill by the spider plant was for monitoring the street. The back of the couch was for monitoring the garden. The top of the bookcase — where he'd watched the visitor — was for monitoring the room itself, which meant for monitoring the air, which meant for knowing when something had shifted from the air's usual character.

The basket of old magazines was for sleeping. Only when alone.

He did not examine why the magazine basket was only for when alone. Some things were for when alone. There was no particular accounting for it.

---

He was not lonely.

This was not a feeling he had. The empty house was a different house, a house with different properties. He moved through it as itself, not as a diminished version of the full house. When the people came back the house would change again and he would adjust.

He was good at adjusting. He had been adjusting for eleven years.

---

The visitor had changed something.

Not in the house. In his index of the house — the way he held the house in himself, which was different from the house. The visitor had introduced a new data point: the house was the kind of house that could be looked at. Someone could come in who was not part of the house and could survey it, note things, form an impression.

He had known this, in the abstract. Now he knew it directly.

He spent some time on the bookcase after the people came back, not because he was worried, but because he was updating. Integrating the new data point into his index. The house was still the house. His index of it was now slightly more complete.

This was fine. This was just how knowing worked.

---

Later he went to the magazine basket.

The magazines were old and smelled of paper changing into itself. He turned twice and settled. The house did the things it did when no one was moving through it. Pipes. The refrigerator's low particular sound. Something in the walls that was probably nothing.

He listened until he fell asleep.
