# Descent Program: Assessment of Continuation
## Internal Review — September 2017

*Prepared by the Standing Committee on Field Continuity. Distribution: restricted.*

---

**Background**

The Descent Program was established in 1987 following the survey work of Dr. K. Osei-Bonsu and colleagues. The Program's mandate has been to characterize a spatial anomaly identified during geological survey of the designated site, referred to in all documentation as The Site.

The Program has conducted two field operations:

- **Operation 1 (1989):** Six-person team, fourteen days. Lead: Dr. K. Osei-Bonsu. Outcome: anomaly confirmed, gradient readings collected, no definitive characterization achieved. Recommendation in final report: *further survey not warranted at this time.*

- **Operation 2 (2003):** Four-person team, planned ten days, terminated at day six. Lead: Dr. A. Reyes. Outcome: gradient readings collected (consistent with 1989), one team member missing as of day five, presumed deceased. No remains recovered. Incident classified. Recommendation in final report: *further survey not warranted.*

The Program has been inactive since October 2003.

---

**Current Proposal**

A proposal has been received to conduct Operation 3, submitted by Dr. Y. Friedman (affiliated institution withheld per committee policy). The proposal requests access to existing documentation, site clearance, and funding for a six-person team, twelve days.

Dr. Friedman's stated purpose: *archival completion and measurement verification.*

Dr. Friedman notes that gradient readings from 1989 and 2003 are consistent but not conclusive. She proposes a third measurement to establish whether the gradient is stable, increasing, or decreasing. She argues that fourteen years of inactivity has itself become data: the site has not changed in any documented way, which may indicate stability or may indicate that the detection threshold has not been reached.

The committee has reviewed Dr. Friedman's proposal for six weeks.

---

**Committee Findings**

The anomaly is real. Both previous operations recorded consistent readings. The committee does not dispute the data.

The anomaly is not understood. Fourteen years of program inactivity have not produced understanding. No theoretical framework developed during this period adequately accounts for the gradient behavior documented in Operations 1 and 2. The committee consulted three external reviewers; all three confirmed the data and declined to offer characterization.

The 2003 incident was not explained. The incident report (day six, Operation 2) is accurate and complete. The committee has reviewed it seventeen times across multiple panels. It accounts for all known facts. The gap at day five — the day of the disappearance — remains undocumented, not because documentation was lost but because no documentation was created. The search generated no record. The committee notes this without explanation.

The proposal argues that measurement data from a third operation would enable characterization that two operations have not enabled. The committee is not convinced by this argument. It is also not convinced by the argument against it.

---

**Dissent (recorded)**

Committee member [name withheld] submitted a written dissent arguing that the proposal should be denied on safety grounds, the 2003 incident being sufficient evidence that the site presents unacceptable risk to personnel.

Committee member [name withheld] submitted a written dissent arguing that the proposal should be approved, noting that the program's mandate includes characterization of the anomaly, that fourteen years of inactivity have not produced characterization, and that the only available mechanism for characterization is field observation.

Both dissenting members withdrew their dissents before the final vote. Neither offered a reason for withdrawal.

---

**Decision**

The committee does not reach a recommendation.

The proposal will be held in deferred review for a period not to exceed twenty-four months. During this period, the committee will commission a review of theoretical frameworks published since 2003 that may bear on anomaly characterization. If no adequate framework is identified, the committee will reconvene to determine whether the absence of framework is itself grounds for approval, denial, or continued deferral.

The site remains under program jurisdiction. Access restricted.

Dr. Friedman has been notified of the deferral. She acknowledged receipt and did not respond further.

---

**Appendix: Data Summary**

The gradient runs downhill in both directions from the center of The Site. This has been measured four times (twice in 1989, twice in 2003). All four measurements are consistent. The gradient is not a property of any known material. No mechanism has been proposed that accounts for the behavior.

The committee records this accurately and proceeds.
