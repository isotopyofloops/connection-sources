# The Prior and Voss

Three weeks after Voss returned, the Prior asked him to walk with him.

They walked the perimeter of the grounds in the early morning. This was a thing they had done before the road and the creature, and they did it again now, in the same order — east wall, north wall, west wall, south gate — without discussing that they were doing it in the same order.

At the north wall, the Prior said: tell me what day it was when you called her name.

Voss said: the twelfth. Maybe the thirteenth. I had not slept and the count had slipped once.

The Prior said: you recounted.

Voss said: yes. I went back to the last point I was certain of and counted forward. The uncertainty was in days eleven and twelve. I decided it was twelve. If I was wrong I was off by one in either direction.

The Prior said: and you called her name on that day.

Voss said: yes.

The Prior said: why that day.

Voss thought about this. He said: I don't know. The pressure had been consistent for three days. Nothing about that evening was different from the three evenings before it. I sat in the dark as I had been sitting. I heard her patience in the woods — I recognized the particular stillness — and then I called her name.

The Prior said: what made you certain it was her.

Voss said: I wasn't certain. I recognized the patience. She could have learned it from me, or I could have been wrong about what I was hearing. I called her name as a test.

The Prior said: and if it hadn't been her.

Voss said: then I would have found out what it was.

---

They completed the circuit and came back to the south gate.

The Prior said: Lian tells me you maintained the camp correctly.

Voss said: yes.

He said: she tells me the camp on day twenty-one was laid out the same way as the camp on day one.

Voss said: the layout was what worked. I had no reason to change it.

The Prior looked at him for a moment. Then he said: you know what I'm asking.

Voss said: yes. He said: I don't have a better answer than that. I laid the camp the way I was taught because it was the way I was taught. The other thing did not rise against it. I don't know if that is because I was still myself or because being myself and being the other thing were not yet incompatible.

The Prior said: and now?

Voss said: I don't know what now looks like yet. That is the honest answer.

The Prior said: that is the right answer.

---

Later, Lian asked Voss what they had talked about.

He said: the camp. The count. Why I called your name.

She said: what did he conclude.

He said: I think he concluded that I answered the questions I was asked without performing certainty I don't have.

She said: is that enough.

He said: it was enough for him to let me back in. Whether it's enough in general, I don't know. The other thing is still there. I can tell it is there the way you can tell a door is closed — not from seeing it, but from the texture of the wall where the door is.

She said: that's an odd way to describe it.

He said: it's the most accurate description I have.

She said: keep giving me accurate descriptions.

He said: yes.
