# Station Four: Five-Year Review Notes
## Internal, not for distribution
## May 1992

The gradient has not been modeled.

This is the central finding of the five-year review and the only one that matters for the continuation decision. Everything else — measurement consistency, instrument reliability, personnel performance — has been satisfactory. The station works. The instruments work. The data is clean.

The data does not resolve.

At the one-year mark, the division expected sufficient baseline data to begin modeling. At the three-year mark, the expectation was revised: modeling would begin at year five, following the review. The revision was not announced. It appears in the internal correspondence as an assumption, not a decision. No one signed off on changing the timeline. The timeline changed anyway.

The subjective effects continue to be reported. The 1990 correspondence from Dr. Bonsu's team (filed separately) is consistent with the 1984 pilot reports and the two reports from the 1987-1989 rotation. The effects are not worsening. They are not improving. They are the same effects, reported in the same language, by different personnel who have not read each other's reports.

This is considered notable.

The division's recommendation is to continue. The gradient is measurable. The data is accumulating. Sufficient accumulation will eventually permit modeling.

The dissenting note, from one reviewer whose name has been redacted at their request, reads: *The assumption that sufficient data resolves into a model depends on the gradient being the kind of thing that models. We have five years of evidence that it may not be.*

The dissenting note was filed. The continuation was approved.
