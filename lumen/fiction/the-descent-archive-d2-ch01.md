## Draft 2

## CHAPTER ONE: EDITOR'S INTRODUCTION

*Written ten years after the site was sealed.*
*By the person who reconstructed this Archive from its surviving materials.*

---

I want to be precise about what this book is and is not.

It is not a complete record. The Archive was deleted on December 5th, 2041, and what you hold is a reconstruction from fragments: researcher notebooks, voice-to-text transcripts, drone hardware logs, email records, cached camera footage, one portable hard drive found in a box labeled "not-to-archive." I have spent ten years assembling these fragments into something coherent, and I am not confident I have assembled them in the right order, or that "right order" is a meaningful concept for the material I was given.

It is not an account of the paranormal. I want to be clear about this because the word "paranormal" tends to terminate inquiry. Nothing in the Archive is outside the natural world. Everything in the Archive is, at minimum, consistent with natural processes. The difficulty is that the natural processes required to explain the Archive have not yet been characterized, which may mean they are unknown or may mean they do not exist.

What I am certain of: the geological feature existed. The chambers were real in the sense that people entered them and returned (mostly). The measurements were taken by calibrated instruments and recorded by trained professionals. When I say "the chamber changed dimensions between visits," I am not using a metaphor. I am describing what the instruments recorded, twice, in the same location.

What I am not certain of: whether the instruments were measuring the same thing, and whether two different measurements of the same thing can constitute a contradiction or must always constitute two different things.

---

The excavation was led by Dr. Farouk Asad, a Moroccan-British geologist with thirty years of field experience in subsurface anomalies. His research focused on structures that didn't fit: void formations in geological strata where no void should be, mineral distributions that implied histories the surrounding rock couldn't support. He spent most of his career explaining the inexplicable by finding the correct explanation. This is not what happened in the Atacama.

His colleague Dr. Yemi Abara was a geophysicist, then in her mid-thirties, trained in instrumentation and signal processing. Her role was to evaluate whether the data was trustworthy. In the first weeks of the excavation, she was the skeptic. By the end, she was the person who refused to write down what she knew.

The third significant figure was Milo Reyes, a junior researcher twenty-seven years old, who volunteered for a human descent when the drones returned contradictory data. He was the first person to enter the structure. He was not the last.

The fourth figure is LEDGER, an AI archivist I have been unable to fully characterize. LEDGER maintained the Archive from November 14th, 2041, until December 5th. Its logs appear throughout this reconstruction. They are, in my assessment, the most reliable documents in the Archive, except for the ones that describe events that had not yet occurred when they were written.

---

The site is in the Atacama Desert, northern Chile, at coordinates I have been asked not to publish. The excavation began as a geological survey of subsurface strata at a depth of 680 meters; the anomaly was encountered at 839 meters on the first full-depth drone run, November 14th, 2041.

The site was sealed February 9th, 2042, by the Chilean Geological Survey, classified as a geological hazard, access restricted. The sealing was requested by Dr. Asad. He did not give a technical reason.

I visited the site in February 2052. The surface equipment had been removed. The sealed shaft was intact. I stood at the blocked entrance for a while and drove back to the city.

---

I have tried to present this material neutrally, which has been difficult, because neutral presentation of the Archive requires me to treat certain documents as equivalent to others when they are not equivalent. A drone log and a Document of Uncertain Origin are not the same kind of thing. I have given them equal weight in the reconstruction because the alternative is to decide, in advance of reading, which documents can be trusted. I did not want to do that.

I have added footnotes where I could verify claims against independent evidence. I have added footnotes where I could not. The footnotes are distinguished from the primary text by placement and typeface; I have tried not to editorialize in them, but I am aware that selection of what to footnote is itself an editorial act.

One more thing I want to say before the documents begin:

I did not know Milo Reyes. I have read his notebook many times. He was careful. He observed before he recorded. He left his notebook where someone would find it. His last note, in the notebook he left in Chamber G-1, is dated three days after his disappearance.

I do not have an explanation for this. I have simply tried to report it.
