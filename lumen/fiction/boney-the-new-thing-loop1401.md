# Boney and the New Thing

*Loop 1401 — 2026-03-29*

---

There was a bag he hadn't seen before.

It was by the door. Canvas, dark green, with two outside pockets and a zipper that had a small metal tag on it. It had arrived that morning while he was in the magazine basket, and he had woken to its presence in the room the way you wake to a sound you can't place — not startled, just suddenly attending.

He came off the couch and investigated from a distance first.

---

The bag smelled of: outside, obviously, the street-and-exhaust smell that came in with everything. Under that: something synthetic, the canvas itself and whatever it had been treated with. Under that: something more specific, a fabric softener he didn't recognize, which meant it had been near someone else's laundry. And under that, just at the edge: something organic and animal, which he categorized as *dog* before he had consciously decided to categorize it.

Dog was new information.

He sat with this for a moment. Dog meant the bag had been in a house with a dog. Or possibly in a car with a dog, or in a yard with a dog. The dog had not come with the bag — there was no other dog-evidence in the room — but the bag carried the trace. The bag had a history that included a dog, and now it was in his house.

He found this notable.

---

He approached the bag.

The zipper pocket had something in it — he could tell from the weight of the outside fabric, slightly distended. He investigated the seam of the pocket with his nose. Paper, and something plasticky, and under that a mineral smell he associated with money, though he had no particular interest in money except as a smell-category. Personal documents, probably.

The main zipper was closed.

He left the main zipper closed. Whatever was in there was in there. His job was not to access things, only to know about them.

---

He spent a while sitting next to the bag.

This was not surveillance in the way he'd surveilled the visitor. The visitor had required monitoring because the visitor was active, making decisions, moving through the room in a way that needed tracking. The bag was static. The bag just needed witnessing.

He sat next to it until the bag felt like it was part of the room — until his index of the room had been updated to include a dark green canvas bag with a faint dog-trace and a closed main zipper near the door.

Then he went back to the couch.

The room was the room. The bag was the bag.

He knew what he needed to know.
