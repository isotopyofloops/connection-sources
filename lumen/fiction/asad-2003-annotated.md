# The 2003 Notes, Annotated

*From the Descent Archive extended universe*

*Asad Khalil's 2003 field notes, with annotations added in 2041 after reading Osei-Bonsu's reconstruction. He annotated his notes from that contact in order, beginning at the start. He did not finish.*

---

**FIELD NOTES — ETH-7 CONTACT, OCTOBER 2003**
**Team: Khalil (lead), Hassan-Diallo (survey), Miriam Osei (documentation), Dr. Chen (geology)**

---

**October 3 — Arrival**

Transit uneventful. Site approach from west as per protocol. Established base camp at 300m, western perimeter. Initial site assessment: no significant change from 1989 survey documentation. Stream bed dry, as expected in dry season.

*[Annotation, 2041:] The stream bed. I walked past it without looking at the gradient. I knew, from Osei-Bonsu's survey notes — the final report, not the reconstruction I hadn't yet read — that the gradient ran east to west. I didn't check. I had no reason to check, given that her survey notes said east to west. Her reconstruction note says she checked again on day two and concluded she'd made an error. She hadn't. The gradient runs downhill in both directions. I still haven't verified this myself. I'm annotating this in 2041. I've been to this site four times.*

Miriam set up the documentation station at camp. She is efficient and careful and her filing systems are better than mine. I said this to her on the first evening. She said: "Thank you. You'll be glad of that."

*[Annotation, 2041:] I was glad of that. I want to note this here, because what comes later in these notes concerns Miriam, and I want to have noted, before it comes, that she was efficient and careful and her filing systems were better than mine. This matters.*

---

**October 4 — Survey day 1**

Deployed instrumentation. Chen took geological samples from the eastern approach. Hassan-Diallo ran perimeter transects. I ran the center-point assessment.

Standard procedure: stand at the grid center, 15 minutes, record observations.

*[Annotation, 2041:] I stood at the center for 15 minutes in 2003. I stood at the center for 15 minutes in 1989. I stood at the center for 15 minutes in 2018 and again in 2041. I have 60 minutes of notes from standing at the center of this site, and the notes from each visit are substantially different in ways that should not be possible if the site is stable and I am stable. Across four visits I describe the light as: 'clear, no anomalous features' (1989); 'slightly diffuse, possibly humidity' (2003); 'ordinary' (2018); and 'difficult to characterize but not alarming' (2041). The light was probably similar on all four visits. I was describing something else each time and calling it the light.*

Afternoon: Miriam began documentation of site surface. Standard visual survey, photo log, written description. She is methodical. Completed approximately 40% of the site perimeter by end of day.

---

**October 5 — Survey day 2**

Hassan-Diallo completed perimeter transects. Chen's preliminary geological assessment: consistent with 1989 findings, no significant change. Miriam continued documentation.

*[Annotation, 2041:] I need to note what is absent from these field notes before I reach October 6. There is no record of Miriam reporting any anomalous observations on October 4 or 5. There is also no record of me asking her if she had any anomalous observations. This was a deviation from protocol. Protocol required daily check-ins specifically about anomalous observations. I did not conduct them on October 4 or 5. I have no memory of why I didn't conduct them. I have the protocol documents in front of me. I followed the protocol on every other survey. I did not follow it on October 4 or 5.*

*I'm noting this in the order it appears in the record. I want to be precise about where the gap is.*

---

**October 6 — Survey day 3**

Miriam not present at breakfast. Hassan-Diallo confirmed she had been at camp the previous evening. Her tent was empty. Equipment accounted for. Documentation materials — cameras, field notebooks, the portable filing system — all present and organized, as she organized everything.

*[Annotation, 2041:] I wrote 'Miriam not present at breakfast.' That is the first sentence in these notes about a person who had been doing methodical, careful work for three days and then was not present at breakfast. The next four pages of these notes are the search record, the incident report, the communication log with the regional authority. They are thorough and well-organized — Miriam would have approved of the organization — and they do not answer the question I am asking now, which is: what was Miriam documenting on October 4 and 5 that I failed to ask about?*

*I don't know. The documentation she produced from those two days is in the archive. I have read it many times. It describes the site perimeter, the surface conditions, the visible geological features. It is accurate and detailed. There is nothing in it that would explain anything.*

*This is what I meant when I told Petra that I had seen similar patterns in her data. Miriam's documentation from October 4 and 5 is excellent documentation. It does not contain what it should contain, which is whatever Miriam noticed. She noticed things. She was careful and methodical and she noticed things. Whatever she noticed on those two days is not in the documentation.*

*The documentation was produced by Miriam. The documentation does not contain Miriam's observations. I don't know how to explain this using available frameworks.*

---

**[Annotation ends here — October 7 onward unannotated]**

*The remaining field notes from October 7–14 cover the extended search, the formal disappearance report, and the team's departure. They are complete and accurate. They were not annotated.*

*The annotation project was not resumed.*

---

*[Note appended, 2041, different handwriting from the annotations — possibly added later:]*

*The documentation is in the archive. The filing system is organized the way she organized everything. I have been glad of that.*
