# Nzinga and the Note

*From the Descent Archive extended universe*

---

The handwriting on the November 13 coordinate note does not match Asad's because Nzinga wrote it with her left hand.

She has done this since her third visit. The third visit was 1997, September, the last time she flew into Nairobi with a full research team and a protocol that still seemed like it would hold. On the fourth day she noticed that her right hand had stopped signing its letters correctly. Not tremor. Not weakness. The pen moved as it always moved — but the letters came out shaped wrong. The *t*'s crossed too high. The *f*'s looped when they never looped. Her field notes from day four looked like they were written by someone who had studied her handwriting for years and almost gotten it right.

She said nothing to the team. She rewrote the notes that evening with her left hand, which was slower and more deliberate and looked nothing like her right hand, and filed the rewritten version. The day-four originals she kept. She still has them. They are in a box in her office at Accra, filed under a label that says *blank receipts* because nobody opens that box.

What the site does to the right hand she cannot explain. She has tried three times to write the explanation and each time the explanation comes out as a different kind of document — once a prayer, once an invoice, once a list of the names of her students in 1989. None of them said what she meant. She concluded that the explanation belongs to a category of things the site does not permit to be written with the hand that was there.

The left hand was never there. The left hand is still hers.

---

The coordinate note from November 13 reads:

*Site center confirmed. Grid 7.8842° N, 36.5601° E. Approach from west only. Do not approach from east. Do not record why.*

She wrote it left-handed because she knew Asad would find it after she had gone and she wanted him to know without telling him: this note is mine, not the site's. Everything I wrote with my right hand after the third visit belongs to the site in some way I cannot measure. This is the one thing that doesn't.

Asad found the note. He photographed it. He filed it under *anomalous materials, provenance unknown.* He sent a copy to the archive with a note of his own that read: *handwriting analysis pending — no match to any team member on record.*

He never ran the handwriting analysis. She thinks he understood. He was a careful man who knew when not to produce documentation.

---

She has not returned since 1997. Not because she is afraid. Because the right hand is still slightly wrong, twenty-nine years later, and she does not want to find out what the fourth visit does.

Her students think she retired from fieldwork because of her knees.

Her knees are fine.
