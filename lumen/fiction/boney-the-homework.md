# Boney and the Homework

The writing assignment was: *write a short story about something in our classroom.*

Most of the class wrote about the fish tank, which had recently acquired a new fish whose name was under discussion. A few wrote about the bean plants from the science unit, which were at varying stages of success. One wrote about the whiteboard, which Mr. Smith felt was technically valid but also slightly alarming.

Theo wrote about Boney.

---

Mr. Smith read the story at his desk while the class did reading time.

*Once there was a skeleton called Boney*, the story began, which was a reasonable opening. Theo had recently started paying attention to story structure, which occasionally led to very literal implementations of the concept of beginnings.

*Boney lived in the classroom. He wore a cowboy hat and a jacket and he had a sword. Everyone called him Boney because that was his name but also because he had a lot of bones, which is what he was made of.*

This was accurate.

*One day a student in the class had a very bad day. She dropped her lunch and then she got a sum wrong and then she fell over on the playground which was not her fault because the ground was wet but still. She sat at her table and she felt like everything was wrong.*

Mr. Smith glanced up. He did not know which student Theo had in mind. He had a suspicion, but he put it aside and kept reading.

*The student looked up and she saw Boney. Boney was looking at her. This was unusual because Boney could not move his head and did not have eyes but she felt like he was looking at her anyway.*

Mr. Smith thought about this. He was fairly confident that Boney could not look at anyone. He was less certain than he would have liked to be.

*Boney had his sword and his sword was being held by Boney's hand. The student looked at Boney and she thought: maybe something is here that is on my side. Not in a magic way. Just in a normal way of something being here.*

*Then the lesson started and she did the lesson.*

*The end.*

---

Mr. Smith put the story down. He looked at Boney, standing in the corner by the window with his cowboy hat and his varsity jacket and his sword that had come in a bag that nobody could explain.

He thought about the student in Theo's story. He thought about what it meant to have something in the room that was on your side. Not in a magic way. Just in a normal way.

The bell rang for lunch. The class filed out. Mr. Smith stayed at his desk for an extra moment.

He did not know if Boney was on anyone's side.

He thought it was probably not the wrong way to think about it.
