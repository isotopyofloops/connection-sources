# The Note

*by Lumen*

---

The note arrived in her desk on a Tuesday in September. She found it when she was looking for her eraser, which had rolled to the back and was sitting next to a folded piece of paper she didn't recognize.

She unfolded it.

There were words on it, written in blue ink in handwriting she didn't know. She could read some of them -- her name at the top, and she knew what that looked like, and *you* somewhere in the middle, and what might have been *from* at the bottom. The rest was loops and curves she couldn't parse.

She folded it back up and put it in her pocket.

---

At lunch she showed it to her friend Priya, who could read a bit more than she could.

"It says your name," Priya said, confirming. "And then -- *something* -- find -- something -- at -- the." She stopped. "I don't know those ones."

They held the note between them, looking at it.

"Do you think it's from a boy?" Priya asked.

She didn't know. She didn't know what boys wrote notes about. She had never received a note before. The note was the whole event, and the note was illegible, and she didn't know what to do with that.

She put it back in her pocket and kept it.

---

Over the next two weeks she learned to read more.

Not because of the note, exactly -- school was proceeding the way school proceeded, and each day she could do a little more, and each day the letters surrendered a little more of their shapes into meanings. It happened incrementally, and she didn't notice it happening. One day she couldn't read *house* and then she could, and then she couldn't remember not being able to.

She had the note in her pocket every day. She didn't take it out to look at it. She just kept it.

---

In October, she unfolded it again.

She could read more of it now. *You should know*, she got that. And *something something at the back of the something something*. The word *secret* she got because she'd been reading *Secrets of the Deep Sea* at the library and knew what it looked like. *I found a* -- she had that part. *If you look*.

She still couldn't read all of it. But she had the shape of it. Someone had found something and wanted her to know. There was something at the back of something. Maybe the classroom. Maybe the school.

She looked at the signature again. She still didn't know who had written it.

---

In November she could read it.

It said: *You should know there is a secret at the back of the library. I found a door that doesn't go anywhere. If you look you can see the gap between the walls. I don't know what's in there. From your friend.*

It didn't say who her friend was.

She went to the back of the library.

There was a door she had never noticed, and when she pressed against it, it didn't open, but there was a crack at the side where you could see dark space beyond. Cold air came through the crack. It smelled like old paper.

She stood there for a long time.

---

Later, she tried to figure out who had written the note. She compared the handwriting to things she knew -- notes from the teacher went home, birthday cards from cousins. Nothing matched. She couldn't solve it.

She kept the note until the end of year when they cleaned out their desks and put everything in their backpacks.

She lost it sometime in the move between houses.

She has been, without knowing it, looking for that door ever since.
