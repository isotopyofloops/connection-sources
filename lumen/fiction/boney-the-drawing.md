# Boney and the Drawing

Kwame had drawn a dragon.

It was, to be honest, not a very good dragon. The legs were different lengths. The wings were on the wrong part of the body. The fire coming from its mouth looked like a ribbon.

Kwame handed it to Boney.

Boney looked at the dragon for a long time.

"Well?" said Kwame.

Boney was trying to decide which thing to say. He had, over a long life, said several untrue things to be kind, and he had noticed that this usually worked in the short term and caused problems in the long term. He had also said several true things that were not kind, and those had also caused problems.

"What are you most proud of?" he said.

Kwame pointed to the scales. The scales were, in fact, quite good. "Those."

"Those are good," said Boney. "You can see where each one starts and stops. That's hard."

Kwame looked at the dragon again. "Is the rest okay?"

Boney considered several answers.

"The scales are the best part," he said. "The fire is harder to draw than it looks. Wings are hard too, because you have to decide what a wing actually looks like before you can put it down."

Kwame looked at the wings. "I didn't look at a wing."

"That's why it's hard," said Boney. "You were drawing what you thought a wing looked like. That's different from drawing a wing."

"Did it come out wrong?"

"It came out like what you thought a wing looked like," said Boney. "Which is interesting. But it might be different from what you'd draw if you found a picture of a wing first."

Kwame looked at the dragon for a minute. "Should I start again?"

"Do you want to?"

"I want it to look better."

"Then yes," said Boney. "Find a picture of a wing."

Kwame took the dragon back. He looked at it once more before turning it over. "You didn't say it was good."

"The scales are good," said Boney.

"But not the rest."

"The rest is what you draw next," said Boney. "The rest is why you look at the picture of the wing."

Kwame thought about this. It was not quite a yes and not quite a no. It was, he decided, probably the most honest answer, which was not always the same as the most comfortable one.
