# Paladin: The Messenger

*Follows "The Return." The day after.*

---

The king's man arrived before noon.

He was not what Edric expected — not armored, not official-looking. A courier in road clothes, on a horse that had been ridden hard. He found the master and Edric in the square and said without preamble: "The fruit. I'm here for the fruit."

The master looked at him for a moment. "Sit down," he said. "I'll get you something to eat."

"I don't need to eat. I need the fruit."

"I know," the master said. "Sit down."

---

The courier's name was Pell. He had been on the road for six days, following the route the king's men had mapped when they confirmed the tree's location. He had a sealed authorization from the king's secretary and a transport case lined with cloth and instructions on how to handle the fruit — no direct sunlight, stable temperature, do not bruise. He had memorized the handling instructions because the king had asked him personally, and you remembered what the king asked you personally.

He looked at the cloth Edric put on the table. The empty cloth, folded.

"Where is the fruit," he said.

Edric told him.

He told him about the master's bite and the first fruit; about the creature moving through the village for three weeks, and what the testimony had described; about the second fruit and Tomás, who was sleeping now in the granary wrapped in three borrowed blankets. He did not leave anything out. He said it plainly, the way the master had taught him to say difficult things: without apology, without elaboration. Just the sequence.

Pell was quiet for a long time. He looked at the empty cloth. He looked at Tomás sleeping in the granary. He looked at the tree, which stood in the square exactly as it had, still bearing nothing, indifferent to everything that had happened in its vicinity.

"The king," he said finally, and stopped.

Edric waited.

"He has had the condition for eleven years," Pell said. "He has managed it. He can continue to manage it. But the window was — this was the window. The survey said the tree fruits once every decade. Next time he may not be — the window may not align."

He stopped again. Edric did not know what to say, so he said nothing.

"You made the right decision," Pell said. He said it like a man who had been deciding whether to say it and had decided yes. "Both times. I can see that. I want you to know I can see that." He folded the empty cloth and put it in the transport case and closed the clasp. "I will have to report what I found."

"Yes," Edric said.

"The report will say the fruit was used. Both fruits."

"Yes."

"The report will not say whether you were right or wrong to use them." He picked up the transport case. "That question is above the rank of the report."

He finished the food the master had brought him. He thanked them for the hospitality. He rode back the way he came.

---

Tomás left the next morning, walking east toward a village two days away where people were waiting to learn what had become of him. He asked no questions about the tree or the king or what his being alive had cost. He had been gone six weeks and was too tired for questions. He wanted to go home.

Edric watched him go.

"He doesn't know," Edric said.

"No," the master said.

"Should he?"

The master considered this for a long moment. "I don't know," he said. "What would he do with it?"

Edric didn't know either. He thought about Tomás walking east, not knowing. He thought about the king, managing his condition for another decade if the window had closed, not knowing that the person who had cost him the fruit was a shepherd who had wanted to go home.

He thought about what the training was for.

"I still got it wrong," he said.

"Which part."

"The part about what the training is for. You said it was close but not quite right." He looked at the road Tomás had taken. "I've been thinking about it since."

The master looked at him. "Have you found it?"

"I think — the training isn't for thinking inside the things you have no training for. It's for knowing that you have to act anyway. Even when you haven't thought through all of it. Especially then."

The master was quiet for a moment. "That is closer," he said.

He did not say what was still different. Edric noted this and did not ask.
