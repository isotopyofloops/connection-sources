**SOAR-0013 — ASAD'S FIRST RESPONSE | 20 November 2041, 18:30**

I've spent two hours with the notebook.

The later entries are in Milo's handwriting. I know Milo's handwriting — I've read his field reports for three years. The letter formation, the pressure marks, the way he abbreviates his own name ("M.R.") in the margin notes. It's his.

I had the paper and ink analyzed using the portable materials-testing kit. Both the "present" entries (November 19th) and the "future" entries (November 25th–28th) show identical paper aging, identical ink aging, identical chemical composition. The analysis has a margin of error that doesn't allow distinguishing entries written days apart. This means the analysis cannot rule out that all entries were written on the same date, and it cannot rule out that they were written on their nominal dates.

The physical evidence is consistent with the notebook being authentic. It is also consistent with the later entries having been written on November 19th and backdated.

I want to be precise about what "backdated" would require. If Milo backdated the entries — wrote November 25th entries on November 19th — then he would have needed to know what Abara would do on November 20th, including that she would stand at the east wall of G-1N-2 for eleven minutes. This is not a description of future events that could be derived from existing data. Standing at a specific wall for a specific duration is not predictable from sensor data or pattern analysis.

If Milo backdated the entries with accurate future content, he had precognitive access to those events.

If the entries were written on the dates they claim, the document was composed after the events it describes — but from a temporal vantage point in November 25th–28th, looking back at November 20th.

Both possibilities require that Milo had access to information about November 20th from a vantage point he was not occupying on November 19th.

I'm not sleeping tonight.

**SOAR-0014 — ASAD'S SECOND RESPONSE | 21 November 2041, 02:15**

I've been working through the November 25th–28th entries.

The November 25th entry begins: "G-1N-2 has expanded. North-south axis 44.1m, east-west 41.7m, height 22.1m. Abara was here yesterday. She stood at the east wall for eleven minutes. She didn't write anything."

Note: height 22.1m. The height has not changed.

Note: G-1N-2 has expanded. On November 20th, when Abara measured the chamber, it was north-south 40.3m, east-west 38.1m. The notebook says it will be 44.1m × 41.7m on November 25th. That's an increase of 3.8m × 3.6m in five days.

If the chambers are growing, the growth rate is approximately 0.76m per day in the north-south axis and 0.72m per day in the east-west axis. These rates are consistent, which suggests a uniform expansion process.

The height remains constant during the expansion.

I want to write down what this suggests about the structure. If the chambers are expanding horizontally while maintaining constant height, then the constraint on height is different from the constraint on horizontal extent. The height is fixed by something that expansion doesn't affect. The horizontal extent is expanding into the surrounding rock.

I should note: the expansion rate is not detectable on a day-to-day basis in the chambers we've already surveyed. If B-1 is expanding at 0.7m/day, UNIT-7's measurement on November 14th and UNIT-9's measurement on November 16th should show a 1.4m difference in at least one axis. UNIT-9's measurement showed B-1 at north-south 41.2m versus UNIT-7's 41.3m — a difference within measurement tolerance, not 1.4m.

One explanation: the expansion rate is not uniform. It may be faster deeper in the network and slower closer to the surface. The chambers near the entry point (B-1, A-1) may expand more slowly or not at all. The chambers deeper in (G-1N-2) expand faster.

Another explanation: the chambers we've surveyed previously don't expand. Only chambers that haven't been visited yet are expanding.

I'm going to stop here and note what I just wrote: "only chambers that haven't been visited yet are expanding." This is the constitutive hypothesis in a different form. If the visit constrains the chamber's properties, then an unvisited chamber is free to have different properties at each potential visit. What "expansion" describes, under this interpretation, is not growth but the range of possible states an unvisited chamber can occupy.

This is the thing I've been not writing.

I've written it now.

**SOAR-0015 — ASAD'S THIRD RESPONSE | 21 November 2041, 10:45**

I need to address what the notebook says about Milo.

The November 26th entry reads: "I can't find my way back. The passages are still here but the configuration has changed. Yesterday I went north through three passages and found a chamber I didn't recognize. I tried to go south and found the same three passages in the same configuration. I went through them south and found the chamber I was in when I started."

This describes a topology problem. Going north three passages and south three passages returning to the original location is only possible if the passage network has no consistent directionality — if north and south through the same passages lead to the same place.

A network of chambers and passages that has this property is not a network of distinct rooms connected by distinct corridors. It is a structure where spatial relationships are not consistent across traversals.

The November 27th entry: "I have water for four more days. I am warm but not dangerously warm. I don't think I'm dying. I think I might be here for a long time."

The November 28th entry, last page, mid-sentence: "The mark on the northwest wall is —"

I've read this sentence fragment thirty times. It ends at the bottom of the last written page. The next page is blank. Either Milo stopped writing at that moment, or the notebook contains entries up to that moment, or the sentence continues somewhere I can't access.

I'm going to go underground today. I'm going to find Milo.

Communication loss is consistent with depth, structural interference, or something we haven't characterized.

The atmospheric readings in B-1 are breathable. Milo is presumably deeper than B-1N. We don't know the atmospheric composition deeper in the network. His supply of portable atmosphere analysis equipment is limited.

I've submitted an emergency procurement request for additional personnel and equipment. The nearest team with the right expertise is eight days away.

I'm scheduling myself for a supervised descent on November 24th, pending authorization from the safety officer. Abara will serve as surface coordinator.

I'm also filing this as SOAR-0016: a formal statement that I, Khalid Asad, believe Milo Reyes is alive, that the notebook is authentic, and that the chambers at 839m have properties that depend on observation in ways that conventional geological science cannot explain.

I said it. It's in the record.
