I'm going to present UNIT-9 and UNIT-10 in sequence and then open the floor. I want everyone's read before I give mine.

That's unusual.

**Asad**

Yes.

**11:04 — UNIT-9 Summary presented (Asad)**

UNIT-9 confirmed Chamber A-1. The geometry matches UNIT-7 within tolerance. This is the result that supports the calibration-error interpretation — UNIT-8 probably found A-1 in a miscalibrated state, and UNIT-9's clean reading confirms the chamber's real dimensions.

I'm going to pause there before moving to the temperature data. Abara, does that summary change your read from yesterday?

**Abara**

It improves it. If UNIT-9 matches UNIT-7, I'd say Interpretation 1 is back above 50%.

**Petra**

I'd agree. The geometry confirmation is what we needed.

**Milo**

Before you move on — what did UNIT-9 find in the temperature?

**Asad**

Twenty-one point four Celsius. UNIT-7 measured fourteen point two.

*[Silence.]*

**Abara**

That's... forty-eight hours.

**Asad**

Yes.

**Abara**

Seven degrees in forty-eight hours.

**Asad**

Yes.

**Petra**

Geothermal?

**Asad**

UNIT-7, UNIT-8, and UNIT-9 all checked for heat-source indicators. None found.

**Petra**

Sensor error on the thermal reading?

**Asad**

Both GEOMAP-9 units measured consistent temperatures within their own datasets. UNIT-7 measured 14.2°C across multiple readings in A-1. UNIT-9 measured 21.4°C across multiple readings in A-1. The units agree with themselves. They disagree with each other.

**Abara**

So the thermal change either happened or the sensor is wrong about the temperature but right about the geometry.

**Asad**

That's the question I don't have an answer to. Now let me present UNIT-10.

---

**11:22 — UNIT-10 Summary presented (Asad)**

UNIT-10 used a TERRASCAN-4 package. Different manufacturer, different acoustic resonance algorithm. It did not find Chamber A-1. It did not find Chamber B-1. It found a third chamber. North-south axis 39.4m, east-west 36.1m, height 22.1m.

*[Silence. 4 minutes, 7 seconds. No audio input detected.]*

**Abara**

Height.

**Asad**

Yes.

**Abara**

The height is the same.

**Asad**

22.1 meters in all three chambers. Within measurement tolerance across all sensor packages.

**Petra**

That's —

**Milo**

That can't be coincidence.

**Petra**

It's not impossible. If the chambers are in the same geological layer, and the layer has a consistent vertical thickness —

**Milo**

Three different horizontal extents and the same height. The layer thickness would have to be controlling for height across a span of eight meters east-west while allowing variation in the horizontal plane. That's not how layers work.

**Petra**

I'm aware of how layers work.

**Milo**

Sorry.

**Petra**

No. You're right. That's not a geological layer effect.

**Asad**

TERRASCAN-4 also detected the 0.87-meter acoustic pattern. The same interval as GEOMAP-9. Different algorithm, same interval.

**Abara**

So it's not an artifact.

**Asad**

I don't see how it can be.

**Abara**

The pattern is real.

**Asad**

The pattern is present in the material. I can tell you it's measured. I can't tell you what it is.

**Abara**

*[Long pause.]*

Okay. I want to put the calibration-error interpretation to rest. UNIT-10 used independent hardware and found a third chamber of the same type at the same depth. That's not what a calibration error looks like. A calibration error gives you wrong measurements of a real object. It doesn't give you a third real object.

So we have three chambers. Possibly more.

**Asad**

Possibly more.

**Milo**

The north passages.

**Asad**

Say more.

**Milo**

B-1 had a north passage: 0.9 meters wide, 2.1 meters high. C-1 had a north passage: same dimensions. A-1 didn't. Either A-1 doesn't have a north passage and B-1 and C-1 do, or A-1 has a north passage and we missed it, or something about when we visited A-1 — UNIT-7 and UNIT-9 — means the passage wasn't there then.

**Asad**

There's a fourth option you're not saying.

**Milo**

I'm not saying it yet.

**Asad**

We're going to need to say it eventually.

**Milo**

I know. I'd like more data first.

**Asad**

So would I.

---

**11:48**

**Abara**

I want to go back to the temperature. Seven degrees in forty-eight hours in a sealed chamber with no heat source. This doesn't fit multiple-chambers either. If there are three separate static chambers, one of them — A-1 — is heating at a rate that can't be explained. That's a second anomaly on top of the geometry anomaly. We're not simplifying by adding more chambers. We're adding more problems.

**Petra**

Unless the heating is why the chambers are different.

**Asad**

Say that again.

**Petra**

If the chambers aren't stable — if something about the space at 839m is changing — then the temperature changing and the geometry changing might be part of the same process. Not two anomalies. One anomaly with two expressions.

**Abara**

What would a changing chamber look like physically?

**Petra**

I don't know. That's why I'm asking the question instead of answering it.

**Asad**

I've been thinking about this for three days. I've been resisting thinking about it for three days. The geometry. The temperature. The humidity. The north passage that appears and doesn't appear. The height that doesn't change. The 0.87-meter pattern in the walls. If you looked at all of those as a single system —

**Abara**

You'd need a mechanism.

**Asad**

Yes.

**Abara**

And there isn't one.

**Asad**

Not in any literature I've found.

**Milo**

What would we need to see before we were allowed to propose the mechanism we're not proposing yet?

*[Silence.]*

**Asad**

I don't know. Something that can't be explained by the alternatives. The alternatives are: sensor error — very low probability now, I'm putting it at 10% — and multiple static chambers with independent heating problems. If I can't explain the temperature rise in A-1 with multiple static chambers, and I've looked, then — I'm at 25%. For the thing we're not proposing.

**Abara**

Twenty-five percent for a mechanism with no precedent and no known physics.

**Asad**

It's an honest number. Twenty-five percent for "something we don't understand" isn't 25% for a specific bizarre hypothesis. It's 25% for "the other interpretations are wrong."

**Abara**

That's still 25% for something that would require us to revise a significant amount of physics.

**Asad**

I know.

Not yet. I want one more drone run first. UNIT-11 — dual sensor packages, independent calibration, 25-meter GPS lock. If UNIT-11 finds what we've found, I want to go in.

I want to understand what we're measuring before we send a person in.

Agreed.

Are you going to share yours?

**Asad**

After UNIT-11 returns. Yes.
